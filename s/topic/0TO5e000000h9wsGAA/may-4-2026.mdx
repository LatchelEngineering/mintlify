The following are the Latchel updates for the week of May 5, 2026.

### **New Features**

1. **Improved Duplicate Detection: Multiple Open Job**

We've overhauled how Latchel handles situations where multiple work orders come in for the same property or issue. Here's what now happens automatically:

- **True duplicate:** A WO submitted for the same issue as another open WO is automatically cancelled as a duplicate
- **Potential recall:** A WO submitted for the same issue as a WO completed in the last 6 months automatically triggers a recall project
- **Shared SP:** A new issue that can be handled by the same SP already assigned to another open work order at the property → automatically assigned to that same vendor (they're notified and can remove themselves if needed)
- **Same category, no SP yet:** A new issue in the same category as an open job with no SP assigned → new WO stays in Pending until a vendor is assigned on the other job, then auto-assigned to the same SP Less manual coordination, fewer duplicate dispatches, and better vendor continuity — handled for you.

2. **More Control Over Work Order Budgets**

A quick refresher on how Latchel's budget system works — because this update makes it more powerful:

- Your **Soft Budget Limit** is the ceiling shown to service providers. If a job will exceed it, they contact Latchel for approval before proceeding.
- Your **Hard Budget Limit** is the maximum Latchel can auto-approve on your behalf. Estimates that land between the soft and hard limits are approved automatically — no notification to you. Anything above the hard limit gets escalated to your team.
- The **Approved Budget** on an active work order starts at your soft limit — but it can move. It updates if you request a one-off amount for a specific job, or if a vendor submits an estimate that falls under your hard limit and gets auto-approved. Either way, the SP always sees the current approved amount on their end.

<Check>
  **What's New:**

  Here's where the gap used to be — if a vendor submitted an estimate below your hard budget limit, Latchel would auto-approve it without notifying you, even on jobs you wanted to review closely. The only workaround was lowering the soft limit to \$1, but that didn't stop the auto-approval from happening within the hard limit.

  Now, Latchel Ops and Property Managers can set a **per-WO hard budget limit** — think of it as "nothing above this amount is approved on this specific work order, no matter what." Any estimate over that threshold will require explicit approval before it moves forward, overriding the auto-approve behavior for that job.
</Check>

If you have a specific work order you want this updated, feel free to reach out to Ops via the webchat or update the WO yourself via the Budget tab!

4. **PM User Replacement Prompt When Disabling a User**

When you disable a PM user in Latchel, you'll now be prompted to select an active replacement before the change takes effect. Previously, disabling a user didn't automatically hand off their work orders — leaving WO communications without an owner.

Now, updates, resident communications, and escalations flow to the right person from day one — no manual reassignment needed.

5. **Appfolio: Work Orders Now Sync Every 15 Minutes**

Good news for Appfolio customers — faster WO syncs are now live for everyone..

We've split the Appfolio background sync into two tracks: work orders now sync every 15 minutes, while property and tenant data syncs once daily. Your WO data in Appfolio stays more current, stale statuses are reduced, and fewer manual refreshes are needed.
