# **Purpose**

<Note>
  **Purpose of this document**

  Customer Meeting Framework - Your goal should be to move your customer meetings from being merely **tactical** (e.g., check-in with the customer on this issue) to being **strategic** (e.g., check-in on this issue and ensure the customer's long-term success).

  To accomplish all goals at the same time, you're going to need to expand how you think about your customer meetings overall. Let's explore ways you can improve your approach. This template will guide your meeting and keep the conversation on track.
</Note>

**Who should read this document?**

Customer Success Team

<Check>
  ### **Overview**

  ### **1. Focus on the customer's goals.**

  Whatever their goals are, make it central to the agenda. For every topic you talk about, first, make sure you've framed it in the context of what's in it for the customer.

  ### **2. Simplify your meetings.**

  Budget your time so you're likely to finish on time, even if it means accomplishing less. If you must cover more topics than you have time for, make sure to send along instructions before the meeting to speed things up. Such instructions might read, _"Please take a look at the attached report, as we only have about 30 minutes allotted for our call."_

  ### **3. Use action-oriented agenda items.**

  For example, instead of, "Updates" even a short, "What's new?" is much more engaging.

  ### **4. Share your meeting agenda with the customer.**

  When appropriate, you can ask your customers to contribute to the meeting plan as well. Ask them what they would like to cover, and make sure to include it in the agenda.

  ### **Customer Meeting Follow-Up Actions**

  On the same day as the meeting, share your notes with your customer via email. Base your notes on the agenda, filling them in with slightly more background information, and include any decisions and next steps.

  Also, link or attach any documents or reports that you chatted about during the meeting. For the next steps, list out who's responsible and give a deadline if possible. You can do this formally in the notes themselves, or include it as part of a personal message, e.g., _"Great meeting today. Jennifer will reach out by Friday with the work order report we discussed."_

  If appropriate, you can also use these notes to build enthusiasm for your next meeting. You may want to check in on the status of various challenges and most likely, you'll want to revisit all action items that came about from the last meeting —unless they're long since resolved and no longer relevant.

  By bringing up the success of the last meeting and showing the impact the session had on your overall relationship, you're demonstrating an essential pattern to your customer: When you meet with us, things get done.

  You're ready to plan, host, and follow-up with your next customer. But, before you get started, save some time when creating your agenda by using this nifty template below.

  Now that you have prepared for your customer meeting, it's time to run a great session.
</Check>
