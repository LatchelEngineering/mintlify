### **Objective**

‌

The goal of this page is to outline clear steps for handling Assembly tickets, ensuring that SLAs are met and requesters are kept informed with updates on their requests.

‌

### **Introduction**

Latchel has adopted Ask Assembly as the primary ticketing tool for the Data Support team to manage incoming requests and improve response times. Assembly uses four key ticket statuses to track the progress of each ticket:

* **Breaching:** When a ticket is nearing its first response SLA deadline.
* **Needs Response:** The status assigned when a ticket is first received in Assembly.
* **Open:** Set when an associate begins working on the request.
* **Closed:** Indicates the ticket has been successfully completed.

All workflow requests that are posted in the following Slack Channels will create a ticket in Assembly:

### **The Ticket Statuses**

#### **A. Needs Response**

With Slack integration, any requests posted in the aforementioned channels will sync to Assembly as a ticket and will automatically be assigned to this status. All tickets are also assigned to <custom data-type="mention" data-id="id-4">@</custom> to ensure they are routed to the correct team, except for tickets originating from <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-5">https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C04E9CLPX5W</custom> which will be assigned to <custom data-type="mention" data-id="id-6">@</custom>.

In addition, the Data Support Team is required to respond to the ticket within 5 days to avoid breaching the SLA. On the 3rd day, they will be notified that they are approaching the SLA breach. For tickets originating from <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-7">https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C04E9CLPX5W</custom> the SLA is 24 hours, with a notification sent after 12 hours.

#### **B. Breaching**

Any tickets that have breached the set SLA will appear under this status. The Data Support Team is required to respond immediately to tickets approaching an SLA breach to avoid impacting performance metrics. These tickets must be prioritized over other tickets.

‌

#### **C. Open**

A Data Support Associate must do the following to properly handle this type of tickets:

1. **Start the ticket Timer as soon as you are about to start working on the ticket.**

    1. Once you begin working on a ticket, click the **Start** button for the timer.

2. **Respond to the Requester:**

    1. Immediately reply to the requester, informing them that the ticket is now in progress. This changes the status of the ticket to **Open**.
    2. Acknowledge the request and briefly outline the next steps.  
    
        ![](blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=78839807-6354-4ecd-ad50-a0509ff960c2&&collection=contentId-3220111374&height=853&occurrenceKey=null&width=1211&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null)
    
         

    
    
3. **Provide Progress Update:**

    1. Before the end of the day, send an update to the requester regarding the progress of the ticket.
    2. Include any relevant details or changes to the estimated completion timeline, if necessary.
    
4. **Respond to New Messages within the ticket:**

    1. If a new message or response to an inquiry is received within a ticket while it is in Open status, the ticket’s status changes to Needs Response. Therefore, the assigned Data Support associate must acknowledge it by replying via the ticket, which will automatically change the status back to Open.
    

‌

#### **D. Closed**

A Data Support Associate must do the following to close a ticket:

1. **Verify Completion:**

    1. Ensure that all tasks related to the ticket have been completed and the requester’s needs are fully met.
    
2. **Document Resolution:**

    1. Add notes or comments in the ticket detailing how the issue was resolved or what actions were taken. This helps maintain a record for future reference.
    
3. **Notify the Requester:**

    1. Send a final message to the requester, informing them that the ticket is closed.
    2. Include a brief summary of the resolution, the current property and resident count after updating the customer data in Latchel, etc. and thank them for their patience.
    
4. **Confirm Feedback:**

    1. If applicable, ask the requester for feedback on their experience to improve service quality.
    
5. **Update Ticket Status:**

    1. Change the ticket status to Closed in Assembly to reflect that the work is finished.
    
6. **Add Internal Notes:**

    1. Provide internal notes relevant to the quality audit. This includes:
    
        1. Final property and resident counts for property and resident listing tasks, count of service provider added and prioritized, etc.
        2. Google spreadsheet link to the worksheet.
        3. Any additional notes that can facilitate a seamless auditing process.
        
    2. Notify your supervisor that it is ready for audit via <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-8">https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C07RDJP9RGV</custom> 
    

‌

All responses sent within the Slack thread of the request will sync to the corresponding Assembly ticket. Similarly, all responses sent by the Data Support Associate via the Assembly ticket will be reflected in the Slack request.

_**Unfortunately, there is no mechanism to send a private notification to the requester when a new message appears in the Slack thread of the posted request. Therefore, the requester must always check their thread notifications in Slack.**_

### **Frequently Asked Questions in Handling Tickets/Tasks**

**Q:** _**What should I do if the description or instructions in the requested task are unclear? What if there is missing information?**_  
**A:** Immediately respond to the ticket and address it to the requester of the task. If the missing information prevents you from moving forward with the task, wait for the requester’s response before starting work. If there are other parts of the task within the same ticket that can be completed while waiting, proceed with those to save time, proceed on other tickets if none.

**Q:** _**What should I do if the property manager report is missing, or if I need permission to access the Google spreadsheet report?**_  
**A:** Respond to the ticket and ask the requester for the complete report or access to the spreadsheet.

**Q:** _**What should I do if I have opened the ticket but was unable to finish it within the day?**_  
**A:** Provide an update to the requester on your progress via the ticket. Explain the actions you have taken so far and outline your next steps for the following day. There is no need to change the status of the ticket; leave it as Open.

**Q:** _**What should I do if there has been no response from the requester since the last time I inquired about the task?**_  
**A:** Send a follow up message if no response is received from the requester after 24 hours. Report to your supervisor if no response was received after 48 hours.

**Q:** _**What should I do if I receive a new update from the requester regarding a blocked ticket, but I am already working on a new one?**_  
**A:** Assess your progress with the ticket you are currently working on. If you are close to finishing the new ticket, complete it first, then proceed with the blocked ticket. If the blocked ticket is easier to complete, stop working on the new ticket, add an internal note indicating that you have paused work on it to prioritize the blocked ticket due to the recent update.

### **Suggested Blurbs When Responding to Tickets**

* **When opening or starting a task:**

    * “We’ve received your request and have started working on it. We’ll keep you updated with any progress or if further information is needed.”
    
* **When a discrepancy is found within the data in Latchel versus the PM report:**

    * “We’ve identified a discrepancy between the data in Latchel and the PM report. Could you please review and confirm the correct information so we can proceed?”
    

Make sure to include information about the discrepancies found, or _**provide a report that lists all the discrepancies.**_ 

* **When following up for the last inquiry/concern/message sent:**

    * “We’re following up regarding our last inquiry. If you could provide the requested information, we’ll be able to continue processing your request promptly.”
    
* **When acknowledging or thanking the requester for their latest update:**

    * “Thank you for the updated information. We’ve received it and will continue working on your request.”
    
* **When providing an update on your progress on the request:**

    * “Here’s an update on your request: we’ve completed _**\[specific part of the task\]**_ and are currently working on _**\[next step\]**_. We’ll reach out if any additional information is needed.”
    

Note that _**this should be sent at the end of the day**_ in case a _**task is still in progress**_ and would have to continue the next day.

* **When completing a ticket:**

    * “Your request has been successfully completed. If there’s anything else we can assist you with, please don’t hesitate to reach out. Thank you!”
    

Include a _**summary of the customer’s data in Latchel**_ after completing the request. Also state if there are any discrepancies found and recommendation on how to fix the missing data. _**Provide a report that lists all the discrepancies.**_

_**Make sure that you are addressing your message to the requester of the ticket at all times.**_

* **When confirming completion of service provider onboarding in Nomad and Rent Redi tickets:**

    * “The service provider has now been added to the Latchel console and prioritized based on skills and territory. Thank you.”
    
* **When onboarding a service provider or owner, but cannot proceed due to missing information:**

    * “We’re currently unable to proceed with the onboarding of the service provider/new owner due to _**\[specific reason\]**_. Could you please provide _**\[specific information needed\]**_ so we can resolve this issue? Once we have this information, we’ll be able to proceed. Thank you.”
