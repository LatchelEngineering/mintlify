## Sales Cycle & Expectations

The ideal Latchel sales cycle has 5 steps that, on average, span ~14 days for Latchel's typical customer. If we are selling only Emergency 24x7 and Optional Benefits, the Sales Cycle should be shorter, from 7 to 14 days max. It should only go above that if it is a large company, which is not within our target customer type.

Our Sales Cycle has 5 main stages:

**(1) SDR** lead's initial engagement or outreach

- The goal is to confirm interest and schedule a meeting to qualify the lead

**(2) Qualification**

- The goal is to assess if the lead is a good fit for Latchel and should be moved forward in the Sales Process

**(3) Discovery Meeting**

- Understand the challenges or problems the lead is facing and how Latchel can help solving them

**(4) Demo Meeting**

- Show our product and how we can solve the problems discussed in the Discovery Meeting

**(5) Sign Contract**

- Ensure the lead understands our contract and expectations from them. Move forward with turning them into a Customer!

## Process Overview - Happy Path

1. Leads are generated by Marketing through several strategies and are categorized in Tiers, which has been evolving.
   - The definitions of each Tier are being continuously adjusted. To find the latest information, search the [glossary page](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2902917222)
2. Leads, prioritized by Tiers, are assigned to Sales Development Representatives (SDRs) that will engage those leads using different mechanisms.
3. Once a Lead demonstrates interest, the **SDR** will qualify the lead according to Latchel's [Qualification Criteria](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2868248756), schedule a Discovery Meeting with an Account Executive (AE), and [create an "Opportunity" in Salesforce](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2880143483).
   - From this point forward, the details of this potential deal will be tracked via the Opportunity Pipeline and this process is owned by the AE.
4. If the Opportunity is successfully closed (Won), the Lead becomes a Customer and is sent to Customer Success to be Onboarded

## Definition of Stages and Substages

- **What is the definition of a "Pipeline Stage"?**
  - Pipeline stage refers to the main steps, or milestones, that are completed as our team engages and works with a Lead throughout our sales and onboarding process.
  - When naming a stage, we will use verbs that indicate the completion of that milestone within the process to convert Leads into Closed Deals
- **What is the definition of a "Substage"?**
  - Substage is a traceable action that the **SDR**/AE takes within a certain Pipeline Stage that will either show progress towards completing the stage, or that will show an attempt to bring the Lead back on track to progress within that specific Pipeline Stage
  - When naming a substage, we will use verbs that indicate the completion of an action, or that a certain action is being performed, which may take a certain amount of time to be completed. We want to have an indication of how long that action takes and if it is something that we should or not invest time on, or streamline in the future.
  - To avoid having an overwhelming number of substages, we will focus on having substages that will either help us to have visibility into progress within a week, or that reflects a significant action completion within that stage. We should NOT create substages for every single action a **SDR**/AE takes.
  - Note: Within the "Prospecting" and "Discard" stages, we are using a logic a bit different. Within those stages, our current goal is to group the accounts based on their "status" to make it easier for Marketing to know to engage or not engage with them

## Account Ownership Overview

- The **SDR** pipeline starts with getting a lead assigned and it ends when a Sales Opportunity is created AND there is a meeting scheduled with the Account Executive AND the Lead shows up to the meeting.
  - **Note:**
    - We will use a substage to draw an ownership line between SDR and AEs, so we know where the "baton" is being passed and we can manage accountability properly.
    - In this case, if the Opportunity stage is "Discovery Appointment Scheduled" and substage is "Rescheduling Discovery Meeting" the SDR owns, all other stages under "Appointment Scheduled" are owned by AEs.
- The AE pipeline starts when a Disco Meeting starts AND the Lead is present, and it ends when the **Onboarding** Kick-Off meeting is scheduled AND the Lead is present
  - **Note:**
    - Similarly to the end of the SDR pipeline, if there is a "No Show" in the first Onboarding Meeting (Kick-off/ KO), the AE is the owner of that account. All other substages are owned by Onboarding.
- The **Onboarding** pipeline starts when there is an onboarding meeting scheduled AND the Lead is present, and it ends when the customer's account goes live. At that point, the account is owned by a **CSM**.

## Account Pipeline Overview

The Account Pipeline shows the progression of a Lead to become a Customer. The "ownership" of the Lead changes from Marketing, to **SDR**, to AE and to Customer Success as the account progresses through the pipeline.

Leads can enter the **SDR** Pipeline in two ways: (1) If Marketing generates the lead and it is assigned to the **SDR**, either by someone in Marketing or Management, or by an Assignment Tool; (2) If the **SDR** is asked to look for their own leads. Therefore, there are two starting points, or "Stage 1" for their pipeline

Note that under "Active Sales Account", there is a substage called "Open Opportunity", which triggers a separate pipeline (i.e. Opportunity Pipeline) that will track a potential deal with that account.

1. One account can have multiple opportunities attached to it.
2. Once the Opportunity is "Closed Won", the Account Pipeline Stage changes to "Customer"

When certain progress expectations are not met (e.g. the lead gets stuck in a substage), the **SDR** will move the account to "Prospecting" Stage, Substage "Marketing", which will change ownership of that Lead back to Marketing. We should have different Reason Codes for the team to select from when moving to that stage **(TBD)**. If there is an Opportunity Open, the Opportunity will be "Close Lost" and the AE will select the Reason Code for it. When AE does that, the Account should be moved into "Prospecting/Lost Opportunities"

The "Discard" account stage is used to "separate" accounts that should not longer be engaged. We have 2 reasons to discard the account:

1. **Unsubscribed** — use when marketing gets a request from the Lead to not contact them again. SDRs and AEs shouldn't use this stage
2. **Wrong Industry** - use when the contact is not in real estate or property management at all. If we need to remove a contact from a marketing list for other reasons like extreme negative reaction, it would go into unsubscribed.

Under **Prospecting Stage**, the only substage that SDRs should use is **"Prospecting"**. This is the substage to be used when working new outbound leads.

All other substages under the Prospecting Stage are owned by the Marketing team:

**"Prospecting"** is also the substage that will store new leads coming from Marketing that are not yet ready to be assigned, like Tier 3s and Tier 4s, and marketing will continue to work to nurture them. The other substages under "Prospecting" are owned by Marketing:

1. **Inbound Unassigned** - This substage will house the inbound leads that are not auto-assigned to SDRs. See in [this page](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2931032140) which types are or not auto-assigned
2. **Returned to Marketing** - This is the substage that SDRs will move all accounts that didn't make through their pipeline. This is the stage where marketing can select accounts to run "drip campaigns"
3. **Lost Opportunities** - This is the substage that AEs will move all accounts that had an active opportunity and ended up as Close Lost (i.e. if the account is not a current customer and the opportunity was an upgrade). Churned customers will also be moved into this substage. This is the stage where marketing can select accounts to run other targeted campaigns, knowing they had a higher level of interest and know more about Latchel.

For more details on how the SDRs work through the account stages, see this page: [Draft - SOP - SDR Inbound Process: How to Process a Lead](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2868543685)

## Opportunity Pipeline Overview

The Opportunity Pipeline starts when the **SDR** schedules a Discovery Meeting with the AE and progresses until either the deal is closed won, or closed lost. Some opportunities may be in a holding period called Long-term nurture.

For more details on how AE works through the Opportunity stages and substages, see this page: [DRAFT - SOP - AE Process Steps - How to Process an Opportunity](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/edit-v2/2886828062?draftShareId=fbee911f-8a5c-4b67-a722-775bc7191c0f)
