## Role Description & Expectations

### Role Overview

95%\+ of your [opportunities](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2902917222) will be sources by Latchel's SDR (Sales Development Team). The other 5% comes from conferences, personal connections and referrals.

Your job is to turn an Opportunity into a Customer, by having a contract signed and transferring the Customer to the Onboarding them. Your ownership of a customer "ends" when the customer shows-up for the Onboarding kick-off meeting.

You do the job by:

- **Preparing.** Study the prospects, consult with the SDR (if there is one) and learn the details before your first meeting.
- **Consulting.** Be genuinely curious. Understand the prospect's business, what drives their interest in Latchel and what problems Latchel can solve for them.
- **Tailoring.** Once you understand the prospect's business, use Latche's standard presentation materials and hide slides that are not relevant to the prospect's operations. Do not duplicate materials as it becomes very hard to keep control of multiple versions and ensure your information is updated. Your demo meeting should be a conversation, getting the prospect revved up about how Latchel will enhance the prospect's business.
- **Following through on a plan.** At the beginning of every call, [have a plan](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1OZ72Vb1b3aNzdvsD_sdTRwScEtHEpgZD7EWoZO4Rcvw/edit). What's your goal at the end of the call? How are you going to reach that goal? Be nimble. Your plan might change, but at the end of every call you want to be aligned with the prospect on next steps.

### Expectations

- **Quota and goals.** Each quarter, meet or exceed the [team goal](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2927132673) and your quota. Monthly, revisit and update your goals with your manager.
- **Ownership and accountability.** Take ownership of your career and success. Latchel's goal is to empower you to become the best salesperson you can, at Latchel. We'll offer tools, support, coaching and other resources. But ultimately, you're in the driver's seat. We expect you to develop and iterate on a plan and a path to success.
- **Follow Team Standards.** Use the standard decks, scripts and other resources. If something doesn't look right or updated, or you believe something could be done in a better way, bring it up for discussion so we can update the standards.

### Salesforce

Salesforce is Latchel's CRM and where you will spend most of your day. In addition to reducing your workload, Salesforce will log daily activities allowing you to measure your performance and improve iteratively.

To get started with Salesforce:

- Log-in to your [Salesforce account.](https://login.salesforce.com/) If you don't have your login information, contact your manager
- Next set up your Calendar by navigating to the Calendar tab
- Update your email settings.
  - Click the small gear located at the top right side of the page. This will launch Salesforce setup
  - Locate the drop down menu titled "Email" on the left hand side of the page
  - The expanded drop down menu will show three sections: "My Email Settings", "My Email to Salesforce" and "My Unresolved Items". Click through these sections to update your email settings in Salesforce
- [Link your Gmail Account with Salesforce](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/edit-v2/2917269517?draftShareId=a2f79ff9-e26b-4686-91cb-40122c9316a3)
