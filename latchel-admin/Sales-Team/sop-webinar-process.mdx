## Step-by-Step Guide

### Process Overview

### Step 1 - Topic Planning

Come up with topic(s)

### Step 2- Outreach

Reach out to guest(s) to participate in webinar and set a 30 minute coffee meeting

### Step 3 - Introductory Meeting

During the meeting, discuss topic idea(s) and settle on a topic/title. During this meeting, you should also secure a date/time to host the webinar. The date/time of the webinar should be 2-3 (preferably 3) weeks out from the initial meeting.

### Step 4 - Webinar Content Planning

After the meeting, the host should spend about 1 week creating a collaborative agenda and deck.

### Step 5 - Marketing/Webinar Promoting

During the 2-3 weeks between the meeting and webinar date, promotional marketing assets should be created and distributed to appropriate lists 2 weeks leading up to the webinar.

The following marketing assets include:

- Registration page
- Add to digital events page
- Social media posts (LinkedIn, Facebook and Instagram)
- Email marketing to appropriate lists
- Promote in Property Brew newsletter email
- User Pilot pop up to customers

### Step 6 - Share with Guest(s)/Cross-Promote

Marketing should share these promotional assets with any guests participating in the webinar. Guests should share these items with their marketing team to cross promote.

### Step 7 - Dry Run

Webinar host and guest should meet the week of (or week before) the webinar to discuss the agenda, deck and do a dry run.

### Step 8 - Host the Webinar

Host the webinar! Host and guest(s) should arrive at least 10 minutes early to discuss any last minute questions and make sure everything is working from a technical stand point.

## Post-Webinar Process

### Step 1 - Publish Recording

Add the on-demand webinar recording to YouTube and [digital events page](https://latchel.com/latchel-digital-events/)

### Step 2 - Share Recording and List

Share the webinar recording and registration guest list with the guest(s)

### Step 3 - Email Nurturing Campaign

Create an email nurturing campaign to enroll non-customer webinar registrants in feeding them related content and encourage them to book a demo

### Step 4 - Repurpose Content

Repurpose the webinar recording to use for content across marketing channels (social media, blogs, Property Brew, cold emails, and more)
