## Purpose

<Note>
  **Purpose of this document**

  The purpose of having a script and templates is to provide guidance and tips on how to communicate certain key selling points and collect key information from the lead.

  The goal is not to read the script, but to ensure the key items are included in the communication. For email templates, although you can copy and paste the template, you should make some small adjustments to add your personal touch!
</Note>

<Note>
  **Who should read this document?**

  SDRs
</Note>

## When to use these Templates?

This script is to be used when the SDR receives a notification that they have a Tier 2 lead assigned. Tier 2 leads are the ones that have been highly engaged with our content, but have not requested a demo yet. For Tiers definitions, check the [Glossary Page](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2902917222).

SDRs may receive their name, phone, email, and small information about them (estimate unit count). From there, the SDR should immediately call them and ask the qualifying questions / set up a time on the AE's calendar to move forward.

This script can be used inside or outside a cadence, or any time we are contacting a lead for the first time.

## Prior to Calling…

[PREPARE! See the same section on the Tier1 page](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2872901638/Inbound+Tier+1+-+Communication+Templates#Prior-to-Calling%E2%80%A6)

For Tier 2, expect having longer conversations, ~15ish minutes, as these are not direct requests to talk to us. In these calls, expect and prepare for the following:

- These conversations can open can of worms and lead to tons of discovery notes
- The pre-qualification looks more like a discovery, they want to talk about pain
- Quick note taking on call & digging into pain points is essential, so the AE has all of this information for the Discovery Meeting and we avoid asking repetitive questions
- Unsure if voicemails and emails are best practice here - call only could be more effective because it's a difficult conversation to open up - leaving a tidbit (a small piece of interesting information) via email or voicemail might not be the move

## Script for Call - if T2 came from Webinar

> _Hey Name, this is NAME from Latchel. We haven't spoken before but I saw you signed up for our webinar about \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_. I'd like to use you as a resource and I was curious what you were hoping to learn from the webinar?_

_-Small talk-_

> _How familiar are you with Latchel?_

_-Small talk-_

> _The webinar is a very general presentation that covers many different types of residential portfolios. I was hoping to learn more about you, Then I could get you some information more directly targeted for you and your company._

[(Prequalify – attempt to ask some of the qualification questions )](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/pages/resumedraft.action?draftId=2903506946&draftShareId=a3db05e8-2010-4841-a331-96d4d4ea51e2)

**If Webinar hasn't happened:**

> _I know we just met but what if I could get a specialist that works with portfolios just like yours on the phone right now. That way I could save you time later!_

**If Webinar has happened:**

> _Have you been able to attend all of the webinars?_

**_-If not-_**

> _I understand property management is super busy and it's hard to make time for extra stuff. I don't normally do this, but what if I could set up a time to cover what we can do for (A,B, and C), that way we could catch you at a good time and get the right information over to you._

**_-If yes-_**

> _What have you learned from it so far, did you feel like any of the topics discussed would be impactful for your business? I can set up a customized demo of Latchel to show you exactly how we would solve these issues for a company like yours._
