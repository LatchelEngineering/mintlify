## Use the Sales Engagement App

When you log into Salesforce, you will be directed to the Sales App

<Frame>
  ![Sales App landing page in Salesforce](/images/Sales-128.png)
</Frame>

Although you can work and access the sales engagement (cadences) features from there, it is more user friendly to work from the Sales Engagement App, as it allows you to open multiple tabs within the same window.

To switch to the Sales Engagement App, click on the nine dots icon and type "Sales Engagement" and click on it

<Frame>
  ![Switching to the Sales Engagement App using the nine dots icon](/images/Sales-129.png)
</Frame>

The app will open. In the home page, you can see a link to your Work Queue, your tasks and events for today, your recent contacts, a link to Cadence performance Dashboard, etc. This page can be configured to show other information, if needed

<Frame>
  ![Sales Engagement App home page](/images/Sales-130.png)
</Frame>

If you need access to something else, click on the drop down menu in the home tab to see a list of other features. If you what you are looking for is not in that list, you can click on edit to find many other features

<Frame>
  ![Drop down menu in the home tab showing other features](/images/Sales-131.png)
</Frame>

The item selected will open in the tab that was the home screen. Then, if you click on something inside the page, it will open as a new tab

<Frame>
  ![New tab opening from a selected item](/images/Sales-132.png)
</Frame>

## Add Contact(s) to a Cadence

### Individual Contacts

Go to the contact page you want to add to a cadence and click on "Add to Cadence",

<Frame>
  ![Add to Cadence button on a contact page](/images/Sales-133.png)
</Frame>

Next, you will be prompted to select the cadence and click on "Add"

<Frame>
  ![Prompt to select a cadence and click Add](/images/Sales-134.png)
</Frame>

You can also add the contact to the cadence by scrolling down in the contact page until you see the "Sales Engagement" section on the left side of the page. There is another "Add to Cadence" button there:

<Frame>
  ![Add to Cadence button in the Sales Engagement section](/images/Sales-135.png)
</Frame>

### Multiple Contacts

To add multiple contacts to a cadence, you can go to the contacts tab in the navigation menu to see a list of contacts, select the ones you want to add to the cadence and click "Add to Cadence"

<Frame>
  ![Adding multiple contacts to a cadence from the contacts list](/images/Sales-136.png)
</Frame>

## Initiate Cadence Actions (Run the Cadence)

As a contact is added to the cadence, Salesforce will create tasks for you to to according to the progress of the cadence. There are multiple ways for you to see your cadence related tasks:

Option 1: Go to your "To Do List" at the bottom of the screen

<Frame>
  ![To Do List at the bottom of the screen](/images/Sales-137.png)
</Frame>

Option 2: Go to your "Work Queue"

Click on the 9 dots on top left side and type "Work Queue"

<Frame>
  ![Navigating to Work Queue using the nine dots icon](/images/Sales-138.png)
</Frame>

Option 3: From the Contact Page

### Option 1: To Do List

You can find the tasks in the "To Do List" Section and initiate the action directly from there:

<Frame>
  ![Initiating an action from the To Do List section](/images/Sales-139.png)
</Frame>

You can also choose to skip the step of the cadence, manually mark as complete, or adjust the timing for the step to start.

In your "To do List", you can click on the drop down menu for the cadence task to see those options:

<Frame>
  ![Drop down menu for a cadence task in the To Do List](/images/Sales-140.png)
</Frame>

### Option 2: Work Queue

Click on the 9 dots icon in the navigation bar and type "work queue"

<Frame>
  ![Navigating to Work Queue from the navigation bar](/images/Sales-141.png)
</Frame>

There will be three sub-menus: (1) Cadences; (2) My feed; (3) My List

Cadences – will show all cadences that you have running, the contacts under them and next cadence actions. You should be able to take actions from this screen

My Feed - shows emails that you send and had some type of engagement

My List --- **not sure what this include yet! Please update as you learn about it**

<Frame>
  ![Work Queue sub-menus: Cadences, My Feed, My List](/images/Sales-142.png)
</Frame>

## Enter Call Results

Once the call ends, a screen with a task will pop up, and the dialer will show that you are on an "After Call Work" state. It will be in this state for 60 seconds.

<Frame>
  ![After Call Work state in the dialer](/images/Sales-147.png)
</Frame>

During this time, you should update the "Call Result" field in the task to reflect the outcome of the call. This will define the next step on the cadence.

<Frame>
  ![Call Result field in the task](/images/Sales-148.png)
</Frame>

After selecting the appropriate call result, click on Save

<Frame>
  ![Save button after selecting a call result](/images/Sales-149.png)
</Frame>

Go to available:

If you initiated the call when your dialer was in Available

You can either wait for the clock of the After Call Work to run out and the dialer will update automatically to available.

OR, you can go to the dialer and click on "Close Contact", which will make the dialer go back to available.

If you initiated the call while you were offline or another state, you will need to change the status into available to update the cadence correctly.

<Note>
  If you changed your status to something else other than available after the call, you MUST got back to available
</Note>

## Remove From Cadence

To remove a contact from cadence, you can go to the contact and click on the "Remove from Cadence" bottom on the top right of the screen

<Frame>
  ![Remove from Cadence button on a contact page](/images/Sales-152.png)
</Frame>

Or, you can scroll down in the contact page until the Sales Engagement section, find the cadence that is being ran, click on the drop down menu and select "remove from cadence"

<Frame>
  ![Remove from cadence option in the Sales Engagement drop down menu](/images/Sales-153.png)
</Frame>
