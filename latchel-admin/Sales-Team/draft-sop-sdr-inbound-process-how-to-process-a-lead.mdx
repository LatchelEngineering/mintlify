## SECTION A) Happy Path Process

### Process Overview

The flow below shows the steps of how a SDR will handle a lead from the time it is assigned until an Opportunity is created in Salesforce and the lead attends the Discovery Meeting, which at that time, the ownership of that lead is transferred to the Account Executive (AE).

The "Happy Path" assumes that everything went well and the lead moved to the next step of the process. When there is an issue during that step, refer to [Section B](#section-b-how-to-handle-issues-along-the-process) of this document to know how to proceed.

These steps are aligned with the accounts stages and substages in Salesforce that we will use to track the status of each lead and measure performance of each process step. This tracking will enable us to define and prioritize process improvements.

Throughout the process, you will be taking notes of your engagement with the lead and some key account information. Please refer to this SOP on where the notes should be left: [SOP - How to Leave Notes for an Opportunity in Salesforce](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2907144246)

### Step 5 - Set Appointment for Qualification Meeting

**How to work this step?**

1. Once there is a reply from a lead wanting to move forward, the SDR should qualify that account according to our current qualification criteria, which is done in the Qualification Meeting (step 6)
2. In order to qualify the prospect, the SDR should schedule a meeting to collect the required information. This meeting should be schedule via ……..**not sure how this works…**
   1. Try to ask the lead about their timing to make a decision, so you know if we need to look for ways to expedite the process and avoid losing the opportunity. If they are on a short timeline, it is possible they are already in advanced conversations with our competitors.
   2. Try to also ask if they are exploring other options, but do NOT engage in discussing how we compare with them, or what makes us better, or talk about pricing. If the lead asks any question in that direction, say that the AE is the best person to answer their questions, and you can work on finding a time with them asap.

<Note>
  If the AE needs to reschedule a meeting to meet with this lead, before committing to that, make sure you are considering, or have discussed with the AE, the size of this opportunity, so we are not creating churn for the AE for something very small. For example, if it is a deal ~700 units, it is likely it could take priority over other appointments.
</Note>

3. **Do we send reminders X days or hours prior to the meeting? … template? .**

**Salesforce Tracking**

- The Account Stage will be "Confirmed Interest"
  - This means:
    - The lead has clearly indicated they are interested in moving forward by saying they want to meet or have a meeting soon; therefore, they are engaged in scheduling a meeting.
    - This status should NOT be used if the lead asked for more information via email or asked to talk at a later date. Therefore, a vague indication of interest should NOT be considered as Confirmed Interest.
    - If the SDR is unable to confirm interest within 30 days, the SDR should move the account to "Account Stage "Prospecting"/ Substage "Marketing" and the lead should be returned to Marketing
  - How does the lead get to this stage?
    - The SDR assesses the prospect's level of interest according to the criteria described above and manually moves the lead to this stage
  - Substages:
    - **Scheduling Qualification Call**
      - This means the SDR and the lead are working together to find a common time that the meeting attendees are available to schedule the Qualification meeting.
      - The SDR will move the lead to this substage manually.
      - If unable to confirm interest and schedule the qualification call within 30 days, the SDR should move the account to "Account Stage "Prospecting"/ Substage "Marketing" and the lead should be returned to Marketing
    - **Qualification Call Scheduled**
      - This means the Qualification Call has been scheduled
      - The SDR will move the account to this substage manually or can this be automated? (TBC if it will be possible to automate)
      - What if the lead asks to reschedule or the SDR needs to reschedule? SDR moves substage back to "Scheduling Qualification Call" and try to reschedule the meeting
      - What if the lead requests to cancel? SDR moves substage back to "Scheduling Qualification Call" and try to reschedule the meeting
      - What if the lead is a no-show in the Qualification call? SDR moves substage back to "Scheduling Qualification Call" and try to reschedule the meeting
    - **Qualification Call Follow-up (incomplete)**
      - This means the Qualification Call has happened (i.e. Lead showed up and they talked), but the meeting time was not enough to complete the Qualification and another meeting will need to happen. The account will remain in this state while the follow-up call is being scheduled and the call is happening.
      - The SDR will move the account to this substage manually
      - If unable to finalize the qualification 30 days, the SDR should move the account to "Account Stage "Prospecting"/ Substage "Marketing" and the lead should be returned to Marketing
  - How long should the lead be in this stage (i.e. Confirmed Interest)?
    - Max duration = 1 week

### Step 7 - Create Opportunity

**How to work this step?**

1. To create an Opportunity in Salesforce, follow this SOP - [SOP - How to Create an Opportunity in Salesforce](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2880143483)

**Salesforce Tracking**

- The Account Stage will be "Active Sales Account" and Substage will be "Open Opportunity". In the Opportunity pipeline, the Stage will be "Discovery Appointment Scheduled"
  - This means:
    - The SDR has created an Opportunity in Salesforce and has confirmed the potential customer has scheduled an appointment with an AE.
    - Once the account is moved into "Active Sales Account" stage, the ownership is moved to the AE, unless there is an issue with the Discovery Meeting Scheduling or the Lead is a "No-Show" in the meeting, or the meeting needs to be rescheduled for any reason:
      - What if the lead asks to reschedule or someone in Latchel needs to reschedule?
        - SDR leaves Account in Stage "Active Sales Account" and substage "Open Opportunity".
        - SDR leaves Opportunity stage as "Discovery Appointment Scheduled" and changes substage to "Rescheduling Discovery Appointment" (Max duration = 2 weeks)

<Note>
  We considered changing the process to not create an opportunity until the discovery meeting starts and the lead is present, but this will require major changes to current automations and reports, so we decided to keep the process as is, at least for now.
</Note>

**How to work this step?**

2. If yes, then the AE will take ownership moving forward
3. If not, then send email reminder to the lead (template?)
