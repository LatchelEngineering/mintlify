## SECTION A) Happy Path Process

### Process Overview

The flow below shows the steps of how a SDR will handle a lead from the time it is assigned until an Opportunity is created in Salesforce and the lead attends the Discovery Meeting, which at that time, the ownership of that lead is transferred to the Account Executive (AE).

The "Happy Path" assumes that everything went well and the lead moved to the next step of the process. When there is an issue during that step, refer to Section B of this document to know how to proceed.

These steps are aligned with the accounts stages and substages in Salesforce that we will use to track the status of each lead and measure performance of each process step. This tracking will enable us to define and prioritize process improvements.

Throughout the process, you will be taking notes of your engagement with the lead and some key account information. Please refer to this SOP on where the notes should be left: [SOP - How to Leave Notes for an Opportunity in Salesforce](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2931032140)

### Step 1 - Lead Assigned

**How to work this step?**

A. In Slack, inbound leads are given out in a round robin style distribution.

1. If the lead hasn't been assigned to you in Salesforce, you will need to self-assign (how?)
   - Unless Keyrenter, RPM and rent-manager ---\> checking with Jen what to do when this happens
2. Before doing that, you should first verify if that contact is a member of another account already assigned in Salesforce. In order to do that, search accounts in Salesforce by the following inputs, if available:
   - Full contact name (to see if they pop up under a different company)
   - Phone number (to see if it pops up under different contact - indicates if its a shared number or not)
   - Contact email (not domain)
   - Company name (but only after a google search as some people have a DBA business names vs registered business names)
3. If there is an existing company, check if that is assigned to another SDR. If yes, leave the assignment as is. If not, assign it to yourself by changing the account ownership to yourself.

B. We have 3 channels that leads will be posted based on their Tiers.

1. The definition of each Tier is continuously being adjusted. To find the latest information, search the [glossary page](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2902917222).
2. The goal is that the higher Tier level, higher the potential of conversion. T1 \> T2 \> T3 \> T4. Therefore, SDRs should start working on the Tier 1 leads, then Tier 2 leads, etc.
   - Some of those tiers will NOT be auto-assigned to SDRs and SDRs should only work on those if they have not leads assigned to them. See this page to understand which lead types are auto-assigned. In the case the SDR will work an unassigned lead, in addition to self-assign, the SDR also needs to update the stage to Lead Assigned.
3. These are the Slack channels:
   - **#tier-1-notifications** - Tier 1 leads are the ones that have gone on to our website and clicked "Request a Demo". They're your best chance at booking a meeting.
   - **#tier-2-notifications**
   - **#other-tier-notifications**
   - Note: As we implement Salesforce for SDRs and clean up current pipeline, we will adjust this process to have leads assigned directly in Salesforce vs. Slack channels, and have reporting for SDRs to see real time the leads that were assigned to them.

**Salesforce Tracking**

The Account Stage will be "Lead Assigned"

1. This means: 
   - The lead has been assigned to the SDR and no action has been taken by the SDR yet
2. How does the lead get to this stage? 
   - When the lead is auto-assigned, the Stage and Substage will be automatically set as "Lead Assigned". 
   - For Leads that are not auto-assigned, they will be created in Salesforce under Stage "Prospecting" and the Substage will be "Inbound Unassigned". In this case, the SDR should manually move the lead to Stage "Lead Assigned", Substage "Lead Assigned" when they self-assign a lead.
3. How long should the lead be in this stage? 
   - Max duration = 1 day

**Do not contact vs do not call**

We need to be careful here so we don't get Latchel into trouble. It's really important to recognize the difference between these two phrases.

Do not contact means all communication must stop going forward. In addition to removing the account from any cadence they are in, this account needs to be flagged to Marketing so they can move it into a "Do Not Contact" stage in Salesforce. ----- how to communicate this to marketing? – The same process applies if they ask to be "Unsubscribed from or e-mails" – confirm with Jen

If they tell you not to call, they haven't said not to email. We don't need to turn this into a spam-fest, but we can still attempt to engage the prospect via email, SMS, or video messaging. Oftentimes, people have a kneejerk reaction and tell us not to call, without ever knowing what they are declining. This is still an opportunity to provide some value in order to get the prospect to take a call with us.

Note: At the end of the cadence, if the SDR hasn't received any reply (engaged) from the prospect, the lead should be moved into Account Stage "Prospecting"/ Substage "Marketing" and this will be sent back to the Marketing team.
