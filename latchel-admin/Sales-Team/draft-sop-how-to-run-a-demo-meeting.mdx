## Goal of the Demo Meeting

Educate the lead on what Latchel does and how Latchel will address their pain points and help them to achieve their goals.

Sell and close in a short, consultative, tailored sales cycle.

## How to Run the Demo Meeting

### Prepare for the Meeting

1. Review Recap and Notes from the Disco Meeting
2. Create a customized slide with their company information. Include information like:
   - Unit count
   - Property type (SFR, Multi, section 8, low income, vacation rentals, home warranties, HOAs)
   - Software
   - Ownership (3rd party or own/operate)
   - How do your tenants currently submit work orders?
   - Maintenance Workflow (in house/ 3rd party, how software is being utilized for work orders currently, after-hours call center & their processes currently)
   - Lease type (annual, 6 mo, month to month)
   - RBP (do they offer anything currently? If so, do they have room to add more?)
   - Ratings/Reviews (Do you track these currently?)
   - Goals (give examples if they're stuck - streamline maintenance to support growth/ reduce team time and overhead relating to maintenance/ improved resident experience
3. Check the people who is attending and their role in the company
   - Anyone you haven't met before?
4. Plan your **Demo Strategy**
   - Decide what you will show (1-3 things) and highlight the selling points of each of those that is aligned with their goals and pain points. We should never promise we will solve everything 100%!
     - Are you talking about Maintenance only? which areas you will highlight?
     - Are you also talking about Benefits? which ones you will highlight?
   - Throughout the demo, ensure they are participating! Make it conversational, so plan ahead for questions you will ask, in case they are not proactively asking questions or sharing their thoughts.

<Tip>
  Make sure to ask at least 1 open-ended question throughout each demo (EX: how do you see your residents/ vendors / in-house team utilizing this feature?)
</Tip>

5. **Set an Agenda** for the meeting
   - This should reflect your strategy to approach the conversation
   - Have your goals clearly defined

<Tip>
  Current Selling Focus:

  Product to sell: **24x7 Emergency** and **Optional Benefits** (or mandatory, if they prefer),

  1. Focused on our proprietary benefits
  2. Introduce marketplace benefits if they are comparing us with other benefits package
</Tip>

### Join the Meeting

- Join the video meeting using Google Meeting or Zoom
- Join a few minutes before the start of the meeting to ensure you have everything ready.
- You should never be late!

### Meeting Intro

- Greet the attendees
  - If there is someone in the meeting that were not in the Disco Meeting, make sure to introduce yourself and give time for them to introduce themselves
- Share agenda for the demo

### Meeting Presentation & Discussions

- Recap prior meeting takeaways and answer any pending questions
  - If needed to refresh memory, or to clarify the pain points, use the Discovery Deck. It will help to surface questions they would not normally think about and to identify the key areas the demo should be focused on
  - See the Discovery Decks in this page - [SOP - How to Run a Discovery Meeting](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2883518493/SOP+-+How+to+Run+a+Discovery+Meeting)
- Tailor the Demo to prospect's specific needs and pain points, for example, while showing the Disco Deck at the beginning of the Demo Meeting, the lead asked to see the Resident Experience, so you can prioritize showing, or answering questions, of that part of the application.

### Help them with their Imagination!

Some people struggle to visualize a tool or a process in action. You can use stories or real life examples to walk them through the process and how the experience will look like, for example:

- This is how your resident will easily submit a request.
- Now your team/ John the MC will receive actionable - high quality requests..
  - He doesn't have to call each resident to troubleshoot.
  - He can now schedule a vendor quicker, saving him some time.
- Suzie doesn't have to answer emergency calls at 3 am anymore. She can sleep in peace.. Etc etc…
- Now when resident Larry's cat breaks the blinds.. He will replace them instead of temporarily "fixing" the issue with cardboard.. Because.. REIMBURSEMENT! Etc..etc..

## Wrap-up the Meeting

Talk about next steps:

1. [Agree on Contract and Pricing](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2897248263)
   - Send contract for them to sign
   - Schedule meeting to review and discuss contract, if needed
2. If the Decision Maker was not in the meeting, ask when we can bring this person into a call in order to make a decision
3. If they say they need to go through an internal process before making a decision, try to understand how you can be part of that process or help with it.
4. Confirm target go-live date, i.e. when they would ideally want to implement this by, assuming they move forward
   - Set up next steps with a timeline for when they should be completed in order to hit their target live date.
   - Ideally, you should be scheduling the next meeting prior to hanging up the phone.
5. Is there anything they want from you?
   - References, a copy of our marketing/leasing language/ something else?
   - If needed, ask them for a tradeoff. (EX: Yes, I can provide you with a handful of references if you can provide DM's email address to invite to the next meeting… or something)

## Appendix A - PM Dashboard Selling Points

While talking to the Property Manager about the PM Dashboard, you can also mention some of the following selling points:

You're going to see how Latchel puts your maintenance on auto-pilot while giving you unparalleled visibility of your maintenance AND keeps you in full control of your operation. You get to see everything that's happening, but you only have to work on the most pressing issues.

1. **Ease of Use:** We make it super easy for you to see right at the top of your dashboard which work orders require you to step in. This makes it super easy for your team to know what they need to work on and then they can get back to the highest value tasks for your business. About 70-80% of work orders will never require a single touch from you. The other 20-30% will require some quick action like for this work order.
2. **Visibility:** You pop over to the work order, see exactly what's going on, take a look at what we need from you, and then in just a couple clicks you can keep the job moving forward. So you see we need your approval. You can complete the task. Click the Take Action button, and then approve the job in just a couple clicks.
3. **Control:** Back on the dashboard you can see there's no work orders left for your team. You're in the clear, no worries, you can get back to the highest priority work and rest assured that everything is going right for your maintenance operation.
4. **Realtime Health:** One of my favorite parts of the dashboard is the sidebar that shows you real-time how many work orders are in flight and how many in each category require some action. This makes your job so much easier.
5. You can also track which jobs are held up by residents and vendors and can see every single day how your residents are rating you and your maintenance process.
6. **Visibility:** We also make it super easy for you to search for any work order that you want to find and we show you how many work orders are in each status. For the first time maybe ever, you have a simple dashboard that tells you almost everything you'll want to know about your operation.
7. **Ease of Use:** Our dashboard is super easy to use. Your team will know exactly what work orders to spend time on without waiting to get angry resident calls or confused calls from techs.

## Appendix C - Service Provider Dispatch

While talking to the Property Manager about the Service Provider Dispatch, you can also mention some of the following selling points:

With Latchel, you can get your jobs scheduled faster and more accurately while keeping all the control in your hands. You put all your knowledge about what your vendors or in-house techs can work on right in Latchel so it is documented and you only have to update it if your service techs change.

1. **Ease of Use:** You can easily add new vendors that you've uploaded and set the skills that they work on. And then you can easily prioritize them based on which ones you want to get offered a job first. Now when we have an appliance job, Blue Chip will be offered the job first.
2. **Control:** You can also customize how much time we'll give them before we open up the job to the next person on your list. While the job is open, any vendor can take it, but once one of your techs claims the job the others will no longer see it to claim it. This means we have the fastest turnaround on job scheduling in the industry. Lot of customers drop that time down to 1 minute so it is effectively first come first serve.
3. **Accurate Dispatch:** You can also control if vendors or in-house techs should be used for certain types of jobs like just emergencies or only regular requests.
4. **Control:** My favorite part about this is that this dispatch list can be unique for each portfolio or even down to an individual home if you wanted. You truly have full control of your dispatch preferences so they're documented right in Latchel and they're always dispatched based on your rules.

**FAQs**

1. **What if I don't want to offer it to everyone at the same time?**
   No problem, you can set the time window to 24 hours and we'll wait an entire day before moving to your next vendor.
2. **What happens if nobody takes the job?**
   The Latchel team will start calling your vendors to reengage them and if another 24 hours passes after that, we'll pop the job up on in your property manager queue on the dashboard so that you can help.

## Appendix D - Vendor Experience

While talking to the Property Manager about the Vendor Experience, you can also mention some of the following selling points:

One of the biggest struggles today is vendor followup. You send out an email. You pray that your vendors get to it. And you only find out nothings been done when you get an angry resident call.

(if in-house) And for your in-house techs it can just be a mess for them to stay up to date on jobs and get notified for new jobs without a ton of time going into planning.

What you'll love about Latchel is how easy it is for your vendors. As long as they have a smartphone, it is super easy for them to interact with us over text.

- **Ease of Use:** Either on their phone or email, your vendor just clicks any of the links we send them and they don't need any new usernames or passwords. All mobile friendly. And the job is clearly laid out with a budget limit, shows the triage results, the location, and it is super easy for the vendor to manage the work. But my favorite part is how much easier it is for them to schedule work and how we feed that back into visibility for YOU.
- **Visibility:** When the vendor accepts the job, we've already collected the residents' availability and we make it so easy for them to pick the time that works best for them. So let's say the vendor picks Friday 8am to Noon here.
- **Easy of Use:** And in just a couple clicks without having to play phone tag with the resident, your vendor has confirmed an appointment time. And you see we've texted both the vendor and resident a confirmation.
- At any time the vendor can access the job from the links we've sent them or save their dashboard to their home screen and again, no logins or passwords or downloads required.
- **Visibility:** Your techs can provide an assortment of real time updates that automatically feed back to you and your team. They'll give you more information as they complete jobs so you know if additional work is needed and if it was tenant caused. (This conveniently feeds right back into the resident damage reimbursement.)
- **Ease of Use:** And of course we make it super easy for the vendors to upload invoices or create them so we can send that to your accounting team. We do no markups or anything like that. So if maintenance is a profit center for you, you get to keep making revenue there. And at any time the vendor can see all their jobs on an easy to use dashboard so you ensure faster scheduling and faster updates from them.

**FAQs**

1. **What happens if a vendor claims a job, but then they never schedule?**
   They get 24 hours to schedule the job before it would drop back into Latchel's queue and get offered again to other vendors or in-house techs.
2. **Can the vendor change the budget amount before accepting a job?**
   No, the vendor isn't allowed to change a job budget, but when they go to extra options they can add an estimate and then you can approve the new estimate to change the budget limit.
3. **What does the need help button do?**
   It triggers the Latchel team to call your vendor or in-house tech.
