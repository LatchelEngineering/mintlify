## Summary of the SDR Role

~99% of your Leads will be [Tier 1 and Tier 2 Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs)](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2902917222) from inbound leads. The majority of the balance of your Leads will be [Tier 3 and 4 MQLs](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2902917222) ), which [will not be auto-assigned to you](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2931032140/Lead+Assignment+Rules+Notifications) and you should only work them if you don't have Tier 1 and Tier 2 available.

Only if you have no inbound leads, you will fill your availability with outbound prospecting. In that case, you can contact cold accounts that we have opportunities lost in the past that may be open for re-starting a conversation (check the notes on why they didn't move forward!). The further in our pipeline they got, most likely they may be to be open to re-engage.

Lastly, and only if absolutely needed, look for leads by doing internet research. Searching leads on your own is very inefficient, so it is highly encouraged to first verify with your manager if there is no other option before going on this route.

Your job is to turn the MQLs into Opportunities who meet with AEs and ultimately become happy customers.

You do the job by:

- **Working smart.** Running standard team cadences, but personalized and tailored to the lead's interest and needs based on their engagement with us and your own research
- **Strategizing.** Watch the calendar, industry news and events and look out for Latchel product releases, all to optimize the chances for prospect engagement. Your goal is to reach out with the right information at just the right time to land that meeting.
- **Qualifying.** Make sure the prospect is qualified to use Latchel by asking the Discovery qualification questions. If they aren't qualified, make sure they're left with a favorable impression of Latchel.
- **Follow Team Standards.** Use the standard decks, scripts and other resources. If something doesn't look right or updated, or you believe something could be done in a better way, bring it up for discussion so we can update the standards.
- Monitoring your metrics to understand what activities are most effective.

### Meetings with Prospects

As an SDR you are expected to be on time (if not slightly early) and attend every discovery meeting you have booked with a prospect. The only exceptions to this is if you are out due to sickness, PTO, or if you have a previous obligation already approved by your manager.

### Internal Latchel Meetings

Latchel as a whole really aims to not overload our calendars with unnecessary internal meetings. Please ensure you are on time and attend every shared meeting on your calendar unless you are out due to sickness, PTO, or if you have a previous obligation already approved by your manager.

### 1-on-1's with your Manager

There are several items you will discuss with your manager during your weekly meeting.

**Professional Development**

Leadership knows being an SDR is hard, it's one of the hardest jobs someone can do. It is Sales Leadership and your manager's intent that the SDR position is entry level and temporary. The goal of this position is for you to gain proficiency in what Latchel offers (our product offering), hone your sales skills, and be provided with a path for career growth within Latchel. Currently the clearest path for growth is within the sales organization of the company, with the SDR role being a feeder position for our AE (Account Executive) role.

Please keep in mind this is not the only opportunity or path for an SDR, if you do not want to move from the SDR role or wish to move to a different department these options can be discussed in detail with your manager during your 1-on-1's.

**Roadblocks**

It is your responsibility to let your manager know of any roadblocks you may be experiencing in your position. This can range from personal, technology, or work. It is your manager's responsibility to remove any roadblocks from your path in order for you to be able to perform your job functions. These roadblocks can come in any form, but can be identified as something that is causing you to underperform or stops you from being able to produce at your expected level.

<Warning>
  **Please note:**

  You do NOT need to wait for your weekly 1-on-1 to bring up any roadblocks, if something is impacting you from doing your job you should let your manager know as soon as possible. Contact with your manager can include email, slack, phone call, or text message.

  example: power outage, death in the family, a tool is down, etc.
</Warning>

### Tracking your Metrics

As a SDR that gets paid commission, it is in your best interest to keep track of your metrics should a report ever be missing anything.

There are dashboards you can use to see if you are on track for your daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly goals.

LINK TO HOW TO: VIEW OR CREATE SALESFORCE DASHBOARD

You can also have your own tracker. If you need a template, you can use this page as a guide - [SDR Role - Personal Meeting Tracker](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/edit-v2/2868281514?draftShareId=35c0ffb4-b675-4a0c-b7c8-2420ea19fac0)

The following metrics are items that you should keep track of:

WEEKLY: amount of emails sent, how many qualification calls/meetings you have (even if it doesn't result in a meeting), how many total units from those qualification calls/meetings

MONTHLY: number of discovery meetings set, number of discovery meetings ran (prospect showed up), demo's ran, which prospects didn't show to their meetings (no-showed), and time to meeting.

Time to meeting can be calculated by: qualified prospect on 2/12, discovery booked for 2/13 - time to meeting is 1 day. If you book someone on a Friday for a Monday meeting, the weekends DO NOT COUNT, time to meeting would still be 1 day.

### Inbound Lead Scripts

It is the idea that you learn Latchel as a product forward and backward so you can have high-level conversations with prospects. The goal is to give them enough information to answer their questions but also not give everything away (we do not want the qualification call & discovery call to be the same thing). You need enough information for the AE to be able to speak to the current pains the property manager discussed with you, the AE should not be spending the discovery call figuring out what the property manager wants to address.

### Outbound Lead Scripts

Outbound calling is a whole other beast. You have a lot of factors going against you: prospect is busy/not expecting your call, prospect may not have any pain we can speak to, you may not be able to get in contact with a decision maker. There's plenty more, but the idea is that when you do speak to someone - its is very important you know Latchel and the pain we aim to fix. You have a small window to get that idea through to who you speak with.

Our current outbound scripting incorporates a lot of trial and error, in addition to the new [messaging guidelines](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2848522265/Log+Lines+Latchel+Messaging+Guide#Messaging-Slide-Dec) recently released. The official script provided to the SDRs is intended to be closely adhered to; however, it doesn't mean you should read word for word. Successful conversations rarely follow the flow of the script in its entirety. SDRs have an obligation to be so familiar with the script that not only are they able to deviate off script when the conversation requires it, but also that they are able to return to the script seamlessly in order to continue the conversation to a successful conclusion. It is recommended that you don't follow the script word for word, but that you utilize words and phrasing you are comfortable with while staying close to what the script is prompting. This will allow for a more natural tone and eliminates the impression that you are reading off of a script. [Script - Outbound Call](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2872967184)

Here is a really great guide with some tips on how to successfully outbound prospect [https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1dlcmrGKWcjy\_ZolKfRiJFy9Vetf2uL9oTrFgoWlE0N8/edit#slide=id.p](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1dlcmrGKWcjy_ZolKfRiJFy9Vetf2uL9oTrFgoWlE0N8/edit#slide=id.p)

**Prospects No-Showing to a booked meeting**

We combat a prospect no-showing to our scheduled meetings by setting stellar meeting expectations, as well as following up with the prospect 24 hours prior to the meeting. This must be done every single time to minimize the amount of no-shows. You, as the SDR, maintain the responsibility to work in conjunction with the assigned AE (Account Executive), where possible, to get the prospect to a future meeting. At the end of the day the obligation to get the meeting ran with an AE falls on your shoulders.

One tip for stopping no-shows:

Email all guests 1 min after meeting started.
