## Our Archetypes

Property managers' operations, goals and strategies come in a smorgasbord of flavors. But, Latchel's primary customers tend to fall into one of five archetypes, or categories:

(1) professional property manager,

(2) real estate brokerages,

(3) venture backed property managers,

(4) institutional owner / operators and

(5) Build to Rent.

(6) Other profiles we're willing to work with

## Professional Property Managers

Latchel's "bread and butter" customer archetype, the traditional property manager, makes up the majority of Latchel's customer base. Learn [here](https://latchel.coassemble.com/student/course/58657#/) about a property manager's job and role. Know this archetype well. Very well. This is the customer you'll be selling to for your first 90 days at Latchel and likely beyond.

### Their key characteristics:

- Number of Units: 250 to 1,000
- Average Deal Size: $2,500 to $10,000
- Geography: Generally focused on a particular region (e.g., Southeast United States)
- No. of Employees: 2 to 10
- Decision makers: Usually titled Property Manager or Broker/Owner. Less frequently, CEO or President

### Business Objectives:

- Reduce maintenance overhead, including time, anxiety, costs, personnel
- Grow door count
- Create scalable maintenance process so that they can grow door count without growing employee base

### Average Sales Cycle:

Depends on the size of their company, but it is ~ 14 days to close.

### Primary Pain Points:

- Maintenance
  - Overwhelming, chaotic and time consuming
  - Is a cost center that is a time void with little visibility on efficiency (e.g., how are vendors performing)
- Thin profit margins
- Poor online reputation, damaging competitive ability to attract owner clients and renters

For a deeper dive on pain points, please [learn more here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1w-0d0h4Bem3L932_KhanZ_f2L2cjSkNGEQfuOUU_BfM/). Visit also our [Log Lines & Latchel Messaging Guide](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2848522265) page to learn more on how we turned those pain points and tensions into our Log Lines.

### Why they're willing to talk to Latchel

- To reduce time spent thinking about and coordinating routine maintenance
- To scale maintenance as they grow
- To satisfy resident demand (or quell complaints) for faster, better response times
- To advertise an amenity that will attract new owners
- Because they think Latchel is quick and easy to implement.

### Property Management Technology they already Use:

Property management software that helps with lease administration and accounting. For example, Rent Manager, Appfolio, Yardi, Propertyware, Buildium.

Technology that facilitates tours and viewings, such as Rently, Show Mojo, Show Digs and lead management, like Tenant Turner

### Best times to Engage:

Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday are best for calling, Mornings \> Afternoon

Tuesday and Wednesdays are particularly good days to start cadences
