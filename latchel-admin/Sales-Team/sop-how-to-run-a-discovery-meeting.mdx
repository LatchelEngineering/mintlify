## Goal of the Discovery Call

The goals of the Discovery meeting are to: (1) build a profile of the prospect– be curious!, (2) educate and excite the prospect on key benefits of Latchel, based on the prospect's profile and (3) verify the prospect is qualified (SDRs should already have qualified them!)

## How to Run the Discovery Call

### Prepare for the Meeting

- Research
  - Read notes from the SDRs
  - Research the Company and the Company contact online
  - Look at how a resident would interact with their website.
  - Find out how they are presenting benefits, if they have them.
  - Based on your current knowledge, identify the value props most likely to address anticipated pain points, but be prepared to talk about other pain points as you will only truly identify their pain-points during the meeting
- Set an Agenda for the meeting
  - This should reflect your strategy to approach the conversation
  - Have your goals clearly defined
  - Current Selling Focus: [Sales Strategy 2024](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2940928007)

### Join the Meeting

- Join the video meeting using Google Meeting or Zoom
- Join a few minutes before the start of the meeting to ensure you have everything ready.
- You should never be late!

### Meeting Intro

- Beginning of the call, SDR introduces the AE, maybe recaps the last convo with the lead and then hands the call over for the AE and jumps off.
- Present the agenda for the meeting and set expectations for the meeting goals
  - We'd like to find out if we are truly a fit for them today. If we are a good fit, explain what next steps would be, and who needs to be involved in those next steps.

### Meeting Presentation & Discussions

AE should build rapport with the lead and ask questions to learn more about their business, process, gaps and goals. While the lead is providing their answers, listen and take notes. Do not interrupt!

### Wrap-up the Meeting

1. Recap the call.
2. State next steps/ expectation and ask the lead to bring questions/ email additional questions for the next call, as we would like to make sure we get any questions answered,
3. Schedule Demo
   - Does anyone else need to be involved here?
     - Decision Maker? Maintenance? Property Manager? Office Manager?
   - Pick a date and schedule the demo to as soon as possible
4. If it possible, ask how soon would you like to start implementing our service?

### Follow-up after the Meeting

1. If not done yet, schedule the next meeting with all of the attendees and send the invite
2. Send a follow-up email with the follow-up package (link below) attached:
   - Use the applicable the email template from this [file](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1f6cMSkcBXD5hRYKWkjCyJzcTeoPbAGsjafkucylRDH8/edit). The key information to include are:
     - Make sure to customize the greeting and email close message to your style
     - Make sure to adjust the information to the specifics of your conversation
     - Set expectations for the next steps
       - Demo - Talk about Latchel capabilities, demo features, talk pricing and steps to implement Latchel. Answer any questions.
       - Include date and time of demo
     - Let them know we would be happy to answer any questions before the meeting, if they have any
