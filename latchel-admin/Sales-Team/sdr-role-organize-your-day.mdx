## Outline of Your Day

### Start your day (~10 min)

1. Define your 3-5 goals for the day!
2. Log into your [email](https://mail.google.com).
3. Log into [SalesForce](https://login.salesforce.com).
4. Log into the [Latchel app](https://app.latchel.com).
5. If you're feeling social, send a good morning to your team on slack
6. Warm up your voice (especially good for those morning meetings!)

- The "Hum." Exhale slowly, humming until you have exhaled all of your air. Repeat approximately five times
- The "Ha." Stand and place your hand on your abdomen. Breathe in by expanding your stomach outward; you are now breathing from your diaphragm. Exhale slowly, uttering, "ha ha ha ha." Push you abdomen in with every syllable. Repeat.
- Lip trills and flutters. Roll your tongue on the roof of your mouth to make "trr" or "rr" sound.
- Descending nasal consonants. Say the word "onion," stretching the "ny" sound and voice it downward in pitch.
- Tongue twisters. Memorize a few of tongue twisters, like "red leather yellow leather" and repeat them to get your mouth loosened up.
- Yawn and sigh. Open your mouth as if to yawn and let your voice sigh loudly from the top of your register down to its lowest note.

### Answer Emails (~40 min)

Make sure you're regularly catching up on emails with prospects. This will be one of the best ways to get prospects into discovery calls with you or to get them into exploratory calls if you're able to qualify them over an email conversation.

Prospects expect quick responses, at minimum, you should be checking your email in the morning and afternoon. If you have high email volume, it sometimes make sense to check your email three times a day.

---

### Complete Assigned Tasks (~1.5 hrs)

Start your day by completing any pending tasks that are due. This is the most important part of your day because this is when you nurture your existing leads to get them into exploratory meetings. Tasks may include:

1. Follow-up calls
2. Follow-up emails
3. Adding fresh contacts to the [outbound email cadence](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2870804487)
4. Sending meeting updates and reminders
5. Anything the AE's may need help with on your deals

Working your tasks consistently and reliably will be one of the most productive ways for you to build relationships with prospects to get the appointment.

<Warning>
  It is important to know when to stop following up with a prospect. If you've run an entire cadence on a prospect (either outbound or inbound) and if there has been no engagement from the contact - they will be automatically marked as "Send to Marketing" to be put on a long term nurturing email drip. No further action is needed from you.
</Warning>

### Work Your Leads (~4hrs)

\[Note: Take your lunch break in the middle of prospecting. Come back refreshed.\]

**Work Inbound Leads**

1. Inbound Leads:
   - These are your highest quality & most likely to convert to a meeting.
     - To learn about how the inbound leads are segmented into tiers, check the [glossary page](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2902917222)
   - It is suggested you start with these leads first in your day (sort by time zone so you aren't calling someone on the west coast at 6 AM). Some cadences even have a 2nd call on some days if you call early enough (our inbound demo request cadence)
2. Do lead research - it can be highly beneficial to find additional information about the company/person before you call them.
   - Some examples: what property management software are they using? Do they have a blurb somewhere on the website stating how many units they manage? Do they have a team page for you to know the work title of who you're trying to speak with? Etc.
3. There are Service Level Agreements (SLA's) we must adhere to with our inbound leads:
   - When an inbound lead is assigned to you, you have a maximum of 1 day to get them into a cadence.
   - Once a lead is in a cadence they should be there for a maximum of 2 weeks. There are 2 outcomes:
     - 0 response from the lead during the cadence - lead will be moved back to Marketing
     - Lead responses and books a meeting
   - Once a lead has confirmed they want to book, it should take a maximum of a week to qualify them and then set the next discovery meeting with the AE.
   - Once the AE and the prospect complete the discovery call we should get the demo call booked withing a week. This is more on the AE then the SDR, but the SDR may need to help on this.

<Note>
  For a deeper dive on SLA's and the process to work on these leads, please reference [Draft - SOP - SDR Inbound Process: How to Process a Lead](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2868543685)
</Note>

**Work Outbound Leads**

Outbound Leads:

- This if your filler - if you have touched all your highly engaged inbound leads and all your assigned leads then you can start outbound prospecting!
- These people are going about their day when we reach out to them. Whether it be via call, email, text, or on social media, these people are not expecting us to be reaching out. These leads are harder to convert as we do not know if they are actively looking for a solution we can help with.

General prospecting tips/tricks:

- If you seem to have trouble getting in contact with someone, try calling outside of business hours a time or two. Sometimes phone trees are different after hours and you may get a dial by name directory that wasn't there from 9 AM- 5 PM.
  - Also, don't call at the same time every time. Try calling at 9 AM one day, 3 PM the next time you call, 11 AM the next, etc.
- Try texting office numbers, you may be surprised when someone texts you back.
- When adding people to your outbound email cadences always sort by last contacted date - try to hit people we've never reached out to, or when it's been several years - another good strategy is reaching out to people that have agreed to a meeting with us in the past.
- Your manager will give you fresh leads every week, but feel free to find people on your own.
- If you get someone to agree to a meeting, you now need to apply the same Inbound Lead SLA's to this lead.

<Warning>
  Always make sure to do a duplicate check for an account or contact in Salesforce before adding anyone. Do a search for the name, email, and phone number - if all of those are clear you should be OK to add them.
</Warning>

### Lead Research (~1hr)

Here are some tips to follow to find email and phone information for Leads that need more research:

1. Google search the persons name and phone number OR their name and email address. Often times Google will direct you to an About page on their company website with more information like phone numbers and email.
2. If you know their email address, go to their domain name and do some research on their website.
3. If you only have their phone number, you can also try a phone number look up on [yellowpages.com](https://www.yellowpages.com) or in Google.

Here are some tips to for sourcing brand new leads to enhance your lead flow when you need to. Remember that you should only look for leads if there is no other lead available from Marketing.

1. [https://www.allpropertymanagement.com/](https://www.allpropertymanagement.com/) : Search an area by zip code. This resource will show you a list of top management companies for that zip code. Don't click "request quote". Instead copy and paste and name of the management company (and their address) into Google. You'll be taken right to their website and you can do your own lead research.
2. Do a Google Search for "property management company in \[City\], \[State\]". Google usually provides some top results for you to research. Usually companies that appear in the top of this search will be larger from all the SEO they get.
3. Request more leads from your manager. Latchel has a large reserve of leads that we can assign you in batches of 100 leads at a time. Many of these will require more research from you, but it will help get you started.

### Wrap Up Your Day (~10 min)

The most important thing to do when you wrap up your day is to make sure you've done:

1. **Email Check:** If you have any last minute emails to send, now's the time to send those off. If you promised someone that you'd get back to them before the end of the day, make sure you do it.
2. **Say Goodbye:** Give the rest of your team a happy send off when you leave for the day (👋).
