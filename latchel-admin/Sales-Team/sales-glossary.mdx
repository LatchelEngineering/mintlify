<Note>
  **Purpose of this document**

  Provide a list of Sales specific terms, acronyms / abbreviations, and their definitions
</Note>

<Note>
  **Who should read this document?**

  All Sales teams and teams interacting with Sales
</Note>

See below the abbreviations and definitions of terms used by the sales team.

For Salesforce specific terms, consult [Salesforce's glossary](https://developer.salesforce.com/docs/atlas.en-us.salesforce_pubs_style_guide.meta/salesforce_pubs_style_guide/glossary_master.htm#C)

| Word | Abbreviation | Definition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Account (Salesforce) |  | Company Main Record, which could be Potential or an Actual Customer. It is like a "folder" or a "section" in Salesforce in which all company related information, transactions or records will be attached to, i.e. an Account is the parent object of all the other sales and customer support objects (i.e. Opportunities and Cases). For an account to be created, it should have an account name (only mandatory field), which can be filled with a placeholder. Also, the Account will have a high level view of what's happening to that company. If there is an active Sales Opportunity being pursued, we can see the current status of that opportunity by looking at the "Account Lifecycle Stages" and "Lifecycle Substages". After the Opportunity is 'Closed Won', the Account stage will change to "Customer". |
| Cases (Salesforce) |  | Could be Complains, Questions, or any other Situation that requires an engagement between Customer Success and the Account, which has already had a 'Closed Won' opportunity in the past and Account stage is set to Customer. |
| Customer |  | An opportunity that has signed a contract to begin service |
| Discarded Accounts |  | Someone we will not attempt to contact or market to ever again. |
| Engagement Scores |  | It is a measurement of how much a prospect has interacted with Latchel content and marketing initiatives. Engagement Scoring: [https://app.hubspot.com/property-settings/23701847/properties?type=0-1&search=score&action=edit&property=hubspotscore](https://app.hubspot.com/property-settings/23701847/properties?type=0-1&search=score&action=edit&property=hubspotscore) |
| Ideal Customer Profile | ICP | Refers to the characteristics to the specific market segment we are pursuing and qualifying for an Opportunity. Latchel's ICP - [Latchel's Ideal Customer Profile](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/edit-v2/2880110630?draftShareId=3f470f75-406d-40f9-addc-9e4f4dee2de9). SDR qualification criteria - [Qualification Meeting Questions](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2868248756) |
| Inbound |  | When the lead has engaged with one or more of our content, or have reached out to us directly |
| Inbound Tiers - over 50 | T1, T2, T3, T4 | The inbound prospects are prioritized based on their level of engagement so we can prioritize the ones with higher engagement and higher potential for conversion. Tier 1 are the ones with the highest level of engagement. The definition of tiers are being worked on and documented in these files: MQL definitions (as desired): [MQL Tier Definitions](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1gixe9ME6_isIVTD3XIWtlQcfaVdfX8EGtIBvvc00aGU/edit#heading=h.52nksuu2weda). MQL definitions (as implemented): [2023-11-02 Tier Definitions \+ Metrics](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1NOZA-NJSMTPR4xn7xKzs2_Dt2_YwfjgWlkb858t-GdU/edit) |
| Inbound Tier - sub 50 | A, B, C, D | The same definition as the Inbound Tiers over 50, but for prospects with less than 50 units. Tier A→ Self sign-up. Tier B, C, D → What happens to them? |
| Lead |  | Have replied or engaged with our content in a positive way (we don't know yet if they are interested or want to move forward yet, but they are not discarded) and the record exists in Salesforce. Have tiers underneath depending on engagement level |
| Marketing Qualified Lead | MQL | A new inbound prospect that hasn't been reached but some qualifying information might be available based on the form they submitted |
| Opportunity (Salesforce) |  | A lead that meets our ideal customer profile (i.e. "qualified") and has expressed buying intent. Potential Sale Transaction (Deal). It could be either a new opportunity for a brand new account, a new opportunity for an account that hasn't closed before (Re-cycle), or a new opportunity for an existing customer (upgrade). Note: When a new opportunity is created for an existing customer, the account stage remains as "Customer". |
| Outbound |  | When we are reaching out to prospects that we acquire via any "list" or online research. These prospects have not interacted with any of our content and may not even know about us. |
| Prospect |  | Any potential customer that has not responded yet to any of our content/outreach. Any random list with zero engagement - conference list, website list |
| Sales Qualified Lead | SQL | A lead that meets our ideal customer profile (i.e. "qualified") |
