## SECTION A) Happy Path Process

### Process Overview

The flow below shows the steps of how an AE will handle an Opportunity from the time the lead is transferred to them, from the SDR, during the Discovery Meeting, until the lead is sent to Onboarding (i.e. Onboarding Kick-Off meeting is scheduled AND the prospect is present), which at that time, the ownership of that account is transferred to the Onboarding Team.

The "Happy Path" assumes that everything went well and the Opportunity moved to the next step of the process. When there is an issue during that step, refer to Section B of this document to know how to proceed.

These steps are aligned with the Opportunity stages and substages in Salesforce that we will use to track the status of each lead and deal, and measure performance of each process step. This tracking will enable us to define and prioritize process improvements.

Throughout the process, you will be taking notes of your engagement with the lead and some key account information. Please refer to this SOP on where the notes should be left [SOP - How to Leave Notes for an Opportunity in Salesforce](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2907144246)

### Step 1 - Discovery Meeting

**How to work this step?**

Find instructions on how to run this meeting in this page: [SOP - How to Run a Discovery Meeting](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2883518493/SOP+-+How+to+Run+a+Discovery+Meeting)

**Salesforce Tracking**

- The Salesforce Opportunity Stage should be "Discovery Appointment Scheduled" until the meeting is complete
- The "Discovery Appointment Scheduled" stage means: The meeting has been scheduled in the AE calendar by a SDR
- How does the lead get to this stage? SDRs will schedule the meeting and create the Opportunity that will be at this stage
- Substages: If the meeting needs to be reschedule for any reason, the substage should be changed to "Rescheduling Discovery Meeting". If one meeting wasn't enough to finalize the Discovery conversation, the substage should be changed to "Discovery Call Follow-up". If the lead is not moving forward, you will move the Opportunity to Disco Complete before Closing Lost, that way we can ensure the SDRs get their commission paid
- How long should the opportunity be in this stage? Max 3 days

### Step 2 - Book a Demo / Team Call

**How to work this step?**

The AE should already set the appointment for the Demo meeting during the Discovery Meeting to avoid creating delays in the process and creating a risk the lead will disengage

If the Demo was not scheduled or needs to be rescheduled:

- Include suggested demo times with calendar links in the follow-up email after the Discovery Meeting
- Touch account with email or call every three days if no demo booked after Discovery Meeting

**Salesforce Tracking**

- The Salesforce Opportunity Stage should be "Discovery Complete", substage should be either: (1) "Scheduling Demo (AE)" or "Demo Scheduled"
- This stage means: The Discovery Meeting has happened and the deal will move forward, so the AE is either working on scheduling a Demo, or has already had it scheduled
- How does the lead get to this stage? The AE changes to this stage at the end of the Discovery Meeting
- Substages: If the AE has scheduled the meeting, the substage should be "Demo Scheduled". If the AE is working on scheduling the demo, the substage should be "Scheduling Demo (AE)". If the AE is struggling to get a response from the lead to schedule the meeting, they will engage the SDR to help with the re-engagement and demo scheduling. In this case, the substage should be "Scheduling Demo (SDR)" and the AE should change the substage as they engage SDRs to help with the scheduling. If there was already a demo meeting but it was not enough to cover all of the topics or the lead has more questions, the AE will schedule a follow-up meeting and change the stage to "Demo Call Follow-up". If the lead is not moving forward, you will move the Opportunity to Demo Complete before Closing Lost, that way we can ensure the SDRs get their commission paid
- How long should the opportunity be in this stage? max 7 days

### Step 3 - Run the Demo / Team Call

**How to work this step?**

Find instructions on how to run this meeting in this page: [Draft -- SOP - How to Run a Demo Meeting](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2891120719/Draft+--+SOP+-+How+to+Run+a+Demo+Meeting)

**Salesforce Tracking**

- The "Demo Scheduled" substage means: The Demo meeting has been scheduled and the AE is waiting for the meeting date
- How does the lead get to this stage? The AE manually changes to this substage when they schedule the Demo Meeting
- How long should the opportunity be in this stage? max 7 days since booked

### Step 4 - Demo Follow-up Call (MM only)

**How to work this step?**

If a follow-up session to the first demo needs to be schedule to finalize going over all the lead's questions about our products, we should schedule a follow-up demo session.

**Salesforce Tracking**

- The Salesforce Opportunity Stage should be "Discovery Complete", substage "Demo call follow-up"
- The "Demo call follow-up" substage means: The first demo was complete and the lead has remaining or additional questions that will need to be answered in another call. Or the first demo call was not enough time to complete the planned demo.
- How does the lead get to this stage? The AE moves to this substage if needed, at the end of the first Demo meeting
- How long should the opportunity be in this stage? max 7 days since booked

### Step 5 - Build Contract

[SOP - How to Create and Send a Contract in Salesforce](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SAL/pages/2891087913)

**How to work this step?**

- Click "New Document" on the contract page in the Opportunity.
- Find the correct contract for what the PM is signing on with Latchel for.
- Fill in any fields that were no auto-assigned from salesforce

**Salesforce Tracking**

- The Salesforce Opportunity Stage should be "Demo Complete", substage "Building Contract"
- The "Demo Complete" \+ substage "Building Contract" means: All of the demo calls needed were complete and the lead is ready to start working on a contract, so the AE is starting to fill up the contract templates to send to the lead
- How does the lead get to this stage? After the last demo meeting, if multiple meetings, the AE changes the stage to "Demo Complete" and substage to "Building Contract"
- How long should the opportunity be in this stage? 1 day?

## SECTION B) How to Handle Issues Along the Process

General Guideline for Close Lost: At any step in the process, the deal may get stuck. If it the AE is unable to get the deal moving within 4 weeks, the deal should be moved to Closed Lost.

The last sales person to communicate with a Contact associated with the Account should send a breakup email and the Account should move to "Prospecting/Lost Opportunities"

Other Issues:

While working through the steps above, the AE may face a technical issue, or the lead may not be responsive or may respond negatively and don't move forward. In those cases, refer to the table below to know how to proceed.
