<Note>
  This comprehensive guide provides a detailed overview of how record ownership is managed within our Salesforce Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system. Effective record ownership is vital for streamlining processes, enhancing accountability, and ensuring accurate tracking of critical data across our organization.
</Note>

## Account Record Ownership

**Purpose:**

- Indicate which person from the SDR team is working or recently worked on an Account.
- Measure how many accounts were distributed in the previous month.
- Once a deal is closed, this value should remain with the SDR rep name for tracking purposes.

## Opportunity Record Ownership

**Purpose:**

- Indicate which person from the AE team is working on an Opportunity.
- Track the deal conversion rates.
- Pay commissions to the AEs.
- Use the additional SDR Owner field to pay commissions to the SDRs.
- Once a deal is closed, this should remain with the AE rep name for tracking conversion rates and commission payments.

## Customer Record Ownership

**Purpose:**

- The Customer record ownership field does not currently have a specific purpose.
- Sometimes it matches the CSM, but not necessarily.
- The CS team primarily works with the CSM value.
- Customer record ownership is not a critical factor in this context.

## Case Record Ownership

**Purpose:**

- Indicate the representative currently or last responsible for a Case.
- This differs from the CSM since a Case can be worked on by different roles (e.g., Client Care Rep).
