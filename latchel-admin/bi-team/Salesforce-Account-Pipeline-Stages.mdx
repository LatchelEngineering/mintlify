<Note>
  Pipeline Stages for Accounts are called Lifecycle Stages and Lifecycle Substages.
</Note>

## How to see and edit an Opportunity record stage and substage?

<Tip>
  You can open any Account record page and you'll be able to see the Lifecycle Stage in the path on top (1).
</Tip>

<Tip>
  You can see the Lifecycle Substage of an Account record on the details layout (3).
</Tip>

<Tip>
  In order to update a Lifecycle Stage or Lifecycle Substage click on the "Mark Lifecycle Stage as Complete" button (2), then edit the dependencies (4), and then click on Done.
</Tip>

## How to configure the Account Pipeline Stages?

<Check>
  In Salesforce there are different configurations that involve the Pipeline Stages. The first one is the field configuration.

  Go to Setup \> Object Manager \> Account \> Fields & Relationships
</Check>

Once here you should be able to access and configure the Lifecycle Stage and Lifecycle Substage fields:

## What's the relationship between these two fields?

<Check>
  There are dependencies for these two fields.

  Go to Setup \> Object Manager \> Account \> Fields & Relationships \> Field Dependencies \> Edit (Lifecycle Stage controlling field)
</Check>

Here you will be able to see and reassign the dependencies of the two fields:

<Frame caption="View of the Field Dependencies editor">
  ![View of the Field Dependencies editor](/images/DA-6.png)
</Frame>

📌 For the Account is mandatory to have these two fields filled out, otherwise, an error will be prompted when saving the Account record.

## How do I configure the Path?

<Check>
  Go to Setup \> Search for "path settings" in the Quick Find text box \> Click on Path Settings \> Click on Edit on the "Account Lifecycle Sales" path. \> Click on Next
</Check>

Here you should be able to see the Path.

In order to configure the order of the Lifecycle stages, you will have to go to the "Fields & Relationships" configuration on each of the Lifecycle Stage and Lifecycle Substage fields.

<Frame caption="View of the edit screen of the Account Lifecycle Sales path">
  ![View of the edit screen of the Account Lifecycle Sales path](/images/DA-7.png)
</Frame>

If you create new stages that are not added to the "Master Account" Record Type, they will not show in the path.

<Check>
  To fix this go to Setup \> Object Manager \> Account \> Record Types \> Master Account \> Edit "Lifecycle Stage" or "Lifecycle Substage".
</Check>
