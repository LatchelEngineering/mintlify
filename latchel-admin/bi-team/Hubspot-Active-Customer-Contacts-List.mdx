## How does this work in the background:

This list pulls the information from Salesforce using the built-in sync Hubspot plugin. In other words, this list relies on a set of fields that are only updated in Salesforce. These fields are updated once a day and the information can also be seen in the Contact record in Salesforce under the 'Customer Information' section.

## What fields can I use for additional filtering:

### Mandatory filters:

<Warning>
- Email is known
- Active Customer is any of Yes
</Warning>

### Optional fields for additional filtering:

<Tip>
- Console Unit Count: Number of active units (properties) that a customer is currently managing.
- Contract Service Start Date: The commencement date of services as specified in the contract.
- Base Product: Console Subscription most recent Base Product.
- Addon: The most recent Addon unless the company has SaaS and Home Assistant contracted at the same time, in this case, Home Assistant is picked.
- Integration Software: Current active Integration Software.
- WOs Completed (last 30 days): Number of Work Orders completed in the last 30 days.
- WOs Completed w/o PM intervention (last 30 days): Number of Work Orders completed without Property Manager intervention in the last 30 days.
- Calls Handled by Latchel (last 30 days): Number of Work Orders completed without Property Manager intervention in the last 30 days.
- 5-Star Resident Reviews (last 30 days): Number of 5-Star Resident Reviews in the last 30 days.
- Service Time Tags: Category tags based on the service duration. Three possible tags based on the Contract Service Start Date: First 30 Days, First 90 Days, First 180 Days. A company can have more than one of these tags e.g. If a company has 20 days since the contract service start date, then it's in its first 30, 90, and 180 days at the same time.
- Latchel User Id: Unique Latchel Id for the user. If the contact does not have a Latchel User Id, it means, the contact is not in the Latchel console.
- Latchel Status: Latchel console user status (Yes=Active or No=inactive). Valid only for users in the Latchel console (contacts with Latchel User Id). Use 'Latchel User Id' to check if the contact is a user in the Console.
</Tip>

## How to send mass emails from Hubspot using the list:

### Create an email template:

- Click on the "Marketing" drop-down at the top and go to "email."
- Click "create email" in the top right corner.
- Select the type of email you want to create. For this example, you'll want to select "regular."
- You can select a template you would like to work off of
- Once you select a template, you can give your email a name at the top.
- Next, you can easily edit the copy by clicking into each section. You can drag and drop sections, upload photos, add CTA buttons, and more.
- When the body of your email is all set, click on the "settings" tab.
- From here, you'll fill out your "From name," "From address," "Subject line," and "preview text." You'll also want to make sure you have the corresponding subscription type selected along with a campaign if you have one created for this email.

### Send or schedule mass emails:

- Next, select the "send or schedule" tab at the top.
- Click the "Send to" drop-down and select the list you would like to send this to. You can also exclude recipients in the "Don't send to" drop-down.
- You can also send yourself a test email by clicking "send test email" in the top right corner and entering your email address if you want to see how the email will look in your inbox.
- Last, you can choose to either "send now" or "schedule for later." If you choose to "schedule for later," be sure to select the check box that says "send based on contacts' time zones instead."
- When your email is ready to go, click "review and schedule" in the top right corner.
