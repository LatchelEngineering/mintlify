This tool allows the Customer Success department to convert to Cases all new incoming emails that are received at [success@latchel.com](mailto:success@latchel.com).

**Important notes:**

- [success@latchel.com](mailto:success@latchel.com) is a mask of [onboarding@latchel.com](mailto:onboarding@latchel.com)
- All emails that are received at [success@latchel.com](mailto:success@latchel.com) will be forwarded to the “Email Services Address” which is generated by SalesForces and can be located on Salesforce on Setup \> PLATFORM TOOLS \> Feature Settings \> Service \> Email-to-Case.

<Frame>
  ![Email-to-Case configuration](/images/MC-1.png)
</Frame>

- Additionally, [onboarding@latchel.com](mailto:onboarding@latchel.com) has been configured to never send any emails to the spam folder. This can be configured on Gmail on Settings \> Filters and Blocked Addresses

<Frame>
  ![Gmail filters configuration](/images/MC-2.png)
</Frame>

- “Case Owner” needs to be empty so when a new Case is created is assigned to Customer Success.

<Frame>
  ![Case Owner field](/images/MC-3.png)
</Frame>

- “Create Task from Email” has been disabled on Salesforce. This means that every time a new Case is created due to an incoming email, no task will be generated.

<Frame>
  ![Create Task from Email setting](/images/MC-4.png)
</Frame>
