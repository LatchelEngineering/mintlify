---
title: "Email-to-Case"
---

This tool allows the Customer Success department to convert to Cases all new incoming emails that are received at success@latchel.com.

**Important notes:**

success@latchel.com is a mask of onboarding@latchel.com

All emails that are received at success@latchel.com will be forwarded to the “Email Services Address” which is generated by SalesForces and can be located on Salesforce on Setup > PLATFORM TOOLS > Feature Settings > Service > Email-to-Case.

  <img src="/images/MC-1.png" alt="Email-to-Case configuration" />
</Frame>

Additionally, onboarding@latchel.com has been configured to never send any emails to the spam folder. This can be configured on Gmail on Settings > Filters and Blocked Addresses

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/MC-2.png" alt="Gmail filters configuration" />
</Frame>

“Case Owner” needs to be empty so when a new Case is created is assigned to Customer Success.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/MC-3.png" alt="Case Owner field" />
</Frame>

“Create Task from Email” has been disabled on Salesforce. This means that every time a new Case is created due to an incoming email, no task will be generated.
