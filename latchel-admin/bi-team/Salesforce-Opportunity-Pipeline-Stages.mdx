<Note>
  Pipeline Stages for Opportunities are called Stages and Substages.
</Note>

<Note>
  Unlike the Account object, for the Opportunity we have 4 record types instead of just 1.
</Note>

## How to see and edit an Opportunity record stage and substage?

<Tip>
  You can open any Opportunity record page and you'll be able to see the stage in the path on top (1).
</Tip>

<Tip>
  You can see the substage of an Opportunity record on the details layout (3).
</Tip>

<Tip>
  In order to move a Stage or Substage click on the "Mark Stage as Complete" button (2), then edit the dependencies (4), and then click on Done.
</Tip>

## Opportunity Record Types:

<Check>
  For the purposes of this documentation, an Opportunity Record Type determines the specific stages of each of the pipelines in an Opportunity. Even though the stages and substages are added in the fields "Stage" and "Substage", a Record Type creates the space to define which stages will belong to each pipeline.
</Check>

<Check>
  Go to Setup \> Object Manager \> Opportunity \> Record Types
</Check>

<Frame caption="List of the current active Record Types">
  ![List of the current active Record Types](/images/DA-11.png)
</Frame>

## Sales Processes:

<Check>
  If the record type gives us the space to define the stages per each pipeline, a Sales Process difines the exact stages and order per each pipeline.
</Check>

<Check>
  Go to Setup \> Search for "sales processes" in the Quick Find text box \> Click on Sales Processes \> Click on Edit on the "Account Lifecycle Sales" path. \> Click on Next
</Check>

<Frame caption="List of the current active Sales Processes">
  ![List of the current active Sales Processes](/images/DA-12.png)
</Frame>

## Opportunity Substages:

<Check>
  The only pipeline that has substages is the "Sales Pipeline".

  Go to Setup \> Object Manager \> Opportunity \> Fields & Relationships \> Substage
</Check>

<Frame caption="Stages and Substages in the Sales Pipeline">
  ![Stages and Substages in the Sales Pipeline](/images/DA-13.png)
</Frame>

## Relationship between Stage and Substage:

<Check>
  There are dependencies for these two fields.

  Go to Setup \> Object Manager \> Opportunity \> Fields & Relationships \> Field Dependencies \> Edit (Stage controlling field)
</Check>

Since the Sales Pipeline is the only one that has Substages assigned, we will configure dependencies on those stages only.

<Frame caption="View of the Field Dependencies editor">
  ![View of the Field Dependencies editor](/images/DA-14.png)
</Frame>

## How do I configure the Path?

<Check>
  Go to Setup \> Search for "path settings" in the Quick Find text box \> Click on Path Settings \> Click on Edit on the "Account Lifecycle Sales" path. \> Click on Next
</Check>

In order to configure the order of the stages, you will have to follow the Sales Processes instructions above.
