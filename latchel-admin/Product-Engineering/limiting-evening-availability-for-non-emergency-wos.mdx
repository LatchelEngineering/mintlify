## What is the Product Release?

We have provided the ability for Admins and PMs to limit the evening availability window (4:00 PM - 8:00 PM) on the property, portfolio, or company level for non-emergency work orders only.

## What was the Previous State?

Previously, we did not support any ability for PMs to restrict any availability window for work order appointments. As a result, Residents could provide any availability between 8:00 AM - 8:00 PM and Service Providers could choose any one of those Resident availabilities for all non-emergency work orders.

## Who Is Impacted?

Residents and Service Providers will be the most impacted. When a PM restricts the evening availability window (4:00 PM - 8:00 PM) for a property/portfolio/company, Residents and Service Providers will be restricted from choosing the evening time slots.

## When Is It Relevant?

PMs can choose to apply this to any property, portfolio, or their entire company. When it's applied, the properties impacted will not allow scheduling during the evening window (4:00 PM - 8:00 PM) for non-emergency work orders only. Please note that due to the nature of emergency work orders, we will always allow scheduling for all availability windows for them.

### Disabling the Evening Window

See demo [here](https://www.loom.com/share/68f4896db61c4759a2b908c1e650bf74).

<iframe src="https://www.loom.com/embed/68f4896db61c4759a2b908c1e650bf74" title="Loom video player" frameborder="0" className="w-full aspect-video rounded-xl" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen />

- Latchel Users will be able to disable evening windows at the property, portfolio, or company level.
- Once the User decides on the level, they need to then go to the property, portfolio, or company page.
- In those pages at the bottom left is a header called "Non-Emergency Scheduling" and expand it.
  <Frame>
    ![Non-Emergency Scheduling header](/images/Eng-62.png)
  </Frame>
- There will be a toggle for "Disable Evening Time Slots". When the toggle is turned on (see screenshot below), it means that evening windows have been disabled for that property, portfolio, or company.
  <Frame>
    ![Disable Evening Time Slots toggle](/images/Eng-63.png)
  </Frame>

<Note>
  Please note that inheritance for this toggle will work as it does for everything else in Latchel today. So, for example, if you start with disabling evening time slots for the entire company, it'll apply it to all portfolios and properties in that company. If you'd like to allow some of those properties to schedule for evening windows, then you'll have to turn the toggle off for those specific properties.
</Note>

### What Happens for Vacant Properties

See demo here.

<iframe src="https://www.loom.com/embed/7d6211f160444a8da21d63910316ba6b" title="Loom video player" frameborder="0" className="w-full aspect-video rounded-xl" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen />

- For properties that are marked as vacant and also have their evening windows disabled:
  - Service Providers will be limited to setting the start time of their appointments to 4:00 PM and the end time of their appointments to 6:00 PM.
  - PMs and Admins will not be limited to disabling the evening windows. However, if they choose an appointment that starts past 4:00 PM and ends past 6:00 PM, they will be given a warning message (see below). PMs and Admins can choose to override this warning by pressing "Yes". Pressing "No" will take them back to allow them to change their times to something that is before the evening window.

<Note>
  Please note that the warning message in the screenshot above is dynamic. It will inform the User at what level the evening window disability is taking place. In the screenshot above, it says that the Property has its evening windows disabled. However, if it were to be happening on the Portfolio or Company level, it will say that instead.
</Note>

## What Are Some Risks or Ways Things Could Go Wrong?

There is one edge case where if a Resident has existing evening availability on their account prior to a PM turning on the "disable evening availability" toggle, then a Service Provider will be able to choose that evening availability time slot as long as it's active.

We did not choose to cover this use case as part of this release because the solution was too complicated and it's only a temporary edge case. Since Residents are only able to submit availability up to two weeks out, this would only be a potential problem for a maximum of two weeks after a PM chooses to disable the evening availability window for those Residents.
