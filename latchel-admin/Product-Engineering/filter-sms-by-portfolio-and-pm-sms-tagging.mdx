## What is the Product Release?

We have built the ability for PMs to filter their incoming SMS by portfolio. We do this through a new auto-tagging logic that determines what work order the person is sending an SMS about. However, due to the logic ultimately being an estimation, we've also built the functionality for PMs to update the tag and/or forward the conversation to the proper portfolio. Please note that every conversation having a tag is also the stepping stone to being able to see specific SMS conversations on WO pages.

<Frame>
  ![Filter SMS by Portfolio feature overview](/images/Eng-18.png)
</Frame>

## What was the Previous State?

Previously, PMs would receive all their incoming SMS in one inbox. Enterprise customers would especially have trouble with this because there was no way to effectively split up the work amongst their team based on ownership lanes (portfolios).

## Who Is Impacted?

PMs will be using this set of features actively. Residents, Service Providers, and Owners will benefit from PMs being able to respond to them more quickly.

## When Is It Relevant?

Filter SMS by Portfolio is relevant for PMs managing incoming SMS conversations. SMS tagging and forwarding is relevant for any time a PM is having an SMS conversation.

As a result, this section outlines the options that PMs can take to update those tags and/or forward it to the proper portfolio.

#### Updating the Tag on an Existing SMS Conversation

This functionality is applicable for when a PM would like to edit the tag on the current conversation that they are having with the Owner, Resident, or Service Provider. Some examples of this include:

- Our initial auto-tagging logic was incorrect in determining that the incoming SMS was about a WO at 159 Weidmann in NJ, but it's actually about 258 Livingston in NJ. The PM who opened this incoming SMS owns the NJ portfolio and is able to help the person on this question, but needs to update the tag to the proper WO first. This is so they can reference this SMS conversation at a later date on the WO page (note that this is a future feature).
  - This example can also work for if the incoming SMS does not come in with a tag at all.
- Our initial auto-tagging logic was incorrect in determining that the incoming SMS was about a WO at 159 Weidmann in NJ, but it's actually about 425 Route 31 in NY. The PM who opened this incoming SMS owns the NJ portfolio so is unable to help the person on this question. As a result, the PM updates the tag to the proper WO and then chooses to forward it so that the proper NY portfolio owner can help them.

Please see the SOP for this workflow below. Due to this splitting out into two separate workflows at the end, the demo links will be referenced there.

#### Starting New Conversations within an SMS Window

This functionality is applicable for when the person sending an incoming SMS has more than one question about different work orders. Some examples of this include:

- Our initial auto-tagging logic was correct in determining that the incoming SMS was about a WO at 159 Weidmann in NJ. However, the person has another question about a different WO, which is at another property 258 Livingston in NJ. The PM who opened this incoming SMS owns the NJ portfolio and is able to help the person on both these questions. However, the PM will need to start a conversation for the 2nd question because it's for a different WO.
- Our initial auto-tagging logic was correct in determining that the incoming SMS was about a WO at 159 Weidmann in NJ. However, the person has another question about a different WO, which is at another property 425 Route 31 in NY. The PM who opened this incoming SMS owns the NJ portfolio so they can help with the first question. However, they are unable to answer the second one about the property in NY. As a result, the PM answers the first question and then has to start a new conversation and then chooses to forward it so that the proper NY portfolio owner can help them.

## What Are Some Risks or Ways Things Could Go Wrong?

- With this launch, PMs can only tag each conversation to one WO. As a result, they will have to start new conversations each time the topic changes. We'd like to change this in the future, but this was to de-risk the P0 launch.
- PMs must always finish any conversation they are having with the Owner/Resident/Service Provider before forwarding an SMS conversation to another PM. Due to all SMS conversations operating from one phone number, continuing a conversation with the Owner/Resident/Service Provider after it has been forwarded will result in the forwarded message not being shown as an unread message for another PM to pick up.
