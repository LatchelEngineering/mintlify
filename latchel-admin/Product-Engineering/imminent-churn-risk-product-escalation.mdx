## Overview

The purpose of this document is to define the process for a member of CS or executive leadership to request product & engineering support when an important customer is at imminent risk of churning

## How is this different than our current process?

Our current intake process consists of the following

- **#bug-report**: low-urgency bugs. Product evaluates and determines validity & priority
- **#tech-high-sev-escalations**: high-urgency bugs. Immediate allocation of engineering resources.
- **Stakeholder intake**: Regular bi-weekly or monthly rituals for CS, svc ops and finance. Product evaluates and determines priority in conjunction with stakeholder

As part of the process being discussed here, we introduce a new mechanism:

- **(NEW) Imminent Customer Risk Escalation**: high-urgency product support request from CS or executive leadership in order to (hopefully) save a customer from churning.

## Who is impacted?

The following individuals and groups are impacted:

- Head of CS
- CEO
- COO
- Product team
- Head of engineering

## When Is It Relevant?

The simplest definition we have for the moment is "when an important or strategic customer is an imminent churn risk and CS or executive leadership believes a high-urgency engineering intervention is critical to saving the customer."

## What was the Previous State?

No process was defined. [This escalation](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C03P9VB4JK1/p1674582281426159) in the #growth-product-requests channel was the primary catalyst for recognizing a process may be needed.

## What is the process?

There are three steps to the process, described below!

**Submitting the escalation**

Navigate to #churn-risk-product-escalation and bring up the shortcut menu and choose "Churn Risk Escalation." Fill out the form!

<Frame>
  ![Churn Risk Escalation shortcut form](/images/Eng-10.png)
</Frame>

**Product response**

The product team will verify receipt and ask clarifying questions. A product owner will be assigned. The product owner will conduct research and meet with stakeholders design and engineering stakeholders. We will aim to come back to the group in one business day with the following

- Re-state the problem
- Describe solution options
  - How it addresses customer problem
  - Time estimate to deliver solution
  - Pros/cons or any customer-experience tradeoffs
  - Intervention cost: what we will drop/delay (and by how much) to take up this intervention
- Make a recommendation

**Stakeholder alignment & implementation**

We'll discuss the options either async or in a meeting and come to an agreement on the best path to take!

## What Are Some Risks or Ways Things Could Go Wrong?

This is a low risk change. We can adjust as needed. Here are two risks to monitor:

- **Nuclear option**: we should probably view each use of this mechanism, while necessary and good for Latchel as a defect to our overall process. Something had to have been missed either upstream or in the regular product prioritization process for us to land here. The price of context switching is high and we've seen historically that this type of approach can lead to bespoke solutions not useful for other customers and lower quality engineering work.
- **Administrative creep**: Another slack channel to manage. Could see that being annoying for stakeholders outside of the product team.
