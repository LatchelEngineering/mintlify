## What is a design handoff?

A design handoff is the process where designers provide finalized design assets and specifications to engineers for implementation. This is a synchronous meeting.

## What is the purpose?

The primary purpose of a design handoff is to accurately communicate and confirm design intentions for the feature being delivered. This process serves as a bridge between design and development, helping to clarify any elements and aspects of the design intended for implementation. Although the grooming phase should ideally address discussions related to technical scope, this meeting can also tackle technical feasibility concerns, if necessary. The ultimate goal of this meeting is to ensure the final product built by the engineers aligns closely with the initial designs.

## When does it happen/not happen?

When the Product team has approved the final design mock-ups and the Engineering team is prepared to begin work, a design handoff is required. We've established a period of 1 to 1.5 hours following our morning daily stand-up, specifically dedicated to this handoff process. This time slot is intended exclusively for design handoffs when needed.

## Who is involved?

In this context, the key participants in the process are the designer and the engineer. The product manager also plays an integral role in confirming and discussing business requirements. At this time, the designer will be responsible for coordinating this meeting.

## What are their roles/responsibilities in the process?

The designer's role is to deliver finalized designs and specifications. The engineer's responsibility is to translate these designs into functional code, and to raise any need for clarifications or identify feasibility issues. If additional discussions are needed to clarify the scope of implementation, a follow-up session is expected. The product manager's role is to address any business requirements that may need to be clarified and why certain design elements may not be implemented due to constraints such as budget.
