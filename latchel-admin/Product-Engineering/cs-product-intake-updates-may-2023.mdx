## **Overview**

This page outlines updates to the CS product intake process, which currently involves #growth-product-requests and #bug-report Slack channels, the [Customer Growth & Support Jira board](https://latchel.atlassian.net/jira/software/c/projects/CGS/boards/33/backlog?selectedIssue=CGS-514&issueLimit=100 "https://latchel.atlassian.net/jira/software/c/projects/CGS/boards/33/backlog?selectedIssue=CGS-514&issueLimit=100"), and the bi-weekly "CS - Product Feature Intake" meeting.

## **What is changing?**

Key changes include:

- Submission of all requests (bugs and feature requests) in one channel
- Optimization of the bi-weekly "CS - Product Feature Intake" to address the most urgent product support CS requests

## **Who Is Impacted?**

The following groups are impacted:

- CSMs
- Product managers

## **When Is It Relevant?**

This applies when a CSM needs to communicate a customer's critical pain point or improvement idea.

## **What was the Previous State?**

Previously, the exact purpose of the CS - Product Feature Intake meeting was unclear, stakeholders felt stagnated, and product prioritized all bug reports, which didn't align with urgent customer needs.

## **New process details**

The updated process aims to:

- Provide CSMs visibility on product support and expected updates
- Clarify request prioritization in bi-weekly and quarterly processes
- Avoid resource waste on less impactful bugs
- Maintain or increase value delivery via the CS product intake process (current average: ~5.5 tickets/month)

## **#growth-product-requests**

New form fields and details:

**Urgency:** Main field for bi-weekly prioritization. We want to know how important the request is to keeping your customer(s) happy and/or saving them in a desperate situation. For sake of simplicity and discussion, I propose we use the same ones as the data-bi-request form:

1. If we can only get one thing this month, this is it
2. Very important
3. Important, but not urgent
4. Nice to have

**Difficulty (optional):** Assessed by the product team, used for backlog review and roadmap planning.

## **#bug-report**

CS will discontinue using #bug-report, and bugs will be reported via a dedicated bug report shortcut in #growth-product-requests. Bug reports will land in the customer growth and support channel and undergo the same triage as feature requests.

## **Bi-weekly “CS - Product Feature Intake” meeting**

- The meeting will:
  - Review new requests from the past two weeks to determine urgency and potential displacement of ongoing work
  - Review in-progress work, share updates, request assistance, and discuss as needed

## **What do the categories in [Customer Growth & Support Jira board](https://latchel.atlassian.net/jira/software/c/projects/CGS/boards/33/backlog?selectedIssue=CGS-514&issueLimit=100 "https://latchel.atlassian.net/jira/software/c/projects/CGS/boards/33/backlog?selectedIssue=CGS-514&issueLimit=100") mean?**

- “Top Requests” means themes (or in many cases specific feature requests) that Product has confirmed that (a) they could complete in a matter of weeks, if prioritized and (b) will work to include in a sprint within the next 2 months. Beginning May 22, 2023, these are monthly re-evaluated by the CS team and any proposed re-prioritization is shared with Product in bi-monthly Product \<\> CF Feature intake meeting.    
- “Consider for Roadmap” means BIG themes CS REALLY cares about– they’ll move the needle for customers– but will take significant Prod / Eng resources and potentially months to complete.  
- “Backlog” means all other requests that have been reviewed but do not fall into any other categories or elsewhere on the kanban board.  CS will attempt to review the backlog at minimum once every 6 monts.  
- “New” means all #growth-product-requests that have not been reviewed.  These should be reviewed at the 3rd monthly team meeting via prioritization poker. The goal is for CS to have a proposal for how each of these should be categories prior to the bi-monthly CS \<\> Product Feature Intake Meeting.

## **What Are Some Risks or Ways Things Could Go Wrong?**

The primary risk is a delayed response to #tech-high-severity-escalations initially posted through the normal intake process. CS should use their judgment and double-check with the team if unsure.
