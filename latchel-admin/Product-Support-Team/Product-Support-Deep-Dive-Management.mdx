# **Purpose**

This document provides a clear overview of what a _deep dive_ is, how it is diligently investigated, and how each Product Support Specialist is expected to work on it. The Product Support team receives deep-dive requests from all customer types that **Latchel** supports — including property managers, residents, owners, and service providers.

---

## **What is a Deep Dive?**

A _deep dive_ is a detailed investigation and analysis conducted by the Product Support team in response to a customer request or internal escalation. This process aims to thoroughly understand and resolve complex issues, challenges, or inquiries raised by various stakeholders.

Deep dives are also used to:

- Identify and flag gaps in Latchel’s products and processes.
- Propose improvements and process optimizations.
- Capture and better understand customer experiences to prevent repeat issues.

---

## **Deep Dive Ticket Management**

The Product Support Team uses Assembly as the primary platform for creating and managing deep-dive requests. These are submitted as _Assembly tickets_ and categorized into two main types:

1. **Customer Escalation Deep Dive (DD) Tickets**

- These tickets are raised internally by Latchel team members.
- Typically initiated when an incident requires investigation to determine root causes, clarify the nature of the failure or complaint, and propose solutions to prevent recurrence.
- Requests are submitted through the Slack workflow.

2. **Feedback Dive (SDDI) Tickets**

- These are created to review low ratings and feedback collected from various sources, including Work Order Reviews, CSAT surveys, Google Reviews, and TS Reviews.
- These are considered “mini dives” focused on understanding process gaps or points of friction.
- Findings are shared internally to inform process improvements, even if no external communication is required.

---

## **Why Deep Dives Matter**

- **Root Cause Identification:** Prevent similar issues from happening again.
- **Product and Process Improvement:** Surface opportunities to enhance Latchel’s workflows, features, or vendor performance.
- **Customer Experience Insight:** Capture patterns in resident, PM, owner, or SP feedback.
- **Cross-Team Alignment:** Ensure stakeholders across Product, Ops, and CS are aligned on resolution paths and learnings.
