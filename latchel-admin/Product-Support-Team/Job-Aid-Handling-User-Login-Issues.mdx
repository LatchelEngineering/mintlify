---
title: "Job Aid: Handling User Login Issues   "
---

<Note>
  **Objective:** Assist users (Service Providers, Property Managers, and Residents) experiencing login issues by confirming email validity and facilitating access to their accounts.
</Note>

---

### **Step 1: Confirm User is Active**

- **Access User Profile:**
  - Navigate to the user’s profile in the system.
  - Verify that the user account is active.

---

### **Step 2: Email Validity**

- **Access User Profile:**
  - Verify that the email is correctly entered and valid for the user experiencing login problems.

---

### **Step 3: Resolve Login Issues by User Type**

**A. Service Providers**

1. **Access User Profile:**
   - Locate the Service Provider’s profile.
2. **Initiate Login Email:**
   - Click the three dots located on the user profile.
   - Select "Send Account Email."

<Frame>
  ![3195076642 1](/images/3195076642-1.png)
</Frame>

- This will trigger a login email to the Service Provider.

**B. Property Managers**

1. **Access User Profile:**
   - Locate the Property Manager’s profile.
2. **Initiate Login Email:**
   - Click the three dots located on the user profile.
   - Select "Send Account Email."
   - This will trigger a login email to the Property Manager.

**C. Residents**

1. **Access Resident Profile:**
   - Locate the Resident’s profile.
2. **Obtain Public Dashboard URL:**
   - Navigate to the resident’s miscellaneous settings.
   - Copy the Resident Public Dashboard URL.
     - Refer to [Job Aid: How to Locate Public Dashboard URLs](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/3175841803)
3. **Send URL:**
   - Email the Resident Public Dashboard URL to the resident.
   - This allows the resident to access their dashboard directly.

---

**Step 4: Follow-Up**

- Ensure confirmation of receipt of email or URL by the user.
- Provide any additional assistance if further issues arise.

<Warning>
  **Note:** Always maintain privacy and security protocols when handling user information and sending emails. Confirm the user's identity by asking for their address, phone number, or another unique piece of information on file.
</Warning>
