## **Purpose of this document**

<Note>
  **Purpose of this document**

  The purpose of this document is to provide instructions for Product Support on how to escalate Subscription Billing inquiries to Accounts Receivable

  **Who should read this document?**

  Product Support Team
</Note>

### **Step-by-Step Guide**

#### **Starting the workflow**

1. In the [<u>#accounts-receivable</u>](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C05N2RRD14P "https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C05N2RRD14P") Slack channel, hover over Workflows in the toolbar and click the start icon on the Subscription Invoicing Request workflow.

<Frame>
  ![3179446305](/images/3179446305.png)
</Frame>

#### **Completing the workflow**

1. **Property Manager Company Name:** Enter the PM company name as it appears in Salesforce
2. **Salesforce Opportunity Link:** Insert the link to the **_Live_** Salesforce Opportunity. _Note: Some Property Managers have multiple Salesforce Opportunities so be sure to include the link to the one that is marked Live. Example:_

<Frame>
  ![3179839492 1](/images/3179839492-1.png)
</Frame>

3. **CSM:** Search for and select the customer’s CSM (as indicated in the Salesforce Opportunity) from the dropdown menu. If the customer does not have a CSM assigned in Salesforce, select your own name from the dropdown.
4. **Priority:** Select a priority level from the dropdown using the following criteria
   - URGENT
     - Any submission for which Category = Billing Dispute (CS)
     - Any submissions for which Category = Change Request (CS) AND the request comes in between the 1st and 8th working day of the month
     - Any submission for which the customer is clearly upset
   - Medium
     - Default setting
   - Low
     - Any submission that doesn’t require someone to follow up with the customer

<Frame>
  ![3178987662 1](/images/3178987662-1.png)
</Frame>

5. **Category:** Select from the following options. _Note: Options ending in (AR) are for AR’s use only._
   - Billing Dispute (CS): A customer is questioning the quantities and/or rates billed on a monthly subscription invoice; A customer claims they were charged more than once for the same invoice; A customer claims they are on autopay and were never billed; A customer is requesting a refund or credit
   - Change Request (CS): A customer wants to change their payment method; A customer wants to enroll in autopay; A customer wants to update their billing POC
   - General Question (CS): A customer asks us to confirm their payment method on file; A customer asks us to confirm to which address invoices are being sent (their billing POC); A customer asks us to resend them a specific invoice(s); A customer is having difficulty paying their invoice; A customer can’t access their invoice links; A customer asks what rate they are billed at
   - Past Due Invoice(s): A customer reaches out to confirm if any of their invoices are past due; A customer reaches out to request a payment plan for past due invoices

<Frame>
  ![3179282513 1](/images/3179282513-1.png)
</Frame>

6. **What do you need help with?** Summarize the request from the customer as stated in the Salesforce case. Upload any pertinent attachments or screenshots shared by the customer to the thread of the workflow submission. **Note: Before escalating, make sure you understand the customer’s request.** For example, if they state “this invoice is wrong,” respond to the customer to obtain more information about what seems incorrect to them (ex: unit count number, rate applied, a line item they don’t understand, etc.) before escalating to Accounts Receivable.

#### **Next steps**

1. Accounts Receivable will provide a response to the post by EOD PST the next day.
2. Product Support will handle the case to completion, closing the loop with the customer.
   - Because the CSM is tagged in the workflow and follows these posts, the CSM may comment in the thread to request that PS reassigns the case to them if they’d like to handle it (ex: billing question becomes a contract question).
