<Info>
  The purpose of the Weather Advisory email to PMs is to acknowledge an upcoming extreme weather event, check in on our customers, and educate them on how they can best prepare in Latchel.
</Info>

## Review Process

1. Shift Support monitors extreme weather events on a weekly basis and reports them in the [<u>#shift-support</u>](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/CUMBLQDRU) Slack channel using the Weather Report workflow. Ex: <img src="attachments/2802614303/2803204100" />
2. The Weather Report workflow sends a notification to the Product Support Manager to review the post.
3. If the Product Support Manager deems that the weather event is likely to have a major impact on our customers, the Product Support team will be instructed to follow the process below. _Note: Our goal is to send no more than 4 Weather Advisory emails per year to avoid over-notification._

## Product Support Process

Once the Product Support team has been notified by the Product Support Manager that we will alert affected PM customers about an upcoming extreme weather event:

1. Determine which PMs are affected. The following pgAdmin query returns all active units for all active customers by state. [This Loom video](https://www.loom.com/share/938542f5564e478ba58d9e93eba04d6c) provides an overview of how to use it in conjunction with [this worksheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1SbRD_K00to64JdUhnhOlofOEi6u0HLjM4Gaw2SQTtD4/edit#gid=0) to identify which PMs should receive an email. `SELECT p.address1, p.city, p.state, p.pm_company_id AS company_id, c.name FROM property p LEFT JOIN company c ON c.company_id = p.pm_company_id WHERE p.is_active = 1 AND c.is_active = 1` The following query retrieves a list of emails for property manager users whose companies are located in specific states (see states in line 15). You can change the state abbreviations in the query based on where you need to focus your attention, such as specific weather-related events.

```text
SELECT distinct c.name as pm_co_name, u.name as user_name, con.value as email 
from property p
JOIN public.company c
ON p.pm_company_id = c.company_id
JOIN public.subscription s
ON c.company_id = s.relation_id
AND s.relation_type = 'Latchel\Company'
JOIN public.user u
ON c.company_id = u.company_id
JOIN public.user_contact uc
ON u.user_id = uc.user_id
JOIN public.contact con
ON uc.contact_id = con.contact_id
WHERE TRUE
AND UPPER(p.state) in ('TX','OK','KS','NE','IA','IL','OH','PA','LA', 'AR', 'TN','MS','WV','VA','NC','SC','GA', 'IN', 'MO','AR' )
AND p.is_active = 1
AND s.ended_at is null
AND u.is_active = 1
AND u.user_type = 'pm'
AND con.method = 'email'
AND c.name NOT ILIKE '%test%'
```

_Note: Some weather events (ex: a major winter storm) could be almost nationwide. In those cases, it would be appropriate to email all customers. Consult with the Product Support Manager if you’re unsure._

2. Product Support sends the following email template with the list of affected PMsvia hubspot at least 48 hours before the weather event. Be sure to update the fields in \[red\] accordingly.

## Email Template

Dear \[Customer Name\],

Latchel is actively monitoring \[weather event\] which is forecast to impact \[geographic area\] in the days ahead.

**_How does Latchel prepare?_** Our Service Operations team regularly monitors extreme weather events across the US and adjusts staffing to ensure continuity in intaking resident calls, troubleshooting and dispatching emergencies.

**_What should you do in Latchel?_**

- Review your [Severity Settings](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/Pre-Approval-Severity-and-Responsibility-Settings) and [Emergency Temperature Thresholds](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/How-to-Add-Emergency-Temperature-Thresholds) in Latchel and make updates if needed.
- Verify your [Emergency Escalation Contacts in Latchel](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/How-to-Update-Your-Escalation-Contacts), then make sure they’re prepared for a potential increase in calls during the weather event. [Here](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/Emergency-Outbound-Communication-Process) is how Latchel communicates to Property Managers during emergencies.
- Reach out to your Service Providers to understand if they’ll be working extra hours and able to reach property locations to handle additional volume if needed. Verify your [Service Provider prioritization in Latchel](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/How-to-Add-Manage-Service-Providers). Consider whether there are back-up Service Providers you wish to contact in the event your primary Service Providers become overwhelmed or are unable to reach an address.
- Prepare for extreme weather and set resident and owner expectations. We recommend the following resources:
  - US Department of Homeland Security: [https://www.ready.gov/](https://www.ready.gov/)
  - CDC Natural Disasters and Severe Weather: [https://www.cdc.gov/disasters/index.html](https://www.cdc.gov/disasters/index.html)
- During the event, check your [Latchel dashboard](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/Dashboard-2-0) frequently to monitor the number of emergencies Latchel is handling, how quickly your Service Providers are responding and determine whether circumstances require you to reach out to residents or owners with additional communication.

If you need assistance completing any of these steps, don’t hesitate to reach out to your Product Support team at [<u>success@latchel.com</u>](mailto:success@latchel.com).

Take care and stay safe!

The Latchel Team
