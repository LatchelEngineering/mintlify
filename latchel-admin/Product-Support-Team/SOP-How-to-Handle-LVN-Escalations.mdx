<Info>
  [Latchel Vendor Network](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/BICMsQ) describes a group of service provider companies that Latchel has a special arrangement with. In return for being offered real work orders, LVN vendors agree to:

  - use Latchel technology to interact with all work orders
  - get paid exclusively via Latchel’s direct deposit payout technology
  - guarantee all technicians are vetted, licensed, bonded and insured
  - offer free recalls to Latchel customers
</Info>

<Note>
  You can find the full list of [Latchel’s partner LVN companies here](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/BICMsQ).
</Note>

## How to: Remove a Tech

<Info>
  Do not reassign a job to a different LVN company if the original LVN company has already visited the property. See specifics below:
</Info>

### **Scenario 1:** A PM reaches out to remove an LVN Company provider from the job

1. PS will respond with the [Remove LVN from WO SF Template](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/EmailTemplate/00XUS00000CaE8n2AF/view):
2. If the PM responds to the email and**is ok**with this approach,
3. Submit the request via the “**Tech Change Request**” workflow in the\*\*#lvn-escalations\*\*Slack channel.
4. If the **PM is not ok** with this approach and wants a NEW LVN Company provider (not a new tech within the same LVN Company (e.g. PM wants to switch the provider from Puls to Keepe),
5. Confirm whether or not the original LVN company has visited the property and/or if payment has been made to this provider.
6. If this is TRUE (a payment has been made or the provider visited the property), confirm with the PM that they agree to pay for the original invoice. There will likely be a dispatch/service fee owed...
7. If this is NOT TRUE (nobody has visited the property), cancel the original work order and create a new one.
8. \*\*\*\*Request with the PM what SP the WO should be assigned to.\*\*Assign that SP to the new WO and [**lock in the SP**](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/How-to-Enable-Service-Provider-Locked-In-on-a-Work-Order-New-Experience).

<Warning>
  \*\***This is a required step!** We must have an SP assigned to the new WO so we can lock them in on the WO to avoid automation ever taking over and the WO getting reassigned to original LVN.
</Warning>

### \*\*Scenario 2:\*\*If the PM mentions they never want to work with an LVN Company again, continue the steps below:

1. First, send [this Blacklist LVN Company SF Template](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/EmailTemplate/00XUS00000CY6gn2AD/view) to the customer via SF Case. The setting for the LVN is all or nothing → This means we cannot blacklist a specific company in the LVN. The template describes this to the customer.
2. The PM will either choose to turn off ALL of LVN or keep LVN ON.
3. To turn **OFF / opt-out**LVN:
4. Go to the PM Company page
5. Navigate to the Vendor Assignment Automation box in the Account Details tab.
6. Toggle the “Use Vendor Provider Networks” setting OFF.
7. To keep **LVN enabled**, ensure this setting is toggled ON: No further action is required.

## How to: Handle LVN Complaints

- When a complaint is received from a PM regarding an LVN company, it is important to get all the relevant details from them while communicating via the case.
- After gathering all the information from the PM, review the WO in question as it pertains to the PM’s complaints.

<Warning>
  If you are not able to identify the root cause of the problem or answer the customer’s question using the resources available, or if your investigation will take 10\+ minutes, [submit the complaint for a Deep Dive](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/r4KTlw).
</Warning>

- The complaint will either be VALID (there is an issue) or INVALID (nothing went wrong and expectations need to be reset with the customer).
- For BOTH Valid and Invalid complaints, share the conclusion with the customer regarding the LVN complaint and your findings/next steps.

This approach ensures the customer feels heard and valued even if their complaint doesn't lead to the resolution they initially expected.

| **Next Steps:** | **When:** | **Action:** | **Additional Resources:** |
