### **What’s the difference between Product Support and Ops Agents?**

 

**Product Support (PS)** manages issues involving product knowledge, platform behavior, and helping property managers (PMs) update workflows to meet their goals. PS addresses work order escalations, Deep Dive submissions, invoice disputes, feature requests, and service issues, focusing on functional product problems and certain business outcomes. PS also handles initial responses, troubleshooting, and gathering additional details on customer support issues before escalating to ensure the Customer Success Manager (CSM) receives comprehensive information.

**Ops Agents** handle straightforward requests and account updates. Any request beyond a basic Tier 1 issue will be escalated to PS for more specialized assistance.

---

Below is a summary of the roles of Product Support and Ops Agents; and **how you can tell the two apart**:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Product Support">
    - Involves straightforward assistance with technical problem solving and troubleshooting.
    - Answering customer questions about the product.
    - Fulfilling specific customer requests.
    - Looks into isolated interactions such as work orders, reviewing calls and dispatch attempts and configuration setting issues.
    - Subscription questions and system functionality.
    - Account Configurations.
    - Focuses on immediate issues (short-term view).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Ops Agents">
    - Assists customers in updating contact information.
    - Basic settings listed below and account updates.
    - Assist SPs in making updates to their profiles.
    - Escalating to PS more complex product questions and account updates.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

    [https://help.latchel.com/s/topic/0TO5e000000h9wqGAA/How-to-Change-Reset-the-Password-of-a-Latchel-User-s-Login](https://help.latchel.com/s/topic/0TO5e000000h9wqGAA/How-to-Change-Reset-the-Password-of-a-Latchel-User-s-Login)

    Before sending any login information or password reset links, the associate must strictly verify the requester’s identity to protect account security and prevent unauthorized access. Follow these steps:

    1. Check if the Property Manager contacting us has an active user profile associated with the account.
       - If the requester does not have an active profile, do not proceed.
    2. Ask the requester to confirm the email address where they expect to receive the login information or password reset link.
       - Cross-check this email address against the one listed on the user’s profile in the system.
       - If the email addresses do not match, do not send any login information. Advise the requester that, for security reasons, login details can only be sent to the email address on file.

    Only proceed with sending the login information once both points have been confirmed.
  </Accordion>

    Check if the PM has a payment method added in their profile:

Thank you!”_
    - **If the PM agrees** to have LCN/LVN ON for all future WOs, proceed to Step 2.
    - **If the PM does not agree**, then no changes shall be made.
    - Scroll down and look for “Payment Settings”. Under this setting, locate the "Latchel Service Provider Dispatch & Payments".
      - Set the toggle to the **_right (ON)_** to **enable** LCN/LVN
