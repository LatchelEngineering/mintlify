---
title: "SOP: Latchel Department Escalation Process for Product Support Cases"
---

<Info>
  **Objective**: This SOP outlines the process for Product Support (PS) associates to escalate client questions or concerns to the appropriate Latchel department, ensuring efficient handling, tracking, and communication during escalations.
</Info>

## **1. Purpose of Escalation**

Client inquiries may require escalation to other Latchel departments for additional clarity or next steps. Before escalation, PS must gather all relevant information to ensure a smooth handoff and quick resolution.

## **2. Pre-Escalation Preparation**

Before escalating any issue, PS associates must thoroughly vet the client’s concern by:

1. **Clarifying Details**:

- Ask the client for all necessary information regarding their inquiry or issue.
- Use the [**Clarify Escalation with PM Template**](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/EmailTemplate/00XUS00000BOiuP2AT/view?queryScope=userFolders) to gather detailed responses from the Property Manager (PM).

2. **Review Case Information**:

- Ensure that all relevant details, including the client's concern and background, are documented in the case.

## **3. Escalating to a Latchel Department**

Once all details have been clarified, escalate the case to the appropriate department using Slack. Follow these steps:

1. **Select the Correct Slack Channel**:

- Refer to the [**Job Aid: Slack Channels & Escalation Workflows**](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/H4BAqw) to ensure you are using the correct channel for escalation.

2. **Provide Key Details**:

- In your Slack message, include the following:
  - A clear summary of the issue.
  - All relevant details gathered from the client.
  - A link to the case URL for easy reference.

3. **Post Slack Link to the Case**:

- After submitting the escalation in Slack, copy the Slack message link:
  - Click on the three dots in the upper-right corner of the message.
  - Select **Copy Link**.
- Add this link as a **Post** in the case for tracking. <img src="attachments/3198353472/3209396254.png?width=760" width="760" />

## **4. Communicating the Escalation to the Client**

After escalating the case internally, inform the client about the escalation:

1. Use the [**Department Escalation - Initial Escalation SF Template**](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/EmailTemplate/00XUS00000CduHx2AJ/view?queryScope=userFolders) to notify the PM.
2. Manually update the case status to **“In Progress”**.

## **5. Handling Responses from Other Latchel Departments**

#### **Scenario 1: The Escalation Contact is Unresponsive**

1. **Monitor the Case Re-Opening**:
2. If the case automatically re-opens or the PM responds before you have received an update from the Latchel department, follow up on this escalation in the original slack thread.
3. **Push for Timely Updates**:

- Tag the relevant department or @mention the responsible parties (e.g., @maddie) in Slack to request an update.

3. **Document Follow-Ups**:

- Every follow-up in Slack should be tracked in the case:
  - Copy the **new Slack message URL** and **Post** it to the case.
- Use the **[Department Escalation - Touch Base Templat](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/sObject/00XUS00000CI7yb2AD/view?queryScope=userFolders)e** to send a follow-up to the PM, keeping them informed.

4. **Tracking**:

- Ensure all communications are documented in the Salesforce thread.
- Track progress through the [PS Dashboard’s Activity Report.](https://sites.google.com/latchel.com/product-support-reports/04-last-activity-by-agent)

5. **Case Status**:

- Keep the case status as **“In Progress”** until a resolution is achieved.

#### **Scenario 2: The Latchel Department is Responsive**

1. **Share the Response**:

- Once the department responds, share the details, next steps, or resolution with the PM.
- Ensure all communication is documented in the case and the Salesforce thread.

2. **Close the Loop**:

- After resolving the issue, follow up with the PM to confirm closure.
- If resolved, mark the case as **Closed**.

#### **Scenario 3: The Escalation Contact is initially Unresponsive and Follow-Ups after the Case is Closed.**

There are instances where [CS has attempted to reach out to a client due an escalation](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/VQDamw) but received no response and then the Escalation Contact follows up later.

- If the CS Reach Out is from **30 days or less**, PS can loop back in the team member who initially contacted the client by:
  - Adding an internal note to the Case Thread with a link to the original case where the client was unresponsive.
  - Send [the Escalate to CSM SF Template](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/EmailTemplate/00XUS00000B8TUj2AN/view?queryScope=userFolders) to the client.
  - Ensure the new case is assigned to the CS team member who initially contacted the client.
  - Move the Case into an Escalated state.
- If the CS Reach Out is from **over 30 days past**, PS should treat the case as a new inquiry and follow the [normal escalation process](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/VoDpnQ) if needed.

## **6. Special Escalated Cases**

Certain types of escalations, such as **Data Team Bulk Account Updates**, **Feature Requests**, or **System Bugs**, may not have defined resolution timelines. In these cases:

- Use SF templates that do not commit to specific updates or timelines.
- Do not leave the case open while awaiting resolution.

---

### **Key Reminders**:

- Always provide the correct case details when escalating.
- Ensure that communication with the client is clear and consistent throughout the process.
- Regularly update the case with relevant Slack threads and communication logs.
