title: "SOP: Handling Work Order Complaints from Property Managers"
---

<Info>
  This document outlines the steps the Product Support (PS) team should take when addressing a work order complaint received from a property manager.
</Info>

## Step-by-step Guide:

### Step 1: Gather Details

- **Clarify:** Promptly ask clarifying questions to obtain comprehensive details about the complaint.
- **Request Documentation:** Ask for any supporting files, such as recordings, screenshots, or pictures, to aid in the investigation.

### Step 2: **Review the Situation**

- **Examine Details:** Analyze the specifics of the work order issue, focusing on areas such as delays, miscommunications, automations, and settings.

### Step 3: Conduct [Investigation](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/3201728543/SOP+Submitting+a+Deep+Dive+Request#1.-When-to-Request-a-Deep-Dive)

#### **Scenario 1: Investigation Exceeds 10 Minutes**

- Submit the complaint and associated details for a[Deep Dive Request](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/3201728543/SOP+Submitting+a+Deep+Dive+Request#1.-When-to-Request-a-Deep-Dive).

#### **Scenario 2: Investigation Under 10 Minutes**

- Document all discovery details, findings, and resolution. Prepare to share these with the property manager.

### Step 4: Communicate Resolution

<Note>
  **Method of Sharing Findings:** Whether conclusions arise from a deep dive or a personal investigation, ensure the information and next steps are conveyed clearly to the property manager.
</Note>

#### <u>Scenario 1: Resolution from a Deep Dive (DD)</u>

1. The Deep Dive Conclusion will be posted in the corresponding case feed in Salesforce.
2. The DD Submitter and Case Owner will be tagged in the case to alert them; the case will be updated to 'Escalated'.
3. Draft the conclusion in #ps-team-chat using the DD Conclusion Workshop for review. Aim for clear, professional communication.
4. Once approved by the PS Manager, communicate the conclusion to the property manager and confirm closure.
5. _Note:_ An escalation to the CSM may be necessary, depending on the situation **[<u>See guidelines here</u>](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/VQDamw).**

#### <u>Scenario 2: Resolution from Self Investigation</u>

1. **Acknowledge Concerns:** Begin with empathy by recognizing the client's concern and their desired outcome.

- _Example: "I understand that you're seeking to achieve \[desired outcome\], and I'm here to assist in resolving this issue effectively."_

2. **Root Cause Analysis:**
3. If the root cause is **system behavior:**
4. Outline the desired outcome, explain the system's behavior, and its impact.
5. _Example: "You were expecting \[desired outcome\]. This occurred because \[explanation of system behavior\]."_
6. If the root cause is **human error:**
7. Detail the desired outcome, identify the human error, and describe corrective actions taken.
8. _Example: "You expected \[desired outcome\]. This was due to \[explanation of human error\]. We've implemented \[actions taken\] to prevent recurrence."_
9. **Provide Solutions and Recommendations.**

- Offer tools or settings adjustments to align more closely with client preferences.
  - _Example: "Here are some tools you can use to achieve your desired outcome: \[list tools and instructions\]."_
- If necessary, address confusing [notes](2818473985.html)on the account and provide guidance for updating settings.
  - _Example: "We've identified areas for improvement: \[list items\]. Here are the steps to enhance these settings."_

4. **Encourage Engagement:**
5. Invite further questions or requests for support to help with any additional adjustments.
