<Info>
  **Objective**: This SOP outlines the process for managing new tickets, including prioritization, addressing spam, non-actionable inquiries, and handling tickets escalated by Latchel Ops.

  **Preparation**: Before starting, it’s recommended to review the [Ticket Automations](3198648338.html#New-Cases%3A) documentation to understand how different case statuses affect the Product Support (PS) queue.
</Info>

## **Ticket Overview**

- A ticket represents a unit of work for Product Support associates. A **ticket**is created whenever an email is sent to [**success@latchel.com**](mailto:success@latchel.com) by a customer or an internal team member escalating an inquiry via the Slack channel [**#ps-escalation**](https://join.slack.com/share/enQtMTAzMDcxODM5NzMxMjQtODFjNWVkNGFlZTYxYzQyYzQwNDg5MTc2ZDJhZTU0Y2RkZDA4NTAwODBhNjBmZDgzMTA1ZjQ0MGNmNTIyOGE5NQ). The PS team must manually grab the ticket by assigning it to themselves before beginning work.
- When a ticket is created, the sender will receive an automatic response confirming the receipt of their message.
- New tickets are added to the [**Product Support Teamspace**](https://www.dashboard.askassembly.app/teams/0193894a-1308-78ee-8fdd-bd53cd8b90fb/issues) in Assembly and tagged as **"Needs Response."** This queue is your primary working queue.

## **Prioritizing Assembly Tickets**

Upon starting your shift, PS associates must prioritize tickets in the following order:

#### **(1) Breaching (Highest Priority)**

- Any tickets that have already breached our 8 Business-Hour SLA fall into this bucket and must be handled **immediately**. <img src="attachments/3198681116/3823468548.png?width=760" />

#### **(2) Needs Response**

This is the **primary working queue**during your shift.

1. Start with **brand new Needs Response**that have not received a human response. These must be responded to in order to meet the **8 business-hour First Response SLA**. - These are identifiable by: A **time stamp (not a date) b** often being **unassigned.** - Tickets approaching the 8-hour SLA will display: the elapsed time and how close the ticket is to breaching.

1. **Work Oldest to Newest**: Handle these tickets based on the **Date/Time Opened**, starting with the oldest. - The queue is **automatically sorted** from top → bottom (oldest → newest). - Determine whether: A response or follow-up is required, or the ticket can be closed.

<Tip>
  \*\*Continuously monitor the queue!\*\*New tickets may appear at any time. Toggle back to newly created tickets as they come in to avoid SLA risk.
</Tip>

#### **(3) Escalated**

When a ticket remains in **Escalated** status for more than **3 days**, it will:

- Automatically move back to **Needs Response**and include an **Internal Note Reminder** to review: Slack threads, ASM links, any other relevant follow-up resources. _(If no check-in or follow-up is needed, move the ticket back to Escalated.)_

#### **(4) Open**

- Prioritize by **Last Reply Date/Time (oldest → newest)**; this queue is **automatically sorted** with newest at the top and oldest at the bottom. <img src="attachments/3198681116/3822944263.png?width=462" />

## **Ticket Processing**

When you open a ticket, follow the steps below:

#### **Step 1. Assign yourself as the ticket owner, then start the timer.**

![image-20251217-003550.png](attachments/3198681116/3777953797.png?width=760)

#### **Step 2. Add the appropriate tag for the ticket and update the company name under the customer profile.**

- Under Customer Profile, search the name of the company associated with the email.
- If the ticket is created through an Ops Escalation, Assembly will automatically apply the **“Not Actionable”** label. Ensure the ticket is also tagged as **“Ops Escalation.”** When selecting tags, refer to the [Job Aid: Interaction Tags Explained](2531721244.html) to confirm the appropriate classification. <img src="attachments/3198681116/3780673545.png?width=760" alt="image-20251217-182014.png" /><img src="attachments/3198681116/3780214791.png?width=760" alt="image-20251217-181916.png" />

#### **Step 2. Spam Identification**

- **Definition**: Spam includes unsolicited messages such as junk mail, advertisements, or emails sent to harass or collect unauthorized responses.

If the case is identified as spam, follow these steps:

- **Action**: Tag the ticket as “Not Actionable” and change the status to “Closed-No Workflow”.

#### **Step 3. Non-Actionable Cases**

- **Definition**: Non-actionable emails include automated responses (e.g., out-of-office replies), system-generated notifications, or invalid escalations where no further action is required.

If the case is **Not Actionable**:

- Tag the ticket as “Not Actionable” and change the status to “Closed-No Workflow”. <img src="attachments/3198681116/3780542478.png?width=760" alt="image-20251217-182427.png" />

#### **Step 4. Actionable Inquiries**

If the ticket is actionable:

- The assigned associate should review and take appropriate action to resolve the issue.

## **Responding to Customer-Initiated Tickets**

When the ticket is directly from the customer, it will have the following characteristics:

1. **From Email**: The ticket will originate from a [**non-@latchel.com**](mailto:non-@latchel.com) email address.
2. **Content**: The email will be a standard, typed-out message without the **Ops Escalation Form**.

In these tickets, the associate should respond to the customer, providing assistance as needed.

- For tickets that are **Ops Escalations**, refer to **[SOP: Handling Ops Escalations](3198779395.html).**

## **Escalating to Product Support Ticket Review**

When working on tickets, there may come moments when first reviewing or throughout the ticket lifecycle where a PS Associate may need assistance on next steps or has questions on the best path forward.

#### Step 1: Review the ticket and ask clarifying questions.

- PS should thoroughly vet the escalation, ask clarifying questions and gather all details to ensure they have full context.
- Think about the questions that the PSM may ask you when submitting to [#ps-asm-escalate](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C05L70GA77D) channel.

#### Step 2: Send [PS Case Review template](https://www.dashboard.askassembly.app/preferences/templates) in Assembly to the client.

- Prior to submitting the ticket for review, always be sure to send a response to the client to ensure we are sending first responses to meet our SLA and keeping clients in the loop!

#### Step 3: Fill out the**ASM Ticket Escalation** workflow in the [#ps-asm-escalate](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C05L70GA77D) **Slack channel**.
