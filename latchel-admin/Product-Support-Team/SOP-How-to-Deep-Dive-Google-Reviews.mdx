### **SDDI for Low-Star Rating (3 Stars or Below)**

Low-star reviews provide critical insight into where service or communication may have fallen short. Each of these reviews should be treated as a structured **mini investigation** to uncover root causes, identify opportunities for service recovery, and prevent repeat issues.

#### **Weekly Review:**

- Review all **new Google Reviews from the last 7 days** that received **3★ or below** and are still pending review.
- Log each case in the Google Review Tracking Sheet and initiate an SDDI if the feedback indicates an unresolved issue or process gap.
- These reviews should be reviewed consistently as part of the **weekly cadence**.

#### **Process:**

- **Create the SDDI Ticket**
  - Log the issue under the **PS Deep Dive Teamspace** in Assembly.
  - Set **DD Source** to _“Google Review.”_
  - Link the SDDI ticket to the review entry in the tracking sheet.
- **Analyze the Work Order (WO)**
  - Determine whether a WO is associated with the review.
  - If yes, confirm whether the WO is **Open and Actionable** or **Closed**.
  - Document the current WO stage and any relevant details impacting the customer experience.
- **Escalate When Needed**
  - If the WO is still open and corrective action can be taken, escalate immediately in **#ops\_associate\_escalations**.
  - Include a clear, concise summary of the issue and what support is needed.
- **Identify the Responsible Party**
  - Clarify whether next steps are owned by **Latchel**, the **Property Manager**, or another party.
  - This ensures accountability and prevents issues from stalling.
- **Develop the Action Plan**
  - Outline **specific steps** that can mitigate the current issue or improve the resident/PM experience.
  - Note any process gaps, missed steps, or service opportunities that contributed to the low rating.
  - Include recommendations for what could have prevented the issue to support long-term improvements.

---

### **4. Communicating Findings**

Once the SDDI is complete, findings must be clearly communicated to the **CS/Growth team** to ensure accurate, timely, and empathetic public responses to the review.

- Once the SDDI is complete, convey the findings to **Maddie & Ethan** for inclusion in Latchel’s **public response on Google**.
- Summarize the key points — focus on the root cause, any corrective action taken, and any context the marketing team should know.
- \*\*Update Tracking Sheet -\*\*Mark the review as **“Submitted to Maddie”** in the tracking sheet to close the loop and maintain visibility across teams.

### **5. Additional Procedures**

When a review reveals broader issues, escalate through the appropriate channels to ensure proper follow-up.

- **Child Issue Reporting Tickets**: If the review surfaces a systemic issue, recurring bug, or process gap, follow the standard procedure for Child Issue Reporting Tickets as applicable.
- **PM Feedback**:
  - For feedback specific to Property Managers, send the relevant information via **Assembly**.
- **Associate Feedback**:
  - If feedback pertains to internal team members, fill out and submit the details to the **#svc-ops-feedback**Slack Channel.

---

### **Key Points to Remember**

- Prioritize **low-star reviews (3★ or below)** and take prompt, actionable steps to address concerns.
- **Escalate open work orders** when further action can improve the customer experience and future ratings.
- **Communicate findings clearly** with the Marketing team to support accurate, timely public responses.
- Keep all reviews and actions **logged and traceable** to ensure visibility, accountability, and trend tracking.
