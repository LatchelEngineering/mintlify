title: "How to: Submit a Deep Dive Request  "
---

<Info>
  **Purpose**: A Deep Dive is a **detailed investigation conducted by the Product Support Team** to resolve complex or unresolved issues raised by property managers (PMs), residents, owners, or service providers. This SOP outlines **when and how to submit** a Deep Dive request to ensure issues are escalated efficiently and accurately.
</Info>

## **1. When to Request a Deep Dive**

Submit a Deep Dive request **only after**:

- You’ve **fully investigated the issue** using available resources (e.g., work order history, PM settings, call recordings, message threads, etc.).
- You’ve spent approximately **10 minutes attempting to resolve** the issue and were unable to find a solution.

<Note>
  If the issue can be resolved within that timeframe, do **not** submit a Deep Dive. These are reserved for **complex issues requiring deeper analysis**.
</Note>

## **2. Common Reasons for a Deep Dive Request**

- A customer is **unhappy with how a work order was handled**.
- A PM, resident, or vendor reports **service delays or availability issues**.
- A PM requests **clarification on Latchel’s actions**.
- An agent acted **outside of Latchel’s policies**, and the cause is unclear (even without a complaint).
- A resident submitted a **low work order review score**.

## **3. Common Deep Dive Requesters**

| Role | Typical Scenario |
| --- | --- |
| Product Support Associates (PS) | Receiving complaints through Assembly tickets. |
| Customer Success Managers (CSMs) | Escalations during PM calls, meetings, or Assembly ticket. |
| Finance / Accounts Payable | Service provider complaints about payments or invoices. PMs disputing an invoice. |
| Service Operations Support / Coach | Unusual system behavior or unclear agent actions. |

## **4. Preparing for a Deep Dive Request**

Before submitting a Deep Dive request, make sure you have:

- A **clear and specific description** of the problem and any key questions. (If vague, ask the customer for clarification.)
- **Relevant details** from the complainant — dates, times, names, contact info, and any context tied to Latchel interactions.
- Your **initial investigation notes**, including findings and reviewed resources.

---

## **Step-by-Step Guide to Submit a Deep Dive Request:**

### **Initiate the Request in Slack**:

1. Navigate to the `#ps-dd-sddi` Slack channel.
2. Select the \*\*“DD Request”\*\*workflow.

<img
  src="attachments/3201728543/3644162092.png?width=630"
  alt="image-20250929-214633.png"
  width="630"
/>

### **Complete the Deep Dive Workflow**:

Fill out each field carefully :

- **Company Name**: PM or SP name as listed in Latchel
- **Who is the CSM?**: Slack User of the assigned CSM for the Company.
- **ASM Link**: Paste the Assembly link for the complaint. If not applicable, write "N/A".
- **Type of DD?:**
  - Standard Dive
  - Invoice Disputes
- **Work Order Link**: Provide the link or "N/A".
- **Describe the Issue**: Brief summary of the complaint (e.g., "PM Disputing Charges for Invoice #1234").
- **If Invoice Dispute, what \$ amount is being disputed?**: If applicable, include the total amount disputed. Attach the invoice if possible.
- **What initial information/review can you provide?**: List the tools and resources used during your investigation and any findings.
- **F_or Invoice Dispute Deep Dives, you must also include:_**
  - _What was the date and time the PM reported the dispute?_
  - _What was the date and time the invoice was submitted?_
    - _This information is crucial for resolving disputes in line with[Latchel’s Guarantee and Refund Request Policy.](https://help.latchel.com/s/topic/0TO5e000000h9wqGAA/Latchel-Guarantee-and-Refund-Request-Policy#latchel-guarantee-and-refund-request-policy)_
- **What questions do you need the specialists to answer?**: Clearly state the questions that need to be addressed by the Product Support Specialist.

### **Submit the Request**:

1. Once the form is completed, click **Submit**.
2. The request will be posted in the Slack channel and **automatically generate a ticket** in the PS Deep Dive Tickets Teamspace in Assembly.

  src="attachments/3201728543/3634200580.png?width=760"

## **After Submission**

Once the Deep Dive request is submitted, the assigned Product Support Specialist will review and thoroughly investigate the issue. You will receive updates and findings either through Assembly or in the same Slack thread where the request was originally posted.

**It’s important to remain engaged throughout the process by responding to any follow-up questions and monitoring the progress to ensure the matter is resolved in a timely manner.** Deep Dive requests are most effective when requesters provide clear context and stay actively involved in the resolution process.
