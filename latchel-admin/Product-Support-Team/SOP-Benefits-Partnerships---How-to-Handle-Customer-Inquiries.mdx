## **Goals**

1. Understand which teams should answer which types of benefits and partnerships inquiries
2. Provide clear, accurate and customer-obsessed responses to our customers' benefits and partnerships questions

## **How do customer inquiries become Assembly Tickets?**

1. An email is received at [<u>sucess@latchel.com</u>](mailto:sucess@latchel.com)
   1. This includes tickets escalated from Service Ops [How to: Handle Home Assistant and Benefits Inquiries](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/OPS/pages/2571730945)
   2. Case Owners are assigned by Product Support following the table below.
2. A CSM receives a question during a phone call or meeting
   1. After the meeting, the CSM creates an Assembly tickets and assigns a Case Owner based on the table below. The Case should include a summary of the customer’s question and a link to the call or meeting.

<Info>
  When closing out a benefits/partnerships Case in Salesforce, be sure to select **Case Type = Resident Benefits** and the most pertinent **Case Motive** (descriptions [<u>here</u>](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/2531721244/Job+Aid+Interaction+Tags+Explained#Resident-Benefits "https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/2531721244/Job+Aid+Interaction+Tags+Explained#Resident-Benefits")). This is super important for tracking and improving this program!
</Info>

         2. FAQs
            - How are utilities transferred to a new resident from the owner?
              - Utility Valet does not actively transfer utility accounts, as only the account holder can initiate a transfer. However, when they assist in setting up new accounts for residents, the utility provider typically cancels the existing account once proof of the new account holder’s occupancy, such as a copy of the lease, is provided.
            - Why does Utility Valet ask residents for social security numbers?
              - They are required for the following reasons:
                - Utility providers need socials to activate and initiate accounts.
                - Most internet providers need socials to do a soft credit check to initiate services.
                - Vivint ([Vivint Home Security & Smart Home Systems](https://www.vivint.com/)), a smart home security offering by Utility Valet, needs socials to do a soft credit check to initiate services.
                - Information is **deleted from the CRM 3 days after move-in** (**_That said, all residents' information is protected)_**
                - Phone recordings are **paused during collection of personal information** such as **credit card and SSN gathering**

  <Accordion title="How are residents reimbursed?">
    When a resident submits a Typeform request, they see the following message on the final screen: _“We will review your submission and contact you with any questions. You can expect a follow-up within 5-7 business days. If your reimbursement request is approved, you will receive an email from Ramp to provide your bank details.”_<br /><br />Once a reimbursement request is approved by Latchel, the resident will receive an email from Ramp asking them to provide their bank details. Our default reimbursement method is via ACH. However, a paper check can also be issued upon resident request.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="PM request for benefits usage data">
    1. Reframe the conversation to focus on the total value of the benefits offering, rather than the specific number of claims submitted and reimbursed. Template (edit #s accordingly):<br />_”We recommend highlighting the **total potential value of the benefit package**, which exceeds **\$2,000 per home**. For your **523 active homes**, this represents over **\$1,000,000 in resident protections and service-level guarantees**—a strong selling point for prospective tenants.”_\\
    2. If the PM still wants to know specific usage stats, tag Sammy in #partnerships-perks so she can provide this info. When responding to the PM, provide helpful explanations for low submission rates and suggestions on how to educate residents. Template (edit #s accordingly):<br />_”5 reimbursement requests were submitted by your residents in the past year, and all 5 were approved and reimbursed. Low submission rates are often due to either **excellent tenant screening** and a low rate of resident chargebacks or **a lack of resident awareness** about the benefits package when chargebacks occur. A best practice we recommend is **re-educating residents** about their reimbursement benefits at the time of a chargeback. This can help soften the impact and ensure residents feel supported, even when chargebacks are necessary.”_

    Example case: [Assembly](https://www.dashboard.askassembly.app/issue/ASM-22496)

  <Accordion title="PM is asking if a Latchel work order is required for a resident to be eligible for an Overnight Stay Reimbursement.">
    It is only required if the Property Management Company is using our maintenance subscription. If the PM is on a Benefits Only plan, a work order does not need to be created in Latchel. A screenshot of the work order in their Property Management System (PMS) is sufficient, as we just need visual confirmation that an actual overnight emergency occurred.
