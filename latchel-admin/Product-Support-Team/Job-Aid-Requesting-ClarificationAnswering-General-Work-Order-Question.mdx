<Info>
  **Purpose of this document**

  This document serves as a comprehensive guide for handling property managers' requests for more information on their accounts. It includes clarifying questions, best practices, and response templates that product support (PS) can use to communicate desired workflows and potential caveats with property managers (PMs).
</Info>

## **Important Note**

<Warning>
  These templates are just a **starting point**. Always personalize them based on the situation and the conversation you’ve had with the PM.
</Warning>

## **Process Overview**

- **Lead with Questions**: Always ask clarifying questions and present **options**, not just solutions.
- **Customer Effort**: Minimize the effort for the customer. Your goal is to paint a clear picture for them and make their next steps as easy as possible.
- **Be the Expert**: Don’t just take the PM's word for it. You are the Latchel expert—use your knowledge to share best practices and highlight any concerns or caveats that may arise.

Before responding, thoroughly review:

- The **PM’s case**
- The **PM account** (active product package, portfolio setup, [pre-dispatch and critical notes](https://latchel.atlassian.net/l/cp/D152Tmw0), severity settings, owner setup, etc.)

  Additional Resource: [https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/XoDIm](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/XoDIm)

    **Process**: The lack of requirement for pictures is intentional. There are accessibility laws/regulations (Fair Housing Act and more local stuff) that make us uneasy about requiring photos for a maintenance request (for example, for someone who has a vision disability). Its kind of a gray area but that is the reason.

    **Proposed Explanation:** Let the PM know the BRAND for appliances is REQUIRED; However, the model and serial number/pics are optional. There is typically not even a way to guarantee that a serial number is present. They are usually on a sticker that is often removed during installation or is worn and faded. So mandatory isnt even really an option. Same with model - It's usually not necessarily present on the appliance itself. It's on the manual/email receipt, things like that, that usually get thrown away.

  <Accordion title="Differences Between Escalation Contacts and Pre-Dispatch Instructions">
    Our standard process is:

    - Latchel will call [escalation contacts](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/How-to-Update-Your-Escalation-Contacts) 1x (with a voicemail).
    - Latchel will only call the designated escalation contacts.
    - **Latchel will only call these escalation contacts for situations considered[Property Damaging or Extreme Habitability](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/Emergency-Escalations-Property-Damaging-and-Extreme-Habitability-Situations)at all hours of the day.**
      - Meaning, if the situation does not fall under our property damaging/extreme habitability situations, **no call will be made to the escalation contacts**. The work order will Forward to PM and notify the assigned [User Group](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/How-to-Create-User-Groups) of that property via email for next steps.

    **Pre-Dispatch Instructions** are essentially instructions to be carried for **emergency WOs only**that are outside of our normal standard process listed above ^. You can find approved pre-dispatch instructions here: [Pre-Dispatch Instruction Guide](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/How-to-Send-Latchel-Pre-Dispatch-Instructions).

    Now as far as settings flowing from company, portfolio to property level, please see this: [Configuration Inheritance (Property, Portfolio, & Company Level)](https://help.latchel.com/s/article/Configuration-Inheritance-Property-Portfolio-Company-Level)

    - When a WO is created, the Latchel system first looks at the property level settings.
    - If nothing specific is configured at the property level setting, the system will move to the portfolio level settings.
    - If nothing specific is configured at the portfolio level setting, the system will move to the company level settings.
    - Essentially meaning, the property is inheriting the settings that have been default set at the company level.

    Another way to think about it is,

    - When a WO is created, the Latchel system first looks at the property level settings.
    - If there is a specific setting configured at the property level, this essentially **overrides** the next levels of settings. The WO would follow what is placed at the property level.

    The same logic applies to portfolio settings!
  </Accordion>
