# **Product Support SDDIs**

The SDDI Assembly tickets are created in response to negative feedback received through the following channels: **Work Order (WO) Resident Reviews, CSAT (Customer Satisfaction) Surveys,andGoogle Reviews.**

## **Feedback Sources & Triggers**

### 1. WO Resident Reviews

- **Source:** Automatically sent via SMS after WO completion (\*filling out the review is completely optional to the resident).
- **Action:** Residents may provide 1–5 star ratings and optional written feedback.
- **Internal Workflow:** Feedback generates a task assigned to `PSS Investigations` for daily monitoring by the Product Support team.

**When to Dive:**

- Resident reports a new issue or states the issue remains unresolved (recall scenario).
- Rating is **3 stars or below** with an actionable concern.

### 2. CSAT Surveys

- **Source:** CSAT surveys are sent via text message after an interaction with a Latchel representative.
- **Trigger:** Ratings of 3 stars or below automatically flag the feedback for review.
- **Process:**
  - Review all CSAT feedback via: [https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1PG\_UqlMVMyB7XgQdnoHYWJfGY2E4LFVnMyjupsXww0U/edit?gid=0#gid=0](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1PG_UqlMVMyB7XgQdnoHYWJfGY2E4LFVnMyjupsXww0U/edit?gid=0#gid=0)
  - For each low-rating entry, conduct a **Mini SDDI Review** to determine if a ticket should be created and escalated.
  - Complete the Required Columns in the Tracker:
    - \*\*L:\*\*SDDI NEEDS TO BE CREATED?
    - **M:** If yes, paste the ASM LINK once created.
    - **N:** LVN or Standard Platform?
    - **O:** Special Notes
    - **S:** NOMAD Owner?

<Note>
  **PM Feedback Escalation**

  If the **feedback comes from a PM** and indicates dissatisfaction:

  - Email the PM through Assembly to acknowledge receipt of their feedback.
  - Request supporting details such as WO numbers, dates, or context to better understand the issue.
  - Submit a **Deep Dive Request** to investigate further.
</Note>

### 3. Google Reviews

- **Source:** Public Google reviews on Latchel’s profile.
- **Trigger:** Reviews rated **3 stars or below**, or comments with unresolved issues.
- **Objective:** Address issues, identify root causes, and support the Marketing team with a proper public response.
- **Reference**: [https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/XABIpw](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/XABIpw)

## **Urgent Save Experience**

When reviewing **all feedback sources** tickets, there may be instances where the feedback requires **immediate follow-up or escalation** to prevent further resident dissatisfaction. The two most common scenarios are:

- **New Issue Reported:** The resident mentions a completely different issue that was not part of the original WO.
- **Unresolved Issue ([Recall](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/x/AYCNnQ)):** The resident indicates that the problem still exists or wasn’t fully addressed.

### **1. For Non-Emergency Work Orders**

Create a **Resident Follow-Up** task to ensure prompt action the next business day:

1. **Task Creation**

- Subject: `Resident Follow-Up`
- Schedule: **Next business day at 8:00 AM**.
- Assignment: `Resident Follow-Up User Group`(Latchel) or PM User Group (if under Autopilot subscription.)

2. **Task Settings**

- Priority:
  - **High** for urgent issues
  - **Medium** for standard/non-urgent issues
- Auto-cancel on any **state change** (e.g., WO updated or closed)

3. **Task Description**

- Paste the **exact resident feedback**.
- Clearly outline the **next steps or instructions** for the assigned team.

### **2. Emergency Work Orders**

If the feedback involves an **emergency** or the resident requests immediate support:

- Escalate the issue **right away** in the `#ops_assistance_hub` Slack channel.
- Include:
  - Work Order number
  - Resident feedback
  - Summary of the situation and urgency level
  - Any previous actions taken

## **SDDI Process:**

An **SDDI (Structured Deep Dive Investigation)** is a **mini deep dive** — a focused, fast-turnaround investigation designed to:

- Identify the **root cause** of low-rated feedback (3★ or below)
- Determine whether immediate action or escalation is needed
- Surface **product gaps** or **process improvement opportunities**

### 1. Document Findings

All findings should be recorded directly on the **SDDI ticket** in Assembly. Your notes should be:

- **Concise** but detailed enough to explain what happened
- **Structured** with a clear summary of:
  - The feedback received
  - Relevant WO details (e.g., WO number, dates, service provider, issue category)
  - Any supporting context or observations uncovered during review (e.g., delays, troubleshooting issues, communication gaps)

### 2. Assembly Ticket Creation

- Follow the standard steps for [**submitting an SDDI ticket**.](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/3214147586/Job+Aid+SDDI+Tickets#Steps-to-Create-an-Assembly-Ticket-from-Resident-Reviews%2C-Google-Reviews%2C-and-CSAT-Feedback-\(Product-Support-Specialists-Only\)%3A)
- Select the **correct review type** (e.g., Resident Review, CSAT, Google Review).
- Add clear, organized notes that can be easily referenced by other teams.

### 3. Determine WO Status

- **Open WO:** Work order is still active or in progress.
- **Closed WO:** Work order is completed, cancelled, or inactive.
- Document the **current stage** of the WO (e.g., Awaiting SP, Scheduled, In Progress, Completed, Cancelled).

| **Scenario 1: Escalation for Open WOs** | **Scenario 2: Closed WOs** |
| --- | --- |
| If the WO is **open** and further action can address the complaint: - Escalate in the Slack channel ( `#ops_assistance_hub`) or assign the task to the responsible team. - Include:   - WO number and summary of the issue   - Resident or PM feedback   - Recommended next steps or required action - Clearly identify whether the **next steps fall under Latchel’s responsibility** or the **Property Manager’s responsibility**. - Ensure the issue is moved forward promptly. | If the WO is **closed**: - Review the WO timeline carefully, focusing on **key milestones** and **handoff points**. - Identify the **root cause** or any gaps that contributed to the low rating (e.g., delays, vendor errors, communication failures). - Document a **clear, high-level overview** of:   - What happened   - Where the process failed   - What could have been improved |

### **4. Communicate Findings**

Once the deep dive is complete:

- Share your findings with **Maddie** (PSM) for leadership visibility through `#pm-complaint-to-serv-ops-leadership`.

Ensure the findings are:

- **Clear**: Structured summary with key events and context
- **Actionable**: Ownership, follow-up steps, and recommendations
- For **Google Reviews**, coordinate with the **CS/Growth Team** for appropriate public responses.

<Tip>
  ### Key Points to Remember

  - Prioritize **3-star and below** feedback for review and action.
  - Escalate **open WOs** quickly where action can still improve the outcome.
  - Document **clear, actionable findings** — not just a summary.
  - Share insights with the **Marketing Team** and leadership to support external communications and internal process improvements.
  - Surface **recurring gaps** to inform product or operational enhancements.
</Tip>

## **When an SDDI Is Required**

The team reviews both **rating** and **feedback content** to determine whether an SDDI is necessary. An investigation is required if the feedback meets any of the following:

| **Type of Review** | **Actual Sample Comment** | **Coordination Score** | **Vendor Score** | **Why It Requires a Dive** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Low rating with complaint** | _“There was a problem reaching Latchel via the preferred method but I was able to contact someone to assist with the washing machine. Mike from All American Appliance was great in solving the problem with the washing machine.”_ | 3 | 5 | There’s an identified **communication gap** despite vendor resolution. Review response times and escalation handling. |
| **High rating but negative comment** | _“My request took over two weeks to be resolved. Latchel is unhelpful.”_ | 4 | 4 | Even though the rating is above 3, the **feedback indicates dissatisfaction**. Investigate delays or process failures. |
| **Low rating with positive comment** | _“The vendor came quickly, got the job done immediately! But the agent I spoke with was rude!”_ | 3 | 3 | The vendor performed well but the **Latchel interaction caused dissatisfaction**. Review agent behavior, scripting, or communication handling. |
| **Low rating with no comment** | _“None”_ | 2 | 4 | Low rating but no context provided. A **timeline review** is needed to identify delays, communication issues, or escalation failures. |
| **Unresolved issue noted** | _“When will they come up and close up the hole in the ceiling from plumbing work?”_ | 4 | 5 | Indicates an **incomplete or temporary resolution**. Review whether a recall or follow-up is needed. |
| **Unresolved issue with additional complaints** | _“I would need water to be delivered if the issue happens again. I waited two weeks for Roto-Rooter to say he could not fix the well pump completely.”_ | 3 | 3 | Highlights **resolution gaps and vendor performance issues**. May require escalation or PM follow-up. |
| **Escalation-worthy operational failures** | _“I called multiple times but no one followed up for days.”_ | 2 | 2 | Feedback shows a **clear operational breakdown**. Requires review of call logs, timelines, and escalation compliance. |
| **Vague but negative low rating** | _“Latchel could have done better.”_ | 3 | 3 | Though unclear, a **quick review** of the WO timeline is needed to rule out gaps. |
| **Platform / automation concerns tied to resident frustration** | _“The system is too automated, I couldn’t talk to anyone.”_ | 3 | 4 | Even if not Ops-driven, feedback indicates a **product gap or UX friction** that should be logged for Product. |

## **When an SDDI Is Not Required**

Avoid deep diving into matters that **do not directly involve Latchel** or lack actionable content. These may still be logged as product feedback if relevant, but do not require escalation.

**Examples:**

- “Someone came and looked but didn’t test for mold.”
- “I can't cancel the WO after the SP is assigned.”
- “The vendor wasted my time and made me wait four hours.”
- “So much automation.”

<Note>
  Quick review is okay. If communication between the resident and Latchel is solid and the feedback is vague or not actionable, no SDDI is required.
</Note>
