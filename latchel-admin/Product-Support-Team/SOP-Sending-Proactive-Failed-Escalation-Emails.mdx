## **Context**

Once the Quality team validates an escalation failure and submits it through the Failed Escalation Report workflow, the Product Support team is responsible for notifying the impacted property management company. These emails are proactive alerts to PMs that their contacts or service providers are unresponsive.

By proactively communicating, we:

- Help PMs fix problems before they impact emergency work orders.
- Prevent churn by addressing issues before PMs escalate to us.
- Strengthen trust by showing that we are monitoring their systems on their behalf.

## **What We’re Trying to Solve**

- **Ensure contact accuracy** for escalation lists, on-call rotations, and service providers.
- **Reduce delays** in emergency work orders caused by unresponsive contacts.
- **Position Latchel Operations as proactive**, identifying issues before PMs do.

## **Purpose**

This SOP provides step-by-step guidance for the PS team to send the correct **Proactive Failed Escalation Email** to PM users once a validated escalation failure is received from the Quality team. Refer to the quality SOP here: [How to: Audit Failed Escalation (PM Feedback)](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/OPS/pages/3611623425/How+to+Audit+Failed+Escalation+PM+Feedback)

---

## **Step-by-step process**

### **Step 1: Receive Validated Escalation Report**

- Monitor the `ps-escalation` Slack channel or workflow submissions.
- Each submission will include:

  - PM company name & ID
  - Type of failure (On-Call, Escalation Contact, Service Provider, Pre-Dispatch)
  - Job URL(s)
  - Reporting period
  - Relevant contact names/numbers/emails

---

### **Step 2: Select the Correct Email Template**

Use the failure type to choose the right proactive email template:

1. **Escalation Contacts Unreachable** → [*Escalation contacts email template*](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/edit-v2/3615883271#1.-Escalation-Contacts-Unreachable)
2. **Primary Technician Unresponsive (On-Call)** → *On-Call email*[*template*](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/edit-v2/3615883271#2.-Primary-Technician-Unresponsive-(On-Call))
3. **Pre-Dispatch Contacts Unresponsive** → *Pre-Dispatch email*[*template*](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/edit-v2/3615883271#3.-Pre-Dispatch-Contacts-Unresponsive)
4. **Preferred Service Provider Unresponsive** → *Service Provider email*[*template*](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/edit-v2/3615883271#4.-Preferred-Service-Provider-Unresponsive)

---

### **Step 3: Personalize the Email**

- Replace placeholders with actual details from the workflow submission:

  - **[link, property address]** → Job URL + property address from the work order.
  - **[text in escalation contacts field]** → paste escalation contacts.
  - **[name, number]** → paste technician name & phone (from oncall_failures report).
  - **[text in pre-dispatch contacts field]** → paste pre-dispatch contacts.
  - **[text in service provider contact field]** → paste provider(s) info.

---

### **Step 4: Send the Email**

- **Recipients:** ALL PM users associated with the company.
- **CC:** CSM assigned to the account.

---

## **Quality Standards**

- Email must be sent within **1 business day** of receiving the validated workflow.
- Template must be fully customized with actual contact/job details.
- CSM must always be CC’d for visibility.

## **Proactive Escalation Email Templates (PS Team)**

---

### **1. Escalation Contacts Unreachable**

**Subject:** Action Needed: Escalation Contacts Unreachable for {{PM_Company_Name}}

**To:** ALL PM Users
**CC:** {{CSM_Name}}

Hello {{PM_FirstName}},

I hope this message finds you well. We are writing to inform you of a potential issue with your **emergency escalation contacts**. During recent attempts, we were unable to reach any of the contacts listed in your escalation contacts list. This could lead to delays in addressing emergency work orders that may be causing property damage.

Escalation contacts provide a backup communication path for emergency situations. Latchel escalates in [these scenarios](https://help.latchel.com/s/topic/0TO5e000000h9wqGAA/Emergency-Escalations-Property-Damaging-and-Extreme-Habitability-Situations).

To ensure seamless communication and timely service, we recommend reviewing and updating your escalation contact list. Our Product Support team is here to assist you with this process and ensure all contacts are current and reachable.

[**How to Update Your Escalation Contacts:**](https://help.latchel.com/s/topic/0TO5e000000h9wqGAA/How-to-Update-Your-Escalation-Contacts#how-to-update-your-escalation-contacts)

1. Log into your Latchel Portal
2. Click the dropdown menu (far right)
3. Select **Account Settings**
4. Under the **Details** tab, scroll to **Maint. Communication Settings**
5. Update your Escalation Contacts List and Failed Escalation Email List
6. For multiple emails, separate with commas: `test@gmail.com, test1@gmail.com`

Please reply to this email if you require assistance with this update or if you have any questions.

Thank you for your attention to this matter,
**Latchel Product Support Team**

## **2. Primary Technician Unresponsive (On-Call)**

**Subject:** Action Needed: Primary Technician On-Call Unresponsive for {{PM_Company_Name}}

**To:** ALL PM Users
**CC:** {{CSM_Name}}

Hello {{PM_FirstName}},

We are reaching out to notify you of a situation concerning your **primary technician on-call**. It appears that alerts are not being acknowledged, which could impact the timely resolution of emergency issues.

To prevent any service disruptions, we suggest verifying the availability and contact information of your primary technician. Our team is available to help you review and update this information to ensure all alerts are promptly addressed.

[**How to Modify Your On-Call Rotation:**](https://help.latchel.com/s/topic/0TO5e000000h9wqGAA/How-to-Set-up-an-On-Call-Rotation#how-to-set-up-an-on-call-rotation)

1. From your portfolio dashboard, go to the **On-Call** tab
2. Verify/Update the desired technician
3. Use the trash icon to remove technicians if needed
4. Verify/Update a backup contact in case the primary technicians can’t be reached
5. Click **Create** to finalize the setup
