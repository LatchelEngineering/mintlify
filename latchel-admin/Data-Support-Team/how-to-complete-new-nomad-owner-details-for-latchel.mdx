### <custom data-type="emoji" data-id="id-0">:bookmark_tabs:</custom> **Introduction**

The process of adding and putting owners in charge of properties is handled by the [**Data Support Team**](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/overview#%F0%9F%93%B8-Meet-the-team) to make sure property ownership goes smoothly.

To facilitate the process, Latchel, in partnership with NOMAD, has established a procedure to streamline the addition and assignment of owners to their respective properties.

‌

### <custom data-type="emoji" data-id="id-1">:green_book:</custom> **Objective**

The purpose of this page is to provide appropriate steps to take in order to handle these automated emails from Nomad’s Hubspot that consist the details of a new owner, and the properties they own.

### <custom data-type="emoji" data-id="id-3">:bookmark:</custom> **How to Process New Nomad Owner Details**

Requests will come in a form of a case in Salesforce, or as a ticket in Freshdesk. The Data Support Associate must have access to both applications. Here’s the sequential steps to follow:

1. From the dashboard of either application, look for the ticket/case with a subject line: **New Nomad Owner Details for Latchel.**
2. Review the ticket/case and record the following information in the shared spreadsheet <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-4">[https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/18u2GsV5Wz0OGfzctcMD2Y8Vd0C2irfh2pFW5847KV5E/edit#gid=2129093277](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/18u2GsV5Wz0OGfzctcMD2Y8Vd0C2irfh2pFW5847KV5E/edit#gid=2129093277)</custom> in the **Owner Onboarding** tab:
   1. Date when an action is taken to complete the case/ticket
   2. Ticket or Case number
   3. Property Address, and Unit address
   4. Owner Details - Name, Email address, and Phone number
   5. Indicate any remarks depending on the action taken to complete and close the case/ticket.

<img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=f53404e3-ccd0-4096-aff9-8cf1330804e3&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=56&occurrenceKey=null&width=1593&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" /> 3. Take action on the request in the Latchel console by following these steps:

1. Go to the [Nomad property manager page](https://app.latchel.com/admin/pm-company/18261/properties) in Latchel. Go to Properties tab, and then search for the property address. You might find two identical properties as the result. Additionally, both search result might show inactive properties. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=f0149559-c0fd-4c06-979a-6f2955a88427&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=1080&occurrenceKey=null&width=1920&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
2. Click on both search result and check for an active residents. Enable the property with active resident leaving the property with no active resident as a disabled property. _Note that you might see Nomad as a resident in one of the two properties, this is most likely the one to be left disabled._ <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=2de5f4d6-aba9-408f-b0b1-025bddd4afa1&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=312&occurrenceKey=null&width=1187&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
3. In the active property at the details tab, scroll down until you find the **Owner** section. Click the 3 dots, then click **Change Owner**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=b27da3ac-0a5a-4d43-8eeb-2b0b70798ec1&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=643&occurrenceKey=null&width=584&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
4. Search for the owner using either the name, or any of the contact information available. If found, click the owner name, then **Submit** to add/assign it in the property. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=15832127-9aa4-4870-b0d4-8a09fdd5823d&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=535&occurrenceKey=null&width=727&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
5. If the owner is not found when searched, add the owner by clicking **Create New Owner**. Add the owner name, email address, and/or phone number. Click **Continue**, then **Submit**. The owner will then be assigned to the property. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=4f092409-aefc-4bc0-b3d2-fdee860952dc&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=1080&occurrenceKey=null&width=1920&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
6. Next, assign the property to the owner portfolio. Go to the **Portfolios** tab of the property management page. If the owner is already existing in Latchel prior to assigning it to the property, most likely a portfolio for that owner is already existing. Click the **portfolio name** which **_corresponds to the Owner Name_**. Go to the **Properties** tab of the portfolio, click **\+Add Property,** search for the property address, click it, then hit **Submit**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=d1be6b44-6706-4dd8-bf41-5ef92862b2aa&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=393&occurrenceKey=null&width=1199&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" /> <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=3d630812-f647-420d-bf42-7e8561f05a1c&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=536&occurrenceKey=null&width=747&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
7. If the owner was just added, and there is no existing portfolio for the new owner, create a new portfolio. In the Portfolios tab, click **\+New Portfolio**. Use the name of the Owner as the portfolio name, then click **Create**. Then add the property to the new portfolio as indicated in step f. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=7d8ccad8-f8df-481d-ad58-323703ec1745&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=425&occurrenceKey=null&width=1224&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" /> <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=e72d921e-b884-42d2-b32d-cf902f7c07ef&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=428&occurrenceKey=null&width=735&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
8. Additionally, make sure that the owner is also assigned as the Portfolio Owner. If there is no owner assigned to the portfolio, assign it by going to the owner section of the portfolio. Click the **3 dots**, click **Change Owner**, search for the owner name, then click **Submit** to save the changes. Similar to how you added/assigned the owner in the property shown in step c and d.
9. Next, **override the Emergency Escalation Contact List** of the portfolio. Scroll to the **Emergency Escalation Contacts** and expand the section by clicking the **arrowhead**. Click **override** under the Emergency Escalation Contacts List. Add the name and the contact details of the owner as the new emergency escalation contact. Also, add additional note: \*\*“Call the number 4 times before escalating to PM” \*\*right after the owner's contact information. Click **Save** to save the changes made.

Similarly, add the owner's email as an additional contact in the **Failed Emergency Escalation Contact (FEEC) list**. Click **Override**, **enter the owner's email first**, followed by the **default FEEC email** — [maintenance@nomadlease.com](mailto:maintenance@nomadlease.com) — separated by a comma. Click **Save** to apply the changes

10. Lastly, turn on the Budget Approval owner maintenance communication settings in the owner profile page. Go to the Owner profile (you may do this by clicking the hyperlinked name of the owner in the portfolio or in the property of the owner), expand **Maint. Communication Settings** by clicking the **arrowhead**, then **turn on the toggle** for **Budget Approvals**. The toggle is ON if it became **blue**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=599c485c-e61e-40de-b1b1-93bc46b42b0a&&collection=contentId-3002793988&height=1080&occurrenceKey=null&width=1920&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
11. After completing all steps mentioned above, go back to the case/ticket and close it. Make sure to send a reply — **_“Assigned owner and properties to the portfolio, create owner their own portfolio and Set emergency contact to the owner.”_**  to the ticket. This will serve as a note indicating that the ticket has been processed accordingly.

On the contrary if the ticket is blocked due to insufficient information needed, you may escalate the ticket to [**#latchel-nomad**](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C03DSKCGYKT) following the Data Support Escalation process.

It is recommended to hop on the case/ticket and take action as soon as you can, ideally within the first 24 hours of it being submitted. This ensures that the 24 hours service level requirement is met in Assembly.
