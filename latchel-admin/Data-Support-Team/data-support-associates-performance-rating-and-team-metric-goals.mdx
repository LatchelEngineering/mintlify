### **Purpose**

The purpose of this article is to identify and evaluate the current standing and performance of both the team and individuals based on their weekly scores. Moreso, the purpose of this article is to help managers identify high-performing employees and provide them with **recognition**, **rewards**, and **career advancement opportunities**, while also identifying underperforming employees and developing strategies to improve their performance.

### **What is the main goal of the Performance Ratings?**

The ultimate goal of the performance rating is to provide the employee with the tools and resources they need to succeed in their role as a data support associate, while ensuring that the team and Latchel’s expectations are clearly communicated and met.

### **Individual Performance Ratings and Expected Scores**

The Data Support Associates are expected to achieve the target for all performance metrics. The associates' performance shall be evaluated every week based on their **Account Update** **quality score**, **average ticket handling time**, and the **Average Queue Wait Time** . The evaluation will determine their performance rating, which will be communicated to them. The associates will be rated based on their performance against the established metrics and provided with feedback to help them improve their performance.

1. **Outstanding** - This data support associate is already performing at the full level above their current level.
   1. Account Update Quality Score  - avg. is **100%**
   2. Avg. Assembly Ticket Handling Time - 8 hours or below
   3. Average Queue Wait Time  - 72 hours and below
2. **Excellent** - This associate is performing within the score level expectations below:
   1. Account Update Quality Score - avg. is **96%-99.9%**
   2. Avg. Assembly Ticket Handling Time - above 8 hours up to 10 hours
   3. Average Queue Wait Time  - 73 to 120 hours
3. **Great Work** - This associate does not have any significant challenges that are hindering their job performance, but must be meeting the following scores below:
   1. Account Update Quality Score - avg. is **91%-95%**
   2. Avg. Assembly Ticket Handling Time - above 10 hours up to 14 hours
   3. Average Queue Wait Time  - above 120 hours up to 144 hours
4. **Meets Expectations** - This person’s performance level meets the established standards or requirements of their job role.
   1. Account Update Quality Score - avg. is **90%**
   2. Avg. Assembly Ticket Handling Time - above 14 hours up to 16 hours
   3. Average Queue Wait Time  - above 144 hours up to 168 hours
5. **Needs Improvement** **(Challenge)** This person has more Challenges than expected at their current job level and it is hindering their job performance. Moreover, the current scores he/she is meeting may be as follows:
   1. Account Update Quality Score - **below 90%**
   2. Avg. Assembly Ticket Handling Time - more than 16 hours
   3. Average Queue Wait Time  - more than 168 hours

### **Coaching Session**

During one-on-one coaching sessions, Data Support Associates will receive weekly performance score updates and have the opportunity to discuss their strengths and areas for improvement with their supervisor. If an associate receives a "Needs Improvement" rating for three consecutive weeks, they will receive feedback during their coaching session, encouraging the associate on improving their performance, and explore alternative options for improvement. If there is no improvement by the fourth week, a Performance Improvement Plan notice will be served. This process is designed to help associates improve their performance and succeed in their roles.

### **Performance Improvement Plan for Needs Improvement (Challenge) Employee**

Latchel believes in providing its employees with the support and guidance they need to succeed. An associate's overall score must pass consistently each week for four consecutive weeks in order to avoid being placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). During this period, the associate will receive support and guidance to improve his/her performance, which reflects the company’s commitment to helping employees succeed in their roles.

It is important to note that showing improvement for four consecutive weeks during the PIP period allows the associates to continue their employment. However, in rare cases where an associate is unable to meet the expected performance criteria within the PIP period, he/she may be at risk of terminating his/her employment.
