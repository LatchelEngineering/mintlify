## Objective:

The objective of this audit is to publish a well-documented and more accurate help guide that will help our customers with their day-to-day work using the Latchel platform and services.

‌

## Help Guide Checklist:

| **Checklist** | **What should be accomplished to achieve a Yes?** |
| --- | --- |
| 1.) Is the title short and precise with proper format? | The title of the Help Guide must be appropriate, short but precise according to the description and problem/inquiry to be solved by the Help Guide. First letter of important words must be capitalized. The format of the title _must follow_ the **Chicago Manual of Style Capitalization Rules** or the **Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (APA) Capitalization Rules.** The data support is advised to use [Capitalize My Title tool](https://capitalizemytitle.com/#:~:text=The%20rules%20are%20fairly%20standard,prepositions%20\(regardless%20of%20length\)) – a quick guide on formatting subject, articles and document titles. Failure to adhere with the proper title format will result to a **NO** on this question. |
| 2.) Are the instructions and steps provided easy to understand and concise? | The instructions and step-by-step procedure to perform what is asked in the Help Guide must be detailed with accuracy. The steps must be comprehensible by the audience of the help guide. Unclear instructions and misleading steps will merit a **NO** on this questions. |
| 3.) Are the information provided based on the current and standard process of Latchel? | All information and steps provided in the Help Guide must adhere current standard processes, new product releases, and policies of the company. The data support specialist or assignee may check relevant information about the help guide through Confluence, [New Product Release](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C038Z6M0T6H) slack channel, and any News Letter or Announcement from all company platforms. Any inaccurate details included in the Help Guide that are out of published documents and SOP job aid will result to a NO on this question. |
| 4.) Did the data support associate include relevant photos and videos? | Relevant photos of the step-by-step procedure must be included based on the viewer’s perspective or viewpoint of the Help Guide. Viewers are: the property manage or customer, the resident, the service provider, and any Latchel Admin Users. Failure to meet this requirement will result to a NO on this question. |
| 5.) Did the data support associate use annotation colors based on <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-0">[https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/2634481857](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/2634481857)</custom> job-aid? | The data support must use the appropriate Latchel Brand Colors to annotate pictures and videos based on the [Latchel Brand Colors](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/2634481857) job-aid. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=effbcc25-6aa5-4116-b333-020a65458edc&&collection=contentId-2666397706&height=267&occurrenceKey=null&width=505&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" /> Failure to meet this requirement will result to a **NO** on this question. |
| 6.) Did the associate correctly categorize and set the visibility of the help guide based on the viewers? | It is imperative that the associate correctly categorize the help guide based on its intended viewers. The viewers can be any of the following: Property Managers, Service Providers, Residents, or Internal personnel. It is crucial to accurately categorize the guide to ensure that it is accessible and relevant to its intended audience. Failure to property categorize and choose the visibility of the article will mark the associate **NO** for this question. |

| 7.) Is the status of the Help Guide ticket changed appropriately? | The Help Guide ticket’s status can be changed by the assignee or Project Manager depending on it’s current progress.  **TO DO** - means it has not been started yet **IN PROGRESS** - the assignee has started and is working to execute the task as required. **NEED HELP** - when a ticket is changed to this status, the reporter is notified via email. This is applicable for tasks that require attention for something that is blocking the assignee to move forward in completing the task. **DONE** - use to tag the ticket as complete. As soon as the assignee of the task tag the task to Done the task goes to “TO DO AUDIT” status and will be assigned to the Program Manager for audit. Failure to change the status of the Jira ticket appropriately and as needed will result in a **NO** on this question. |
| 8.) Did the data support add the START work time of the day in the Jira ticket's comment section? | As soon as the data support set the task to IN PROGRESS, a timestamp must be placed in the comment section that tells the time the task is started. This is to ensure that the data support has changed the status of the ticket accurately.  In addition to that, this will identify the legitimacy of the actual tracked time of completing task. No started work time in the comment section of the ticket will have a **NO** as an answer on this question. |
| 9.) Did the data support provide updates of the help guide task progress in Jira ticket? | For any unfinished tasks (In Progress) which are not to be completed due to more urgent tasks that needs to be attended or will require days to complete, the specialist must provide a comment in the ticket of any progress updates including the time spent on working on the task for the day, property time logged, and/or the reason why the task needs to stay in the In Progress status. If the ticket is placed in NEED HELP, the specialist must provide a brief details in the comment section of the reason why the ticket is placed to Need Help. |
| The assignee will merit a **NO** on this question if failed to adhere. |  |
| 10.) Is the link to the drafted Knowledge base from the Salesforce provided in the comment section? | The drafted Knowledge based link must be added in the comment section of the ticket for easy access to the Project Manager or auditor. This will also ensure that the auditor is viewing the right Knowledge based the assignee created or updated. The assignee will merit a **NO** on this question if failed to adhere. |

### The Goal

**The current goal is to finish, complete, or publish at least 1-2 Help Guides per associates per week.**<br />**The current audit score must be to get at least 85%.**

## The Scoring

In each of the 9 questions/checklists, the associate must get a YES. **The goal is to get at least 85%.** Below is how the score will be computed.

(Total # of Yes  / 9 questions) x 100 = Total Score for the Audit

Example: (9 Yes / 9 questions) x  100 = 100%  score
