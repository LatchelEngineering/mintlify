### **Introduction**

This standard operating procedure outlines the ticket workflow involving the **_Data Support_**, **_Product Support_**, **_Customer Profitability_**, and **_Customer Success teams_**. The goal is to ensure efficient handling of requests, clear communication between teams, and prompt resolution of customer issues. This process ensures that all customer requests are thoroughly addressed, and the loop is closed with customers for a seamless experience.

‌

### **Objective**

The objective of this SOP is to provide a structured approach to handling account update requests. By defining each team's responsibilities and the steps in the workflow, we aim to enhance collaboration, streamline processes, and ensure timely resolution and communication with customers.

‌

### **Team Responsibilities**

- **Customer Profitability Team:** Ensure the submission of data entry tasks for optional benefits customers is accompanied by an accurate and complete report to Data Support.
- **Product Support Team:** Review customer reports, manage any needed communication and clarifications with the customers, and guarantee all relevant data is prepared and sent to Data Support.
- **Data Support Team:** Accurately update the system according to the reports submitted, with no direct interaction with customers.

**CPT-Driven Report:**

1. For optional benefits customers, the CPT team generates a report detailing unit updates.
2. The report is to be sent to the Data Support team via [#account-update-request](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C05CGVC9FPV) channel.
3. Units not listed on the report will be disabled as previously defined with the customer.

Check <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-0">[https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/3401875467](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/3401875467)</custom>for more info.

**Customer-Initiated Adjustments:**

1. Customers send unit/resident adjustments to [success@latchel.com](mailto:success@latchel.com).
2. The PS team receives these reports and is responsible for verifying and clarifying information with the customer if there are any discrepancies.
3. If the information is accurate, PS send the report to Data Support via [#account-update-request](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C05CGVC9FPV) channel

**Automated Report Exception:**

1. Automated reports, once vetted and approved by PS or the CS team, may be directly sent to [datateam@latchel.com](mailto:datateam@latchel.com) for processing.
2. Ensure that any automated report has been verified by PS or CS to contain accurate and necessary information before turning on automation.

### **Ticket Workflow Steps**

1. **Data Support Ticket Submission**
   1. All requesters must follow the steps in outlined in <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-1">[https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/3222437894](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/3222437894)</custom> when requesting for any type of Data Support tickets.

**Exception to the Rule:**

The Product Support Team will no longer use the [#account-update-request channel](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C05CGVC9FPV) when submitting request to the Data Support Team. Instead, they will be using Assembly, and use the Escalation option to forward any bulk requests from the customer.

2. **Data Support Team Processing**
   1. The data support receives the ticket and process it in Assembly as indicated in the page <custom data-type="smartlink" data-id="id-2">[https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/3220111374](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TST/pages/3220111374)</custom>
3. **Completing the Assembly Ticket (For Data Support Associate)**
   1. The Data Support team must document all steps and actions taken in the internal and public notes, as outlined in the ticket’s request description. Additionally, any discrepancies found should be noted along with recommendations for resolution.
      1. **_If the ticket is requested by a CSM_**, and it’s an **_onboarding request_**, a public note must be added in order to inform them that the ticket is already complete. No need to escalate the ticket to the PS Team to close the loop with the customer, unless otherwise advised.
      2. **_If the ticket is requested by a CSM_**, but **_NOT an onboarding request_**, a public note must be added in order to inform them that the ticket is already complete. However, **_these type of requests must be escalated to the PS Team_** in order for them to close the loop with the customer.
      3. **_If the ticket is requested by a CPT_**, public note must be added, then close the ticket as usual.
   2. Once ticket is complete, the data support must mark the ticket as Closed.
   3. Add an internal note stating that the ticket has been completed including the following:
      1. the worksheet URL
      2. actions taken, and all the relevant information with the update made such as (but not limited to) final total active unit and resident count, etc.
4. **Closing the Loop with the PM: Escalating to PS Team**

   1. Review the completed/escalated ticket, and communicate the resolution or updates to the customer.

### **What happens if data support has clarifying question?**

1. The Data Support must notify the requester via the Assembly ticket.
2. Ensure that the requester is properly mentioned in the ticket in order for them to be notified. Use @ \+ the name of the request, and then link the requester’s name.
   1. **_If the ticket is Onboarding and is submitted internally,_** tag the requester of the ticket. Defer any questions to the assigned CSM, unless stated that it must be escalated to the PS Team.
   2. **_If the ticket is for Optional Benefits,_** tag the requester of ticket from the Customer Profitability Team.
3. The data support must clearly state the clarification needed.
4. Set the status of the ticket to Escalated, and await a response before proceeding with ticket completion. Ideally, if the requester responded to the ticket, the status of it will change to Needs Response. This notifies the Data Support Team that the ticket has a new update.
