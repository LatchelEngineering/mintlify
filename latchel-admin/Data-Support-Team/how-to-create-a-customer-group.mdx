This document outlines the steps to follow for creating a new customer group.

**What is a Customer Group?**

Customer Group is a useful way to organize all tickets sourced from different Slack channels into one. It can also be used as a filter option when creating a Custom View for your team.

### <custom data-type="emoji" data-id="id-0">:bookmark:</custom> **The Steps**

1. Click the **dropdown** arrow right beside Latchel at the top left corner of your dashboard. Then, select **Preferences**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=660f224c-9ff6-4c7b-90a7-b8e0bd5163e3&&collection=contentId-3200712730&height=693&occurrenceKey=null&width=1232&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
2. Then go to **Customer Groups**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=f6c1b905-4a09-447e-adb5-5a5742d308a8&&collection=contentId-3200712730&height=711&occurrenceKey=null&width=1121&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
3. Click **Create Customer Group \+**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=a9b15b4f-d278-4c85-96c1-2dc874e1948e&&collection=contentId-3200712730&height=726&occurrenceKey=null&width=905&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
4. Type your **Customer Group Name**, then click **\+ Add Source**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=d5c3c414-8e14-4653-b30a-e20bb6e552b9&&collection=contentId-3200712730&height=548&occurrenceKey=null&width=955&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
5. Click **Source** with the dropdown arrow, and choose <custom data-type="emoji" data-id="id-1">:slack:</custom> **Slack** as the filter broadcast. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=ccfbfc56-2655-4e97-bd7e-c74c994739d8&&collection=contentId-3200712730&height=672&occurrenceKey=null&width=948&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
6. Next, in the **Select Channel** box, you may look for your integrated Slack channels to be added within your customer group. **_Note that only Slack channels that are integrated to Assembly will populate from the channel list._** You can add multiple channels in one go. Once done, click **Save**. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=7f59ee03-9bf1-4833-93a1-a12e1a6ac7c4&&collection=contentId-3200712730&height=869&occurrenceKey=null&width=1920&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
7. Your newly created customer group will now be listed as a Customer Group on the page. You can now use it as a filter in a custom view. <img src="blob:https://media.staging.atl-paas.net/?type=file&localId=null&id=862f4d24-8c98-42f8-b6ea-5a67b1626510&&collection=contentId-3200712730&height=715&occurrenceKey=null&width=796&__contextId=null&__displayType=null&__external=false&__fileMimeType=null&__fileName=null&__fileSize=null&__mediaTraceId=null&url=null" />
