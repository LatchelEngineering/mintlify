<Info>
**Purpose**: This procedure outlines how to handle cases related to residents who are associated with a Property Manager. The majority of Resident Cases will be shared with their property manager but there a few scenarios where PS will directly communicate with the resident.
</Info>

### **Scenarios Approved to Communicate directly with the Resident:**

- Scenario 1: [How to: Handle Resident Responsible WOs](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/OPS/pages/2748317710/How+to+Handle+Resident+Responsible+WOs).
- Scenario 2: If the Resident Profile is ACTIVE, PS can send[their Public Dashboard URL](3175841803.html) if the resident is looking for ways to access their dashboard.

<Warning>
All other scenarios should be sent to the Property Manager to communicate to the Resident. **Continue to the steps below if this is an unpproved scenario and an escalation to the PM is required.**
</Warning>

### **Step 1: Access the Resident Profile**

1. Search for the resident profile in Latchel by using their **email address**.
2. Ensure that the **“Disabled” option** is checked when performing the search to include deactivated accounts.

### **Step 2: Identify the Resident’s Property Management (PM) Company**

1. Once on the Resident Profile, you will automatically be on the Details tab.
2. Click on the **Property**listed below the Details tab.
3. On the Property page, scroll down to the **Details** tab.
4. Locate the **Property Manager** section to find the **PM Company** associated with the owner.

### **Step 3: Find a PM Contact Email**

1. Identify the **Primary User** for the PM Company from the Property Manager information.
2. Review the **User Groups** section for the **Default User Group**.
3. Select the most appropriate email for reaching out to the PM, typically from the Primary User or Default User Group

### **Step 4: Contact the PM Regarding the Resident’s Inquiry**

1. Use the **Resident Inquiry for PM Template** in Assembly to craft your email to the PM.
2. Send the email to the selected PM contact to address the resident’s inquiry.
