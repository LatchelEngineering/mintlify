# SOP: Guidelines for Notifying Property Managers of Severe Weather Events

The purpose of the Weather Advisory email to PMs is to acknowledge an upcoming
extreme weather event, check in on our customers, and educate them on how they can
best prepare in Latchel.
Review Process
Shift Support monitors extreme weather events on a weekly basis and reports them in the

Note: Our goal is to send no more than 4 Weather Advisory emails per year to avoid over-
notification.
Product Support Process
Once the Product Support team has been notified by the Product Support Manager that we will
alert affected PM customers about an upcoming extreme weather event:
Determine which PMs are affected. The following pgAdmin query returns all active units for all
1.
active customers by state. This Loom video provides an overview of how to use it in
conjunction with this worksheet to identify which PMs should receive an email.
1 SELECT p.address1, p.city, p.state, p.pm_company_id AS company_id, c.name
2 FROM property p
3 LEFT JOIN company c ON c.company_id = p.pm_company_id
4 WHERE p.is_active = 1 AND c.is_active = 1
The following query retrieves a list of emails for property manager users whose companies
are located in specific states (see states in line 15). You can change the state abbreviations in
the query based on where you need to focus your attention, such as specific weather-related
events.
1 SELECT distinct c.name as pm_co_name, u.name as user_name, con.value as email
2 from property p
3 JOIN public.company c
4 ON p.pm_company_id = c.company_id
5 JOIN public.subscription s
6 ON c.company_id = s.relation_id
7 AND s.relation_type = 'Latchel\Company'
8 JOIN public.user u
9 ON c.company_id = u.company_id
10 JOIN public.user_contact uc
11 ON u.user_id = uc.user_id
12 JOIN public.contact con
13 ON uc.contact_id = con.contact_id
14 WHERE TRUE
15 AND UPPER(p.state) in ('TX','OK','KS','NE','IA','IL','OH','PA','LA', 'AR',
'TN','MS','WV','VA','NC','SC','GA', 'IN', 'MO','AR' )
16 AND p.is_active = 1
17 AND s.ended_at is null
18 AND u.is_active = 1
19 AND u.user_type = 'pm'
20 AND con.method = 'email'
21 AND c.name NOT ILIKE '%test%'

Email Template
Dear [Customer Name],
Latchel is actively monitoring [weather event] which is forecast to impact [geographic area] in
the days ahead.
How does Latchel prepare? Our Service Operations team regularly monitors extreme weather
events across the US and adjusts staffing to ensure continuity in intaking resident calls,
troubleshooting and dispatching emergencies.
What should you do in Latchel?
Review your Severity Settings and Emergency Temperature Thresholds in Latchel and make
updates if needed.
Verify your Emergency Escalation Contacts in Latchel, then make sure theyʼre prepared for a
potential increase in calls during the weather event. Here is how Latchel communicates to
Property Managers during emergencies.
Reach out to your Service Providers to understand if theyʼll be working extra hours and able to
reach property locations to handle additional volume if needed. Verify your Service Provider
prioritization in Latchel. Consider whether there are back-up Service Providers you wish to
contact in the event your primary Service Providers become overwhelmed or are unable to
reach an address.
Prepare for extreme weather and set resident and owner expectations. We recommend the
following resources:
US Department of Homeland Security: https://www.ready.gov/
CDC Natural Disasters and Severe Weather: https://www.cdc.gov/disasters/index.html
During the event, check your Latchel dashboard frequently to monitor the number of
emergencies Latchel is handling, how quickly your Service Providers are responding and
determine whether circumstances require you to reach out to residents or owners with
additional communication.
If you need assistance completing any of these steps, donʼt hesitate to reach out to your
Product Support team at success@latchel.com.
Take care and stay safe!
The Latchel Team
