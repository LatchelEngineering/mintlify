# SOP: Sending Proactive Failed Escalation Emails

Context
Once the Quality team validates an escalation failure and submits it through the Failed
Escalation Report workflow, the Product Support team is responsible for notifying the impacted
property management company. These emails are proactive alerts to PMs that their contacts or
service providers are unresponsive.
By proactively communicating, we:
Help PMs fix problems before they impact emergency work orders.
Prevent churn by addressing issues before PMs escalate to us.
Strengthen trust by showing that we are monitoring their systems on their behalf.
What Weʼre Trying to Solve
Ensure contact accuracy for escalation lists, on-call rotations, and service providers.
Reduce delays in emergency work orders caused by unresponsive contacts.
Position Latchel Operations as proactive, identifying issues before PMs do.
Purpose
This SOP provides step-by-step guidance for the PS team to send the correct Proactive Failed
Escalation Email to PM users once a validated escalation failure is received from the Quality
team. Refer to the quality SOP here: How to: Audit Failed Escalation (PM Feedback)

Step-by-step process
### Step 1: Receive Validated Escalation Report
Monitor the ps-escalation Slack channel or workflow submissions.
Each submission will include:
PM company name & ID
Type of failure (On-Call, Escalation Contact, Service Provider, Pre-Dispatch)
Job URL(s)
Reporting period
Relevant contact names/numbers/emails
### Step 2: Select the Correct Email Template
Use the failure type to choose the right proactive email template:
Escalation Contacts Unreachable → Escalation contacts email template
1.
Primary Technician Unresponsive (On-Call) → On-Call email template
2.
Pre-Dispatch Contacts Unresponsive → Pre-Dispatch email template
3.
Preferred Service Provider Unresponsive → Service Provider email template
4.
### Step 3: Personalize the Email
Replace placeholders with actual details from the workflow submission:
[link, property address] → Job URL + property address from the work order.
[text in escalation contacts field] → paste escalation contacts.
[name, number] → paste technician name & phone (from oncall_failures report).
[text in pre-dispatch contacts field] → paste pre-dispatch contacts.
[text in service provider contact field] → paste provider(s) info.
### Step 4: Send the Email
Recipients: ALL PM users associated with the company.
CC: CSM assigned to the account.
Quality Standards
Email must be sent within 1 business day of receiving the validated workflow.
Template must be fully customized with actual contact/job details.
CSM must always be CCʼd for visibility.
Proactive Escalation Email Templates (PS Team)
1. Escalation Contacts Unreachable
Subject: Action Needed: Escalation Contacts Unreachable for {{PM_Company_Name}}
To: ALL PM Users
CC: {{CSM_Name}}
Hello {{PM_FirstName}},
I hope this message finds you well. We are writing to inform you of a potential issue with your
emergency escalation contacts. During recent attempts, we were unable to reach any of the
contacts listed in your escalation contacts list. This could lead to delays in addressing
emergency work orders that may be causing property damage.
Escalation contacts provide a backup communication path for emergency situations. Latchel
escalates in these scenarios.
To ensure seamless communication and timely service, we recommend reviewing and updating
your escalation contact list. Our Product Support team is here to assist you with this process
and ensure all contacts are current and reachable.
How to Update Your Escalation Contacts:
Log into your Latchel Portal
1.
Click the dropdown menu (far right)
2.
Select Account Settings
3.
Under the Details tab, scroll to Maint. Communication Settings
4.
Update your Escalation Contacts List and Failed Escalation Email List
5.
For multiple emails, separate with commas: test@gmail.com, test1@gmail.com
6.
Recent Work Order Example:
{{Job_URL}}, {{Property_Address}}
Unresponsive Escalation Contacts:
{{Escalation_Contacts}}
Please reply to this email if you require assistance with this update or if you have any questions.
Thank you for your attention to this matter,
Latchel Product Support Team
2. Primary Technician Unresponsive (On-Call)

appears that alerts are not being acknowledged, which could impact the timely resolution of

To prevent any service disruptions, we suggest verifying the availability and contact information

to ensure all alerts are promptly addressed.

1.
Verify/Update the desired technician
2.
Use the trash icon to remove technicians if needed
3.
Verify/Update a backup contact in case the primary technicians canʼt be reached
4.
Click Create to finalize the setup
5.
