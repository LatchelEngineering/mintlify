# Job Aid: PS DD and SDDI Category Matrix

This job aid provides guidance on how to categorize deep dive (DD) and SDDI
investigations. It is designed to ensure consistency and accuracy in identifying and
analyzing trends across customer complaints.
DD vs. SDDI — Whatʼs the Difference?
DD (Deep Dive) → Deep Dives are investigations initiated by Property Managers or
Customer Success Managers (CSMs).
These typically involve escalated issues, complex scenarios, or process breakdowns
that require root cause analysis and documentation.
SDDI (Survey & Review Deep Dive Investigation) → SDDIs are proactively initiated by
the Product Support team in response to poor customer feedback (ratings of 3 or below)
captured through:
CSAT Surveys
Google Reviews
Work Order Resident Reviews
These dives help uncover patterns that may not have been directly escalated but still
impact customer experience.
Complaint Category
The Complaint Category is the primary classification used to group related issues for
reporting and trend analysis.
It is found on the Parent Ticket and automatically cascades to any associated Child Tickets.
This category reflects the broader issue as perceived by the customer, based on their
experience and the specific complaint they submitted.
Categorizing accurately is essential to ensure that patterns and pain points are correctly
identified and addressed.

Child Issue Ticket Titles
Child Issue Tickets capture the underlying issue(s) identified during a deep dive
investigation that contributed to the original complaint.
These tickets are not a repeat of the customerʼs experience — they document the root causes,
gaps, or contributing factors uncovered through the investigation.
A Child Issue Ticket may highlight:
System deviations or platform errors
User actions (PM, resident, vendor, or internal team) that impacted the flow
Process breakdowns or unclear workflows
Potential feature requests or product improvement opportunities
Depending on the complexity of the case, a single deep dive may result in multiple Child Issue
Tickets, each representing a distinct root cause.

Issue Found and Root Causes
Root Causes represent the specific, underlying factors within the Issue Category that directly
contributed to a customer complaint.
They are the core issues that Latchel can control, influence, or correct through process
improvements, coaching, or product enhancements.
A well-defined root cause is:
Accurate — clearly tied to the findings from the deep dive investigation.
Actionable — points to something that can be addressed or improved.
Linked to both the problem and the solution — serving as the bridge between what went
wrong and how to prevent it in the future.
Accurately identifying the root cause ensures proper corrective actions, reduces repeat issues,
and strengthens overall service delivery.
Multiple Root Causes and Child Issue Ticket Creation
A single Parent Ticket may have multiple root causes, especially in more complex or layered
complaints. Each root cause represents a distinct contributing factor that should be addressed
individually.
For every root cause identified, a corresponding Child Issue Ticket must be created. This
ensures that:
Each factor is clearly documented and traceable
Follow-up actions can be routed to the appropriate team (e.g., coaching, SOP update,
product escalation)
Trend reporting remains clean, accurate, and actionable
