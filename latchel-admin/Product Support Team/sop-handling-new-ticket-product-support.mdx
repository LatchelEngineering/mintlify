Ticket Overview
A ticket represents a unit of work for Product Support associates. A ticket is created
whenever an email is sent to success@latchel.com by a customer or an internal team
member escalating an inquiry via the Slack channel #ps-escalation. The PS team must
manually grab the ticket by assigning it to themselves before beginning work.
When a ticket is created, the sender will receive an automatic response confirming the receipt
of their message.
New tickets are added to the Product Support Teamspace in Assembly and tagged as
"Needs Response." This queue is your primary working queue.
Prioritizing Assembly Tickets
Upon starting your shift, PS associates must prioritize tickets in the following order:
## (1) Breaching (Highest Priority)
Any tickets that have already breached our 8 Business-Hour SLA fall into this bucket and
must be handled immediately.
## (2) Needs Response
This is the primary working queue during your shift.
Start with brand new Needs Response that have not received a human response. These
1.
must be responded to in order to meet the 8 business-hour First Response SLA.
These are identifiable by: A time stamp (not a date) b often being unassigned.
Tickets approaching the 8-hour SLA will display: the elapsed time and how close the ticket
is to breaching.
Work Oldest to Newest: Handle these tickets based on the Date/Time Opened, starting with
2.
the oldest.
The queue is automatically sorted from top → bottom (oldest → newest).
Determine whether: A response or follow-up is required, or the ticket can be closed.
Continuously monitor the queue! New tickets may appear at any time. Toggle back to
newly created tickets as they come in to avoid SLA risk.
## (3) Escalated
When a ticket remains in Escalated status for more than 3 days, it will:
Automatically move back to Needs Response and include an Internal Note Reminder to
review: Slack threads, ASM links, any other relevant follow-up resources. (If no check-in or
follow-up is needed, move the ticket back to Escalated.)
## (4) Open
Prioritize by Last Reply Date/Time (oldest → newest); this queue is automatically sorted
with newest at the top and oldest at the bottom.
Ticket Processing
When you open a ticket, follow the steps below:
### Step 1. Assign yourself as the ticket owner, then start the timer.
### Step 2. Add the appropriate tag for the ticket and update the company name under the customer profile.
Under Customer Profile, search the name of the company associated with the email.
If the ticket is created through an Ops Escalation, Assembly will automatically apply the “Not
Actionable” label. Ensure the ticket is also tagged as “Ops Escalation.” When selecting tags,
refer to the Job Aid: Interaction Tags Explained to confirm the appropriate classification.
### Step 2. Spam Identification
Definition: Spam includes unsolicited messages such as junk mail, advertisements, or emails
sent to harass or collect unauthorized responses.
If the case is identified as spam, follow these steps:
Action: Tag the ticket as “Not Actionable” and change the status to “Closed-No Workflow”.
### Step 3. Non-Actionable Cases
Definition: Non-actionable emails include automated responses (e.g., out-of-office replies),
system-generated notifications, or invalid escalations where no further action is required.
If the case is Not Actionable:
Tag the ticket as “Not Actionable” and change the status to “Closed-No Workflow”.
### Step 4. Actionable Inquiries
If the ticket is actionable:
The assigned associate should review and take appropriate action to resolve the issue.

Escalating to Product Support Ticket Review
When working on tickets, there may come moments when first reviewing or throughout the
ticket lifecycle where a PS Associate may need assistance on next steps or has questions on the
best path forward.
### Step 1: Review the ticket and ask clarifying questions.
PS should thoroughly vet the escalation, ask clarifying questions and gather all details to
ensure they have full context.
Think about the questions that the PSM may ask you when submitting to #ps-asm-escalate
channel.
### Step 2: Send PS Case Review template in Assembly to the client.
Prior to submitting the ticket for review, always be sure to send a response to the client to
ensure we are sending first responses to meet our SLA and keeping clients in the loop!
