# SOP: Handling Ops Escalations

Service Ops associates may require assistance from the Product Support team to resolve complex customer inquries. Ops escalations
are emailed to success@latchel.com from team@latchel.com. This job aid will walk you through how to spot an ops escalation and best
practices for handling them.
To learn more about the ops escalation path, refer to How to: Escalate to Product Support

How to spot an escalation from Latchel Ops:
The email will be from team@latchel.com and contain the Product Support Escalation form with all relevant details filled out:
Step-by-step Guide:
### Step 1. Thoroughly review the escalation. This should involve:
The Escalation Summary provided by Ops.
It is very important that we do not simply take the Escalation Summary as the source of truth when reaching out to the POC. PS must
do additional research to ensure they have the full scope of the escalation and understand the context.
The WO Shared (if applicable).
Settings based on the Escalation Summary.
This can include: the severity settings, prioritized service providers for the property, the subscription, owner settings, portfolios, pre-
dispatch instructions, etc.
### Step 2. Navigate to the user in question and review the chat transcript of the person who reached out in Latchel ops.
### Step 3. Navigate back to the case in Assembly. In the Case Thread Feed, choose the +Create then Escalationvia Email option. Important
Note: This option clears all past email thread correspondence and creates a new thread.
### Step 4. Determine the type of user that is inquiring. To do so, access the Latchel profile URL that was in the original ops escalation form.
From there, you will see the user type in Latchel at the very top of the profile page.
If it is a Property Manager, continue to step 5.
If it is a Service Provider, refer to Handling SP Cases.
If it is a Resident, refer to Handling Resident Cases.
If it is an Owner, refer to Handling Owner Cases.
If it is a RentRedi Owner, refer to Handling RentRedi Cases.
If it is a Nomad Owner, refer to Handling Nomad Cases.
### Step 5. Insert the Assembly Template called “Clarify Ops Escalation w/ PM” by clicking this button at the bottom of the email composition
box.
### Step 6. Fill out the Clarify Ops Escalation w/ PM Template with relevant details based on what is needed.
### Step 7. Based on how the case goes, the next steps will be to follow either:
Response PM - Closing Cases in PS
Unresponsive PM - Handling Unresponsive PMs
In the Ops Escalation Form, if the question: “Work Order on Hold Due to Pending PS Resolution?” has a YES, PS needs to:
Email team@latchel.com that the situation was resolved and how the WO should proceed.
