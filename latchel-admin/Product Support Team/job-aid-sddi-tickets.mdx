Urgent Save Experience
When reviewing all feedback sources tickets, there may be instances where the feedback
requires immediate follow-up or escalation to prevent further resident dissatisfaction. The two
most common scenarios are:
New Issue Reported: The resident mentions a completely different issue that was not part of
the original WO.
Unresolved Issue (Recall): The resident indicates that the problem still exists or wasnʼt fully
addressed.

SDDI Process:
An SDDI (Structured Deep Dive Investigation) is a mini deep dive — a focused, fast-
turnaround investigation designed to:
Identify the root cause of low-rated feedback (3★ or below)
Determine whether immediate action or escalation is needed
Surface product gaps or process improvement opportunities
1. Document Findings
All findings should be recorded directly on the SDDI ticket in Assembly. Your notes should be:
Concise but detailed enough to explain what happened
Structured with a clear summary of:
The feedback received
Relevant WO details (e.g., WO number, dates, service provider, issue category)
Any supporting context or observations uncovered during review (e.g., delays,
troubleshooting issues, communication gaps)
2. Assembly Ticket Creation
Follow the standard steps for submitting an SDDI ticket.
Select the correct review type (e.g., Resident Review, CSAT, Google Review).
Add clear, organized notes that can be easily referenced by other teams.
3. Determine WO Status
Open WO: Work order is still active or in progress.
Closed WO: Work order is completed, cancelled, or inactive.
Document the current stage of the WO (e.g., Awaiting SP, Scheduled, In Progress,
Completed, Cancelled).

Key Points to Remember
Prioritize 3-star and below feedback for review and action.
Escalate open WOs quickly where action can still improve the outcome.

communications and internal process improvements.

When an SDDI Is Not Required
Avoid deep diving into matters that do not directly involve Latchel or lack actionable content.
These may still be logged as product feedback if relevant, but do not require escalation.
Examples:
“Someone came and looked but didnʼt test for mold.”
“I can't cancel the WO after the SP is assigned.”
“The vendor wasted my time and made me wait four hours.”
“So much automation.”
Quick review is okay. If communication between the resident and Latchel is solid and the
feedback is vague or not actionable, no SDDI is required.
