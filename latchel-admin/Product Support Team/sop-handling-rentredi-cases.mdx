# SOP: Handling RentRedi Cases

Latchel is proud to partner with RentRedi and Nomad Property Management to offer
dedicated support and services specifically tailored for their property owners. These are
the only two partnerships that exist today (Oct 2024) for direct owner support.
Review How to: Handle DIY Owner Contacts for approved actions and insight into actions
Latchel Ops Agents can take for RentRedi Owners.

Interactions with RentRedi Owners are managed in a manner similar to how we handle
traditional customer (Property Manager) interactions, with responses and concerns being
sent directly to the owner's primary email address.
The RentRedi Help Center is the primary resource for customer guides designed to help
resolve customer inquiries. Since RentRedi owners operate through the RentRedi portal
and do not have access to Latchel, our help guides are not relevant to their inquiries.
Always include RentRedi help articles in your responses when applicable. All RentRedi |
Latchel Help Guides HERE.

RentRedi Owner Access & System Limitations
Owners do not have Latchel logins.
Owners cannot access Latchel webchat support or Latchel Help Guides.
Sharing Help Guides with RentRedi Owners
Service Ops agents may send RentRedi Help Guides to RentRedi owners.
RentRedi Help Guides should be shared as if they were Latchel Help Guides.
If the owner pushes back, agents may proceed with any approved actions listed in the
referenced table. How to: Handle DIY Owner Contacts
Work Order Status Updates & Owner Communication
Automated emails are not sent to owners when a work order moves to "Pending" status.
If owner input is required due to a status change:
Contact the owner directly.
a.
If unresponsive, follow up via SMS or email.
b.
Clearly explain why the ownerʼs input is required and what action is needed.
c.
Property Damaging (PD) & Extreme Habitability (EH) Situations
For PD/EH work orders:
Make three (3) call attempts in a single follow-up when contacting residents and/or service
providers.
Non-emergency tasks: Defer to the next business day.
Emergency tasks: Defer to the following day.
All updates and outcomes from resident or service provider communications must be
communicated to the owner.
Handling Owner Requests
Straightforward Requests
Examples include:
Updating owner approval
Canceling a work order
Marking a work order as completed
Action: Update the work order accordingly and move it forward without delay.
Requests to Follow Up with Resident or Service Provider
Action: Make the first call attempt to the resident or service provider and document the
outcome in the work order.
Requests to Add Preferred Service Providers
Refer owners to the following guide: Add Preferred Vendors to Latchel | RentRedi Help Center
All RentRedi | Latchel Help Guides are available through the RentRedi Help Center.
Documentation Requirements
All owner communications, follow-ups, and outcomes must be documented in the work order.
Ensure notes are clear, time-stamped, and actionable for future reference.
