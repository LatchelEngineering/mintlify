# SOP: Latchel Department Escalation Process for Product Support Cases

Objective: This SOP outlines the process for Product Support (PS) associates to escalate
client questions or concerns to the appropriate Latchel department, ensuring efficient
handling, tracking, and communication during escalations.

Key Reminders:
1. Purpose of Escalation
Client inquiries may require escalation to other Latchel departments for additional clarity or next
steps. Before escalation, PS must gather all relevant information to ensure a smooth handoff and
quick resolution.
2. Pre-Escalation Preparation
Before escalating any issue, PS associates must thoroughly vet the clientʼs concern by:
Clarifying Details:
1.
Ask the client for all necessary information regarding their inquiry or issue.
Use the Clarify Escalation with PM Template to gather detailed responses from the
Property Manager (PM).
Review Case Information:
2.
Ensure that all relevant details, including the client's concern and background, are
documented in the case.
3. Escalating to a Latchel Department
Once all details have been clarified, escalate the case to the appropriate department using
Slack. Follow these steps:
Select the Correct Slack Channel:
1.
Refer to the Job Aid: Slack Channels & Escalation Workflows to ensure you are using the
correct channel for escalation.
Provide Key Details:
2.
In your Slack message, include the following:
A clear summary of the issue.
All relevant details gathered from the client.
A link to the case URL for easy reference.
Post Slack Link to the Case:
3.
After submitting the escalation in Slack, copy the Slack message link:
Click on the three dots in the upper-right corner of the message.
Select Copy Link.
Add this link as a Post in the case for tracking.

Scenario 2: The Latchel Department is Responsive
Share the Response:
1.
Once the department responds, share the details, next steps, or resolution with the PM.
Ensure all communication is documented in the case and the Salesforce thread.
Close the Loop:
2.
After resolving the issue, follow up with the PM to confirm closure.
If resolved, mark the case as Closed.
Scenario 3: The Escalation Contact is initially Unresponsive and Follow-Ups after the Case is Closed.
There are instances where CS has attempted to reach out to a client due an escalation but
received no response and then the Escalation Contact follows up later.
If the CS Reach Out is from 30 days or less, PS can loop back in the team member who
initially contacted the client by:
Adding an internal note to the Case Thread with a link to the original case where the client
was unresponsive.
Send the Escalate to CSM SF Template to the client.
Ensure the new case is assigned to the CS team member who initially contacted the client.
Move the Case into an Escalated state.
If the CS Reach Out is from over 30 days past, PS should treat the case as a new inquiry and
follow the normal escalation process if needed.
6. Special Escalated Cases
Certain types of escalations, such as Data Team Bulk Account Updates, Feature Requests, or
System Bugs, may not have defined resolution timelines. In these cases:
Use SF templates that do not commit to specific updates or timelines.
Do not leave the case open while awaiting resolution.
Key Reminders:
Always provide the correct case details when escalating.
Ensure that communication with the client is clear and consistent throughout the process.
Regularly update the case with relevant Slack threads and communication logs.
