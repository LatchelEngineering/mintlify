Pre-Dispatch Instructions are essentially instructions to be carried for emergency WOs

Now as far as settings flowing from company, portfolio to property level, please see this:
Configuration Inheritance (Property, Portfolio, & Company Level)
When a WO is created, the Latchel system first looks at the property level settings.
If nothing specific is configured at the property level setting, the system will move to the
portfolio level settings.
If nothing specific is configured at the portfolio level setting, the system will move to the
company level settings.
Essentially meaning, the property is inheriting the settings that have been default set at the
company level.
Another way to think about it is,
When a WO is created, the Latchel system first looks at the property level settings.
If there is a specific setting configured at the property level, this essentially overrides the
next levels of settings. The WO would follow what is placed at the property level.
The same logic applies to portfolio settings!

in New Hampshire and Florida that Latchtel should not do anything with, so the integration

The sync only has access to the property groups that the user who logs into Appfolio has.

Manager XYZ has access to all property groups.
Maintenance Coordinator ABC only has access to the Florida and New Hampshire
property groups.
If Manager XYZ runs the sync while he is logged-in to his Appfolio profile, the Latchel data
sync will have access to all of Manager XYZ's property groups. If Maintenance Coordinator

Florida and New Hampshire property groups.
So, two possible solutions:
Create a "Latchel" user in Appfolio, only grant them access to desired property groups.
ONLY run the sync when logged in to that Appfolio account.
Make sure whoever runs the sync doesn't have access to property groups you don't want
Latchel to see.
Or, you all can also send a report of the properties should be active in Latchel and we can
disable those properties in Latchel that are NOT in the report. Meaning, we will have the
Latchel account match the report you send.

Service Provider Related Concerns:
Differences Between LCN and LVN
We wanted to clarify the difference between LCN (Latchel Contractor Network) and LVN
(Latchel Vendor Network).
LVN providers exclusively use Latchelʼs direct deposit payout technology, and all
technicians in the LVN are guaranteed to be vetted, licensed, bonded, and insured. They
also offer free recall services for any issues, ensuring higher service quality and
accountability. Additionally, LVN providers operate through a central dispatcher who
coordinates all work orders, ensuring the best technician is assigned for each job.
In contrast, LCN do not guarantee vetted technicians or free recall services. The LCN
operational model is more varied and flexible, without a central dispatcher managing the
process. Our Latchel associates find LCN service providers through search tools like
google, angieʼs list, yelp, etc.

General Information on how to turn on VIP for Service Providers
Until the vendor accepts and schedules the appointment, the exact property address will not
be released. We did this to avoid vendors seeing the address, not actually accepting the WO
and not being assigned, and then coordinating directly with the resident outside of Latchel
and leading to a double dispatch situation as Latchel would continue to find a service
provider.
To understand this process live, I highly recommend watching this quick video!
With VIP turned on, the resident info and property address are shared to the service provider
as soon as they accept the work order. They can then schedule the appointment at a later
date but the system does send them reminders to schedule the appointment. Depending on
the severity of the WO, if no appointment is scheduled, a task and notification will be sent to
your user group to make you all aware of the situation so that you can reach out and
schedule the appointment or reassign the WO to a different vendor.
