## PRE-ONBOARDING

### Step 1. Sales Team kicks off Onboarding and Requests Required Information

1. Sales team closes on the product sale 🙂
2. Sales Executive explains the Onboarding process and collects the required information
3. Once a contract is signed by all parties, the Salesforce opportunity will move to the stage "Closed Won" with the substage "Waiting Setup Fee". At this point a CSM will be automatically assigned to the opportunity.
4. The deal will be posted with a link to the Sales notes and customer profile in the #sales channel in Slack.
5. Once the AE is notified that the customer has paid their set-up fee they send one of the following (depends on their integration type) to the customer introducing them to their assigned CSM. In some cases, they state the date and time of the scheduled kickoff call if it was scheduled during a call or they include the CSM's unique Calendly link to schedule at their convenience in the email.
   - Mary: [https://calendly.com/mary-dickenson/latchel-kickoff](https://calendly.com/mary-dickenson/latchel-kickoff)
   - Victoria: [https://calendly.com/victoria-latchel/latchel-kickoff-call](https://calendly.com/victoria-latchel/latchel-kickoff-call)
   [Click here to expand...](#) [email drafts](#)
6. At this point, the customer is considered transitioned to the Customer Success team.
7. Once the Kick Off meeting is scheduled the CSM will move the opportunity substage should be updated to "Kick Off Scheduled".

## ONBOARDING PHASE 1

### Step 2. Customer Success Team Starts Phase 1 Onboarding - Creation & Basic Configuration - (Pre-Kickoff - Kickoff Call)

1. Create a New Customer Folder in the [Property Manager Files folder](#) a. Click the down arrow next to the "Property Manager Files"
   b. Select New Folder
   c. Copy the company name from the opportunity in SalesForce
   d. Paste \[Company Name\] as the Label of the new folder
   e. Add the new customer folder to starred while the customer is in onboarding. To provide easy accessibility for the team.
   i. Need additional help? [Click here to learn how](#) to create a new folder in Google and add the starred category.
   ii. View the [How to: Handle Property Manager Specific Files](#) for additional guidance
2. Copy Templates into the Property Manager Folder
   a. [Onboarding Checklist](#) (Leave it open).
   b. [Latchel Launch Deck](#)
   c. [Onboarding Notes](#)
   d. [Resident Maintenance Notification](#)
3. Complete Onboarding Checklist
   a. Open the Onboard Checklist in the Property Manager file
   b. Customize with PM Company Name
   c. Open the Opportunity in Salesforce and add the following information to the onboarding checklist you created on the "Pre-Kickoff Launch Details" tab.
   [Click to view the information to add.](#)
   1. Customize with PM Company Name
   2. Open the Opportunity in Salesforce and add the following information to the onboarding checklist you created on the "Pre-Kickoff Launch Details" tab.
      a. Locate the following information on the Details \> Onboarding Tab
      - Service Start Date
      - Contracted Units
      - Software
      - Company website
      b. Locate the following information on the Details \> Contract Tab
      - Primary Decision Maker
      - Primary Latchel User
      - Primary Maintenance User (POC)
      - Any notes in the Contract Modifications section
      c. Locate the client's website
      d. Notes and attachments (Contract PDF)
      e. Review notes on the Notes Tab [Click here to view an example](#)
   3. Once the account is created, copy the last five numbers on the URL link
   4. For example, copy 21286. `https://app.latchel.com/admin/pm-company/21286`
   5. Add those 5 numbers in their Salesforce profile under the Property Manager Information section, Latchel ID.
   [Click here to expand steps.](#)
   d. Check off the items on the "Pre-Kickoff Checklist" tab on the onboarding checklist while you complete steps e - j below.
   e. Create the property manager account in the Latchel console
   f. Copy the Latchel ID from the new Latchel account into their Salesforce profile.
   g. Set up their Product package and Subscription
   i. The account page will open on the "Details" tab and navigate to the "Product Package" button.
   ii. Next, navigate to the "Subscriptions" tab on the top toolbar.
   1. Click the \+ New Subscription button.
   2. Select the appropriate subscription that corresponds with their product package
   iii. Once the product package and subscription are added the Latchel-Assigned Phone Number and the Latchel Communication Phone Number will automatically be added to the account on the "details" tab under the Maint—communication Settings.
   h. Configure the "Details" tab:
   i. Address Section [Click here to expand steps.](#)
   1. Navigate back to the "details" tab.
   2. find the "address" section
   3. Click the downward arrow next to Address a. enter the customer's address b. Click Save
   ii. Maintenance Communication Settings [Click here to expand steps...](#)
   1. the Maintenance Communication Settings will be left as is when entering the main information of the communication profile. Once this information is collected during the kickoff meeting, it will be filled out. a. Use this SOP to learn [How to: Add Pre-dispatch Instructions](#) b. Use this SOP to learn [How to: Add Default Access Instructions](#)
   2. If that information is available:
   iii. Portal Settings [Click here to expand steps...](#)
   1. Go to the "Portal Settings" section and update it with the information collected from their website. Click Save after updating each field.
   2. Note: Application Slug is an internal field that does not need to be filled up at the account creation, so leave it blank.
   3. Add the website URL under the "Website URL" section and click save.
   4. Visit the customer's website
   5. Locate the Resident Review URL on their website.
   6. If the information is available add it under the Resident Review URL section and click save. If that information is not available leave blank. This information will be collected during the kickoff call.
   7. Download a copy of their company logo.
   8. Upload the file under the company logo section and click save.
   9. Use their website branding to select a Nav Bar Color and click save
   10. This can be changed if the customer prefers or has specific logo files, but it shows customer obsession by having their branding before the first onboarding meeting.

You will know which diagnostic workflow to choose by checking it against their subscription (see the table in the [Diagnostic Workflow Types and Assignment Guide](#) section above). As you type, options will show up in the drop-down and you can choose the correct one.
      **Please note that the default for any onboarded customer is "\[MASTER\] Troubleshooting Flow", which is intended for Premium and Home Assistant customers.**
   2. Once you have chosen the diagnostic flow, press "Save".
   3. To validate that the diagnostic workflow has been saved properly, refresh the page and check that the updated diagnostic flow is in the "Default Diagnostic Flow" field for that customer page.
   4. Ensure the "Use Default Action Set Groups" toggle is blue and toggled on. If it is not toggled on it will appear grey and will require you to toggle it on.
   5. The Emergency Escalation Contacts, Work Order Budget Limits, and the Emergency Temperature Settings will remain as is until post "Configuration" Call.

     2. Link to enter a maintenance request.

   b. Review the Sales notes in SalesForce and all notes you added to the Onboarding Checklist and fill out any information you can on the notes page.
   c. If you do not have adequate information or Sales notes reach out to the AE and request to schedule a Pre-Kickoff meeting for the two of you. ## ONBOARDING PHASE 2

### Step 3. Customer Success Team Starts Phase 2 Onboarding - Additional Configurations & Customizations

1. Post Kick-Off Call
   a. The Customer Success team reviews the information collected during the call vs. what is missing, and summarize what information is still needed, so it can be included in the follow-up email.
   i. Ensure your Kickoff Notes are incorporated into Onboarding Notes doc.
   b. Immediately following the call send the [next steps email](#) right after the kickoff meeting titled Latchel\<\>Company Name Kickoff Call - Next Steps
   c. Loop in AL team (If needed)
   d. Create reminders for the PM task list
   i. If you see no activity reach out via email to ensure they received the checklist with this template
   ii. If you see they have some activity reach out to see if they need assistance with this template.
   iii. If they are more than halfway through or finished no communication is needed at this time
   e. Create Reminders in Slack Lists
   i. Go to your Slack list and navigate to this customer section
   ii. Add a 48 hour post meeting check and communicate a reminder
   iii. Add a 48 hour before the "Configuration" call check and communicate reminder
   iv. Add a 24 hour "Configurate" Call check and communicate reminder.
   v. Depends on the full timeline of their specific onboarding.
   vi. Check the shared PM checklist 48 hours post-call. See if there has been any progress.
   vii. 48 hours prior to the "Configuration" call recheck the progress in the PM checklist. Send a meeting reminder via email and list the outstanding action items if any. Use this template.
   viii. 24 hours prior to the "Configuration" call recheck the progress in the PM checklist. Send a meeting reminder via email and list the outstanding action items if any. If there are no outstanding items simply remind them of the upcoming call. Use this template.
   Depending on the progress at this point you may need to adjust your "Configuration" call agenda

## COMPLETE ONBOARDING

### Step 6. Finalize Onboarding

1. Customer Success Manager should go to Hubspot and move the deal to "Onboard Complete" in the onboarding pipeline - See [Step 6 - Onboard Complete stage](#).
   a. When this happens, we send an "[Onboarding Satisfaction](#)" survey using this email template ([https://app.hubspot.com/templates/6245849/edit/8581515?page=2](https://app.hubspot.com/templates/6245849/edit/8581515?page=2))
   i. To see the responses we are getting, use this link: [https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/126hRt9kIkZpWkaGjJ\_9KWjiBtnFsC\_uNNT1R7qFD73I/edit#gid=2026362744](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/126hRt9kIkZpWkaGjJ_9KWjiBtnFsC_uNNT1R7qFD73I/edit#gid=2026362744)
2. After this, the customer is **100% LIVE! YAY!!** 🙂

### Step 7. Ops Support Post-On-boarding

1. After the account goes live, the Ops team will provide support as requested
2. Around the 15 days mark that the account has been live, the ops team will make an analysis of the account to look for opportunities to educate the Property Manager on how they can better use our product. (This is a new process and we need to create it - link to SOP on how to do that)
3. The Ops team will send a summary of their assessment and recommendations to the Property Manager including an offer to schedule a meeting with us to discuss that information (This is a new process and we need to create it - email template)
