---
title: "International Team Member Non-Birthing Parental Leave Policy"
---

## Purpose/Objective

Latchel will provide paid parental leave to eligible international salaried team members (outside of the Service Operations Department) following the birth of a team member's child or the placement of a child with a team member in connection with adoption or foster care. The purpose of paid parental leave is to enable the team member to care for and bond with a newborn or a newly adopted or newly placed child. This policy will apply to births, adoptions, or placements of foster children occurring on or after January 1, 2026.

## Eligibility

Eligible employees must meet the following criteria:

- Have been employed with the company for at least 24 consecutive months (2 years).
- Be a full-time, regular international salaried team member outside of the Service Operations Department (part-time, temporary employees, and interns are not eligible for this benefit).
- Meet country-specific eligibility requirements as outlined below.

In addition, team members must meet one of the following criteria:

- Be a spouse or committed partner of a parent who has given birth to a child.
- Have adopted a child or been placed with a foster child (in either case, the child must be age 17 or younger). The adoption of a new spouse's child is excluded from this policy.

## Country-Specific Eligibility

**Mexico:** Team members residing in Mexico are eligible for paid birthing parental leave under this policy. While Mexico has some social security provisions, team members are still eligible for Latchel's parental leave benefits.

**Philippines:** Team members residing in the Philippines are eligible for paid birthing parental leave under this policy. While the Philippines has some social security provisions, team members are still eligible for Latchel's parental leave benefits.

**All Other Countries:** Team members residing in countries other than Mexico or the Philippines must submit a qualification request to the People Operations Department a minimum of four months before the requested leave start date. People Operations will determine eligibility based on local government programs, laws, and available options in the team member's country of residence. Eligibility will be granted when Latchel's parental leave benefits provide greater value than local government programs, or when local programs are insufficient or unavailable. All eligibility determinations will be made on a case-by-case basis.

## Amount, Time Frame, and Duration of Paid Birthing Parental Leave

- Eligible team members will receive a maximum of two weeks of paid non-birthing parental leave per birth, adoption, or placement of a child/children.
- Each week of paid non-birthing parental leave will be compensated at 100 percent of the team member's regular, straight-time weekly pay.
- The fact that a multiple birth, adoption, or placement occurs (e.g., the birth of twins or adoption of siblings) does not increase the two-week total amount of paid parental leave granted for that event.
- In no case will a team member receive more than two weeks of paid non-birthing parental leave in a rolling 12-month period, regardless of whether more than one birth, adoption, or foster care placement event occurs within that 12-month time frame.
- Paid non-birthing parental leave will be paid on a biweekly basis on regularly scheduled pay dates.
- Approved paid parental leave may be taken at any time during the three-month period immediately following the birth, adoption, or placement of a child with the team member. Paid parental leave may not be used or extended beyond this three-month time frame.

Team members must take paid parental leave in one continuous period of leave and must use all paid parental leave during the three-month time frame indicated above. Any unused paid parental leave will be forfeited at the end of the three-month time frame.

Upon termination of the individual's employment at the company, the team member will not be paid for any unused paid parental leave for which they were eligible.

## Coordination with Other Policies

The company will maintain all benefit stipend (if applicable) for the team member during the paid non-birthing parental leave period, just as if they were taking any other company-paid leave, such as paid vacation leave or paid sick leave.

If a company holiday occurs while the team member is on paid non-birthing parental leave, such a day will be charged to holiday pay; however, such holiday pay will not extend the total paid birthing parental leave entitlement.

If the team member is on paid non-birthing parental leave when the company offers administrative leave (known as an "admin Friday" or "Shortened Work Week"), that time will be recorded as paid birthing parental leave. Administrative leave will not extend the paid leave entitlement.

Team members will be afforded job protection for the period of time that the team member is on approved paid non-birthing parental leave in accordance with their local laws.

## Procedure to Request Paid Birthing Parental Leave

**For Mexico-Based Team Members:**

The team member will provide their supervisor and the People Operations Department with notice of the request for leave at least 90 days before the proposed leave date (or if the leave was not foreseeable, as soon as possible).

The team member must complete the necessary leave of absence form and provide all documentation as required by the People Operations Department to substantiate the request.

**For Philippines-Based Team Member:**

The team member will provide their supervisor and the People Operations Department with notice of the birth, adoption, or placement and request for the additional one week of PTO at least 90 days before the proposed use of PTO (or if not foreseeable, as soon as possible).

**For Team Members in All Other Countries:**

The team member must submit a qualification request to the People Operations Department a minimum of four months before the requested leave start date.

The qualification request must include expected birth date, adoption date, or placement date; country of residence; and any relevant information about local government parental leave programs.

People Operations will review the request and provide a written determination of eligibility, leave duration, and compensation.

Upon approval, the team member will follow the standard leave request procedure, providing their supervisor and People Operations with formal notice and completing all required documentation.

As is the case with all company policies, the organization has the exclusive right to interpret this policy.
