### **Telecommuting**

As a fully remote company, Latchel requires telecommuting from its team members. These arrangements benefit both the company and the employee. Telecommuting is the practice of working at home or a place closer to home instead of physically traveling to a central workplace or headquarters.

Telecommuting is not designed to be a replacement for appropriate dependent care. Although an individual team member's schedule may be modified to accommodate dependent care needs, the focus of the arrangement must remain on job performance and meeting business demands. Employees are encouraged to discuss the expectations of telecommuting with family members before entering an employment/contract agreement.

Additionally, team members are expected to be on camera for meetings to ensure effective communication and engagement. This requirement helps maintain a collaborative and connected remote work environment.

### **Home Office**

Each Latchel team member is expected to establish an appropriate work environment within their home for work purposes. Latchel will not be responsible for costs associated with the team member's home office setup, such as remodeling, furniture, or lighting, nor for repairs or modifications to the home office space.

#### **Expenses (US Employees Only)**

To support our team members in establishing and maintaining an effective remote work environment, Latchel provides a monthly \$150 benefit for office supplies and expenses. This benefit is designed to cover necessary expenses that ensure employees can perform their work efficiently from home. The following guidelines outline what constitutes acceptable and unacceptable expenses under this benefit:

**Acceptable Expenses:**

- **Internet Service:** You may use the benefit to cover a portion of your internet service costs, as reliable internet is essential for remote work.
- **Phone Service:** Expenses related to phone service that is used for work purposes are allowed, as communication is crucial for remote collaboration.
- **Office Supplies:** Items such as paper, pens, and other basic supplies necessary for completing work tasks.
- **Office Equipment Accessories:** Items like a mouse, keyboard, laptop stand, or ergonomic chair that aid in creating a suitable work environment.
- **Child Care:** Expenses related to childcare services during work hours.

**Unacceptable Expenses:**

- **Grocery Deliveries:** These are considered personal expenses and are not covered by the office expense benefit.
- **Food Deliveries or Meals:** Meal costs, whether delivered or otherwise, are not eligible as they do not directly relate to work duties.
- **Home Renovations:** Costs associated with home remodeling or construction to create a home office space are not eligible.
- **Non-work-related Subscriptions or Services:** Any services unrelated to work activities are not covered by this benefit.

#### **Equipment**

Latchel may provide necessary tech equipment for a team member's home office. Such equipment will remain the property of Latchel and is provided for the sole purpose of completing Latchel’s duties and tasks. The team member is responsible for the technology upkeep needed for their equipment. Please reach out to [<u>people@latchel.com</u>](mailto:people@latchel.com) and your direct supervisor for approval if you have equipment needs. \
All office equipment provided to Latchel team members must be immediately returned to the People Operations Department upon termination or resignation as set out in the Latchel Property Return Agreement unless other arrangements have been documented in writing.\
Equipment supplied by the organization is to be used for business purposes only. Latchel accepts no responsibility for damage or repairs to employee-owned equipment. Latchel reserves the right to make determinations as to appropriate equipment, and it’s subject to change at any time.

### **Security**

Latchel requires information security from all team members working at home. Team members will be expected to ensure the protection of proprietary and confidential company information that is accessible from their home office, as laid out in the Proprietary Information and Inventions Agreement. Steps include regular password maintenance, internet protection, and any other measures appropriate for the job and the environment.

### **Safety**

Team members are expected to maintain their home workspace in a safe manner, free from safety hazards. Team members are responsible for the safety of their own home offices and should set up their space in such a way that reduces hazards and safety risks and leads to an ergonomic workspace.\
Injuries sustained by the employee in a home office location and in conjunction with their regular work duties are normally covered by the company's workers' compensation policy. Telecommuting employees are responsible for notifying the employer of such injuries as soon as practicable. The team member is liable for any injuries sustained by visitors to their home work site.
