### **Purpose**

Latchel adopts this Conduct and Work Rules Policy to ensure orderly operations and provide the best possible work environment. The primary goal of Latchel’s Code of Conduct is to foster inclusive, collaborative, and safe working conditions for all Latchel staff. As such, Latchel is committed to providing a friendly, safe, and welcoming environment for all team members, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, ability, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, or religion (or lack thereof).

This code of conduct outlines our expectations for all Latchel team members, as well as the consequences of unacceptable behavior.

 In addition to maintaining and enforcing this policy to protect the interests and safety of all employees and the organization, Latchel complies with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations concerning employer/employee rights and obligations.

### **Scope**

The Code of Conduct applies to all Latchel team members. This includes full-time, part-time, and contractor staff working at Latchel and every seniority level. The Code of Conduct is to be upheld during all professional functions and events, including but not limited to during business hours, at any onsite meeting and onsite WeWork locations, during Latchel-related extracurricular activities and events, while attending conferences and other professional events on behalf of Latchel, and while working remotely and communicating on Latchel resources with other staff.

We expect all Latchel team members to abide by this Code of Conduct in all business matters: online and in-person, as well as in all one-on-one communications with customers and team members pertaining to Latchel business.

This Code of Conduct also applies to unacceptable behavior occurring outside the scope of business activities when such behavior has the potential to adversely affect the safety and well-being of  Latchel team members and clients.

### **Culture & Citizenship**

A supplemental goal of this Code of Conduct is to increase open citizenship by encouraging participants to recognize the relationships between our actions and their effects within Latchel culture.

**Be Customer Obsessed.** We aren’t just customer-centric; we are obsessed with our customers. Our primary focus is bringing value to our customers in everything we do. 

**Continuously Improve.** We challenge ourselves to grow, even when it’s uncomfortable. We work hard to make our company and products better every day. Your work at Latchel will be used by other people, and you in turn will depend on the work of others. Any decision you take will affect users and colleagues, and you should take those consequences into account when making decisions. Doing your very best each day to grow and be better than you were yesterday.

**Develop Extraordinary Teams.** We seek out diverse talent with proven track records and high growth potential to expand our culture and prevent groupthink. We expand our own abilities and participate in the coaching and development of others. Being welcoming to others from all walks of life and backgrounds. This includes, but is not limited to members of any race, ethnicity, culture, national origin, color, immigration status, social and economic class, educational level, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, age, size, family status, political belief, religion, and mental and physical ability.

**Think Big.** We have a bold vision for the future and take concrete steps each day to achieve it. We inspire others by being eloquent and audacious. 

**Earn Trust.** We earn trust by delivering incredible results for our customers and peers. Nobody is entitled to another’s trust. We must work hard to earn it. Not all of us will agree all the time, but disagreement is no excuse for poor behavior and poor manners. We build relationships by being respectful at all times. We might all experience some frustration now and then, but we cannot allow that frustration to turn into a personal attack. It’s important to remember that a company where people feel uncomfortable or threatened is neither productive nor pleasant. Latchel team members should always be respectful when dealing with other personnel as well as with people outside of Latchel staff.

### **Procedures**

Latchel is responsible for providing a safe and secure workplace and strives to ensure that all individuals associated with the company are treated respectfully and fairly. 

**The following behaviors are expected and requested of all Latchel team members:**

- Participate authentically and actively. In doing so, you contribute to the health and longevity of Latchel. 
- Exercise consideration and respect in your speech and actions at all times.
- Attempt collaboration before conflict.
- Exercise behavior and speech that cultivate a culture of honesty and integrity.  
- Refrain from demeaning, discriminatory, or harassing actions and speech.
- Be mindful of your surroundings and your fellow participants. Alert Latchel leaders and People Operations if you notice a dangerous situation, someone in distress, or violations of this Code of Conduct, even if they seem inconsequential.
- Remember that Latchel events may be shared with members of the public and Latchel customers; please be respectful to all patrons of these locations at all times
- At any company event or work-related event refrain from indulging in alcohol and the use of any drugs. Limit your consumption to three alcoholic beverages. 

 

**Though it is not possible to list all forms of behavior that are unacceptable in the workplace**, **the following are examples of behavior that would be considered infractions of Latchel's rules of conduct and are considered harassment. Such behavior may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. This list is not intended to be exhaustive**: 

- Theft or inappropriate removal or possession of company property or the property of a fellow team member.
- Willful destruction of company property or the property of a fellow team member.
- Sexual or other unlawful harassment includes, but is not limited to any sexist, racist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or otherwise discriminatory jokes and language.  Unwelcome sexual attention. This includes sexualized comments or jokes; inappropriate touching, groping, and unwelcome sexual advances
- Posting or displaying sexually explicit or violent material.
- Posting or threatening to post other people’s personally identifying information ("doxing")
- Working under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs during business hours.
- Intoxication from consumption of alcohol (more than three drinks as stated in the previous section) or drugs at any work-related event. 
-  Violence, physical or verbal threats of any kind, or violent or profane language directed against another person.
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in person).
- Making malicious, false, and harmful statements about others.
- Publicly disclosing another's private information. 
- Unauthorized disclosure of business "secrets" or confidential information. 
- Falsifying company records or reports, including one's time records or the time records of another team member.
-  Advocating for, or encouraging, or failure to report if witnessed any of the above behavior.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

### **Weapons policy**

No weapons will be allowed at Latchel events, or in other spaces covered by the scope of this Code of Conduct. Weapons include but are not limited to guns, explosives (including fireworks), and large knives such as those used for hunting or display, as well as any other item used for the purpose of causing injury or harm to others.

Anyone seen in possession of one of these items will be asked to leave immediately and will be subject to punitive action up to and including termination and involvement of law enforcement authorities. Latchel staff are further expected to comply with all state and local laws on this matter.

### **Consequences for unacceptable behavior**

Unacceptable behavior from any Latchel team member, including those with decision-making authority, will not be tolerated.

Anyone asked to stop unacceptable behavior is expected to comply immediately.

If a team member engages in unacceptable behavior, Latchel leadership may take any action deemed appropriate, up to and including probation,  suspension, or termination.

### **Reporting Violations**

If you are subject to or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please notify an appropriate member of Latchel leadership or the People Operations Team as soon as possible. Reporting avenues are DM in Slack to leadership or @people, email to [<u>people@latchel.com</u>](mailto:people@latchel.com), or filing an anonymous complaint using the [<u>complaint form</u>](https://forms.gle/oVS1zJ8VofbhAb4dA "https://forms.gle/oVS1zJ8VofbhAb4dA"). 

**It is a violation of this policy:**

- To retaliate against any person making a complaint of Unacceptable Behavior or against any person participating in the investigation of (including testifying as a witness to) any such allegation. Any retaliation or intimidation may be subject to punitive action up to and including termination.
- To falsify any information on a report, make any false malicious statements about another team member's conduct, or interfere with an investigation. Any of these actions may be subject to punitive action up to and including termination.

### Disciplinary Action

Team members who violate this policy may face disciplinary consequences in proportion to their violation. Lathel’s  leadership will determine how serious a team member’s offense is and take the appropriate action

### **Responsibility**

It is the People Operations team’s and the company leadership's responsibility to ensure this policy is followed.
