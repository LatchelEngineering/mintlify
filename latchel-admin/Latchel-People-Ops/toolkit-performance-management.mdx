### **Performance Management**

Performance management is a comprehensive process that involves various activities and strategies to ensure that individuals, teams, and organizations achieve their goals effectively and efficiently. It encompasses the entire performance cycle, from setting objectives and expectations to measuring, evaluating, and enhancing performance. Here are the key components of performance management:

- Performance Planning: Collaboratively defining performance expectations, standards, and targets for individuals or teams. This involves discussing job responsibilities, identifying key performance indicators (KPIs), and setting performance measures.
- Goal Setting: Establishing clear and specific goals that align with organizational objectives and are challenging yet achievable. Goals should be measurable and time-bound.
- Performance Measurement: Collecting and analyzing data to assess performance against established goals and criteria. This involves monitoring key metrics, tracking progress, and identifying areas of improvement.
- Performance Feedback: Delivering timely and constructive feedback to individuals or teams regarding their performance. This can include recognition of achievements, guidance on areas needing improvement, and development opportunities.
  - Ongoing Communication: Regularly engage in feedback conversations and open communication to discuss progress, address challenges, and provide guidance. This includes both weekly 1:1s and bi-annual check-ins.
  - Performance Check-ins: Conduct bi-annual check-ins to assess individual performance. This typically involves reviewing achievements & goals, identifying strengths and weaknesses, and providing constructive feedback.
- Performance Improvement: Collaboratively developing action plans and strategies to enhance performance. This may involve providing training, coaching, or mentoring to address skill gaps, setting performance improvement goals, and monitoring progress.
- Rewards and Recognition: Recognizing and rewarding high performance and achievements to motivate individuals and reinforce desired behaviors. This can include monetary incentives, promotions, public recognition, or non-monetary rewards such as certificates or additional responsibilities.
- Career Development: Supporting employees' career growth and development by identifying their aspirations, providing learning opportunities, and aligning their goals with organizational needs.
- Documentation: Maintaining accurate records and documentation related to performance management processes, including performance evaluations, feedback discussions, goal-setting documents, and development plans.

### **Setting Goals and Establishing KPIs/OKRs.**

At Latchel our Leadership sets the company goals and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) annually in Q4 for Q1 of the following year. In the same time frame, they assign specific metrics and KPIs to departments. With those departmental KPIs the Department heads work collaboratively with their team leads and individual team members to set Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) for each quarter in six two-week sprints.

Latchel uses OKRs to help find and fix the causes if KPIs health metrics turn red. Utilizing OKRs we measure what was done and whether it worked, then create a list of best practices based on historical successes. This facilitates traceability, transparency, and confidence monitoring at any given time.

#### Example:

Let's say a KPI "hit \$500k MRR by the end of the quarter" is failing. Utilizing OKRs, you can connect this target to every sales team member:     

- Objective: Finish the year with convincing sales figures.  
  - Key result 1: Hit \$600k MRR  
  - Key result 2: Close 100 new deals  
  - Key result 3: Hit an average deal size of \$100k 

This sales OKR clearly highlights what sales personnel need to do to boost the KPI's health. By closing more deals at larger deal sizes, they can meaningfully contribute to the overall success of the KPI. 

#### **Setting Goals in Lattice:**

Currently, goal setting in Lattice is not mandatory, and the level of goals tracked in Lattice is at the discretion of the manager.\
The goal is to provide employees with the opportunity to set professional development goals to get to the next level and work collaboratively with their manager to establish individual OKRs that align with the team, department, and company goals. This allows employees to gain visibility into the impact of their role and efforts within the broader organizational context.

#### **Review Schedule**

Latchel’s framework follows a bi-annual cadence conducting check-ins using the "start doing, stop doing, and continue doing" format.

Using this format is not just about providing feedback but also fostering a constructive dialogue that encourages growth, development, and continuous improvement. It should be a collaborative process that empowers the employee to take ownership of their performance and development.

- Weekly 1:1s The purpose of weekly one-on-one meetings to review goals and performance is to provide a regular and structured opportunity for managers to provide frequent feedback and provide an opportunity for managers and employees to discuss progress, address challenges, and ensure alignment proactively.
- January Check-In
  - Developmental-focused check-ins
  - Conducting performance reviews from Q3 and Q4 of the previous year
  - Considerations for nonmonetary promotions
  - Setting goals for the upcoming year.
- July Check-In
  - Goal check-in to track progress
  - Conducting performance reviews from Q1 and Q2
  - Considerations for promotions and market analysis review

### **Rewards and Recognition**

At Latchel we believe that rewards and recognition are powerful tools to motivate, engage, and inspire employees.

#### **Rewards & Recognition Tied to Performance:**

1. Merit Increases: Merit increases are salary or wage increases that are awarded based on an employee's performance. These increases are given annually in Q3 and are determined by evaluating an employee's individual performance, contributions, and achievements.
2. Promotions and Advancement: Advancement opportunities are considered bi-annually in Q1 and Q3, such as promotions or career progression, and can be used as a reward for high-performing employees. Promoting employees to higher-level positions or granting them additional responsibilities and authority recognizes their exceptional performance and provides a clear path for growth within the organization.
3. Performance-Based Equity: At Latchel, equity or stock options are tied to performance. High-performing employees may be eligible for equity grants, providing them with a direct stake in the company's success. This aligns their interests with long-term company performance and creates a sense of ownership and loyalty.

#### **Public Shoutouts and Recognition:**

Utilizing the workflows in Slack in #core-team and the Lattice app to give public shoutouts.  By publicly acknowledging and appreciating individuals' contributions, organizations foster a positive work culture, reinforce desired behaviors, and create an environment where employees thrive and feel valued.

### **Performance Improvement**

#### **Performance Improvement Plans (PIP):**

- Clarify Expectations: PIPs provide a clear outline of performance expectations and goals. They help employees understand what is expected of them and what areas need improvement. This clarity helps align individual efforts with organizational objectives.
- Identifying Performance Gaps: PIPs highlight areas where an employee's performance is falling short of expectations. By identifying specific areas that require improvement, PIPs provide a roadmap for addressing performance gaps.
- Providing Guidance and Support: PIPs offer guidance and support to employees who are struggling to meet performance standards. They outline specific actions and resources that can help individuals improve their skills, knowledge, or work processes. PIPs often involve coaching, training, or mentoring to provide the necessary support.
- Documenting Performance Issues: PIPs serve as formal documentation of performance issues and efforts made to address them. This documentation is valuable for tracking progress, evaluating the effectiveness of interventions, and ensuring fairness and consistency in performance management practices.
- Legal Protection: In some cases, PIPs can provide legal protection for organizations. If an employee's performance does not improve despite appropriate support and guidance provided through a PIP, it can be used as evidence to support further disciplinary actions, including termination, if necessary.

#### **When to use a PIP:**

It's important to note that PIPs should be used as a constructive tool for improvement rather than as a punitive measure. They should be implemented with clear communication, support, and a focus on helping the employee succeed. PIPs should also be implemented consistently and fairly across the organization to maintain transparency and equity in performance management practices.

- Persistent Performance Issues: When an employee consistently fails to meet performance expectations or demonstrates a pattern of underperformance, a PIP can be implemented. This is appropriate when informal feedback and coaching have not resulted in significant improvement.
- Significant Performance Decline: If an employee's performance has significantly declined from previously established standards, a PIP can be utilized to address the decline and help the employee regain their previous level of performance.
- Specific Performance Concerns: When there are specific areas or skills where an employee is struggling, a PIP can be implemented to address those concerns and provide focused support and development opportunities.
- Corrective Action: If an employee's performance issues are serious and impact the overall functioning of the team or organization, a PIP can be used as a corrective action to address the problems and bring the performance back to an acceptable level.
- Goal Alignment: In situations where there is a misalignment between an employee's performance and the organization's goals, a PIP can be used to realign the employee's efforts and ensure they are working towards the desired objectives.
