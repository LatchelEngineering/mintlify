## **Purpose/Objective**

Latchel will provide up to eight weeks of paid birthing parental leave to regular employees who have given birth to a child. The first four weeks will be compensated at 100 percent of the employee’s regular weekly pay. The purpose of the first four weeks is to enable the parent to medically recover from giving birth. The second four weeks will be compensated at 50 percent of the employee’s regular weekly pay. The purpose of these additional four weeks is to enable the parent to care for and bond with a newborn. This policy will run concurrently with Family, and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) leave, as applicable. This policy will be in effect for births occurring on or after January 1, 2023.

## **Eligibility**

Eligible employees must meet the following criteria:

- Have been employed with the company for at least 12 months (the 12 months do not need to be consecutive).
- Have worked at least 1,250 hours during the 12 consecutive months immediately preceding the date the leave would begin.
- Be a full- or part-time, regular employee (temporary employees and interns are not eligible for this benefit).

In addition, employees must meet one of the following criteria:

- Have given birth to a child.

## **Amount, Time Frame, and Duration of Paid Parental Leave**

- Eligible employees will receive a maximum of eight weeks of paid birthing parental leave per birth. The fact that multiple births occur (e.g., the birth of twins) does not increase the eight-week total amount of paid leave granted for that event. In addition, in no case will an employee receive more than eight weeks of paid leave in a rolling 12-month period, regardless of whether more than one birth occurs within that 12-month period.
- Each week of the first four weeks of paid birthing parental leave is compensated at 100 percent of the employee’s regular, straight-time weekly pay. Each week of the second four weeks of paid leave is compensated at 50 percent of the employee’s regular, straight-time weekly pay. Paid birthing parental leave will be paid on a biweekly basis on regularly scheduled pay dates.
- Approved paid birthing parent leave may be taken at any time during the three-month period following the birth. Paid birthing parental leave may not be used or extended beyond this three-month period.
- Employees must take paid birthing parental leave in one continuous period of leave and must use all paid birthing parental leave during the three-month time frame indicated above. Any unused paid birthing parental leave will be forfeited at the end of the three-month period.
- Upon termination of the individual’s employment at the company, the employee will not be paid for any unused paid birthing parental leave for which they were eligible.

## **Coordination with Other Policies**

- Paid birthing parent leave taken under this policy will run concurrently with leave under the FMLA; thus, any leave taken under this policy that falls under the definition of circumstances qualifying for leave due to the birth of a child, the leave will be counted toward the 12 weeks of available FMLA leave per a 12-month period. All other requirements and provisions under the FMLA will apply. In no case will the total amount of leave—whether paid or unpaid—granted to the employee under the FMLA exceed 12 weeks during the 12-month FMLA period. Please refer to the Family and Medical Leave Policy for further guidance on the FMLA.
- After the paid birthing parental leave and any short-term disability leave for employees after birth is exhausted, the balance of FMLA leave (if applicable) will be compensated through employees’ accrued sick, vacation, and personal time. Upon exhaustion of accrued sick, vacation, and personal time, any remaining leave will be unpaid leave. Please refer to the Family and Medical Leave Policy for further guidance on the FMLA.
- The company will maintain all benefits for employees during the paid birthing parental leave period just as if they were taking any other company paid leave, such as paid vacation leave or paid sick leave.
- If a company holiday occurs while the employee is on paid birthing parental leave, such day will be charged to holiday pay; however, such holiday pay will not extend the total paid birthing parental leave entitlement.
- If the employee is on paid birthing parental leave when the company offers administrative leave (known as an “admin day”), that time will be recorded as paid birthing parental leave. Administrative leave will not extend the paid leave entitlement.
- An employee who takes paid birthing parental leave that does not qualify for FMLA leave will be afforded the same level of job protection for the period of time that the employee is on paid birthing parental leave as if the employee were on FMLA-qualifying leave.

## **Procedure to Request Paid Maternity Leave**

- The employee will provide their supervisor and the People Operations Department with notice of the request for leave at least 30 days before the proposed leave date (or if the leave was not foreseeable, as soon as possible).
- The employee must complete the necessary leave of absence form and provide all documentation as required by the People Operations Department to substantiate the request.
