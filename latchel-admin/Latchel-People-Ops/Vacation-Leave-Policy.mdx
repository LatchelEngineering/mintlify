## Purpose

Latchel provides paid vacation leave (PTO) for team members to take time for rest and recuperation.

## Eligibility

All regular full- and part-time team members are eligible for vacation leave benefits. Regular part-time team members working 20 to 29 hours per week will earn vacation on a prorated basis. Regular full-time team members are those working 30 or more hours per week. Vacation accrual begins 30 days of working at Latchel for regular employees and 90 days for all international contractors.

Vacation is accrued according to the schedule in this policy. Vacation can be used after it is earned. Vacation leave will not be earned during an unpaid leave of absence.

## Procedures

### Leave requests

All requests must be submitted at least one week before the requested leave. Unless otherwise approved by their direct supervisor. Team members must ensure that they have enough accrued leave available to cover the time off requested.

Requests will be evaluated based on several factors, including department operating and staffing requirements. The supervisor should indicate whether the request has been approved or denied and submit it through the appropriate platform to the team member within three business days of the date the leave request was submitted. If the request for vacation leave is denied, the supervisor should provide an explanation for the denial of the request returned to the team member.

**Employee:**

To schedule vacation time, employees must submit a completed leave request on their JustWorks dashboard by selecting the "time off" tab on the left toolbar and then clicking the "request time off" button; view the steps [here]().

**Contractors:**

To schedule paid time off must submit their request from their Deputy dashboard; view the steps [here](https://help.latchel.com).

### Vacation Pay / PTO

Vacation will be paid at the team member's base rate at the time the leave is taken. Vacation pay does not include overtime or any special forms of compensation such as incentives, commissions, bonuses, or shift differentials. If a holiday falls during the employee's vacation, the day will be charged to holiday pay, if applicable, rather than vacation pay.

### Use of vacation leave for sick leave

Vacation leave can be substituted for sick leave when the team member's sick leave has been exhausted, and the employee or family member of the team member is sick and they need to take time off. The procedure to request vacation leave as sick leave is the same as submitting a vacation leave request in JustWorks and Deputy, but in the note, the employee should say that they are sick. This omits the two-week submission period. The team member should also notify their direct supervisor via Slack or email that they will be offline and provide the reason and any documentation requested. The supervisor can deny the use of paid leave if the appropriate documentation is not presented when requested.

### Leave without Pay

Leave without pay can be requested and taken only after the team member's vacation leave balance has been exhausted. The only exception would be unpaid leave that qualifies under FMLA for US Employees.

### Carryover of vacation leave

Latchel encourages team members to use available vacation time. If the available vacation time is unused by the end of a calendar year, accrued, unused leave may carry over to the next year. If the total amount of carried-over accrued vacation time reaches 160 hours max, the vacation accrual will temporarily stop. When vacation is taken again, and the total accrued amount falls below the maximum, vacation accrual will begin again.

### Payment upon termination

If employment or contract is terminated for any reason, accrued, unused vacation leave earned through the last day of active employment will generally be paid at the team member's base rate at termination. However, if a team member resigns, they forfeit their accrued PTO balance payout due to the loss in training and hiring costs to the company.

In the event of the team member's death, earned, unused vacation time will be paid to the team member's estate or designated beneficiary.

### State Regulations Compliance

This policy is subject to state regulations. In the event of any conflict between this policy and applicable state regulations, state regulations will prevail. If you have any questions regarding how state regulations may affect this policy, please consult with the People Operations Department.
