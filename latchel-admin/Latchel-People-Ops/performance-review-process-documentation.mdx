---
title: "Performance Review Process Documentation"
---

**Purpose:** The performance review process is a crucial component of our organization's talent management strategy. It allows for meaningful feedback, goal setting, and professional development opportunities for employees. This document outlines the step-by-step process for conducting bi-annual performance reviews using the "start doing, stop doing, and continue doing" format.

## Bi-Annual Check-ins:

Bi-annual check-ins provide an opportunity for managers and employees to reflect on performance, discuss strengths, areas for improvement, and set goals. The check-ins will follow the "start doing, stop doing, and continue doing" format, encouraging constructive feedback and actionable insights. These check-ins will occur twice a year.

## Schedule:

The performance review process will follow a predefined schedule:

**January:**
- Developmental-focused check-ins
- Performance review from the previous year
- Goal setting for the upcoming year

**July:**
- Goal check-in on progress
- Performance review
- Promotion considerations
- Market analysis review

People Operations will communicate the review cycle dates and deadlines each year and set the template and downward review cycles in Lattice. The People Operations team will also send automated reminders through Lattice as well.

Managers will schedule and hold all reviews with their direct reports within the scheduled review window for each cycle.

**January:**

Downward Review Template in Lattice: Managers are responsible for completing the downward review template in Lattice for each direct report. This template allows managers to provide feedback on their direct reports' performance and areas for improvement. It should be completed before the January performance review. The People Ops team will automate reminders to ensure the timely completion of these reviews.

Goal Setting by Direct Reports: Managers should encourage their direct reports to set their goals for the upcoming year before the January review. This proactive approach empowers employees to take ownership of their professional development. Direct reports can track their goals throughout the year, making it easier to evaluate progress during the July review.

Review Meetings: Managers must schedule and hold review meetings within the designated review window for each performance review cycle. These meetings should be focused on providing constructive feedback, discussing goals, and addressing any concerns. Managers should document the discussions and outcomes for future reference in Lattice.

Review Cycle Closure: Upon completion of the review cycle, the People Operations team will be responsible for closing the performance review cycle. They will ensure that all reviews and associated documentation are appropriately recorded and stored

**July:**

All components from the January check-in are repeated with the addition of the compensation review.

Compensation Adjustment and Promotion Considerations: Managers should utilize the leveling matrix, [compensation adjustment calculator](#), and the [compensation brackets](#) for the current year, to calculate salary increases and/or equity awards for any promotions awarded during the performance review process. This ensures fairness and consistency across the organization. Promotion considerations should be thoroughly evaluated based on performance, competencies, and organizational needs.

Managers will need to share the salary and/or equity awards for each team member with the People Operations Team for final approval and administration.

Review Cycle Closure: Upon completion of the review cycle, the People Operations team will be responsible for closing the performance review cycle. The People Operations team will also administer all salary adjustments and equity awards and update all documentation and personnel files.

Following this process, we aim to foster a culture of continuous improvement, professional growth, and fair evaluation across the organization.
