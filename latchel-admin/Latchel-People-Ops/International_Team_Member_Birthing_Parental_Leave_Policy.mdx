# International Team Member Birthing Parental Leave Policy

## Purpose/Objective

Latchel will provide paid birthing parental leave to eligible international salaried team members who have given birth to a child. The purpose of paid birthing parental leave is to enable the parent to medically recover from giving birth and to care for and bond with a newborn. This policy will be in effect for births occurring on or after January 1, 2026.

## Eligibility

The eligible team members must meet the following criteria:

- Have been working with the company for at least 24 consecutive months (2 years).
- Be a full-time, regular international salaried team member (part-time, temporary employees, and interns are not eligible for this benefit).
- Have given birth to a child.
- Meet country-specific eligibility requirements as outlined below.

## Country-Specific Eligibility

**Mexico:** Team members residing in Mexico are eligible for paid birthing parental leave under this policy. While Mexico has some social security provisions, team members are eligible for Latchel's parental leave benefits.

**Philippines:** Team members residing in the Philippines are eligible for paid birthing parental leave under this policy. While the Philippines has some social security provisions, team members are eligible for Latchel's parental leave benefits.

**All Other Countries:** Team members residing in countries other than Mexico or the Philippines must submit a qualification request to the People Operations Department a minimum of four months before the requested leave start date. People Operations will determine eligibility based on local government programs, laws, and available options in the team member's country of residence. Eligibility will be granted when Latchel's parental leave benefits provide greater value than local government programs, or when local programs are insufficient or unavailable. All eligibility determinations will be made on a case-by-case basis.

## Amount, Time Frame, and Duration of Paid Birthing Parental Leave

- Eligible team members will receive a maximum of six weeks of paid birthing parental leave per birth.
- The first three weeks will be compensated at 100 percent of the team member's regular, straight-time weekly pay.
- The second three weeks will be compensated at 50 percent of the employee's regular, straight-time weekly pay.
- The fact that multiple births occur (e.g., the birth of twins) does not increase the four-week total amount of paid leave granted for that event.
- In no case will an international team member receive more than six weeks of paid birthing parental leave in a rolling 12-month period, regardless of whether more than one birth occurs within those 12 months.

## Coordination with Other Policies

The company will maintain all benefit stipend (if applicable) for the team member during the paid birthing parental leave period, just as if they were taking any other company-paid leave, such as paid vacation leave or paid sick leave.

If a company holiday occurs while the team member is on paid birthing parental leave, such a day will be charged to holiday pay; however, such holiday pay will not extend the total paid birthing parental leave entitlement.

If the team member is on paid birthing parental leave when the company offers administrative leave (known as an "admin Friday" or "Shortened Work Week"), that time will be recorded as paid birthing parental leave. Administrative leave will not extend the paid leave entitlement.

Team members will be afforded job protection for the period of time that the team member is on approved paid birthing parental leave in accordance with their local laws for contractors.

## Procedure to Request Paid Birthing Parental Leave

**For Mexico-Based Team Members:**

The team member will provide their supervisor and the People Operations Department with notice of the request for leave at least 90 days before the proposed leave date (or if the leave was not foreseeable, as soon as possible).

The team member must complete the necessary leave of absence form and provide all documentation as required by the People Operations Department to substantiate the request.

**For Philippines-Based Team Member:**

The team member will provide their supervisor and the People Operations Department with notice of the request for leave at least 90 days before the proposed leave date (or if the leave was not foreseeable, as soon as possible).

The team member must complete the necessary leave of absence form and provide all documentation as required by the People Operations Department to substantiate the request.

**For Team Members in All Other Countries:**

The team member must submit a qualification request to the People Operations Department a minimum of four months before the requested leave start date.

The qualification request must include the expected due date, country of residence, and any relevant information about local government parental leave programs.

People Operations will review the request and provide a written determination of eligibility.

Upon approval, the team member will follow the standard leave request procedure, providing their supervisor and People Operations with formal notice and completing all required documentation.

As is the case with all company policies, the organization has the exclusive right to interpret this policy.
