title: "Porting Away a Latchel Maintenance Number (Twilio)"
---

If a customer requests to cancel and would like to port their emergency maintenance number. Here is a [<u>Twilio article</u>](https://support.twilio.com/hc/en-us/articles/223179588-How-do-I-port-my-phone-numbers-away-from-Twilio- "https://support.twilio.com/hc/en-us/articles/223179588-How-do-I-port-my-phone-numbers-away-from-Twilio-") and a few steps to follow to assist the customer through this process.

### **Steps:**\

1. Send the customer the attached Latchel port authorization form and advise them you will reach out to Twilio to start the process.
2. Email Twilio at  [<u>porting@twilio.com</u>](mailto:porting@twilio.com) to request their service address. In the body include the Latchel account SID (ACf37317ccc7089d17cbff379106a18146),  the phone number they are requesting to port, and a screenshot of the number in our Twilio account. I have attached an example screenshot.
3. When you receive the service address and pin back from Twilio send an email with the following information to the customer
   - The Twilio [<u>article link</u>](https://support.twilio.com/hc/en-us/articles/223179588-How-do-I-port-my-phone-numbers-away-from-Twilio- "https://support.twilio.com/hc/en-us/articles/223179588-How-do-I-port-my-phone-numbers-away-from-Twilio-")
   - The last 8 characters of our SID - 06a18146
   - Twilio's  service address and pin
