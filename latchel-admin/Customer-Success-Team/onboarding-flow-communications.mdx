## Objective

### Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide an overview of the Onboarding Process Flow and provide links to relevant SOPs and Templates used to communicate with customers. All communications templates are located in the Customer Success shared drive.

This page can also be used as a guide for new team members onboarding to the Customer Success Team that will be handling Onboarding.

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Introduction to the Onboarding Process (Phases)

Latchel has a lot of customization and configuration options available to our customers. While some customers will require several of those customizations, other customers may not (or could be a plus for them vs. a requirement) and we are unnecessarily overwhelming them with questions. Therefore, in the short-term, we will work on phasing and simplifying the onboarding process to the majority of our customers' hard requirements. We will define basic configurations that must happen before an account goes live and take care of customizations or granular configurations on a second phase.

Therefore, we decided to split the onboarding process into two phases:

**Phase 1 - Standard Package**

This will include the key/minimum required configurations to start an account. In this phase, we will make company and portfolio level configurations, and simple Service Provider Configurations.

**Phase 2 - Additional Configurations & Customizations**

This will include fine-tuning configurations to take care of exception scenarios (like property level special cases), to add more granularity to service providers profiles, or to appropriately handle more complex accounts. For large accounts, phase 2 will also add another batch of portfolios, so we can get started on servicing a portion of their portfolios sooner.

At the moment of starting the onboarding process, we will clarify those phases with the customer and define if they wish to go live after phase 1 or would prefer to wait until finalizing phase 2. In the communication template, this is defined in the sales call. CS will just verify on the call. We will clarify the risks of going live after phase 1 and schedule time to work with them on phase 2.

Next, tech will be working on finding more effective ways to make those customizations. For example, they can create a tool that would allow property managers to "Train the System" to set rules to portfolio, property, and company level based on their preferences as they approve work orders. Once these tech tools are available, we will review our process to reduce the information required during onboarding to address what will not be collected via tech.

To see more details on how the phases are structured and which information is collected on each one of them, refer to: (CS/Admin) Job-Aid: Customer Onboarding Information Checklist

## SALES PROCESS (PRE-ONBOARDING)

### Step 0. Sales Team Pre-Onboarding Kick-Off Flow

Sales team has an initial discovery meeting with the customer:

- A Sales Development Representative (SDR) or Sales Engineer leads the discovery meeting with the customer
- An SDR or Sales Engineer shares relevant sales materials with the customer
  - Pamphlet — link pending
  - Start Guide — link pending
- Sales Engineer has Exploratory Meeting — A Sales Engineer conducts an exploratory meeting and Home Assistant product demo with the customer
- Sales Engineer Closes the Sale — Move to step #1 below and kick off the onboarding process

---

### Step 1. Sales Team kicks-off Onboarding and Requests Required Information

Sales team closes on the product sale:

- Sales Engineer explains the Onboarding process and collects the required information.
- Determine if the customer will start after Phase 1 or Phase 2.
- Send Customer their custom Brevy link/onboarding checklist to collect the required data before the onboarding appointment (this will be sent in the sales channel in Slack for the sale).
- Sales Engineer schedules Onboarding Call with customer

## ONBOARDING PHASE 1

### Step 2. Customer Success Team Starts Phase 1 Onboarding Configuration

- Customer Success checks if the deal was transferred to the Onboard Pipeline.
  - Once the deal is in the onboard pipeline, move the deal to the "Waiting for Information" Stage.
  - If the deal is not in the onboard pipeline, escalate to the Sales Team via slack channel #failed\_onboards and ask that the deal owners transfer the deal to the onboard pipeline.
- Customer Success monitors the team inbox in Zendesk for the information requested.
- Customer Success should be reaching out at least every other day with a phone call for unresponsive customers and follow up with an email.
- After 3 follow-ups (5 days), if the information was not received, the Customer Success Team escalates to the Sales Team via slack channel #failed\_onboards and move the deal in Hubspot to the (Unresponsive - Need Sales Help) stage. Customer Success will use the following templates to follow-up on missing information:
  - If the Property Manager has a software (that is NOT Appfolio) that houses all required information: Use this email template to request the information below.
  - If the Property Manager has Appfolio or DOES NOT have a software or database with the required information, we will ask them to either send their own tracker or files or use our template or send us extracts of Appfolio.
- If we don't receive all of the information required by the onboarding call, we still have the call, but we will include the Sales Team to review the information missing and answer any questions. On the call, we will agree on new deadlines and re-schedule the onboarding call with the customer right away (during the call).
- If information is received, the team starts entering information into the system:
  - Company information: How to: Configure Customer Profile Information (Details Page in Latchel Admin)
  - Property, Resident and Owner Information: How to: Add Properties in the Latchel Console to a New Account
  - Service Provider Information: How to: Configure Preferred Service Providers to a New Account

### Step 3. Customer Success Leads Onboarding Call (Phase 1)

**Prior to the call:**

Customer Success uses the onboarding Check-list (Job-Aid: Customer Onboarding Process & Checklist) to verify what information is missing and will need to be collected during the onboarding call.

Use the Onboarding Call Note Taking Template to take notes of the information you have and highlight the information you don't have and need to ask the customer during the onboarding call.

<Warning>
  ATTENTION — the Customer Success Manager owning this call needs to review all information received so far and ONLY ask questions that we don't have the answer for it. We have received feedback that we ask repetitive questions or questions that they have already provided an answer.
</Warning>

Based on the information you have so far, review whether or not the customer has the following situations. If they do not, you can skip the relevant sections of the onboarding call template. If they do, or you are not sure, check on the information you have available and what you will need to clarify or request during the onboarding call:

- Confirm the onboarding questionnaire was filled and you are clear on the information provided
- Review their response to the "Emergency definitions" questionnaire
- Check if they will have multiple users who will need to access Latchel from the admin portal or be contacted in case of an emergency escalation
- If the customer has Appfolio, we will need to work with the customer on downloading, installing and running the Latchel plugin — How to: Install the Chrome Plug-in (Appfolio)
- Check if they have multiple Portfolios and if you have all information needed to configure the portfolios
  - Were you able to get property information according to how they organize their portfolio?
  - (PREMIUM ONLY) — if they are organizing their properties into a Portfolio based on the property owner, during the call, check if they want to set-up owner communication
  - Were you able to find out which rules are applicable to each portfolio?
  - Were you able to identify the properties that have home warranties or other special considerations that will only go live on phase 2?
- Special Configurations at Portfolio or Company level:
  - Specific pre-dispatch instructions
  - (PREMIUM ONLY) — Permission to enter and default access instructions
  - If the Property Manager wishes to set-up different escalation contacts for certain portfolios, we will need to know who those are and their contact information
  - Home Warranties configurations at the portfolio level (property level configurations will happen on phase 2)
- Special Service Provider Situations (Company or Portfolio level):
  - On-site/on-call property managers who you want to handle emergencies BEFORE contacting a maintenance technician/vendor/owner
  - Internal maintenance technicians
  - Owners who want to have the option of doing work themselves.
- Verify meeting check-list for specific requirements for Home Assistant — Step 11 of Job-Aid: Customer Onboarding Process & Checklist

**During the call:**

Sales Team will lead the beginning of the call and pass on to the Customer Success Manager to lead the rest of the call.

See call expectations and agenda on this SOP ((CS/Admin) Job-Aid: Customer Onboarding Information Checklist) in the "Phase 1 - Executing the Onboarding Call" section.

Note: (HOME ASSISTANT ONLY) Sales team is required to be on the phase 1 onboarding call for Home Assistant onboardings.

Customer Success team takes notes using Onboarding Call Note Taking Template as a check-list during the call to make sure you collect all of the information you identified as missing while you prepared for the call.

**At the end of the call:**

- If the Property Manager decided to have a phase 2 onboarding, set the expectations on what will happen for phase 2.
- (HOME ASSISTANT ONLY) At this time, set a go-live date with the customer.

<Note>
  Service start should always happen on the 1st of a month. We need at minimum 14 days to collect initial opt-outs.
</Note>

- If the customer will go-live after phase 2, schedule phase 2 onboarding 2-3 days prior to the go-live date.
- Instruct the customer to send their resident offer letter BEFORE their official go-live date.
- Let the customer know that their properties will all be opted-in at the start of the service unless they request to opt properties out.

**After the call:**

- The Customer Success team reviews the information collected during the call vs. what is missing, and summarize what information is still needed.
- (HOME ASSISTANT ONLY) If the customer decides on a different go-live date, the Customer Success team prepares/updates the resident offer letter.
- The tech team will need to bulk opt-in all residents to the resident benefit package BEFORE the go-live date. Slack tech the target date, a link to the Property Manager's Latchel profile page, and the request to mass opt-in all residents. Utilize the #tech-escalations channel for this request.
- (HOME ASSISTANT ONLY) The Customer Success team preps the Property Manager's Home Assistant assets:
  - Lease Addendum
  - Resident Offer Letter
  - Resident Flyer
  - Agreement
  - See: (CS/Admin) How to: Prep Home Assistant Materials for the Customer
- The Customer Success team sends an email to Property Manager with action items pending for phase 1 and an Onboarding FAQ.
- Customer Success monitors inbox for missing information and sends follow-ups emails until we have the minimum information required to start service.
- Customer Success should be reaching out at least every other day with a phone call for unresponsive customers and follow up with an email.
- After 3 follow-ups (5 days), if meeting was not scheduled, Customer Success Team escalates to the Sales Team via slack channel #failled\_onboards.

### Step 4. Customer Success Finalizes All Phase 1 Account Configurations

After all information is received and configuration is complete, a Customer Success Manager, preferably someone that has not worked on this account, should audit all applicable configurations using the Go Live Check-list.

Note: make a copy of the Go Live Check-List Template and save that in the Property Manager account folder — How to: Handle Property Manager Specific Files

Use this SOP to guide you through executing the audit: (Draft) Job-Aid: How to Execute the "Go Live" Check-list

If the customer decided to go Live after phase 1, proceed with the next steps:

- Customer Success sends a "Welcome" email.
  - If the Property Manager subscribed to the 24/7 or After Hours Service, the email will contain:
    - Latchel Phone Number
    - Resident and Service Providers Communication Templates
    - Communication on configurations made to their account
  - If the Property Manager subscribed to Premium or 24/7 Home Assistant service, the email will contain:
    - Latchel Phone Number
    - Request to schedule a kick-off meeting with the Premium Customer Success team, including the link to schedule the meeting.
- If the customer does NOT have additional configurations (no phase 2): Customer Success sends a "Welcome" email and proceeds to "Finalize Onboarding"
- If the customer has other configurations to be completed during phase 2, proceed to "Phase 2 Onboarding"

## ONBOARDING PHASE 2

### Step 5. Customer Success leads Phase 2 Onboarding

**Prior to the call:**

If there is a phase 2 onboarding, move the deal back "Waiting for Information" Stage. We will send the customer an email outlining actions that need to be taken prior to the second onboarding call. This email should include:

- Special Property Configurations (list of properties and their specific rules):
  - Specific pre-dispatch instructions for certain properties
  - Permission to enter and default access instructions for certain properties (PREMIUM ONLY)
  - Owner communication for certain properties (PREMIUM ONLY)
  - Different escalation contacts for certain properties
  - Home Warranties configurations at the property level
- Special Service Provider configurations (Property level configurations):
  - Prioritization changes, skills changes/removal, property-specific assignments
  - Additional service provider information: license, insurance
  - Owners who perform work on their own units
  - On-site/on-call property managers who handle emergencies BEFORE contacting a maintenance technician/vendor/owner
  - Internal maintenance technicians
  - Vendor lock-ins (PREMIUM ONLY)

Prepare for the call: Review all information received and define what needs to be collected during the call. Use Onboarding Call Note Taking Template.

**During the call:**

Customer Success will lead the call. See call expectations and agenda on this SOP in the "Phase 2 - Executing the Onboarding Call" section.

(HOME ASSISTANT ONLY) If the customer is adding new properties, define the official go-live date for phase 2 properties and update the resident offer letter with the phase 2 go-live date.

The tech team will need to bulk opt-in all new residents to the resident benefit package BEFORE the go-live date. Utilize the #tech-escalations channel for this request.

**After the call:**

- Customer Success team reviews the information collected during the call vs. what is missing, and summarize what information is still needed.
- The Customer Success Manager will send a follow-up email with any pending action items or information missing from the customer.
- Once all configurations are completed (phase 1 and phase 2), a Customer Success Manager should audit all applicable configurations using the Go Live Check-list.
- If the Customer account has gone live after phase 1 and had additional configurations on phase 2, send an email to confirm those configurations were completed and the account is 100% onboarded.
- If the Customer account has NOT gone live after phase 1, then proceed with the same Welcome Process as Phase 1 (step 4).

Proceed to "Finalize Onboarding"

## COMPLETE ONBOARDING

### Step 6. Finalize Onboarding

Customer Success Manager should go to Hubspot and move the deal to "Onboard Complete" in the onboarding pipeline.

When this happens, we send an "Onboarding Satisfaction" survey using this email template: [https://app.hubspot.com/templates/6245849/edit/8581515?page=2](https://app.hubspot.com/templates/6245849/edit/8581515?page=2)

### Step 7. Ops Support Post-On-boarding

After the account goes live, the Ops team will provide support as requested.

Around the 15 days mark that the account has been live, the ops team will make an analysis of the account to look for opportunities to educate the Property Manager on how they can better use our product. (This is a new process and we need to create it.)

The Ops team will send a summary of their assessment and recommendations to the Property Manager including an offer to schedule a meeting with us to discuss that information. (This is a new process and we need to create it.)
