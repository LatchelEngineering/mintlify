## **Replying to customers in Salesforce**

1. You should reply to all customer emails in Salesforce from your personal email address. Further customer replies in the email chain will continue to be archived in Gmail but will also come into Salesforce. 

<Warning>
  When emailing a customer following an escalation from Service Ops, make sure to select “New Blank Email” when you reply. This way the customer won’t see the escalation from Ops at the bottom of your email.
</Warning>

## **Outbound and follow-up emails in Salesforce**

1. When following up with a customer (ex: you send them a post-meeting recap), email them from the Account page from your personal email address. Once they respond, a case is created for you as the CSM.

<Note>
  Anytime you respond to a case, it goes into “Waiting for customer” status. If the customer doesn’t respond within a few days, it will go back into “Open” status for you to follow up.
</Note>

2. If you’ve made multiple follow ups and the PM still hasn’t responded, move the case to “Closed Unresponsive” status. If the PM does respond later, the case will automatically be reopened.

<Warning>
  You must send all emails from your personal email address for them to appear in the [<u>CSM Activity Dashboard</u>](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/Dashboard/01Z5e000000eWwdEAE/view "https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/Dashboard/01Z5e000000eWwdEAE/view"). Emails sent from [<u>success@latchel.com</u>](mailto:success@latchel.com) will not appear in the reporting. Be sure to leverage the Product Support Team by having them respond to any [<u>questions about functional problems with the product</u>](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/2614755413 "https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SR/pages/2614755413") so you can focus your time on business outcomes!
</Warning>
