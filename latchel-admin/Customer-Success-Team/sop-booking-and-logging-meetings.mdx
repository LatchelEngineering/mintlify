## **Background**

In this SOP, you will learn how to log meetings with customers in Salesforce so they are reflected in the [<u>CSM Activity Dashboard</u>](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/Dashboard/01Z5e000000eWwdEAE/view "https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/Dashboard/01Z5e000000eWwdEAE/view").

<Note>
  Use this SOP for customer meetings that were conducted via video conference. If your interaction with the customer took place over the phone, please refer to [SOP: Calling & Texting](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/CS/pages/edit-v2/2744352770?draftShareId=2fee6408-76d8-457f-b3dd-e42925d381eb&inEditorTemplatesPanel=auto_closed)
</Note>

## **Kick Off Meeting**

This meeting is booked via Calendly by the Account Executive (AE) after consulting Onboarding and the CSM’s Google calendars. This meeting automatically syncs to Salesforce via the Google Calendar integration. To avoid duplicate records:

1. The CSM should not log a new meeting in Salesforce for the Kick Off meeting. Instead, they should visit the Activity log in the Account page. Example: 
   <Frame>
     ![CSM 33](/images/CSM-33.png)
   </Frame>
    
2. Scroll down the Activity log to locate the Kick Off meeting. Because the meeting is scheduled by the AE with the CSM as the organizer, the meeting will automatically be assigned to you in Salesforce. Example: 
   <Frame>
     ![CSM 34](/images/CSM-34.png)
   </Frame>
3. Open the meeting record and update the following:
   a. Call or Meeting Type field in the Calendar section = **Kickoff**
   b. Disposition field in the Calendar section
   - **Meeting Completed** if the customer attended the meeting
   - **No Answer/No Show** if the customer did not attend the meeting and did not notify you ahead of time that they wouldn’t be available
   - **Meeting Rescheduled** if the customer notified you ahead of time that they’d need to reschedule, or if you needed to reschedule due to illness, scheduling conflict, etc.
   - **Canceled** if the customer notified you ahead of time they couldn’t attend, or you had to cancel for some reason, but you didn’t yet set a new meeting date and time. In this case, also update the Meeting Canceled section fields.

        c. Click Save.

</Frame>

## **Launch Meeting**

This meeting is booked by Onboarding after consulting the CSM’s Google calendar. This meeting automatically syncs to Salesforce via the Google Calendar integration. To avoid duplicate records, please follow the same process as detailed in the [<u>Kick Off Meeting</u>](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/CS/pages/2747334659/SOP+Booking+Logging+Meetings#KickOff "#KickOff") section above, selecting **Launch** for the Call Or Meeting Type field.

<Warning>
  If for some reason you don’t see the Launch meeting in the Account Activity, follow the instructions in the **Meeting does not exist in Salesforce** section below.
</Warning>

## **All Other Meetings**

### **Inviting customers to book with you**

You can invite your customers to book meetings with you via Google appointment schedules. You’ll get a sharable link for customers to book meetings based on the availability you set. Please refer to [<u>this Google Help Article</u>](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/10729749?hl=en "https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/10729749?hl=en") to set up appointment schedules.

<Warning>
  When you set up Google appointment schedules, one of the options is:

  1. Select a location and conference options for the appointment:
     - **Google Meet video conferencing**
     - **In-person meeting**
     - **Phone call**
     - **None / to be specified later**

  In order to use Zoom for your customer meetings, you’ll need to select **None / to be specified later** and then manually add the Zoom video conferencing option to the invite once a customer has booked with you.
</Warning>

### **Logging meetings**

Before manually creating a new meeting in Salesforce, always check the Account Activity to see if one has been created automatically first (either because a colleague booked it or because you have a Google Calendar integration set up).

#### **Meeting already exists in Salesforce**

If you have a Google Calendar integration with Salesforce, all meetings customers book with you should automatically log to Salesforce under the Account Activity. Example:

<Frame>
  ![CSM 36](/images/CSM-36.png)
</Frame>

To finish logging your meeting:

1. Click on the meeting record and update it following the same process as detailed in the [<u>Kick Off Meeting</u>](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/CS/pages/2747334659/SOP+Booking+Logging+Meetings#KickOff "#KickOff") section above, selecting the appropriate Call Or Meeting Type field based on the topic of your meeting.

<Note>
  The Call Or Meeting Type field will soon be updated to include:

  - Kick-Off
  - Launch Call
  - Training 1
  - Training 2
  - Training 3 
  - Training Extra
  - Executive Business Review
  - Quick Chat
  - Other

  In the meantime, please select the most appropriate option you have available.
</Note>

#### **Meeting does not exist in Salesforce**

<Danger>
  **Known issue:** Currently the only way to manually log a new meeting in Salesforce is to do it from the Customer page. The limitation is that the meeting appears on the Customer Activity, but not on the Account Activity. This is an issue because the Account should be the source of truth.
</Danger>

1. See a video overview of how to manually log a meeting in Salesforce [<u>here</u>](https://www.loom.com/share/b4fdf67df81d467b9c52802ff5e24fe7 "https://www.loom.com/share/b4fdf67df81d467b9c52802ff5e24fe7").

## **Meeting notes from Fathom**

1. You should have Fathom Notetaker set up to join all of your external meetings. If you don’t, visit [<u>https://fathom.video/customize</u>](https://fathom.video/customize) to activate this feature.
2. After your call, generate the AI Summary of the meeting.
3. The AI Summary of the meeting will be automatically sent to the Notes & Attachments box in the Account page. Here is a [<u>Loom overview</u>](https://www.loom.com/share/3a10a31ce1b64aef9959bdbfab33b2ea "https://www.loom.com/share/3a10a31ce1b64aef9959bdbfab33b2ea") of where meeting notes can be found.
