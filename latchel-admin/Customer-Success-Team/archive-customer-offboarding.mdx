On the sad occasion that we lose a valued customer. Maybe the client wasn't a long-term fit for the services provided, or in some cases, the service provided was not what the client expected. Whatever the reason, Client Success Managers must have a plan of action to manage churn.

With the right offboarding strategy, we can ensure the transition off of the Latchel platform is smooth and Latchel's former clients can receive all the necessary information to continue running their business effectively. Best-case scenario, they decide to downgrade or stay on their current plan. Below are five steps to structure the offboarding process for each scenario.

---

## Instructions

### 1. Ask for feedback.

If the client emails in, send the "Cancel" request macro in Zen Desk. The purpose of this email is to request a quick offboarding call to answer any final questions and discuss overall sentiment toward Latchel's services. The goal is to openly elicit feedback on what was done right and what could have been done better to advance our efforts.

If the client calls in, request the reason for the cancellation and help answer any final questions. The purpose of the call is to discuss the overall sentiment toward Latchel's services. The goal is to openly elicit feedback on what was done right and what could have been done better to advance our efforts.

Set a meeting to go over their reasons for not wishing to continue, fill out this form when speaking with them: [https://forms.gle/8pfkhpMdTs8VPrc77](https://forms.gle/8pfkhpMdTs8VPrc77)

---

### 2. Close the loop on services owed and rendered.

Refer back to the client's subscription and determine what's owed to Latchel or the client. Latchel clients are billed behind one month. Once determined, clearly communicate any remaining services or amounts owed to conclude the partnership.

- Run a payment for amount owed within Stripe or Pay Simple — How to run a payment in stripe - Pending link
- Send final receipt to the client
- Disable/end the active subscription on the Company account within Stage

---

### 3. Disable the Latchel Account & Deactivate the Assigned Twilio Number

![How to disable the Latchel account](/images/CSM-111.png)

How to release a Twilio number ([https://www.twilio.com/console/voice/dashboard](https://www.twilio.com/console/voice/dashboard))

If the client determines they want the number for their own use, they are able to notify our team within 30 days of us releasing the number.

---

### 4. Offer a status update on all work orders underway.

Latchel clients have access to their accounts even after disabling the company account as long as their user account is still active. Remind the client they will have access to their data but also offer to run a report of all work orders resolved over the course of the partnership as well as all pending work orders that are needing completion.

- Remove their CC Form from the platform
- Remove their CC Form from the platform, and Google Drive
- (Premium Only) Notify the #Operations channel that the account has been closed

---

### 5. Final Notes

Customers leaving is an opportunity to understand what our struggle as a company might be. Sometimes we have 0 control and the customer simply isn't happy and no amount of rationale, discounts, or adjustments will win them back. By filling out the form in #1 we'll get closer to being the success we know we can be.
