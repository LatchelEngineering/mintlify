## Objective

### Purpose

Describe the information we need to collect from a new client in order to complete onboarding and configure their account.

This SOP will also be used as a guide to conducting the onboarding meeting and will also provide links to SOPs and templates with more details on customer data collection and configuration.

### What is happening prior to this process?

Before this process, the customer has agreed to start service with Latchel. The Sales Engineer or Sales Development Rep have collected:

- Payment (How to: Enter Property Management Payment Information)
- Login information/new account created for [onboarding@latchel.com](mailto:onboarding@latchel.com) to access the property manager's software.
- If login information was NOT shared, then an export of properties, residents, and vendors.

The Sales Engineer has moved the sales deal in Hubspot to the onboard scheduled status in the onboard pipeline.

The Sales Engineer or Sales Development Rep has given a brief overview of the onboarding process and aligned with the customer if the account will go live after phase 1 or 2. Refer to this page to understand the overall onboarding process flow → (Draft) Job-Aid: Onboarding Flow & Communications

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Introduction to Onboarding Phases

#### Section 1: Onboarding Phases - Information and Configuration done on each Phase

Check the (Draft) Job-Aid: Onboarding Flow & Communications to have a better understanding on how the onboarding process flow works.

The table below summarizes which information and configuration is included in each phase.

**Phase 1: Standard Package**

| # | Information and Configurations included in Phase 1 - Company and Portfolio level configurations, simplified Service Provider configurations |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Add all users (unlimited) |
| 2 | Company level configuration focused on key "must have" information (contact information, address, invoicing preferences) - (these items are asked in the initial questionnaire) |
| 3 | Initial Property and Resident Data Upload |
| 4 | Upload properties organized by portfolio, but configure rules for up to 5 Portfolios and limit configurations to what is applicable to the whole portfolio |
| 5 | Add all Preferred Service Providers (unlimited) |
| 6 | Customized Emergency Definitions (Must fill out this form) |
| 7 | Miscellaneous Settings (preference on these items are asked in the initial questionnaire) |
| 8 | Home Assistant Plans Only: Resident Offer Letter, Lease Addendum |

**Portfolio Rules:**

We will ask Property Managers how they group their properties, for example: by owner, Property/Portfolio Manager, escalation contacts, geographic region, vendor groups, etc. Once we have a rule selected, we will ask them how we can get a property list based on that rule.

We will make Portfolio level configurations, for example:

- Portfolio with one owner and requiring owner communication will be set-up on phase 1
- Portfolio with one service provider list that requires prioritization will be set-up on phase 1, if property manager requests

**"Blanket Portfolios:"**

All work orders for properties under a "blanket portfolio" will be forwarded to the Property Manager for approval during phase 1. In phase 2, as we add more details for those properties, we will move them to a better fit portfolio or configure property-specific notes and remove the property from a portfolio altogether.

Types:

- "Blanket Home Warranty" Portfolio - We will group properties with home warranties under one portfolio and further configure property specific home warranties in phase 2
- "Blanket Owner Approval Required Portfolio" - We will create this portfolio when we have properties owned by different owners and the Property Manager requested to set-up owner approval for those owners.
- "Blanket Section 8 portfolio" - This is for Home Assistant customers. We will create this portfolio and opt-out all section 8 properties in the portfolio.

If there is a group of properties that the Property Manager wants to have further configurations before going live, we will group those properties into a separate portfolio with pre-dispatch instructions to not dispatch for those (DO NOT DISPATCH, FORWARD TO PM).

Portfolio with multiple owners and required property-specific information will be configured in phase 2.

**Phase 2: Additional Configurations & Customizations**

| # | Information and Configurations included in Phase 2 - Property Level configurations and/or additional portfolios, more granular Service Provider configurations |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Additional Portfolios and/or additional Portfolios Configurations |
| 2 | Owner Communication at the property level |
| 3 | Property Specific Rules (Pre-dispatch instructions, default access instructions, escalation contacts, etc.) |
| 4 | Property Specific Home Warranty Instructions/ Escalations |
| 5 | Preferred Service Provider customizations and additional information (license, insurance, Owners who perform work on their own units) |
| 6 | Multiple Accounts |

### Phase 1: Onboarding Call Instructions

#### Phase 1 - Align on a Plan of Attack and Prepare for the Call

**SALES TEAM**

Prior to the onboarding call, the sales team should have completed all of the items on Step 1 of this SOP: (Draft) Job-Aid: Onboarding Flow & Communications

Sales team should be prepared to lead the beginning of the call as described in the "Executing the Onboarding Call" section below.

Note: If the sales team is unable to attend the call, or there was an agreement with Customer Success that the presence of the sales team is not required for that customer onboarding call in specific, Sales team needs to communicate to the customer ahead of the onboarding meeting that they won't be present and set the expectation for the meeting, including introducing the Customer Success Manager they will be working with. The sales team will call the customer and follow-up with an email including the Customer Service Manager that will be leading the onboarding call.

**CUSTOMER SUCCESS TEAM**

Prior to the onboarding call, Customer Success team should have completed all of the items on Step 2 of this SOP: (Draft) Job-Aid: Onboarding Flow & Communications

Customer Success should PREPARE for the onboarding call as described in Step 3 of this SOP: (Draft) Job-Aid: Onboarding Flow & Communication

Use this template as a "note-taking" tool and highlight the information that needs to be collected during the call: [https://docs.google.com/document/d/10v9Zkyts3jOTOJD4S7wGEPTCIOj88KyhAPjOWT3\_plI/edit](https://docs.google.com/document/d/10v9Zkyts3jOTOJD4S7wGEPTCIOj88KyhAPjOWT3_plI/edit)

  ATTENTION - If there is a need to make adjustments to the plan for a certain customer, Sales team and Customer Success Team should discuss and align on those changes prior to the call

#### Phase 1 - Executing the Onboarding Call

Sales Team will introduce the call and set the agenda based on the information below:

🔴 Record the meeting! Let the customer know you will do that

**Agenda:**

- Introduce the CS manager on the call with you. Explain their role in Latchel and during this call
- Check on subscription questions
- Ask the customer about specific questions they may have about the service or subscription level. Write those down and address each one before moving on with the rest of on-boarding
- Any opportunity to up-sell? Based on their questions and needs, check if this subscription plan is the best for them
- Answer any questions about our product and onboarding process
- Remind them of the phased onboarding process and what was aligned during the prior conversation with them (Go live after phase 1 or phase 2)
- If they have an integration, make sure to ask if they wanted all properties in their system synced. If not CS will need to go over what properties should and should not be in system. (Appfolio and Rent Manager)
- Pass the call over the Customer Success Manager to go over the open questions or information missing to finalize configuring account according to phase 1 package
- Once the expectations for the rest of the call is communicated, stay on or hop off the call

Customer Success Team will take over the call and proceed to go over the open questions or information missing to finalize configuring account according to phase 1 package.

If the customer will have a phase 2 onboarding, Customer Success will provide an introduction of the next batch of information that will be required from them prior to the second onboarding call. Go over the phase 2 information listed in the "section 1: Onboarding Phases" above. Let them know that you will send all of this information in an email to them once phase 1 is completed.

#### Phase 2 - Executing the Onboarding Call

Customer Success Manager will introduce the call and set the agenda based off the information below:

🔴 Record the meeting! Let the customer know you will do that

**Agenda:**

- Anyone new? - If there is someone new in the call that have not met before, start with introductions
- Celebrate completion of phase 1!
- If the customer has gone live after phase 1, collect feedback so far and answer any questions
- Recap what happened in phase 1 and the plan for phase 2
- Missing information and configurations - Go over the information you haven't received, ask questions and take notes
- Avoid making real time configurations so you are not making the call longer and creating awkward silence, which can be perceived as a waste of time on the customer side

## List of all Information Required for Onboarding

### Step 1: Confirm Users

**Why do we need this?**

We need to grant access to users who will be notified of work orders or otherwise want access to the data within Latchel.

ALL INCLUDED ON PHASE 1

Get the complete list of expected users:

- User name
- User email address (must be unique)
- User phone number (optional. Required if they want to be contacted via phone for emergency escalations.)

Let the customer know they can always add more users in the future via self-service tools in Latchel.
