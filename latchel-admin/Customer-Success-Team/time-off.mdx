title: "Time Off!"
---

### **Goal**

Take a _real_ break!  Time off is intended to be time you can truly step away from work obligations. So you can confidently leave work behind, and feel assured that your customers will be taken care of, we’re aiming to have at least 2 members of this team available for customer escalations each working day (i.e., excluding holidays and weekends). 

### **Before you take off….**

Next time you schedule some much deserved time off...

 If you are taking 2\+ days away from work, please take the following steps.  (Please note that this applies if you’re taking any 2 consecutive, non-weekend days away from work. For instance, if you’ll be OOO Juneteenth as well as the Friday before, from a customer’s perspective you’re OOO 2 consecutive non-weekend days so the policy applies. Exceptions include Thanksgiving, and the Friday following Thanksgiving and Christmas (observed) and Christmas Eve.)

1. **Update the team**. Update the [<u>Customer Success Time Off calendar</u>](https://calendar.google.com/calendar/u/0?cid=Y181cGdhcmtraDhuc28za3RpYXBmOHNuazRwOEBncm91cC5jYWxlbmRhci5nb29nbGUuY29t "https://calendar.google.com/calendar/u/0?cid=Y181cGdhcmtraDhuc28za3RpYXBmOHNuazRwOEBncm91cC5jYWxlbmRhci5nb29nbGUuY29t") at least 1 week in advance. 

2. **Spread the word**. Let customers you work with regularly know that you’ll be OOO and unavailable. Share the dates and let them know that [<u>success@latchel.com</u>](mailto:success@latchel.com) will be covering any time sensitive / critical issues that come up in your absence.

3. **Pick a buddy.**  At least 2 days before you’re out, post in #csm-team-chat your buddy– who’s covering customer escalations while you’re out. Inform Product Support in the #salesforce-case-support channel so they can assign cases/tag escalations to your buddy while you’re away. 

<Note>
  Note: If you have 1 or 2 very active customers (e.g., they’re early in training or you’re actively working through escalations), find another teammate to be dedicated to covering these hot ones.
</Note>

4. **Close out your Cases or Update your Buddy.**

-  Make sure that no more than 1 or 2 New Cases is / are in your queue. 
-  If you have any Cases that are Open and not in “Waiting for Customer” or “Not Actionable”, make sure you’ve added in your “buddy post” a doc with a sentence or 2 about why these cases are Open.  

5. **Email auto-reply and Slack status**.

- Setup an email auto-reply letting folks who email you (a) when you’ll be returning to work and (b) to contact [<u>success@latchel.com</u>](mailto:success@latchel.com) if something requires immediate attention while you’re out.  Include your meeting booking link in the OOO.
- Update your Slack status to “OOO until DATE” and put a vacation icon up so internal folks are reminded that you are OOO.
