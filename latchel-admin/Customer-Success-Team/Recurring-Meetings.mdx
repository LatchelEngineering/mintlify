We have all heard the saying “this meeting could have been an email.” Meetings are vital tools for sharing information, solving complex issues and staying aligned, but can also be distracting if there is no clear purpose, we have the wrong audience, or there is no call to action. Due to this, we should strive to ensure all of our meetings follow the AAAA model:

1. **Agenda** - Does the meeting have a set agenda and desired outcome? Does everyone going in know the purpose of the meeting beforehand?
   - As a best practice, an agenda should always be shared in advance of any recurring or ad hoc meeting, and if possible linked directly to the meeting invite.
2. **Attendees** - Is this the right set of people necessary to reach the desired outcome? Think about the time-value of the meeting and opportunity cost of everyone attending (our time is a valuable resource).
   - If someone can be informed of the outcome of the meeting, they don't need to be an attendee. If someone is important to making the right decision (or providing information to make the right decision) they should probably be at the meeting.
3. **Attention** - When you're in a meeting, make the most out of being there by giving it your **full** attention. We recommend the following 3 actions for all meetings. If you find yourself having difficulty with the following actions, you should question if the meeting follows the criteria listed in steps 1 and 2, and if you aren’t sure, talk to your manager.
   - All of us know that the red bubble in Slack can set us off into anxiety mode. You should feel empowered to _quit Slack_ during any meeting. I also recommend linking your calendar to slack so it’s clear to anyone reaching out to you that you are in a meeting.
   - Have your camera on so the speaker can read your expression. Non-verbal feedback is just as important as verbal feedback.
   - Be engaged with the speaker and ask questions. One of the main goals of any meeting is to get immediate feedback and share ideas which can only happen if everyone is engaged.
4. **Actions** - This is likely our single biggest opportunity to improve meetings: document and publish (in a shared Slack channel) the actions to be taken and the key decisions made in the meeting.
   - Sharing is caring! So share that knowledge and help people be accountable.
   - Pro tip: use Slack's Remind me Later function to keep on top of tasks and remind yourself to follow-up on things.

### **1:1s**

The objective of weekly 1:1s is to ensure we are celebrating wins, identifying challenges, and for your manager to better understand how to support you through the challenges. We will also touch on short term goals weekly and long term goals monthly.

Current Agenda:

- Personal Development (To be filled out by the CSM prior to the meeting)
  - What do you feel positive about over the last week?
    -  
  - Review top 1-2 challenges from last week
    -  
  - What are your top 1-2 current challenges?
    -  
  - What can I do to support you better here?
    -  
  - What are 1-2 short term goals you can achieve in the next 2-4 weeks?
    -  
- Manager (any topics your manager would like to surface that are specific to the CSM)
  - Example: Review upcoming save call gameplan for client that is requesting to cancel
- CSM (any topics the CSM wants to discuss)
  - Example: How to handle common LVN issues surfaced by clients
- Action Items (quick summary of agreed upon actions)
  - Manager - put together a quick training for the team on how to handle common LVN disputes by agreed up date
  - CSM - Post a summary of the save call in [<u>#service-changes</u>](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/CKA2W1KJA "https://latchel.slack.com/archives/CKA2W1KJA") by agreed upon date

### \*\*Onboarding Pipeline Review \*\*

The primary objective of the **Onboarding Pipeline Review** is to foster alignment between departments involved in the onboarding process. The meeting aims to:

- Provide a brief overview of each account, with anticipated Launch Call dates.
- Identify and address areas of misalignment or bottlenecks.
- Evaluate and optimize internal processes for onboarding.
- Provide a consistent onboarding experience for new clients, improve client satisfaction and reduce churn, especially in the first 90 days.

We will use the [<u>LAUNCH: Onboarding Pipeline By Stage</u>](https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/Report/00O5e000008F6toEAC/view "https://latchel1.lightning.force.com/lightning/r/Report/00O5e000008F6toEAC/view") report as a guide for the meeting, starting from the bottom (accounts that will be launching soon) and working our way back.
