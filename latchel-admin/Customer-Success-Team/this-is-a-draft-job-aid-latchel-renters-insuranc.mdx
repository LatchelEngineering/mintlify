# **Introduction**

[<u>Get Covered</u>](https://getcovered.io/products/#master-liability-program "https://getcovered.io/products/#master-liability-program") is partnering with Latchel to ensure residents are insured. For residents with insurance, they even provide 3rd party verification and tracking throughout the lease term, alleviating extra work for our Property Managers.

### **FEE STRUCTURE**

The management company is named insured, while residents are charged the cost of the coverage/fee with their rent each month.

The resident charge will be added to the tenant ledger as a separate charge code, specifically for GC insurance.

### **REVENUE OPPORTUNITIES**

Ancillary revenue opportunities are available through administrative fees (at the owner/manager’s discretion)

### **Step 1. Resident moves in and at signing the lease says they are required to sign up for insurance.**

### **Step 2. The resident is auto-enrolled into the Latchel Master Insurance Policy.**

### **Step 3. The insurance charge code is bulk added to every tenant’s ledger.**

### **Step 4. For residents that purchase HO4 or through a 3rd party, they must add GC as the interested party to the insurance policy.**

1. That mechanism of adding “Get Covered” as an interested party is how “Get Covered” knows the resident has an active insurance policy, which is required per the lease agreement.
2. For residents that have selected 1 of these 2 options, the charge code will be **removed** from their ledger.

### **Step 5. Monthly, Latchel tracks which residents paid and did not pay.**

1. Latchel knows who is paying by analyzing the Property Manager’s General Ledger Report
   - Latchel delivers a list of units and names of the residents who have paid to GC by uploading the data into their platform (@Will Gordon to confirm)

### **Step 6. Monthly, Get Covered will notify Latchel of ANY lapse in coverage for residents that have selected the third-party insurance option**

1. In other words, if the resident stops paying or is no longer covered, “Get Covered” will be notified, as they are an interested party.
2. If Get Covered tells Latchel that a tenant is not paying/has a lapse in coverage, Latchel will assist the PM to begin billing the resident for the master policy (if the resident's other policy lapses) by adding the insurance charge code to their rent moving forward.
   - If we fail this step on our end, we **must** inform the Property Management company and have them add that charge code on their end.

### **FAQ:**

1. **How are we adding it with the assisted ledgering moving forward?**
   - It will have its own ledger code (insurance)
2. **How is Latchel managing this for them? (Might be with the first question)**
   - See steps 5 & 6 above.
   - _Note: GC bills in arrears. At the end of the month, we need to know who paid that month and then the resident is covered for the following month._
3. **If a resident does not pay, who is responsible? Latchel, PM, or late fee to the resident?**
   - The Property Manager needs to make a choice,
     - Does nobody pay for this and risk the property going uninsured? Or…
     - Does the PM pay and charge back the resident with a late fee (violation of lease)?
