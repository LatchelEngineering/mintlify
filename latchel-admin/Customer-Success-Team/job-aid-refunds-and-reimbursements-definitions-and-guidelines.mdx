**<u>Refunds</u>**

These are refunds (pay back) to a property manager, vendor, or resident, due to charges related to:

- Work order issues, want a refund
- Work order cancellations
- Work order charged to resident instead of property manager, or the other way around

<Note>
  **Ops Associates -** Go [**<u>here</u>**](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/CS/pages/2527625217 "https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/CS/pages/2527625217") to learn how to submit a refund request to the Finance team.

  If the customer contacted us asking for an update on an existing request, please follow the same process.
</Note>

**<u>Reimbursements</u>**

These are reimbursements related to the \$1,000/annual home amenity benefit. There are a few eligibility requirements that Finance will verify after the resident submits the request.  The request form will walk the resident through what information they need to provide.

If the resident asks more questions about the program, you can refer them to this link: [Residents | Latchel](https://latchel.com/residents/) . If that doesn’t answer their questions, please create a ticket for Customer Success.

<Note>
  **Ops Associates:**  If a resident is requesting a reimbursement, provide the resident with [**<u>this form</u>**](https://latchel.typeform.com/to/WoB5vi53 "https://latchel.typeform.com/to/WoB5vi53"). The resident must fill this form out to get this process started.

**<u>For FINANCE ONLY:</u> Reimbursement Guidelines:**

Take the following steps when **APPROVING** a reimbursement request:

- Confirm the resident is eligible for this reimbursement (it is part of their amenities package).  To complete this step.
  1. In the Latchel app, enter the resident’s address in the Properties search box

<Frame>
  ![CSM 61](/images/CSM-61.png)
</Frame>

2. Confirm that one of the resident names associated with the address approximately matches the

name provided in the reimbursement request form.

- Confirm the reimbursement request meets the below guidelines.
- If all criteria are satisfied, issue the reimbursement via Ramp.

To **DENY** a reimbursement request:

- Use the [<u>Resident Reimbursement Denial workflow</u>](https://slack.com/shortcuts/Ft06ERDUCH4Z/b7928bd75267e29b68bd2bc2150db42b "https://slack.com/shortcuts/Ft06ERDUCH4Z/b7928bd75267e29b68bd2bc2150db42b") in #cs-escalations, **_making sure to TAG @cs-team in thread_**
- Schedule the rejection email to be sent 24 hours **_after_** posting to #cs-escalations.

 

**Finance’s Benefits Reimbursement Guidelines**

1. You must be an active resident who has signed a lease at the property you are requesting reimbursement for.
2. We reimburse a maximum of \$1,000 per calendar year, up to \$250 per incident.
3. We reimburse up to two incidents per quarter (e.g. January - March).
4. Accidental lockouts are capped at once per year and \$50 per reimbursement.
5. For any repairs, you must submit before and after photos.
6. Repair work must already be completed at the time of reimbursement.
7. You must provide an itemized receipt and contact information for the qualified professional who performed the work.
8. You must be current on your rent and any charges due to your property manager or landlord.
9. Your lease must have an active and current Home Assistant subscription.
