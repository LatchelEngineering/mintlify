## <strong>Purpose</strong>

This document outlines a clear workflow for efficiently managing past-due balances, ensuring overdue accounts are addressed in a structured and timely manner. It also includes the generic templates sent to property managers' billing points of contact (and, if applicable, their Ultimate Decision Maker), tailored to the age and number of overdue invoices.

## <strong>Target Audience</strong>

This document is intended for the Accounts Receivable team to manage customer account statuses based on account balances and to standardize the process and communication for accounts that are past due. It also serves as a resource for the Product Support and Customer Success teams to understand account statuses and provide accurate, informed support.

## <strong>Past Due Vocabulary</strong>

1. <strong>Past Due </strong>

- This pertains to a late or unpaid invoice
- Synonymous with overdue and open invoice

2. <strong>AR Aging Report</strong>

- This is used to identify customers with open invoices, track how long those invoices have been outstanding, and review the current status of each account by categorizing them into age from current, 1-30 days, 31-60 days, and 90\+ days old.

<strong>How To Generate the AR Aging Report</strong>

Step 1:  Go to Rillet and navigate to Reporting \> AR Aging.

Step 2: Export the report by verifying that the date range is current. Once confirmed, proceed to download the report as a CSV file.

1. <strong>Aging and Payment Checker</strong>

- This tracker is created monthly to monitor past due invoices and review the overall status of each account. It consolidates key details such as outstanding balances, autopay settings, payment terms, and other relevant internal factors to support accurate follow-ups and account management.

2. <strong>Automated Payment Reminders </strong>

- Each Rillet customer profile includes a toggle that controls whether automated past-due payment reminders are sent.
- For customers not enrolled in autopay, this toggle should be turned on. For customers on autopay, the toggle should be turned off to prevent unnecessary reminders.
- Rillet is currently configured to send two automated reminders for open invoices:
  1. First reminder: Sent on the invoice due date
  2. Second reminder: Sent 7 days after the first reminder

3. <strong>Expected Failed </strong>

- This is a list of customers who are on autopay with a bank account but have missing Stripe mandate IDs.
- Manually processing failed payments for these accounts is an internal AR action done after the invoices are due, and autopay fails; it doesn’t require customer involvement.

1. <strong>Unexpected Failed </strong>

- This is a list of customers who are on autopay, but payment failed due to several reasons (e.g., insufficient balance, card is blocked, etc).
- There are two reasons why an account can be listed on this tab:

1. <strong>Payment Terms</strong>

- There are several terms of payment based on the customers’ contracts, which are counted in days from the invoice date
  1. Net 5 - 5 days after the 10th of the month when the invoices are sent out
  2. Net 15 - 15 days after the 10th of the month when the invoices are sent out
  3. Net 21 - 21 days after the 10th of the month when the invoices are sent out
  4. Net 30 - 30 days after the 10th of the month when the invoices are sent out
  5. Net 45 - 45 days after the 10th of the month when the invoices are sent out

1. <strong>Past Due Communication Day  </strong>

- This refers to the days when past due reminders, whether auto or manual, are being monitored and sent out.
- As the due date depends on terms, <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1uoF2jdyGVVQSZltRdh3rZ7xvQSsbr8wyLzkYcx03V_g/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.5oym26a5706v">here</a> is the calendar guide of when the past due communications are sent

1. <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1ngsVJmLTE_vSbyZ131tdwEQufSpx9P6gk6WGXullwcg/edit?gid=1658342962#gid=1658342962"><strong>PMs with Add-Ons</strong></a>

- This is a list of PMs with add-ons like Filter, Pinata, and CoverPest
- It is important to consider this when tracking past-due accounts, as it directly impacts the property managers' access to the associated benefits. This information should be communicated alongside the past-due reminder.
- This should be raised in the <em>#ledgering-mkt-opt-benefits</em> Slack channel for visibility every Thursday.

## <strong>Past Due Process</strong>

The past-due workflow is designed to provide a structured and comprehensive approach to addressing overdue balances, ensuring that accounts are monitored and managed effectively. This process involves systematic tracking, communication, and escalation to either recover outstanding payments or, if necessary, terminate the account for non-payment. Each step is tailored to the account's payment terms and number of overdue invoices and ensures a consistent resolution process for every account.

<Note>
  Open balances are tracked based on each customer’s net terms. Accounts with different net terms are reviewed and handled on separate timelines. For example, Net 5 overdue balances are not addressed on the same day as accounts with longer terms. Similarly, reminder notices are not sent to Net 30 customers on the 22nd of the month simply because Net 5 accounts are reviewed on that date.

###### <em><strong>Invoice: Unpaid; Autopay: No </strong></em>

For overdue invoices where the customer is not enrolled in Autopay, the AR team should first verify whether automated payment reminders have already been sent. If reminders have been sent, no immediate action is required, but continuous monitoring of the account is still necessary to ensure timely follow-up.

If a past-due reminder has not been sent, the AR team must ensure that the Payment Reminders toggle in Rillet is turned on and that there is a valid billing POC email associated with the account. If both settings are correct but the automated reminder still does not send, the AR team should manually send the Check 1 template reminder and raise the issue with the Rillet Team for resolution.

<strong>Unexpected Failed Payments</strong>

Unexpected failed payments refer to customers who are on Autopay but whose payments fail due to various reasons, such as insufficient funds, blocked or expired cards, stolen cards, or unauthorized transactions. Unlike the “Expected Failed” list, which is limited to customers missing Stripe mandate IDs, the unexpected failed list can include any Autopay customer whose payment does not go through.

Identifying these customers promptly is critical so that immediate action can be taken. Since reprocessing payments via a bank account can take 5–7 business days, consistent follow-up and monitoring are essential to ensure timely resolution.

Once a failed payment is identified, the “Unexpected Failed Payment” template is sent immediately to the respective billing POC. Similar to other unpaid invoices, these accounts are tracked carefully to maintain accurate records and ensure proper follow-up until the payment is successfully processed.

For all the customers above, we will have to manually run the autopay.

###### <strong>Invoice: Unpaid; Autopay: Yes</strong>

At this stage, we will also be able to verify whether any manually processed payments from the previous week have successfully gone through. AR team should follow up on both Expected and Unexpected Failed customers to confirm the status of their payments.

If a payment has been successful, no further action is needed. If the payment is still in Pending status, no immediate action is required either, as processing can sometimes take more than a week. However, if an Unexpected Failed payment has not gone through, two critical points must be checked before proceeding with a second contact attempt.

Before sending the second contact attempt, two key points must be confirmed, as they influence the content of the second contact attempt:

1. <strong>Alternative Payment Method on File</strong>
2. Review the customer’s Stripe profile via the Overview tab in their Rillet profile. Check if an alternative bank account or credit card is available that can be charged for the open invoices.
3. If there is, make sure that you also track this separately, as you will need to attempt running it after a week. For Net 5, Net 15, and Net 21, this is on Column E of each sheet, while it is on Column D for Net 30.
4. <strong>Late Fee Applicability</strong>
5. If a late fee is included in the customer’s contract, this should be included in the email to remind the customer.
6. If the account is marked as “NO” for late fees, the <a href="https://latchel.com/terms-of-use-msa/"><em>Latchel, Inc. — Master Services Agreement</em></a> takes precedence. A late fee of (1.5%) of the unpaid balance per month should be assessed.

Note: The late fee is mentioned in communications to make the customer aware of the amount they may incur if payment is not received. The actual application of the late fee to the invoice will occur with the generation of the third invoice if the payment remains outstanding, and only after it has been approved by the manager.

#### <strong>Three Past Due Invoices</strong>

For accounts with overdue payments extending across three invoices, the first and second contact attempts will be repeated for the first and second overdue invoices as part of the ongoing payment recovery efforts. If no payments are received after a total of four communications, the account will progress to the Third Invoice Due stage.

#### <strong>Post-Termination Process </strong>

After an account is terminated and there are still open invoices, these invoices will be included in the Monthly AR Aging list. Although these invoices are technically past due, no further past-due communication should be sent.

Accounts Receivable (AR) should write off any open invoices 90 days after termination, and no additional past-due communications should be initiated. Follow the steps outlined for in the <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1PGpRQtcWU7p6mK6ZjMzzeXpNDpHC3OHVIn3XZtzbTDw/edit?tab=t.0">How To Terminate Accounts</a> document to ensure proper handling of these accounts.
