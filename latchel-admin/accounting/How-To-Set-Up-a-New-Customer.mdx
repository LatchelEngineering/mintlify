## Purpose

This document provides a step-by-step guide for creating new contracts for new customers. It also outlines the Accounts Receivable tasks that are handled even before the contract is finalized, ensuring proper setup, accurate billing, and smooth onboarding.

## <strong>Pre-Set Up Process</strong>

### Step 1: Watch for Sales Announcements

When the Sales team secures a signed contract, whether from a new customer or an existing customer making changes (e.g., upgrades or downgrades), they post an update in the #sales channel. AR is automatically tagged on these announcements, so you will receive a Slack notification when a new post goes up.

## <strong>Actual Customer Set Up Process</strong>

Currently, AR considers it a best practice to wait to finalize new customer contracts in Rillet until it is time to prepare for the invoice month of their billing start date. This ensures there is no chance of invoicing a customer early. Also, there is often a lot of discussion in a new customer thread, and sometimes the contract terms are impacted. Giving CSMs time to complete this discussion reduces the chance that updating the contract would be needed. However, a lot of the steps below can be completed without creating the actual Rillet contract.

To execute the new customer set-up process, you will take the following actions:

1. Fill out the customer overview page in Rillet.

5. (If Applicable) Add the customer to the <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1fI8nVm54AoZGNChVlmVLKWRswOitz6I9s7Oen0IO7Ro/edit?gid=0#gid=0">AR Contract Minimum Renewals Tracker</a>

(If applicable) Confirm the customer has been added to the <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1oNIg2c8Pa9AhjG7xcAO29Q7P_LL4N_5CWeXJzY-7EE8/edit?gid=1691082426#gid=1691082426">Billing Inputs Master Doc</a> or <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1dh-0hciIQea-e7JlVs-s4WH65osQKLqkVHLfqlIeGAY/edit?gid=1433076255#gid=1433076255">Bill by Portfolio/Billing Exceptions Tracker</a>

### <strong>Step 1: Fill out the customer overview page in Rillet.</strong>

When a Deal in HubSpot is moved to the “Closed Won” stage, it automatically triggers a notification in the Slack #sales channel and simultaneously creates a customer profile in Rillet with a draft contract. This draft contract must be completed before the account becomes active. This marks the starting point of the new customer setup process.

1. From any page in Rillet, use CTRL+K to bring up a search box and search for the customer you are ready to update.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/AR-6.png" alt="image-20260224-192547.png" />
</Frame>

<Note>
Note: If the customer name contains an ampersand (&), the Rillet search function will not work. You will need to navigate to the side menu ><strong> Accounts Receivable</strong> > <strong>Customers</strong>.
</Note>

1. On the customer overview page, scroll down to the Settings section:
  1. Validate that the Company Name and Invoicing Name match the name in HubSpot.
  2. Validate that the Address matches the address listed in the contract. You can find it on the upper left side of the Order Form.
  3. Validate that the email address matches the contract billing POC. This is sometimes listed on the front page of the contract, other times in the body of the contract.
  4. Automatic Invoicing and Automatic reminders should be toggled off. Online payments should be toggled on. <em>Note: The toggle for Automatic Payments will be grayed out at this point in the process. </em>
  5. Payment terms should be set based on what is stated in the contract. In most cases, contracts are set for net 5. This sets 5 days as the default payment terms for any contract created for this customer. It means the invoice will always have a due date of 5 days from the invoice date. If the customer is on autopay, the payment will be processed on the due date (UTC).
  6. <em>As of this writing, the Tax rate is only applicable for customers with physical addresses in WA or TN. </em>
  7. Make sure to click Save changes before moving on to the next step.

### <strong>Step 3: Add the customer to the </strong><a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1pEcYzTsw3W2rCsJ0wq2GkrRYHCmcn6eZlZCAfHBlPjc/edit?gid=0#gid=0"><strong>AR CC Fee/Autopay Tracker</strong></a><strong>.</strong>

It is AR’s responsibility to ensure that the AR CC Fee/Autopay Tracker remains 100% up to date.

- Every customer has a credit card/processing fee added to their Rillet contract. It is the data in this tracker that determines which customers get the fee applied to their invoices each month. Rillet does not currently support adding fees, so it must be tracked manually outside of Rillet.
- Autopay status is a setting in Rillet, but in the event of a failed payment, it can sometimes be turned off automatically without notification or a log to record the action. For this reason, the AR CC Fee/Autopay Tracker is our only source of truth for which customers should or should not be on autopay.
- Add the customer name. Select <strong>Yes</strong> for both the CC fee and Autopay statuses and add the note <em><strong>“(MM/YY) is the billing start date; for checking on  (NEXT MONTH)  on how invoice was paid. Turning on autopay as confirmed by Will for new customers with CC fee on file ”</strong></em> in the Justification column. This will be helpful when you return to update the statuses in preparation for the next month’s invoices.

<em>Note: As approved by Will, all new customers who have paid for the implementation fee should be tagged as Yes for both CC Fee and autopay unless specifically advised not to.  </em>

### <strong>Step 4: Add the customer to the PM's</strong><a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1Y4X5e5zgA7-Ahyz-bPO7LyKwRVgHReYk/edit?gid=434443624#gid=434443624"><strong> Vital AR Details</strong></a><strong> Tracker</strong>

This tracker contains key AR-related details of each customer, including their status, demographics (essential for tax determination), billing points of contact, autopay status, payment terms, tax rates, and associated Stripe accounts.

Once the Rillet Overview page is updated, this tracker should also be updated accordingly. Keeping both in sync ensures we have accurate information readily available for handling past-due accounts and other billing-related processes.

### <strong>Step 7: Turn off the Hubspot subscription  and finish filling out the Rillet contract to match what is in Hubspot, setting up additional years if required, and turning off the Hubspot subscription  </strong>

<strong>Step 7. A: Turning Off HubSpot Subscription (If Applicable)</strong>

This step only applies to customers with an Implementation Fee. While most new customers are billed this fee, some may have it waived.

When applicable, customers provide their payment details in HubSpot. As a result, once a subscription (or the corresponding line item in the order form) becomes due—starting from the billing start date—it is automatically charged to the payment method on file.

Since this charge is not invoiced and often based on an initial or mock unit count provided by the CSM, it is typically incorrect. To prevent these inaccurate charges from being drafted from the payment method on file, the HubSpot subscription must be canceled.

The detailed process for doing so is documented in this SOP: <a href="https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/~587161297/pages/3462955016/How+cancel+a+subscription+in+HubSpot"><strong>How to Cancel a Subscription in HubSpot</strong>.</a>

### <strong>Step 8: (If applicable) Create an invoice for the implementation fee and match the payment.</strong>

a. Start by going to the Invoice Tab and clicking on "Add Invoice." This will take you to the invoice creation page.

b. Fill out the necessary details. For the Rillet Invoicing Account, select Stripe, as Implementation Fees are processed through this platform. Use the date the payment was received as the Invoice Date. The Due Date will automatically populate based on the account’s Net terms

c. Under the Products section, search for the Implementation Fee, which may be $500 or $1,000, depending on what’s listed in the sales contract.

d. Make sure the quantity is set to 1. Once selected, the invoice preview will be displayed for review. If everything looks correct, click "Save" to generate the invoice. At this point, the invoice will have a status of "Unbilled."

e. Since Unbilled invoices cannot receive payments, you’ll need to update the status to allow payment matching. To do this, click "Mark as Sent" (and not "Sent") to avoid any email notification being sent to the customer and causing confusion

### <strong>Step 9: Link the Stripe account to the customer’s Rillet profile and set up autopay  </strong><a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1_HkVVJbvvGr3_-vaAfHJ1LTDYoTzhHB8iX6lzX_6U_A/edit?gid=0#gid=0"><strong>Rillet Stripe Matching</strong></a>

To enable autopay, the Rillet profile must have the corresponding Stripe profile linked on its Overview page. For this to happen, we need assistance from the Rillet team to connect the two accounts.

a. Fill out the Rillet-Stripe Matching Tracker by providing the following details:

- Name – Enter the Property Management (PM) Company Name.
- Rillet Customer ID – Copy and paste the URL from the Rillet profile’s Overview Page.
- Stripe Customer ID – This is the unique customer ID used in Stripe.
- Stripe Customer URL – Provide the URL link of the customer profile in Stripe that shows the payment.

The Rillet-Stripe Matching Tracker is typically filled out before the end of the month or before the invoice creation process. Once all required details have been added for new customers, the tracker should be submitted to the Rillet team so they can proceed with syncing the accounts.

b. Once you receive confirmation that the profiles have been linked, ensure that the payment method on file used to pay the implementation fee is set as the default payment method.

c. Toggle on the autopay option in the customer’s Rillet profile
