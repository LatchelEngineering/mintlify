<Note>
**Purpose**

This SOP serves as a reference guide for Customer Success Managers (CSMs) and the Product Support (PS) team when handling common Accounts Receivable (AR) inquiries. It provides guidance on how to answer frequently asked billing questions, determine the appropriate AR request category, and identify when a request should be escalated to the AR team.

For requests not covered in this SOP, please submit the inquiry through the appropriate Slack channel or workflow and coordinate with the AR team as needed.
</Note>

## Monthly Subscription Invoicing Overview

- Subscription invoices are generated on the 10th of each month.
- Invoices are based on the total active unit count recorded in Latchel at midnight UTC on the last day of the previous month.
- Property Managers are responsible for maintaining accurate unit counts.
- A reminder email is sent on the 21st of each month to allow time for unit count verification and corrections before invoicing.
- Most customers are billed under Net 5 payment terms unless otherwise specified in their contract.
- Customers enrolled in AutoPay are generally charged on the 14th or 15th of each month.

## Handling Common Requests

### Payment Method Changes

If a customer wants to update their payment method, add or remove AutoPay, or update banking information:

1. Post in **#accounts-receivable**.
2. Submit a **Subscription Invoicing Request** workflow.
3. Select **Change Request** as the category.
4. Include the customer's name and requested changes.

### Billing Contact Updates

If a customer wants to add, remove, or update a billing contact:

1. Post in **#accounts-receivable**.
2. Submit a **Subscription Invoicing Request** workflow.
3. Select **Change Request** as the category.
4. Include the new billing contact information.

### Invoice Copies and Payment History

- Customers can obtain invoice PDFs through the original invoice email sent to billing contacts.
- If they no longer have access to the email:
  - Direct them to contact [billing@latchel.com](mailto:billing@latchel.com).
  - Alternatively, submit a request in **#accounts-receivable**.
- Currently, there is no customer-facing billing portal available.

### Unit Count Questions and Billing Disputes

If a customer questions their unit count or disputes an invoice:

1. Post in **#accounts-receivable**.
2. Submit a **Subscription Invoicing Request** workflow.
3. Select **Billing Dispute** as the category.
4. Include:
   - Customer name
   - Expected unit count
   - Invoice number or invoice month
   - Supporting details provided by the customer

For questions related to future billing counts, refer to the **Intake Process for Active Unit Adjustments**.

### Questions About Increased Invoice Amounts

If a customer asks why their invoice increased:

- Determine whether the increase is due to:
  - Unit count changes
  - Pricing updates
  - Additional products or services
  - Contract changes
- If the reason cannot be immediately determined, submit a request in **#accounts-receivable** for review.
- If the customer disputes the increase, process it as a **Billing Dispute**.

### Current Billing Information Requests

Questions such as:

- What payment method is currently on file?
- Who are the billing contacts?
- Is AutoPay enabled?

Should be submitted to **#accounts-receivable** as a **General Question** request.

## Account Termination Requests

### Permanent Termination

- Account terminations are handled by the assigned Customer Success Manager.
- CSMs should follow the **Account Termination Steps SOP**.
- Product Support should escalate the request through the **Escalate to CSM** workflow and tag the assigned CSM.

### Future-Dated Terminations

- Only the assigned CSM may schedule future account closures.
- The CSM should:
  - Set a reminder for the requested termination date.
  - Follow the **Account Termination Steps SOP** when the date arrives.

### Temporary Service Inactivation

Temporary account inactivation is not currently supported.

## Escalation Guidelines

Submit a request to the AR team whenever:

- A billing record requires modification.
- Payment methods need updating.
- Billing contacts need updating.
- A customer disputes an invoice.
- Unit count adjustments are requested.
- Invoice history is needed and cannot be obtained through existing records.
- The request involves subscription billing and you are unsure how to proceed.

When in doubt, submit the request in **#accounts-receivable** and include all relevant customer information to minimize back-and-forth communication.
