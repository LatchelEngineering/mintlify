## Purpose

This SOP provides guidance on how to accurately complete and maintain the AR Credit and Overpayment Tracker 2026. The purpose of this tracker is to help AR monitor outstanding credits and overpayments, facilitate timely application or refunds of balances, and maintain accurate records for reporting, account reconciliation, and customer account management.

## Procedure

1. Create a new entry in the tracker whenever a customer receives a credit or submits an overpayment that will be applied to a future invoice or requires tracking.
2. On Column A, <strong>Month Granted</strong>, which refers to the month when the overpayment was received, or the credit was granted.
3. Enter the <strong>Month Applied</strong>, which refers to the invoice month where the credit or overpayment will be applied or has already been applied.
4. Column C refers to
5. <strong>Property Manager Name</strong>, using the customer's name as it appears in Rillet.
6. Enter the <strong>Credit Amount</strong>, which should reflect the total overpayment amount received or the credit amount approved.
7. Select <strong>Yes</strong> in the <strong>Rolled from Prev Month?</strong> column if the credit represents a remaining balance carried over from a prior month. Otherwise, select <strong>No</strong>.
8. Enter the <strong>Amount Applied</strong>, which represents the portion of the credit used against the invoice for the month indicated in the <strong>Month Applied</strong> column.
9. Select the appropriate <strong>Reason for Credit</strong> from the following options:

- Overpayment
- Unit Count Adjustment
- Goodwill Credit
- Other approved credit reason

10. If applicable, add the <strong>Slack Thread URL</strong> containing the approval, customer communication, or supporting documentation related to the credit. This is recommended for all credits but is generally not required for standard overpayment cases.
11. Review the autofilled columns to ensure the tracker is calculating correctly:

- <strong>Applied?</strong> will automatically display <strong>Yes</strong> or <strong>No</strong> based on whether an amount has been entered in the <strong>Amount Applied</strong> column.
- <strong>Any to Roll?</strong> will automatically indicate whether a remaining balance exists that should be carried forward to a future month.
- <strong>CC Fee</strong> will automatically indicate whether a credit card processing fee applies.
- <strong>Remaining Credit</strong> will automatically calculate the unused portion of the credit based on the difference between the <strong>Credit Amount</strong> and the <strong>Amount Applied</strong>.

12. At the beginning of each billing cycle, review all entries with a remaining balance and determine whether the credit should be applied to the next invoice, refunded, or continue to roll forward.

## Notes

- All credits and overpayments should be recorded as soon as they are identified.
- Ensure supporting documentation is available for all non-overpayment credits.
- Do not delete historical entries. Update the existing entry as credits are applied over time to maintain a complete audit trail.
- Review the tracker regularly to ensure no customer credits remain unapplied longer than necessary.
