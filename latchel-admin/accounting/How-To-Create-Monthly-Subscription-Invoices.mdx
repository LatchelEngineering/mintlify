<Note>
  **Purpose**

  This SOP serves as the main guide followed by the Accounts Receivable team in creating monthly subscription invoices in Rillet, both via upload and manual creation. It outlines the end-to-end process for generating accurate invoices from different billing sources and ensures consistency in invoicing practices across all accounts.

  This SOP is intended to reduce errors, standardize invoice creation, and ensure all adjustments are properly reflected in Rillet.
</Note>

## Pre-Invoice Creation Checklist (1st of the month)

- Confirm that all new customers starting this month have a contract created.
- Check the (New) #accounts-receivable by status Slack list and ensure all customers are accounted for.
- Verify the unit count data is updated with numbers pulled from the last day of the previous month.
- Fill out the Bill by Portfolio/Billing Exceptions Tracker and Billing Inputs Master Doc

**Sheets to Prepare:**

- Unit Count Data (Prepared by Matias)
- Billing Inputs Master Doc (mostly completed by CPT)
- Bill by Portfolio/Billing Exceptions Tracker
- AR CC Fee/Autopay Tracker
- AR Contract Minimum Renewals Tracker
- Billing@ Undelivered Invoices

## Overview of Steps

1. Populate the Rillet usage template with usage counts
2. Upload your usage template to create draft invoices.
3. Create Manual Invoices
4. Add a credit card/processing fee to applicable draft invoices.
5. If needed, prepare and upload a Rillet usage template fix file.
6. Fix auto-pay status in Rillet where needed.
7. Apply credit to the unbilled invoices of the month for those terminated accounts under the minimum contract
8. Bridge the draft invoice amounts against the previous month
9. Document requests fulfilled in their respective #accounts-receivable Slack threads.
10. On the 10th of each month, send the invoices to customers via Rillet.

**Step 4: Add a duplicate check.**

Ideally, there is only one contract per customer in Rillet with start and end dates that include the current invoicing period. This would mean that there would be only one open contract per customer available to receive usage data. Unfortunately, due to a Rillet limitation(s), that is not the case. It is not always possible to end an existing Rillet contract after adding a new one, so there can be 2\+ contracts for a single PM that have already started but are not yet expired. The formula adds usage data to all the contracts in the usage template meeting that criteria. If uploaded without correction, these duplicate usage entries would create multiple invoices for a single PM when they are only meant to have one. The duplicate check points out these duplicate entries so AR can manually delete from the usage template the line items that belong to the older versions of contracts.

### 4. Create Manual Invoices

There are three main scenarios where a manual invoice must be created:

**No Rillet Contract** — Customers without an active Rillet contract will not appear in the Rillet usage template, requiring manual invoice creation.

**Non-Standard Billing** — This includes one-time charges (e.g., implementation fees) or unique billing scenarios such as Amenify charges and prorated first invoices.

**Credit or Concession Application** — Manual invoices are used to reflect applied credits or concessions clearly. If a PM has multiple adjustments, ensure each credit is listed as a separate line item with a brief note explaining the reason for the credit.

**\$0 Invoices Based on Quantity (Not Credit)** — In addition to invoices that are zeroed out due to applied credits, manual invoices are also created when no GL codes are tracked for the month. In these cases, there is no recorded quantity to generate an invoice automatically.

Since uploading 0 usage does not create an invoice, we issue manual \$0 invoices to:

- Notify PMs that no GL codes were tracked for the month, and
- Maintain complete and accurate invoice records for tracking and reporting purposes.

**How to create a manual invoice**

From the customer invoice page click \+ Add Invoice.

Start filling out the invoice:

- Rillet Invoicing Account – Select the correct account based on the customer's payment setup.
- Invoice Date – If the invoice is intended to be sent alongside regular monthly invoices, set the invoice date to the 10th of the month.
- Product Entries – Add products based on the subscription(s) the PM currently has.
- Rates – Apply rates according to the PM's contract terms.
- Quantity – Determine quantities using the Unit Count Data Sheet, Billing Input Master Document, or based on guidance provided in the request thread.

**Adding Credits and Concessions to Manual Invoices**

Manual invoices allow credits and concessions to be transparently shown as line items. This is a new process, opposed to the previous application of credit memos.

To add:

1. Follow the same manual invoice creation steps.
2. Add a line item labeled as a Credit or Concession.
3. Do not enter the amount as a negative value, so it reflects as a deduction rather than a charge.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/AR-84.png)
</Frame>

<Note>
  When a customer receives a credit or concession for an invoice that is not fully credited and has an applicable processing fee, add the processing fee as a separate line item manually.
</Note>

To calculate the processing fee:

1. Determine the remaining amount due after the credit is applied.
2. Round that amount down to the nearest whole number.
3. Use that rounded value as the quantity for the Processing Fee - \$0.035 per unit line item.
4. The system will automatically calculate the total processing fee.

For example, McGraw Properties has an applicable processing fee, and the credit being applied does not fully cover the invoice. The remaining amount due after the credit is \$818.46, which should be rounded down to 818. This value is entered as the quantity for the \$0.035 processing fee line item, and the system will automatically compute the processing fee as \$28.63.

**Prorated Billing**

Proration typically applies to the first invoice when the account setup is not finalized before the agreed billing start date.

_Prorated Billing Rules_

**A. Go-Live Date between the 10th and 25th**

- Create a manual proration invoice for the remaining days of the month.
- The invoice should reflect the prorated amount based on the contract's monthly fee and the number of days in the billing month.
- Send the manual invoice separately before the first full-month invoice.

**B. Go-Live Date after the 25th**

- Do not create a separate manual invoice.
- Add the prorated amount to the next regular invoice (first full-month billing).
- Clearly label the line item as "Prorated Charges – \[Start Date to End of Month\]."

**Fix File for adding the late fee**

AR tracks past due invoices through AR Aging Reports. For more information on how past due invoices are monitored, please refer to the [How to Identify and Handle Past Due Subscription Invoices](https://help.latchel.com/latchel-admin/accounting/How-To-Identify-and-Handle-Past-Due-Subscription-Invoices).

While the goal is to ensure payments are received on time so that no Late Fee needs to be applied, there may be cases when a Late Fee becomes applicable. In those instances, the Late Fee should be added similarly to how processing fees are handled. However, since this is relatively rare, we currently recommend applying it using the Fix File Template.

To do this, a usage entry should be added based on the Late Fee applicable to the account:

For accounts with a \$100 flat Late Fee:

- Enter 1 as the usage.
- If a processing fee also applies, use the total amount (including the \$100 Late Fee) as the basis for the processing fee usage.

For accounts with a 1.5% Late Fee:

- Enter the whole number value of the total amount due as the usage.
- The system will automatically calculate the Late Fee amount by multiplying the usage by \$0.015.

### 7. Apply credit to the unbilled invoices of the month for those terminated accounts under the minimum contract

Because the Minimum Contract cannot currently be ended in Rillet, apply credit memos to the unbilled invoices for any terminated accounts listed in the AR Contract Minimum Renewals Tracker, so those unbilled invoices are not sent out.

- For each terminated PM in the AR Contract Minimum Renewals Tracker, locate their unbilled invoices in Rillet
- Create and apply a credit memo equal to the unbilled invoice amount so the invoice will not be drafted or sent.

**Applying Credit Memos**

- Check the AR Credit and Overpayment Tracker 2026 to identify PMs with applicable credits and determine the reason for the credit (Column K).
- From the customer's draft invoice page in Rillet, click the three dots (top right) and select Create credit memo.
- Use the trashcan icon to remove any default product(s) listed in the credit memo.
- From the Add Product dropdown, select Credit for goodwill credit, unit count adjustment, and similar cases. For overpayment, excess payment, or double/duplicate payments, select OverPayment. Add the reason in the product name for documentation purposes. Refer to the SOP: [How to Fill Out and Use the AR Credit and Overpayment Tracker 2026](https://help.latchel.com/latchel-admin/accounting/How-to-Fill-Out-and-Use-the-AR-Credit-and-Overpayment-Tracker-2026).
- Enter the credit amount to cover the unbilled invoice. Partial coverage is acceptable, as long as the tracker is updated for any remaining balance to be rolled over next month.
- Review the credit memo in the preview, then click Save.
- A success message will appear in the bottom right corner, and the credit memo will be added as a new line item on the invoice.
