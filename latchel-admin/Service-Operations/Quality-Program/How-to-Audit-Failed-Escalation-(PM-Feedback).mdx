## Purpose

<Note>
Our escalation process is critical to resolving work orders and maintaining customer trust. When on-call technicians, preferred service providers, or escalation contacts are unresponsive, jobs stall, and property managers may feel forced to escalate issues back to us — a direct driver of customer churn.

By introducing automated triggers, we can proactively identify unresponsive contacts and highlight patterns before our customers raise the issue. This shifts us from a reactive to a proactive posture. If a property management company’s contact list is failing, we want to be the ones telling them and not the other way around.

To address this, we’ve built an automated script to detect:

1. **On-Call Failures** → when the primary technician on-call does not acknowledge alerts.
1. **Service Provider Failures** → when preferred service providers never acknowledge our calls.
1. **Escalation Contact Failures** → when no emergency escalation contacts respond after multiple attempts.

Each of these scenarios must persist for **7 consecutive days** before being considered a true escalation failure.
</Note>

#### ** What We’re Trying to Solve **

- **Reduce manual oversight**: Escalation failures are currently identified manually and inconsistently. Automation ensures no failures are overlooked.
- **Validate automation accuracy**: Before we start sending automated escalation emails to property managers, we must confirm the script correctly identifies true failures and doesn’t generate false positives.
- **Improve trust and response rates**: By auditing failures and providing clear reporting, we can give property managers confidence in our escalation process while holding service providers and technicians accountable.

<Note>
This SOP ensures the accuracy of automated escalation triggers before sending automated PM notifications. Auditing helps validate whether unresponsive cases (on-call technicians, service providers, or escalation contacts) are true failures or false positives missed by the automation.
</Note>

## Content

### **Workflow Overview**

1. Automation generates a [**weekly report**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1vIC_9RIbwzwXJXFlxVOMqnfX6vn5THI1VLxEEN1HXqs/edit?gid=348786191#gid=348786191) of companies with **100% failure rate** on all work orders in the past 7 days.
1. Only companies that meet the threshold of **more than 3 failed escalations in the past 7 days** are to be audited. **This threshold applies to all three escalation types** — On-Call Failures, Service Provider Failures, and Escalation Contact Failures — to ensure focus on high-frequency or systemic issues.
1. Auditors validate each trigger against job details and activity logs.
1. Confirm whether the failure is **Valid** or **Voided**.
1. Complete the **Failed Escalation Report workflow** in ps-escalation for confirmed failures.
1. Mark outcomes in the master sheet for transparency and continuous improvement.

### Step-by-Step Guide

#### **Audit Workflow**

Automation produces [**three reports**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1vIC_9RIbwzwXJXFlxVOMqnfX6vn5THI1VLxEEN1HXqs/edit?gid=348786191#gid=348786191) (separate tabs/sheets). Each must be audited before PS escalations are finalized.

#### **1. Escalation Failures (tab: **`escalation_failures`**)**

- **Column E** → Job URL (open to review activity & call recordings).
- **Column M** → Escalation contact list.
- **Column N** → Reporting period (7-day timeframe).
- **Column P** → total # of jobs with escalation failures.
- **Column Q** → total # of escalation failures.

**Validation steps:**

1. Open Job URL.
1. Confirm attempts were made to reach each escalation contact listed in Column M.
1. Check call history/recordings to verify if any contact was reached.
1. If no successful contact in the reporting period → mark **Valid**.
1. If any contact was reached (or automation misflagged) → mark **Voided**.

#### **2. On-Call Failures (tab: **`oncall_failures`**)**

- **Column E** → Job URL.
- **Column S** → Technician name.
- **Column X** → Reporting period.

**Validation steps:**

1. Open Job URL.
1. Review alert history to confirm whether the on-call technician acknowledged.
1. If no acknowledgements in the reporting period → mark **Valid**.
1. If acknowledgements occurred (automation missed it) → mark **Voided**.

#### **3. Service Provider Failures (tab: **`sp_failures`**)**

- **Column E** → Job URL.
- **Column N** → Reporting period.
- **Column P** → `total_jobs_contacted_vendor`.
- **Column Q** → `total_sp_failures`.

**Validation steps:**

1. Open Job URL.
1. Check call logs to see if preferred vendors were contacted and whether they responded.
1. If no responses in the reporting period → mark **Valid**.
1. If vendor answered or returned call (automation missed it) → mark **Voided**.

### **Failed Escalation Report Workflow (in ps-escalation)**

For each **Valid** failure, complete the following fields:

- **Property Management Company Name & ID:** Short answer
- **Type of Failed Escalation:** Dropdown (On-Call / Service Provider / Escalation Contact)
- **Escalation Contacts (optional):** paste unresponsive escalation list (for escalation failures)
- **Technician Name (optional):** paste from Column S (for on-call failures)
- **Job URLs:** paste all relevant job links
- **Reporting Period:** paste from N or X depending on sheet

### **Reporting Sheet Updates**

For each reviewed job, update the sheet:

- **Column X – Status:** `Valid` or `Voided`
- **Column Y – Reason (if Voided):** explanation of what automation missed
- **Column Z – PS Escalation Slack URL:** paste escalation link
- **Column AA – Auditor Name:** your name

### **Quality Standards**

- **Valid =** No acknowledgement/response despite multiple attempts in the 7-day period.
- **Voided =** Any acknowledgement/response was made, or automation flagged incorrectly.
