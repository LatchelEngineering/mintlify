### Purpose

<Note>
  To provide auditors with resources on how to deliver constructive yet encouraging written feedback in every review. This will support our overarching program goals to:

  1. Empower the Quality Team with [inspirational frameworks](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/LTG/pages/1159823387)
  2. Encourage associates to embrace audits as constructive learning experiences
  3. Enhance the quality of audits and auditors' performance
</Note>

### Background

In each quality review or audit, there are two written feedback sections for auditors to complete. One is to recognize what the associate did great (i.e., how they demonstrated [customer obsession](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/LTG/pages/1119880096)) and the other is to provide constructive feedback on how they can improve their customer service skills or better comply with our SOPs. Your role as an auditor is to help associates understand how they can correct any “No” answers in future interactions and encourage them to succeed. Even when an associate receives “Yes” answers to all audit questions resulting in a 100% score, there is **_always something we can learn to continue raising the customer service bar_**!

### Process

When completing a quality audit:

1. If you selected “No” for one or more audit questions:
   1. Briefly describe what the associate could have done differently to receive a “Yes” for each question. Focus on **positively framing** the feedback so that we’re providing encouragement and suggestions on what they can do next time (ex: _In future interactions, paraphrasing the customer’s request will give them the opportunity to correct, clarify, or add additional details about the issue. For example, you could say something like “It sounds like you’re experiencing two different issues – your dishwasher leaks onto the floor when you run it, and there is also a minor leak under the kitchen sink. Did I get that right?”_), rather than pointing out the negative (ex: _“You failed to do X. You forgot to do Y.”_).
   2. **Link to relevant Job Aids and SOPs** so the associate can refer to the support materials we have available to them.
2. If you selected “Yes” for all audit questions:
   1. Ask yourself: \_“This interaction met all quality standards, but how could it have been an even better experience for the customer? How could we raise the bar even higher?”\_
   2. The answer to these questions may be something not currently measured by our quality audits, for example, tone of voice. Maybe the associate met all of the CX standards, but they sounded monotone or unengaged in the conversation.
   3. **Leverage AI**. Latchel has a subscription to [https://app.credal.ai/signin](https://app.credal.ai/signin) (log in with your Latchel Google account), which allows you to use different AI models while protecting sensitive data. Here’s an example query on tone of voice provided:

   5. **Rely on your colleagues**! Still stumped on how to provide feedback on a specific improvement area after completing the steps above? Want someone to peer review your written feedback to suggest how you can phrase it more positively? Post in the [#qa\_auditors](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C050E2BMYG1). Slack channel where the team will be happy to help you out!
