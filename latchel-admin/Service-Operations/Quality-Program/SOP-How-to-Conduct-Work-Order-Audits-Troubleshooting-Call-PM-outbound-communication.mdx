### 🧠 What This Process Is Trying to Solve

<Note>
  This SOP ensures that the Troubleshooting process is audited consistently, accurately, and in alignment with our Calibration Guidelines, PM rules, and AI Intake processing. By following this process, auditors can:

  - Assess the agents' performance consistently and objectively.
  - Identify potential gaps or errors in the AI Intake process, as well as Work Order handling, preventing issues such as setting the wrong severity, missed follow-ups or improper escalation.
  - Provide a standardized method for auditors to conduct reviews, improving audit reliability, reducing confusion, and supporting better outcomes for Latchel and our customers.

  \*\*This process ensures accuracy, accountability, and quality control in the WO lifecycle \*\*while giving auditors clear guidance on how to review efficiently and consistently.
</Note>

### Step-by-step guide

<Callout icon="notes" iconType="duotone" color="#b19cf8">
  Work Order audits follow the same structure for both Emergency and De-escalated samples. Differences are highlighted within each step.
</Callout>

1. **Identify your assignments.**
   1. **Emergency WOs** 
      1. Open the [Work Order Audits](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1pDBRevftU7iNqV7Syc4mb5AeGG8lMmwcjxic88TwI-A/edit#gid=0) Google sheet
      2. Go to the “Reviews of the Week” tab 
      3. Identify your assigned agent (s) for Emergency Work Orders 
      4. Complete 1 WO Audit per assigned agent. 
      5. Ensure alignment with assigned [Customer Experience Review ](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/11g_qx-XbGTujAK04kS-rI8qtHLz6ZqrsFbWLis3knAE/edit#gid=1657910582)agents. 
         1. Use the same troubleshooting as a call sample when applicable 
   2. **De- Escalated WOs**
      1. Open the [Work Order Audits](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1pDBRevftU7iNqV7Syc4mb5AeGG8lMmwcjxic88TwI-A/edit#gid=0) Google sheet
      2. Go to the “[De-Escalated TS](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1pDBRevftU7iNqV7Syc4mb5AeGG8lMmwcjxic88TwI-A/edit?gid=829970507#gid=829970507)” tab 
      3. Check column K “Auditor”
      4. Identify assigned agent(s) for de-escalated WO audits
2. **Select a valid sample.**
   1. **Emergency WOs**
      1. Go to the “[Samples #1](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1pDBRevftU7iNqV7Syc4mb5AeGG8lMmwcjxic88TwI-A/edit?gid=974824724#gid=974824724)” tab
      2. Randomly select a “**job\_id”** from column A
      3. Open the Work Order
      4. Verify severity = EmergencyIf not Emergency → skip and select another sample
      5. Confirm a troubleshooting call was completed<br />NOTE: If your assigned agent does not have any Emergency troubleshooting calls for last week, add the note “_No valid samples_” in column D “Notes” of the “WO Audit assignments” tab.
   2. **De-Escalated WOs**<br />All samples must be audited. <br />The only **exception** where the form should not be submitted is **when the Work Order was de-escalated without a Troubleshooting (TS) call.**<br />**If that occurs, the auditor must select the appropriate reason from Column N (Exception Reason Dropdown) and proceed accordingly.**
      1. **Confirm whether a TS call was completed**
         1. **If a TS call was completed → proceed with full audit (move to step 3)**
         2. **If NO TS call → classify using Column N dropdown below**
            - **PM de-escalated prior to TS: PM updated the Work Order before any agent contact or TS action occurred (this is expected to be rare and should still be recorded)\_**
            - **No TS call – Unresponsive resident:\_ Agent attempted contact, but the resident was unresponsive. De-escalation is based on intake info \+ PM settings alignment\_**
            - **Agent de-escalated without calling (CC agents only): _No TS call was madeDe-escalation is correct and supported by available information and PM settings_**
            - **Wrong de-escalation:**\_ No TS call was made AND decision was incorrect<br />Auditor must: Submit an Agent Feedback ticket, and paste the ticket link in the notes column
3. **Review Work Order & Gather Context**
   1. Open the Work Order details<br />Review all relevant guidance in: [How to: Properly Diagnose Emergency Situations](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/OPS/pages/2475851825) 
   2. Understand that this document is the foundation for all WO audit evaluations
   3. What to review within the WO:
      - Previous and related Work Orders for the same property
      - Critical notes and pre-dispatch instructions
      - Intake summary Photos uploaded by the resident
      - Any contextual information that may affect urgency or handling
4. Review Troubleshooting Call (s)
   1. Listen to the Troubleshooting (TS) call in full. 
   2. Identify:
      1. Information missed during intake
      2. Inconsistencies in resident or PM information
      3. How the agent progressed the troubleshooting process
      4. Whether the correct probing and resolution steps were followed
5. **Complete Audit Form – Core Evaluation**
   1. Review agent actions post-call
      1. Quickly review what the agent did immediately after ending the TS call
      2. Do not spend more than a few minutes on this section
      3. Focus only on understanding the next action taken and whether it aligns with SOPs
   2. Escalation call validation (if applicable)If escalation calls exist, listen to them in full
      1. Validate process adherence using: [Job Aid: PM Outbound Communication for Emergency WOs](https://latchel.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/OPS/pages/2537652248)
      2. Confirm correct escalation handling and communication flow
   3. Complete audit sections. Fill out:
      1. [WO Audit Form #1&2: Troubleshooting Call & PM outbound communication](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSexH6bDrSbzZjw4p21Sf9OrLmPLa04g22FUuAC5FSfbfDjweQ/viewform) 
      2. [Customer experience- Call review](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSf_2FSgn6N-WuO4OwON0KQtnRwc8m8Wgz6kwbdqQz48jMzLKg/viewform) (if the agent is assigned in CX audits)
      3. Re-listen to calls if needed for accuracy
   4. If no escalation call exists
      1. Select “Not applicable” for all PM Outbound Communication questions in the audit form
6. Written Feedback.
   1. Before finalizing, perform a quick review of all selected answers
      1. Ensure all options reflect accurate evaluation
   2. If markdowns are identified:
      1. Provide clear, structured feedback explaining:
         1. What the agent did incorrectly
         2. What the correct action should have been
         3. Reference relevant SOPs, Job Aids, or documented guidance
   3. Feedback balance:
      1. Clearly highlight “What they did great”
      2. Include encouraging reinforcement while keeping feedback objective and actionable<br /><br /><br />Please see [Calibration Guidelines: Emergency WO Audit #1: Troubleshooting Call & Immediate Next Steps](/latchel-admin/Service-Operations/Quality-Program/Calibration-Guidelines-Emergency-WO-Audit-1-Troubleshooting-Call-Immediate-Next-Steps) for guidance on how to respond to each [WO Audit Form #1&2: Troubleshooting Call & PM outbound communication](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSexH6bDrSbzZjw4p21Sf9OrLmPLa04g22FUuAC5FSfbfDjweQ/viewform) form question.<br /><br />

### 🎯 When to Break This SOP

This SOP covers 80–90% of cases. But no SOP is perfect. Break the SOP and escalate when:

- _You encounter an unusual AI Intake or WO edge case that the SOP doesn’t address._
- _You believe an alternate audit action would more accurately assess the WO or better support Latchel and the Property Manager._
