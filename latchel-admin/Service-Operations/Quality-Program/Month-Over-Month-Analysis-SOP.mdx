### 🧠 What This Process Is Trying to Solve

<Note>
  We want to identify and address performance patterns before they negatively impact the customer experience. This process ensures we are proactively assessing monthly QA results to uncover guideline gaps, behavior trends, and coaching needs. The insights from this analysis help supervisors and the QA team implement data-driven actions that elevate support quality across all channels.
</Note>

### 📋 Step-by-Step Process

<Note>
  Every first week of the month, a Quality associate will complete the following steps as part of the monthly report presented to supervisors:
</Note>

#### 1. Update the “Monthly results.”

- Open the **“Team Summary” tab** in each Customer Experience spreadsheet (call, SMS/ Webchat, and Email responses), and update the “**Overall Team by Month**” results

#### **2. Identify guidelines with the lowest scores**:

- Look for the biggest month-over-month drops.
- Prioritize low-scoring guidelines with significant sample sizes.
- Deprioritize low-scoring guidelines if results are based on 1–2 fails in a very small sample.

#### 3. **Assess audits related to failed guidelines**:

- Do the results cluster around specific associates?
- Are these associates also bottom performers overall?
- Are there patterns of failure across the whole team?
- Is the audit feedback clear, actionable, and encouraging? (Include SOP links or guides where applicable.)

#### 4. **Check with supervisors**:

- Are the bottom performers receiving additional coaching, such as 1:1s or reassessments?

#### 5. **Identify trends**:

- Are there recurring guideline issues month-over-month?
- Are some trends channel-specific or affecting all channels?

#### 6. **Create the detailed monthly report**:

- Open the [2026 CX- MoM Analysis](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1PYlkUjHz9J_oulHibnIu4DBWTLekF0C4n1e9EMjZtWw/edit?tab=t.0), and use the previous month’s report for template guidance
- Summarize performance patterns and observations.
- Include a list of specific associates, guidelines, or process gaps as needed.

#### 7. **Build the action plan**:

- Identify the top opportunity for improvement.
- Include ideas for Slack messaging, huddles, or hands-on activities.
- Recommend resources or guidance materials to support agents in improving performance.

#### 8. **Verify documentation**:

- Is the documentation clear and aligned with expectations?
- Recommend updates or improvements if gaps are found.

#### 9. Final step

- **Notify supervisors** in the appropriate Slack channel once the report and plan are complete.

### 🎯 When to Break This SOP

<Note>
  Break or escalate this SOP if:

  - The current performance trends suggest a broader issue not captured by this analysis (e.g., product changes, unexpected volume spikes, system bugs).
  - A guideline appears problematic due to unclear documentation, requiring cross-team review.
  - You identify urgent agent behavior that requires escalation before the report cycle ends._E.g., This SOP covers 80–90% of cases. But no SOP is perfect. Break the SOP and escalate when:_
</Note>
