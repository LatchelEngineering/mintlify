## Purpose

<Note>
**Purpose of this document**

This document outlines the weekly process for updating and reviewing Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) feedback. It includes steps to import Typeform responses, analyze customer comments, identify and report operations-controllable feedback via the #qa_sync Slack channel, and update the relevant data in the Ops-Level Metrics file. The process is performed on a rotating basis by QA Associates and includes both associate and Lucia-related feedback.
</Note>

<Note>
**Who should read this document?**

Quality associates and Managers
</Note>

## Content

### Background and Deviation Guidelines

<Note>
**What This Process Is Trying to Solve:**

This process aims to ensure that customer feedback is consistently reviewed and acted upon by identifying trends and highlighting issues that are within our operational control. By regularly analyzing CSAT comments and sharing relevant insights with the team, we can proactively address service gaps, track the impact of internal improvements, and maintain alignment between QA, Ops, and the rest of the organization. The goal is to close the loop between customer experience and internal performance, creating a culture of accountability and continuous improvement.
</Note>

<Warning>
**When to break this SOP**

This SOP should be followed consistently, but it may be adapted if:

- The Typeform platform or CSAT collection method changes, requiring a new import or analysis approach.
- A critical issue has been identified that requires immediate escalation outside of the standard weekly review cycle.
- There is a team-wide process update that reassigns responsibilities or changes the reporting format.
</Warning>

### Step by step guide

#### Step 1 - Access and Export Typeform Data

1.1 Access the Typeform account.

1.2 Navigate to the **Results > Responses** tab.

1.3 Filter the responses to capture data for the appropriate week.

1.4 Export the filtered responses and download the file to your computer.

#### Step 2- Import Data into Spreadsheet

2.1 Open the CSAT tracking spreadsheet.

2.2 Go to the **“Import Raw Data Here”** sheet.

2.3 Import the downloaded Typeform data into this sheet.

#### Step 3 -Paste Data in the Daily QA Review Tab

**3.1 On Mondays:**

Paste all rows of data for feedback received **Friday through Sunday** into the **Daily QA Review** tab.<br/>**3.2** **On Tuesday–Friday:**

Paste all rows of data for feedback received **the previous day** into the **Daily QA Review** tab.

#### Step 4 -Review and Categorize Feedback

4.1 Screen all feedback for comments related to the **Service Operations associate's performance**.

4.2 Identify and exclude feedback related to **unresolved maintenance issues, software complaints, or other non-Serv Ops issues** – these are handled by the **Product Support team**.

#### Step 5 -Complete Required Columns

5.1 Fill in **columns G–J** in the **Daily QA Review Data** tab based on your analysis.

5.2 Use **column K (Special Notes)** to highlight any patterns or observations that don’t fit predefined categories.

#### Step 6 -Report Negative Feedback Promptly

6.1 If you find **negative feedback** about a Service Ops associate (ops controllable) during your review, post it immediately in the **#qa-sync Slack channel**, tagging **@Jennifer Lye**.

#### Step 7 -Reroute Misplaced Lucia Feedback

8.1 If a complaint about **AI/Lucia** appears in the **associate CSAT form** (instead of the Lucia CSAT form):<br/>– Copy the entry into the **Serv Ops CSAT Survey Results** sheet.<br/>– In **column N**, leave a note: _“Moved to Lucia feedback”_.
