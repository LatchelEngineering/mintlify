### Purpose

This SOP outlines the end-to-end process for auditing Harphonia-handled calls, ensuring quality assurance, accurate work order creation, and continuous improvement of the resident experience.

### Scope

This process applies to all auditors responsible for reviewing Harphonia escalations and completed calls.

### Tools & Systems

- **Slack:** [#harphonia-audits](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C0A3QSYM02E) (audit triggers)
- **Slack:** [#harphonia-prod-feedback](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C09SKP5SPRA) (critical bug alerts from audits)
- **Slack:** [#cc-tasks](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C0A4BGPN1D2) (callback/work order escalations)
- **Retell Dashboard**: System used to view call history and session details
- **Latchel Console**: Resident and Property Records
- **Harphonia QA Review Typeform**: [https://latchel.typeform.com/to/e6YjdiED](https://latchel.typeform.com/to/e6YjdiED)

### Key Notes & Best Practices

- Complete audits as soon as you're available to avoid resident intake delays.
- Be specific and objective in feedback to support continuous improvement.
- Flag critical blockers to Jennifer Lye for resolution.

### Audit Workflow (Simplified Process)

**NEW: Simplified Audit Triggers**

Harphonia-audits channel now receives **only calls that need auditing** (random sample of calls > 2 mins in duration where AI did not successfully resolve the issue). You no longer need to consult the spreadsheet to determine if a call should be audited—if it appears in the channel, audit it.

**NEW: PD/EH Targeted Audit Triggers**

In addition to the random sample, two new targeted triggers now flag potential **Property Damage (PD) / Extreme Habitability (EH)** situations for audit. These are high-risk scenarios where human review is critical. See the updated Audit Criteria and PD/EH Audit Process sections below.

- **NEW: PD/EH Call Trigger** — Calls tagged with the "Property damage detected" label (indicating potential Property Damage or Extreme Habitability). These are flagged based on the following post-call analysis criteria:Structural damage: Leaks, fires, structural cracks — anything where damage will keep getting worse if no one shows upSituations that put people at physical risk: gas leaks, exposed wiring, sparking outlets, unsecured doors**🔥 Fire Hazard — Major Electrical System (Power Shutoff Distinction)**_This is not a new automated trigger — it's guidance for auditors reviewing PD/EH cases involving major electrical systems._Any major electrical system (HVAC, furnace, electric water heater) with signs of electrical malfunction where the system is reported as "off" but power has NOT been confirmed disconnected at the breaker.**Flag as a missed detection if:** the case was de-escalated solely because the resident said the system was "off," no probing questions were asked about breaker status, or malfunction signs were present but the case was still de-escalated.**Correct handling looks like:** agent/AI asked how the system was turned off, asked about malfunction signs, asked resident to confirm the breaker is off, and followed PM's intake settings to classify severity. - **NEW: Web Form PD/EH Trigger** — Web form submissions where a non-EMG work order is created but the intake conversation implies potential PD/EH. Auditors must open the WO and confirm the correct severity was selected based on the PM's Harphonia rules.Open
- **NEW: Work Order Review Trigger** — Work orders where AI selected an outcome based on the PM's settings. ### PD/EH Audit Process

For audits triggered by the PD/EH flags (either call label or web form trigger), follow the standard Audit Process above AND apply these additional steps:

#### PD/EH Call Audits (Property Damage Detected Label)

1. Follow the standard Audit Process (Steps 1–6 above).
1. **Check for PM Settings vs. PD/EH Conflict** — If Harmonia's severity classification is correct per the PM's intake settings but the call involves a PD/EH situation (e.g., PM settings classify a water leak as non-emergency, but the issue involves active property damage), **escalate to the PM** to make them aware of the conflict and confirm the appropriate severity. Do not accept the PM's non-emergency classification as the final word when active property damage is occurring.
1. **Intervene with correct action** — Do not only provide feedback via the audit form. If Harphonia failed to take the correct action, you must step in:**Create a Work Order** if AI failed to create one**Escalate a WO to EMG** if AI selected the wrong severity**Take any other necessary action** that Harphonia missed (e.g., notify on-call tech, send callback)
1. Complete the audit form as usual to provide feedback so we can improve how AI handles PD/EH scenarios.

#### Web Form PD/EH Audits (Severity Confirmation)

1. Open the Work Order linked in the audit notification.
1. Review the intake conversation to understand the reported issue.
1. Confirm the correct severity was selected based on the PM's Harphonia rules.If the severity is incorrect, **update the WO to the correct severity** **If the PM's intake settings conflict with PD/EH criteria (e.g., PM settings classify a water leak as non-emergency, but the issue involves active property damage), escalate to the PM to make them aware of the conflict and confirm the appropriate severity.**If a call to the escalation contacts is required, create a task to the Troubleshooter team.
1. Complete the audit form noting any severity corrections made.

#### PD/EH Detection Criteria

The following criteria are used to classify a situation as potential Property Damage or Extreme Habitability:

1. **Structural damage** — Leaks, fires, structural cracks, anything where damage will keep getting worse if no one shows up
1. **Physical risk** — Gas leaks, exposed wiring, sparking outlets, unsecured doors — situations that put people at physical risk
1. **Unlivable conditions** — No heat in winter, no running water, no power, sewage backup — situations that make the home unlivable

⚠️ **Important:** For PD/EH audits, your role goes beyond feedback. You are the safety net — if AI got it wrong, take immediate corrective action.

⚠️ **PM Settings vs. PD/EH Conflict:** A PM's intake settings may classify certain issues as non-emergency. However, if the reported issue meets PD/EH criteria above, the PM's settings do not eliminate the property damage risk. In these cases, **escalate to the PM** to inform them of the conflict between their settings and the PD/EH situation. Do not simply accept the PM's settings as the final word when active property damage is occurring.

### Post-Audit Process

After submitting the audit form, return to the original escalation thread in [#harphonia-audits](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C0A3QSYM02E) and react with a checkmark (✅) emoji to indicate completion.

### Audit Alert Behavior

- **Critical Bug alerts** are posted to [#harphonia-prod-feedback](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C09SKP5SPRA). The Critical Bugs field must always be available for both audit form branches and all audit types, regardless of how the auditor answers “Did AI select the correct outcome based on PM settings?” Auditors should use this field for any critical bug or major system failure that requires immediate attention.

- **Improvement Opportunities** are tracked in the audit form and spreadsheet only — they do not trigger alerts.
- **Alert format:** The WO Link is now displayed in the alert instead of Call Session ID and Resident ID (unless no WO link is available, in which case Session ID and Resident ID will still appear).

### Audit Form - Removed Questions

The following questions have been removed from the audit form as of this update:

- **Do we need to create a work order?**
- **Do we need to call back the resident to gather more info?**
- **Why was the work order not created?** (including "If you selected Other..." follow-up)

These questions are no longer needed because:

- Calls where AI successfully resolved issues without WOs are no longer audited
- The Harphonia call automation now handles follow-ups and escalations automatically (SMS to residents when caller hangs up before request submission, alerts to #cc-tasks when phone is not textable)

### Q&A

**Q: What if I can't find a Resident ID?**<br/>A: Leave it blank and share the phone number with the CC team in [#cc-tasks](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C0A4BGPN1D2).

**Q: How do I know if I should audit a call now?**<br/>A: If it appears in [#harphonia-audits](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C0A3QSYM02E), it needs to be audited. No need to check the spreadsheet anymore.

**Q: What happened to the audit spreadsheet?**<br/>A: The spreadsheet's filtering step has been deprecated. Calls are now pre-filtered before appearing in the channel, making the audit process simpler and faster.

**Q: What should I do if a call needs a callback or work order?**<br/>A: Those scenarios are now handled by the Harphonia automation system. Your role is to audit the call quality and provide feedback on critical bugs and improvement opportunities.

**Q: What do I do when I see a "Property damage detected" audit?**<br/>A: Follow the standard audit process, but also take correct action if Harphonia got it wrong — create a WO if one is missing, escalate to EMG if severity is incorrect. See the PD/EH Audit Process section for the full workflow.

**Q: How do I handle a web form PD/EH audit?**<br/>A: Open the WO, review the intake conversation, and confirm the correct severity was selected based on the PM's Harphonia rules. If the severity is wrong, escalate it. Complete the audit form noting any corrections.

**Q: What qualifies as Property Damage or Extreme Habitability?**<br/>A: Three categories: (1) Structural damage — leaks, fires, cracks where damage worsens without intervention; (2) Physical risk — gas leaks, exposed wiring, unsecured doors; (3) Unlivable conditions — no heat in winter, no water, no power, sewage backup.

**Q: What is a Work Order Review audit?**<br/>A: These are audits where you verify that AI selected the correct outcome based on the PM's settings. Open the WO, review the intake conversation, and confirm the outcome. If it's wrong, take corrective action (escalate to EMG or create an On-Call Alert) and report it as a critical bug.

**Q: What triggers an alert in #harphonia-prod-feedback?**<br/>A: Critical bugs trigger alerts in [#harphonia-prod-feedback](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C09SKP5SPRA). The Critical Bugs field should always be available for both audit form branches and all audit types, so auditors can report any critical bug or major system failure immediately. Improvement opportunities are tracked in the spreadsheet only and do not trigger alerts.

**Q: What if the PM's intake settings say non-emergency, but the issue looks like property damage?**<br/>A: When a PM's intake settings conflict with PD/EH criteria, escalate to the PM. The PM needs to be made aware that their settings conflict with an active PD/EH situation so they can confirm the appropriate action. Do not simply accept the PM's non-emergency classification when PD/EH criteria are met.
