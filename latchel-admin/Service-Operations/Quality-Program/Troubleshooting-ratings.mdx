### 🧠 What This Process Is Trying to Solve

<Note>
  The purpose of this document is to outline the process for identifying and reviewing troubleshooting interaction ratings submitted by residents. This includes isolating low ratings (below 3 stars) and any comments indicating dissatisfaction, to ensure all proper procedures were followed by the associate and to identify any coaching opportunities. Additionally, this process highlights 5-star ratings with strong positive feedback for the purpose of recognizing outstanding performance. This review helps us maintain high-quality service standards and address gaps in the troubleshooting process when necessary.
</Note>

### 📋 Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1 - Reviewing Poor Troubleshooting Ratings

1. Open the [**Troubleshooting Ratings** ](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1hMT5oOMmZjl0ennUWmW1AJ9lrzU811qX0R1z9MQ2C7g/edit?gid=0#gid=0)spreadsheet.
2. Navigate to the tab: **Troubleshooting data**
3. Filter the data for the correct week: (e.g., if this is Week 28, review Week 27).
   1. Identify the previous week (e.g., if it’s Week 28, filter for Week 27).
   2. Use the filter in **Column J**(Week Number).
4. Apply additional filters:
   1. **Column E ("rated\_item"):** Select only Troubleshooter
   2. **Column B ("Rating"):** Filter to show only scores **below 4**.
5. Identify each entry that meets the criteria.
   1. Go to **Column G** to locate the **Work Order URL**.
   2. Open the Work Order.
6. Review the full Work Order:
   1. **Sort the work order entries from oldest to newest** to ensure you review the interaction in chronological order and understand the full context.
   2. **Listen to the call recording** carefully to assess the communication style, tone, and key details shared.
   3. **Evaluate the troubleshooting process** by verifying whether:
      1. The correct flow was followed
      2. The tone was empathetic and professional
      3. All necessary escalation steps were properly executed
   4. **Identify the likely cause of the low rating**, whether it's a missed step, delayed response, miscommunication, or resident frustration unrelated to the associate’s performance.
   5. Fill out the findings under the Poor Troubleshooting Ratings form in the next step.
   6. If there is feedback to provide, document this in the [svc-ops-feedback](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/G010UBQ995M) Channel. This could be improvement opportunities by not following our SOP’s or behavior concerns.

#### Step 2- 5-Star Troubleshooting Ratings & Shoutouts

1. Go to the spreadsheet titled [**Troubleshooting Ratings** ](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1hMT5oOMmZjl0ennUWmW1AJ9lrzU811qX0R1z9MQ2C7g/edit?gid=0#gid=0).
2. **Navigate to the Tab:**<br />Click on the **“Troubleshooting Data”** tab
3. Filter for 5-Star Ratings:
   1. In **Column B: “Rating”**, apply a filter
   2. In **Column E ("rated\_item")** Select only troubleshooter
4. Scan for Positive Comments:
   1. Look at **Column C: “Comments”**.
   2. Select only the rows that include **clear positive feedback or shoutouts**.<br />(Skip rows with no comments or vague/neutral responses.)
5. Highlight the entire qualifying row in **light green** to mark it for shoutout.
6. **Post the Shoutouts** in Slack:
   1. Go to the [**#core-values**](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/CTPEPAJG1) channel on Slack.
   2. Use the following message template for your Monday shoutout:
      1. Happy Monday, team!  <br />We’re happy to start the week shouting out these 💚 5-Star Troubleshooting rating comments from **Week \[insert week number\], 2025**:<br />🌟 \[Agent Name\] – “\[Insert positive comment\]”  <br />🌟 \[Agent Name\] – “\[Insert positive comment\]”  <br />🌟 \[Agent Name\] – “\[Insert positive comment\]”Huge kudos for going above and beyond…
