<Note>

### 💡 If You Think This SOP Could Be Improved...

Noticed something clunky or confusing in this process? Or had a situation that wasn’t covered here? Or tried something new that didn’t work?<br /> 👉 Share your suggestion in the [SOP Feedback Form](https://slack.com/shortcuts/Ft07C0TDU4N9/9439ee41aae76a4a602b6c186ce4a6cd) or tell your manager— **we want your input!**

A note for Managers or individuals writing SOPs (you can delete this portion of the template before publishing):

- Avoid creating SOPs that are tightly scripted. Let’s encourage agents to think, not just copy/paste scripts. Incorporate language such as, 
  - “Use your judgment here, if the \[WO\] looks slightly different, bring it to your \[manager/ops support team\] to review together.” or 
  - “If you’re unsure whether to \[mark it duplicate or escalate\], flag it in Slack using the `[#wo-check` tag\] for peer review.”
- Replace static copy/paste templates/scripts with scripting frameworks. Instead of giving them the exact text to say, offer it like this:
  - **Before**: “Thanks for your help with this. I’m going to send you a text message right now…”
  - **After**: Say something like… “Thanks so much. I’ll text you in a moment. Can you reply once you hear from the utility company?”🎤 Tip: Ask yourself, would I feel reassured by this response if I were in their shoes right now? Empathy goes a long way here. 
  - This teaches agents how to think about communication, not just repeat it.
