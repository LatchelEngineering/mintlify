---

## Purpose

<Note>
  **Purpose of this document**

  This guide serves as a guide for the Quality department team when implementing new audits or processes within the QA Program. It ensures that every new audit/process is aligned with our SOPs, supported by accurate data, clearly defined, and calibrated across the team before launch. Following this checklist helps us maintain consistency, fairness, and clarity in how we evaluate interactions.
</Note>

<Note>
  **Who should read this document?**

  Quality associates.
</Note>

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Process Overview

#### 1. Identify the need

- What is prompting this new audit?
- Are there missed opportunities in the current associate behavior?
- Is there a new policy or SOP we need to audit?

#### 2. **Check for existing audits**

- Do we already have something similar?
- Can an existing audit be updated instead?
- What has worked or not worked before?

#### 3. **Define objectives & responsibilities**

- What is the goal of this audit?
- Who is responsible for each part of the implementation?(e.g. Martha creates SOP, Diana builds the audit )
- What is the proposed date for presenting the first draft to Jen?
- Confirm required approvals from Jen before rollout.

#### 4. **Audit Criteria design**

- Are the audit points clearly defined, measurable, and objective?
- Are there any overlapping or conflicting items with other audits?

#### 5. **Sample gathering & requirements**

- What type of samples are needed?
- What should the samples include (Keywords, issue type, channel)?
- What is the date range needed?
- Who will provide the samples and by when?)

#### 6. **System & Dashboard**

- Does Joe need to create or update any dashboards?
- Will the new audit impact current reporting? Will new reporting be needed?Are the scoring formulas correct for the new inputs?

#### **7. QA Team feedback & Testing**

- Run a test with 5-10 samples
- Once a test is run by the Quality associates, communicate to the rest of the audit team and request feedback on the new audit.
- Did auditors interpret the criteria consistently?
- Was the auditor's feedback implemented?
- Define with supervisors the quantity of new audits they will be assigned.

#### **8. Final approvals and communication**

- Confirm with Joe that the new dashboard is in place.
- Finalize the new launch date.
- Notify the Supervisors to implement the new audit. Ensure this is covered in the all-hands meeting.
- Ensure coaches review the SOP to schedule the appropriate training sessions.

#### **9. Monitoring & Follow-Up**

- Once live track audit results for consistency.
- Schedule a review checkpoint in 2 weeks.
- Make adjustments if necessary or provide clarity in any SOPs based on the results.
