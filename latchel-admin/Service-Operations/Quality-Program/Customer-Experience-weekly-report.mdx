## Purpose

<Note>
**Purpose of this document**

The purpose of this document is to provide a clear, step-by-step guide on how to update the Weekly Customer Experience Review Report. This SOP ensures that every Monday, the report is consistently reviewed and updated to reflect the previous week's scores. The report serves as a tool to analyze performance trends, highlight key strengths, and identify opportunities for improvement across calls, SMS, and emails, helping the team focus on actionable areas for continued growth in customer experience.
</Note>

<Note>
**Who should read this document?**

QA Associates
</Note>

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Process Overview

This process outlines the end-to-end steps for completing the weekly **Customer Experience Review Report**, including updating audit data across Calls, SMS/Webchat, and Email channels; refreshing weekly reporting tables and formulas; analyzing WoW trends, strengths, and opportunities; drafting the weekly QA report; and publishing the finalized report with supporting screenshots in Slack.

1. 1. Once all three sheets are filtered:Go to the **Customer Experience Review Sheet**.Navigate to the **Weekly Reporting** tab.Locate the **Weeks Table** to begin updating the data for each channel.

#### Step 4- Updating the “Calls” Section

1. In the **Weekly Reporting** tab, scroll to **Row 18** and focus on **Cells I18 and J18** under the “Calls” section.
1. Click on **Cell I18** to view the formula in the formula bar. It should look similar to: Responses!AG2154:AG2178** **This formula references the range of Calls audit data from the **Call Responses – Audits** sheet.
1. Return to the **Call Responses – Audits** sheet:From the filtered data, identify the **first row** and **last row** for the current week's audits.Update the formula in **Cell I18** using the correct row numbers.Example: Responses!AG2160:AG2180Repeat the same update in **Cell J18**.🔁 **Repeat this process** for the **SMS/Webchat** and **Email** sections by going to their respective rows and updating the formulas based on the filtered data from their response sheets.

#### Step 5- Updating the WoW Trends -Strengths WoW Trends-Opportunities

1. In the WoW Trends Section (The second table, Row 22): Copy the content from the **last three weeks**:Specifically, **Rows 24–26 **(Clear)  Paste the copied data into **Row 23**.This will shift previous entries up and leave **Row 26** blank — this row is reserved for the **current reporting week**.
1. Open each of the following response sheets:In each response sheet, go to the **TEAM SUMMARY** tab.In the TEAM SUMMARY tab  for each response sheet (starting around **Row 5**):Update the **week number** to reflect the **current reporting week**.Ensure that all TEAM SUMMARY tabs across all channels are using the **same week number**.
1. Once the week numbers are updated:Return to the **Customer Experience Review Sheet**.Go to the **Weekly Strengths Ops** tab.
1. Identify:**Top Opportunities** (lowest scores)**Top Strengths** (highest scores)
1. Record the findings in the respective fields. (Row 26)

#### Step 6- Updating the Top & Bottom performers table

1. Identify the bottom performers (highlighted in red) and **copy their names **Scroll to the right side, starting at **Cell J32**, to the **Top & Bottom Performers** sectionPaste the bottom performers on the bottom, copy the **Name**, **Sample size** (Total CX Reviews), **Average score** - Past 4 weeks.

Identify Top performersGo to the table: SUPS DASHBOARD WEEKLY PERFORMANCEChoose 3 associates with 100% scores, try to select associates with the most samples (Total CX Reviews section), and also choose associates that have all 4 **Number of  Self-assessment submissions**Copy these onto the table highlighted in green

#### Step 6- Updating the CX Reviewer goals

1. In the **Customer Experience Review Sheet**, go to the tab labeled **“CX Reviewer Goals.”**
1. Scroll to the **bottom of the sheet** and copy the table from the **previous week**.
1. Paste the copied table into a **new row** for the current reporting week.
1. Update the **week number** to reflect the week you are currently reporting.
1. Clear the content in the **“Goal”** and **“Actual”** columns to prepare for new data entry.
1. To confirm the reviewer assignments for the previous week:Open Slack and go to the[ **#quality-programs**](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/C022ZQQD056) channel.Search for the previous week's report titled something like:<br/>**“Week 30 Customer Experience Review Report.”**This post will list the **reviewer assignments** of the prior week.
1. Use the assignment information from that Slack post to **fill out the “Goal ” column** in the new row of the CX Reviewer Goals table.
1. Then, return to the **Weekly Reporting** tab in the same sheet.In the **first table**, you’ll see each auditor's **completion count**, which indicates the number of audits each auditor completed during the week.Use this data to fill in the **“Actual”** column in the CX Reviewer Goals table.Ensure that the “Assigned” and “Actual” numbers match up accurately with both the Slack post and the audit data.

#### Step 7- Verifying Associates’ Acknowledgment of Audit Scores

1. Open the file titled **"**[**Individual Results: Customer Experience Review (Responses).**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/13iotz2uwAImCSs49goI-0h1a20Bwfl9yZh5L0QhToJo/edit?gid=34806261#gid=34806261)**"**
1. Go to the tab named **“Form Responses 1.”**
1. Scroll to **Columns Q and R**:These columns show the **Week** and **Year** of acknowledgment.If you see any **blank cells** under the previous week's entries, **drag down the formulas** to auto-fill the missing data.
1. Next, look at **Column O**, labeled **"Your Name"**:**Copy the associate names** from Column O and **paste them into Column B**.
1. Then, go to **Column C**, labeled **“Week Reviewed”**:**Drag down the data** to ensure each entry reflects the correct week in which the associate reviewed their scores.These step ensures you're capturing accurate acknowledgment records for all associates.
1. In the same file, **"Individual Results: Customer Experience Review (Responses)"**, go to the tab labeled **“Pivot Table 1.”**
1. Ensure that there are **no filters applied** to the pivot table.
1. Within the pivot table:Locate the filter for **“Weeks Reviewed.”**Select **only the previous week** you are reporting (e.g., Week 30).
1. The filtered pivot table will now show acknowledgment data for that week.Focus on **Column L**, where the results are displayed.Any associate that shows `#N/A` in this column **did not review their audit scores** for the selected week.
1. Below the pivot table, locate the table spanning **Columns O to X**.
1. Add a **new row** beneath the last entry in that table**Update the week number** to reflect the current reporting week.
1. Go to **Column K** (Supervisor column) and:Apply a filter to **select only supervisors Lavina and Sheriza**.
1. Go to **Column L (Reviewed)** and **sort from A–Z**.
1. Count **all the associates listed**, including those with `#N/A`.
1. Enter this total in the row you added under **Column P: "Associates Expected to Review."**
1. Identify the entries marked as `#N/A` — these are associates who **did not** review their scores.<br/>Subtract this number from the total to calculate how many **did** review their scores, and enter that result under **Column Q: "Actual # of Associates Who Reviewed."**
1. The **compliance rate** for the team will automatically be calculated and shown under **Column R: "Total Team Compliance."**
1. Go back to **Column K (Supervisor)** and **sort from A–Z**.
1. This will group associates by supervisor, making it easier to **identify which associates from each team did not acknowledge their scores** (marked as `#N/A` in Column L).
1. Use this information to complete **Columns U and X**, which represent:**Column U:** Sheriza Team Compliance**Column X:** Lavina Team Compliance
1. To calculate each supervisor’s compliance percentage:**Count the total number of associates** under each supervisor.**Subtract the number of **`#N/A`** entries** to find how many acknowledged their scores.Then, enter a formula in the corresponding cell (U or X) as follows:Example for Sheriza (9 out of 10 acknowledged): =9/10Press enter and the formula will automatically provide the percentage.
1. If there are **no **`#N/A`** values**, that means **100% of the team acknowledged** their scores for that week.
