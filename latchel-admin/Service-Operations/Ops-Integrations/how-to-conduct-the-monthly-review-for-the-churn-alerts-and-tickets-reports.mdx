title: "How to conduct the Monthly Review for the Churn Alerts & Tickets reports"
---

<Note>
The purpose of this SOP is to provide a step-by-step guide on how to conduct the monthly review for the Churn Alerts and Tickets detected in Assembly for the PS Team’s review.
</Note>

<Tip>
This review is performed once a month, during the first week, and covers the results from the previous month.** For example**:_ If it is September 5th, the monthly review will cover the month of August._
</Tip>

## Step by step

### Step 1: Open the Churn Alerts / Ticket Reports Review Tracker

a. Go to the [**Churn Alerts / Ticket Reports Review**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1c-M6I0QcuDeeFHq2rzssZ9kurRsQiRJ2T-mtJ_YZJOA/edit?gid=1185773828#gid=1185773828) tracker and duplicate the three tabs you will need to fill out for the reporting month. These are:

- **Churn Alerts**
- **Tickets**
- **Month Analysis**

### Step 2: Rename the tabs with the corresponding month to review.

a. Right-click on each duplicated tab and update it with the month you are reviewing. **For example**: _If it is August 5th and you are reporting the information for July, make sure to add “July” to each tab._

### Step 3: Perform the Monthly Review

Depending on the tab you are filling in, make sure to follow these steps:

<Accordion title="Churn Alerts">
<Tip>
When a new alert appears in the [#service-changes](https://latchel.slack.com/archives/CKA2W1KJA) Slack channel, open the `Churn Alerts` tab in your monthly review sheet.
</Tip>

**a. Copy the following information from the Slack message:**

- Property Manager's Name (paste into the `Company Name` column).
- Date of the alert (paste into the `Date` column).
- Slack message link (paste into the `Slack Link` column).

**b. Gather Deal Information from HubSpot**

1. Click the `Link to Deal Record` in the Slack notification to open the deal in HubSpot.
2. Navigate to the `Desire to Churn Tracker` section.
3. Copy the following data and paste it into the corresponding columns in your sheet:
  - `Churn Status`
  - `Primary Reason `
  - `Additional Reason `
  - `MRR` (Monthly Recurring Revenue)

**c. Verify and Correct the MRR**

<Warning>
**Important:** The MRR value in HubSpot is often incorrect. **You must verify it directly in Rillet**. <u>The correct MRR is the value from the month </u>**<u>prior</u>**<u> to the churn alert</u>. (e.g., _for a July alert, you need June’s MRR_).
</Warning>

1. To verify, navigate to the Property Manager's profile in [**Rillet**](https://app.rillet.io/login?restore).
2. Find the accurate MRR for the previous month to the Churn alert.
3. Update the `MRR at Churn Request` column in your sheet with this correct value.

**d. Record Total CMR from HubSpot**

1. In HubSpot, go to the `Customer Account` profile.
2. Under the `Overview` section, find the `Total CMR`.
3. Copy this value and paste it into the `Total CMR` column in your sheet.

**e. Link Related Ticket Reports **

1. Identify all support tickets related to this Property Manager from the **three months prior** to the churn alert. (e.g., for a May alert, review tickets from February, March, and April).
2. Find the links to these ticket reports in **Assembly**.
3. Copy and paste all relevant Assembly links into the `Ticket Reports Related` column.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tickets">
**a. Go to Assembly and click on the Product Support section.**
1. Click on `Analytics`, and in the `Interactions section`, customize the required report.
2. Update the date range to download the previous month’s information. **For example**: _If it is September 5th, the monthly review will cover August 1st–31st._
3. In `+Add Filter` select `source` > `API`
4. Then click on `Download CSV`.

<Warning>
**Downloading the Assembly report can take a long time** — this is a common issue because their site has many problems. <u>If that happens, contact Assembly support to request the report and </u>**<u>provide the agent with the specific details needed</u>**.** **

**<u>If they do not reply within 24 hours</u>**<u>, escalate the issue to Trisha for help downloading the report.</u>
</Warning>

**b. Open a new Google sheet spreadsheet to filter the report.**

1. Ensure you have selected the filter in the top-right corner of the spreadsheet.
2. Click on **File > Import > Upload > Browse  > Select the report**.
3. Choose **Replace data at selected cell** and **click on Import data** to upload it correctly

**c. Filter the report to extract the information for the review.**

1. **Filter column G** to display only the issues that appear in the Tickets tab (Column D: Integration Reason and Column E: Emergency WOs Reason)
2. **Copy all the links** from column E of the downloaded report and **paste them into column F Link to Issue Report of the Tickets tab**.
3. **Copy the dates** that appear in column J of the report and **paste them into column C Date On Assembly of the Tickets tab.**

**d. Classify all the ticket reports.**

1. Open every ticket report in **Assembly**.
2. In the `Tickets` tab, enter the following:
  - `Property Manager`: Find the company name in Assembly (usually at the end of the report, e.g., "J&D Management").
  - `Type of Ticket Report`: Indicate if the ticket was a complaint from the customer (PM Ticket Complaint) or an internal issue Report (Ops Ticket Report).
  - `Reason`: Specify the ticket category (e.g., `Integrations Reason`, `Emergency Work Orders`).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Month Analysis">
**a. Complete the Analysis**

<Tip>
The `Analysis` tab automatically calculates trends and totals based on the data in the `Churn Alerts` and `Tickets` tabs.
</Tip>

1. You must manually copy the data from the `Ticket reports within 3 months before Churn Alert` column (located in the `Churn Alerts` tab) and paste it in the column E Ticket Reports related to the Month’s Analysis.

2. Paste this data into the corresponding section within the `Analysis` tab to complete the report.

**b. Update All Formulas**

<Tip>
All formulas referencing the previous month must be updated.
</Tip>

1. Go through the cells containing formulas in the new `Analysis` tab.
2. Carefully find every instance of the previous month's name (e.g., "June") within the formulas.
3. Replace it with the current month's name (e.g., "July").
4. Ensure **all** formulas are updated to pull data from the correct new tabs.

**a. Update All Formulas**

<Tip>
All formulas referencing the previous month must be updated.
</Tip>

1. Go through the cells containing formulas in the columns B,C and D of `Month's Totals` tab.
2. Carefully find every instance of the previous month's name (e.g., "June") within the formulas.
3. Replace it with the current month's name (e.g., "July").
4. Ensure **all** formulas are updated to pull data from the correct new tabs.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
Once you've finished updating all tabs, please let Trisha know so the _Ticket Analysis by PM 2025_ and _Churn Analysis by PM 2025_ can be updated before informing the PS Team. After you receive approval, proceed with the final step.
</Tip>
