<Note>
  The goal of the weekly reporting process is to proactively monitor property manager accounts, identify new or deleted properties with GL codes, detect specific gaps in compliance, and provide actionable data to the CSM. By doing so, we help customers correct their course, maximize profitability, and realize the full value of their partnership with Latchel.
</Note>

<Tip>
  To conduct the Assisted Ledgering weekly reviews, you should access the **[CPT Tracker](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1bQ45AVpZG5-ONwnSLFlJP8M5GCl7J__VczN1rfeCFQI/edit?gid=0#gid=0).**
</Tip>

## **Step by Step**

<Check>
  Consider the following steps before proceeding with the weekly reporting.
</Check>

### **Step 1 - Establish the criteria for a "ledgerable" property**

**a. Confirm Inclusion Criteria:** A property is considered for tracking only if the tenant signed a new or renewal lease _after_ the customer's "Onboard End Date."

**b. Confirm Exclusion Criteria:** Do **not** track properties that fall under these exceptions:

- The property is a commercial unit.
- The tenant's lease is under the Housing Choice Voucher Program (Section 8).
- The customer has explicitly excluded the property from the Resident Benefit Service.
- The tenant's lease was drafted _before_ the Latchel Service was introduced.

<Note>
  **Note:** An exception to the exception can be made. <u>If a PM adds a GL code to a Section 8 or HOA property, we will count it as ledgerable.</u>
</Note>

### **Step 2 - Analyze individual leases to determine compliance**

a. Access the customer's account through their Property Manager Software.

b. Review all upcoming and expiring leases for tenants who meet the criteria from Step 1.

c. For each lease, investigate two key items:

- _Is the Latchel lease addendum signed and included?_
- _Is the Latchel GL code included in the tenant's recurring charges?_

### **Step 3 - Update the tracker with the correct GL Code Status**

a. Based on your analysis in Step 2, update the "Assisted Ledgering Customers Tracker" for each lease:

<Info>
  **Mark as "With GL Code" if:** The GL code is present in the tenant's recurring charges (regardless of whether a lease addendum is present).

  **Mark as "No GL Code" if:** The GL code is **not** in the tenant's recurring charges. This is considered a "missed opportunity" that the CSM needs to address.
</Info>

### **Step 4 - Manage future-dated leases for follow-up**

a. If you identify a lease that starts in the future (e.g., today is Oct 14, lease starts Nov 1), do not assign a status yet.

b. Leave the "GL Code Status" column blank.

c. Add a remark with the lease start date (e.g., "Future lease: 2022-11-01") to create a reminder for future tracking.

### **Step 5 - Finalize the review and monitor ongoing progress**

a. Ensure that each Assisted-Ledgering customer's tracker is updated at least **once per week**, or before any scheduled meeting between the CSM and the customer.

<Note>
  On the 30th, 60th, and 90th day of the customer's program, capture their overall profitability percentage to track high-level monthly progress. The CSM will use this data in client conversations.
</Note>

<Warning>
  In **Assisted Ledgering**, **once their contract reaches the 12-month period, it will end**; you should stop doing their review. <u>Make sure to check the service start date for your PMs assigned to Assisted Ledgering.</u>
</Warning>
