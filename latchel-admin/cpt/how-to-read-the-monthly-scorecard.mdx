title: "How to: Read the Monthly Scorecard"
---

<Note>
  The purpose of this article is to be a guide of how to read and understand the Monthly Scorecard, which serves as a resource to have effective conversations with Property Managers, analyzing their performance and identifying pain points for further assistance.
</Note>

This will be shared with the Stakeholders every 6th of the month, before invoices have been sent, and the data contained will be from the previous month.

**Example:** The 6th of February the CPT will share the Monthly Scorecard with the data points of January.

## Overview

- **_Column A:_** Here you will find the list of all active Property Managers in Assisted Ledgering, Marketplace Benefits, and Optional Benefits for the month we are reporting.
- **Column B:**<u> </u>Here you will find the type of contract the PM has, either Assisted Ledgering, Marketplace Benefits, Optional Benefits, or a combination.
- **_Column C:_**<u> </u>Here you have the name of the **CSM** that the Property Manager account is assigned to.
- **_Column D:_** Here it is specified how many months the Property Manager has been in Service with Latchel.
- **_Column E:_** The **active units** of the month for each Property Manager are listed in this column. Active units are updated on the last day of the month before the working day ends. **Example:** _On_ \_January 31st we update that number, which will be reflected in February’s Scorecard.  <u>We should remember that active units are also a factor that affects the Property Manager’s RTP. </u>
- Column F: The **Road to Profitability percentage** with which the PM closed the month.

<Check>
  The PMs that are already profitable,were at some point or that are already paying full invoice will be marked in green in the column F with a note that they already are profitable and a note in the column I stating they are paying full invoice and will not be tracked in full anymore.
</Check>

- **_Column G:_** Here it is specified the **extension date as to when the contract will end**. This only applies if Customer Success grants the customer an extension after reaching their first 12 months on AL.
- **Column H: In this column, it is specified if the PM is receiving concessions or not. Example: <u>If the PM is profitable it will be marked “No”</u>**<u> and if the PM is still in the RTP it will be marked “Yes”.</u>
- **_Column I:_** This column shows the **expected RTP percentage** by the end of the Property Manager’s contract, based on the current trend the Property Manager is presenting. (Color code works the same as in column F).
- **_Column J:_** **The total units with a GL code** as of the last day of the month. For PMs who are tracked through the GL report, we will also type the total as of the last of the month.

<Check>
  The Profitability percentage is color-coded, showing in **green** the the Property Managers that have reached 100% of RTP or have exceeded the 100%. Property Managers with an RTP between 50% and 99.99% are shown in **yellow**. And lastly, you will see in red the Property Managers whose RTP is lower than 50%.

  If you see a percentage in **gray**, this means the CPT was not able to update the the RTP because the Property Manager did not send that month’s GL report, and as we rely on them because we do not have access to their PMS, the percentage from the previous month had to be kept.
</Check>

- _Column K:_ The amount of **units needed to reach profitability**.
- **_Column L (Only applicable for PMs on Optional Benefits):_**  The number of residents who opted in on OB during the previous month.
- **_Column M (Only applicable for PMs on Optional Benefits):_** The number of residents who opted out of OB during the previous month.
- **_Column N:_** Here you will find the Missed Opportunities that the Property Manager had by the end of the month. <u>This percentage represents the part of the properties that were ledgerable during that month but did not add a GL code. </u> Example: If the Property Manager had 20 properties that were ledgerable during that month, but 10 of them are missed opportunities, then the percentage shown here will be 50%.
- **Column O**: **The total of opportunities of the month**, those who are ledgerable or opt ins, including those who have GL codes and those who don’t but are part of missed opportunities.
- **_Columns P, Q, and R:_** These 3 columns contain the **3 types of Missed opportunities** that are considered to calculate the percentage of the month. Each column specifies the amount of missed opportunities for each type, of the last month.
- _Column S:_ The total amount of Month to month missed opportunities.
- **Column T:** Here you can find the **total amount of missed opportunities of the month with lease and addendum included**.
- **Column U:** Here you can find the **total amount of missed opportunities of the month**.

Types of Missed Opportunities.

New Leases with No GL code: For new residents that start renting a property after the Property Manager’s contract start date with Latchel.

**Renewals with no GL code:** This is when residents renew their leases, they are ledgerable, but the GL code is not added.

**New Month to Month Leases with no GL code:** When a resident has a monthly lease, it is considered ledgerable, but the GL code has not been added. <u>For this column, we will include only the recent month-to-month opportunities for the month of the current year.</u>

<Warning>
  **By Ledgerable** we mean that <u>the property lease was signed after the Latchel contract was signed</u> and <u>the property is not an HOA or Section 8.</u> (There could be exceptions to this depending on the PM).

  Note: There is an opportunity on Month to Months for CSM’s and CPT to better understand restrictions, regulations and exceptions (if any) to help PMs better with these opportunities.
</Warning>

- **_Column V:_** You see here the Month’s Success stories, if there is something positive to report for a Property Manager this month, it will be noted in this column.

Success Stories scenarios.

Became Profitable: You will see this scenario when the Property Manager reaches Profitability during the month we are reporting. On Track to Profitability: You will see this when, based on the current trend, the Property Manager is likely to reach profitability soon, or before the end of their contract. This should be used when the “Expected % Profitability after 12 months on Latchel” column becomes 100% or greater for the first time during the month you are reporting.

- **_Columns W, and X:_** The Month’s Major risks will be reported in these 2 columns. Column W will be used only when there is more than 1 risk to be reported.

Major Risks Scenarios.

**Removed our Access:** You will see this scenario when we used to have access to their Property Manager’s PMS but we do not have it anymore, or the access was restricted.

**Removed GL codes on all units:** This is included when the Property Manager had a certain amount of GL codes, but they **<u>were all deleted</u>** during the month we are reporting.

**Hasn’t set up GL codes:** This is used when the Property Manager did not add any GL codes during the month (for new customers this is equivalent to the former spotlight approach), or they removed some of them. And this will only be reported if the Property Manager had missed opportunities in the month being reported. <u>Ex. The Property Manager had 50 GL codes in December and kept the same number in January, this will be reported on January’s scorecard. Or, the Property Manager had 50 GL codes in December, and lowered the number to 45, this will be reported on January’s scorecard as well with this same Major Risk scenario.</u>

Started charging break-even rates: You will see this when the Property Manager is charging the same amount for Latchel’s GL code, than the amount they are being charged for each unit by Latchel.

No GL Report received: This is selected when the Property Manager did not send their GL report for the month being reported here. If they haven’t sent it, but you have access, you can download the report needed. This will be reported only if the CPT doesn’t have access to the Property Manager's PMS and the report is not received. However, you should keep in mind that it represents a higher impact if this is being reported for a Property Manager that has not granted access to their PMS, as the CPT will not be able to update their GL codes amount, nor their RTP in that month.

- **_Column Y:_** In this column we will include any additional steps to be taken or updates for the CSM to be informed. **Example:** _We haven't received any GL code information from Mission City Property Management since January to be able to know if they are profitable or not. However, we have the instruction of not asking for the GL report._
- **_Column Z:_** In this column, the CSM will include details of the action plan to work with each PM at risk.

<Check>
  The information reported here will be of use to plan the course of action for the Property Managers presenting any type of risk, which will be discussed during the “Activation and Ledgering” monthly meetings.
</Check>

For more information on the data points reported, see the section below.

<Check>
  ### **How are the main data points calculated:**

  - **% Profitability Attained to Date: We calculate this percentage based on the active units reported as of the last of the previous month x price per unit (if the resident has an <u>additional benefit other than AL such as Filters, Piñata or Optional Benefits</u>, we add the additional amount to the final price per unit). Then we calculate the GL codes added as of the last day of the previous month x the resident charge. Then we use both results, and we divide them to get the percentage for the profitability attained for the month reported.**  \
    \
    **Example:** \
    \
    **Doud Realty Services, Inc. has:** \
    \
    **255 active units x** \$9.99 = \$2547.45\
    218 GL Codes for April x \$**38.49 resident charge =** \$**8390.82** \
    \$8390.82/2547.45 = **329.38% Profitability attained to date**
  - Expected % Profitability after 12 months on Latchel: We calculate this percentage based on the profitability attained to date and divide them by the months of service the PM has during the month reported, and the result is multiplied by 12. This result is compared with:
  - % Profitability Attained to Date
  - % Renewals with no GL code  (Based on the number of GL codes added / number of active units x 100%)

  _Example:_ **Scarpa Properties has:**

  _Profitability attained to date: 131.38% / 3 Months of service during the month reported_ = 43.79 x 12 = **525.51%**

  _Profitability attained to date: 131.38% / Renewals with no GL code: 43.75% x 100% - Renewals with no GL code 43.75% \+ Profitability attained to date 131.38%_ = **387.93%**

  The two calculated values are compared to determine which is the lower. The lowest value between the two will be the one shown in column E.

  - Units needed to hit profitability/breakeven: \*\*We calculate the number of units needed for the PM to reach profitability by dividing the rest of the invoice amount for the months left / the resident charge. \
    \
    _Example_ \
    \
    **RPM Sunshine City** \
    \
    Rest of the invoice amount \$947.40 / Resident charge \$50 = 18.94 = 19
  - Opportunities Missed this month:

  This is the result calculated between the columns J and K x 100 -Total Missed Opportunities this Month

  - Total Opportunities this Month

  Example: \
  \
  **Legacy Oak Realty**

  _Total Missed Opportunities this Month 2 / Total Opportunities this Month_ 4 x 100 = **50.00%**
</Check>
