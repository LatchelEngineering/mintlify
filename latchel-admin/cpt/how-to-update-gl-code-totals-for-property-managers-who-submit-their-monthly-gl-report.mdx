---
title: "How to Update GL Code Totals for Property Managers Who Submit Their Monthly GL Report"
---

<Note>
  The purpose of this SOP is to provide a step-by-step guide on how to report GL code totals on a monthly basis, ensuring accurate data and avoiding incorrect reporting to Finance when applicable.
</Note>

<Accordion title="Metropolitan Real Estate">
  ### Step 1: Verify whether the GL report has been received.

  a. Go to the Assisted Ledgering email and **search for the property manager's name** in the search bar.

  <Frame>
    ![picture.jpg](/images/CPT-660.jpg)
  </Frame>

  ### Step 2: Locate the Rent Roll and Recurring Charges report.

  a. Select **open with \> Google sheets** to open the report.

  <Frame>
    ![picture 2.jpg](/images/CPT-661.jpg)
  </Frame>

  b. A new window with the report will be opened.

  ### Step 3: Customize the report so you can count the properties with GL codes as of the end of the month.

  a. **Click the top-left corner** between column A and row 1 to **apply a filter**.

  <Frame>
    ![picture 3.jpg](/images/CPT-662.jpg)
  </Frame>

  b. Once the filter is applied, click the three lines in column F, select **only the GL code amount**, and **click OK**.

  <Frame>
    ![picture 4.jpg](/images/CPT-663.jpg)
  </Frame>

  ### Step 4: Select all residents shown in column B to see the total number who had a GL code.

  a. Count the total number of residents with a GL code as of the end of the month.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-171551.png](/images/CPT-664.png)
  </Frame>

  b. You can confirm the full amount in the bottom-right corner.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-171719.png](/images/CPT-665.png)
  </Frame>

  ### Step 5: Add the total GL codes in the [CPT Tracker](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1bQ45AVpZG5-ONwnSLFlJP8M5GCl7J__VczN1rfeCFQI/edit?gid=0#gid=0).

  a. Go to the **CPT Tracker \> Overall Details tab**, and **locate the Property Manager** you’ll be reporting. b. **Type the total GL codes in column E** (With GL code) of the corresponding Property Manager.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-172123.png](/images/CPT-666.png)
  </Frame>

  c. Update the date in column C.

  Since this property manager is already profitable and part of Assisted Ledgering, it isn’t necessary to add the total number of GL codes to the Billing Inputs document.

  ### Step 6: Organize the email received with the reports in the corresponding month’s folder.

  a. **Add the corresponding label**, so the received reports can be organized in the appropriate email folder and uploaded to the correct Drive folder automatically.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-174000.png](/images/CPT-667.png)
  </Frame>

  <Check>
    Example: It’s July 1st and you’re looking for the June report. The label applied should be 'GL Reports 2025/June'.
  </Check>

  A '**Processed' label** will be applied automatically, indicating that the report was successfully uploaded to the Drive folder.
</Accordion>

  <Frame>
    ![picture 5.jpg](/images/CPT-668.jpg)
  </Frame>

  ### Step 2: Locate the attached reports and label the email in the corresponding monthly folder.

  a. The reports will be attached at the bottom of the email message.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-173558.png](/images/CPT-669.png)
  </Frame>

  b. **Add the corresponding label**, so the received reports can be organized in the appropriate email folder and uploaded to the correct Drive folder automatically.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-174000.png](/images/CPT-670.png)
  </Frame>

  <Check>
    Example: It’s July 1st and you’re looking for the June report. The label applied should be 'GL Reports 2025/June'.
  </Check>

  A '**Processed' label** will be applied automatically, indicating that the report was successfully uploaded to the Drive folder.

  ### Step 3: Report the total number of GL codes mentioned in the received email in the [Billing Inputs document](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1HG_bRF-JM8DqSz1lusDrfJVGIpohvJwBP4fNAipclvM/edit?gid=494873615#gid=494873615).

  a. **Locate the total number of GL codes in the email body.** That will be the number to report.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-175100.png](/images/CPT-671.png)
  </Frame>

  b. **Go to the Billing Inputs** document and **search for the corresponding month's tab**. **Example:** _The totals for June will be reported in the July tab._ c. Search for the property manager’s name and **add the total number in column T** of the corresponding row.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-175522.png](/images/CPT-672.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="RHOME">
  ### Step 1: Verify whether the GL report has been received.

  a. Go to the Assisted Ledgering email and **search for the property manager's name** in the search bar.

  <Frame>
    ![picture 6.jpg](/images/CPT-673.jpg)
  </Frame>

  ### Step 2: Locate and open the General Ledger report.

  a. Select **open with \> Google sheets** to open the report.

  <Frame>
    ![picture 7.jpg](/images/CPT-674.jpg)
  </Frame>

  ### Step 3: Customize the reports so you can count the properties with GL codes as of the end of the month.

  a. **Click the top-left corner** between column A and row 1 to **apply a filter**.

  <Frame>
    ![Picture 9.jpg](/images/CPT-675.jpg)
  </Frame>

  b. Once the filter is applied, click the three lines in column G (Credit column), select **only the GL code amount**, and **click OK**.

  <Frame>
    ![Picture 10.jpg](/images/CPT-676.jpg)
  </Frame>

  ### Step 4: Select all residents shown in column C to see the total number who had a GL code.

  a. Count the total number of residents with a GL code for the corresponding month. **Example:** _If the month you’re reporting is July, check column B to see the date when the GL code was applied. Residents with a different date should not be counted._

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-183110.png](/images/CPT-677.png)
  </Frame>

  <Warning>
    If a resident appears in multiple reports, count them only once. However, if a resident is repeated within the same report, count each GL code separately.
  </Warning>

  ### Step 5: Report the total number of GL codes in the [Billing Inputs document](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1HG_bRF-JM8DqSz1lusDrfJVGIpohvJwBP4fNAipclvM/edit?gid=494873615#gid=494873615).

  a. **Go to the Billing Inputs** document and **search for the corresponding month's tab**. **Example:** _The totals for July will be reported in the August tab._ b. Search for the property manager’s name and **add the total number in column T** of the corresponding row.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-183906.png](/images/CPT-678.png)
  </Frame>

  c. Repeat the same process for all RHOME portfolios.

  ### Step 6: Organize the email received with the reports in the corresponding month’s folder.

  a. **Add the corresponding label**, so the received reports can be organized in the appropriate email folder and uploaded to the correct Drive folder automatically.

  <Frame>
    ![image-20250806-174000.png](/images/CPT-679.png)
  </Frame>

  <Check>
    Example: It’s July 1st and you’re looking for the June report. The label applied should be 'GL Reports 2025/June'.
  </Check>

  A '**Processed' label** will be applied automatically, indicating that the report was successfully uploaded to the Drive folder.
</Accordion>
