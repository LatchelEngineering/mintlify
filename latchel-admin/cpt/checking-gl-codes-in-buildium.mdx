title: "Checking GL codes in Buildium"
---

<Note>
The purpose of this document is to provide a guide on how to navigate the Buildium tool to be able to track the GL codes for each new added property into the tracker.
</Note>

## **Step-by-step guide**

### **Step 1 - Access the tenant's financial records**

a. Log in to the PM's Buildium account.

b. Use the main search bar to find the tenant you are investigating.

c. From the search results, click on **Lease Ledger** to go directly to their financial profile.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-165.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

### **Step 2 - Verify if an active recurring charge exists**

a. On the tenant's financial page, navigate to the **Recurring transactions** tab.

b. Scan the list for a recurring charge related to Latchel (e.g., "Resident Benefits Package," "Amenity Fee"). This confirms if an automated charge is set up.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-166.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

### **Step 3 - Audit the ledger for historical charges and payments**

a. To confirm past charges and payments, navigate to the **Ledger** tab.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-167.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

b. Scroll through the transaction history to find the first entry labeled as a **"Charge"** with the Latchel GL code in the "Account" description. Note the date.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-168.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

c. To confirm if that charge was paid, look for the subsequent **"Payment"** entry.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-169.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

d. Click on the payment entry to open its details.

- **PAID:** If the Latchel charge amount is listed in the **"Amount"** column, it has been paid.
- **UNPAID:** If the charge amount is only listed in the **"Balance"** column, it remains unpaid.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-170.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

### **Step 4 - Investigate for future-scheduled charges**

a. This is crucial for new or renewing leases. Navigate back to the **Recurring transactions** tab.

b. If you see a recurring charge for Latchel, click on it to open the details.

c. Confirm the "Account" is correct.

d. Scroll to the bottom of the description chart to the **"Example"** section. This section will show the **start date** for the recurring charge, confirming when it is scheduled to begin.
