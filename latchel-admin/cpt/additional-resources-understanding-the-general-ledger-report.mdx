<Note>
The purpose of this document is to provide the information required to understand the General Ledger report, what it displays and how to identify the data we need.
</Note>

### **Step 1 - Confirm the report's scope and identify key columns**

a. Open the downloaded General Ledger report.

The General Ledger Report (GL Report) needs to be sent to our Finance department the last day of each month, therefore the** date range** selected needs to be from the 1st of the month, to the last day of the previous month.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-302.png" alt="20.png" />
</Frame>

b. First, confirm the report covers the correct date range, which should be from the **1st of the month** to the **last day of the month.**

c. Identify the key columns you will be working with:

- `Description`: Contains property/tenant details.
- `Credit`: Contains the **income** charges we need to count.
- `Debit`: Contains the **expense** charges we need to ignore.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-303.png" alt="41.png" />
</Frame>

<Warning>
In the report there will be other charges listed as **(expense)**, that are usually reflected in the **debit** column, and that do not have a tenant’s name. We will <u>not </u>include these charges in our monthly record.
</Warning>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-304.png" alt="50.png" />
</Frame>

### **Step 2 - Filter out irrelevant data to isolate income charges**

a. Focus **only** on transactions listed as `(income)`.

b. These are the charges you will count, and they are typically found in the **Credit** column.

c. **Ignore** all transactions listed as `(expense)`.

d. These are usually found in the **Debit** column and often do not have a tenant's name attached. These should **not** be included in your final count.

## How to manually calculate the GL codes total.

### **Step 1 - Calculate the total count of billable GL codes**

a. First, check the very end of the report. Some reports will provide a total count of tenants who have the Latchel charge. If so, your work is done.

b. If no total count is provided, you must calculate it manually using one of the following two methods:

- **Method A (The "Easy Path"): If all income charges are for the same amount (e.g., $20):** Divide the total sum from the `Credit` column by the standard charge amount.

_Example: `$4,140 (Total Credit) / $20 (Standard Charge) = 207 total GL codes._

- **Method B (The "Manual Count"): If the income charges have varying amounts:**

You must manually count each individual `(income)` / `Credit` line item one by one to get the accurate total.
