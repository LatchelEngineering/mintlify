---
title: "How to Do the Monthly Review in AppFolio for Clients Whose Optional Benefits Contract Has Ended"
---

<Note>
The purpose of this SOP is to provide a step-by-step guide on how to perform a monthly review in AppFolio for clients whose Optional Benefits contract has already ended. Since active units will still be updated by the Data Team, a modified review must be conducted each month.
</Note>

<Warning>
This process only applies to **<u>property managers who have reached the 12-month contract mark and have GL codes.</u>**
</Warning>

<Check>
Update the **active units (Column T) **in the** **_Optional Benefits Overall Details _of the [**CPT Tracker**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1bQ45AVpZG5-ONwnSLFlJP8M5GCl7J__VczN1rfeCFQI/edit?gid=1722062496#gid=1722062496) until you finish the OB Review **only every 1st of the month**, as the active units total will be the same as the one reported and sent to the Data team each month. **<u>This applies to any PM who has GL codes.</u>**
</Check>

## Step by step

## **Step 1: Download the Rent Roll Itemized report**

a. Log in to AppFolio.

b. Navigate to Reports > Rent Roll (Itemized).

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-690.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

## **Step 2: Filter and Update the Rent Roll Report**

a. Search for the GL Code

- You can find the GL code for the Property Manager (PM) in the [**<u>CPT Tracker</u>**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1bQ45AVpZG5-ONwnSLFlJP8M5GCl7J__VczN1rfeCFQI/edit?gid=0#gid=0), under the **Optional Benefits Overall Details** tab.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-691.png" alt="image-20250730-201400.png" />
</Frame>

b. Set the Filters

- **Units:** Select **"Show Active Units"**
- **Search for the GL code**
- **As of Date:** Enter the date of your review

c. Click** "Update"** to generate the filtered report.

<Check>
**Note:** _Select the last day of the previous month as the reporting date. Since the Optional Benefits review is done once a month, the date should be the last day of the previous month to report.** Example:** It’s July 03rd, you will download the report to do the review of June. Please select 06/30/2025._
</Check>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-692.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

## **Step 3: Customize and Export the Rent Roll Report**

a. Click on Customize Columns and ensure the following fields are selected:

- _Unit_
- _Status_
- _Property Address_
- _Tenant_
- _Lease From_
- _Lease To_
- _Eligible For Rent Increase_
- _Move-in_
- _Move-out_

b. Once customized, go to Actions and click Export as CSV.

<Check>
**Note: **_Always verify the unit count (found at the right bottom of the report) before proceeding to ensure accuracy._
</Check>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-693.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

## **Step 4: Set Up the Spreadsheet**

a. Open a new spreadsheet (Google Sheets).

b. Click the blank cell in the upper-left corner (between Column A and Row 1) to select the entire sheet.

c. Click the Filter icon (or go to Data > Create a Filter) to enable filters for all columns.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-694.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

## **Step 5: Import the Exported Rent Roll File**

a. In your new spreadsheet, go to **File > Import**.

b. Click the **Upload** tab.
c. Click **Browse** and select the **CSV file** you exported from PMS.

d. When prompted, choose **"Replace data at selected cell"**.

e. Click **Import data** to load the data into your spreadsheet.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-695.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

## **Step 6: Rearrange Columns**

a. Move **Column A (Unit) to Column C** to match the required format.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-696.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

## **Step 7: Sort and Verify the Data**

a. Click the Filter icon on the column headers.

b. Sort the data A to Z to organize the properties.

<Warning>
**Note:**_ If the report contains more than 300 properties, click Column B, right-click, and select "Sort sheet A to Z"._
</Warning>

c. Verify that the unit counts match the PMS report for accuracy.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-697.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

d. **Click on column E (GL Codes column) **to view the total as of the end of the previous month

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-698.jpg" alt="picture 17.jpg" />
</Frame>

If you’re unable to see all GL codes together in the column, make sure to **apply the filter by selecting column A and choosing 'Sort A to Z'.**

e. **Select all residents in column D who have a GL code in column E**. To confirm the total, check the bottom-right section. This will be total reported in the Billing inputs doc in the tab of the corresponding month.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-699.jpg" alt="Picture 18.jpg" />
</Frame>

### Step 8: Go to the Billing inputs doc and report the total amount of GL codes.

<Check>
If you’re reporting the total for September, you should look for the October tab.
</Check>

a. Search for the property manager’s name and input the total number in **column Z** (# of Benefits Opt Ins).

<Warning>
This step is currently applicable to DS Huber. For other property managers whose Optional Benefits contract has already ended and have GL codes, you should confirm with Will whether the update should be made in the same column or a different one.
</Warning>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-700.png" alt="image-20250918-220736.png" />
</Frame>
