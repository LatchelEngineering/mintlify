<Note>
The purpose of this document is to provide the information needed to be able to check leases and addendums in Buildium to be able to report this in the Tracker.
</Note>

## Checking Leases

## **Step-by-step**

### **Step 1 - Access the tenant's lease summary and file repository**

a. Log in to the PM's Buildium account.

b. Use the main search bar to find the tenant you are investigating.

c. From the search results, click on **Lease Ledger** to go directly to their profile.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-171.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

### **Step 2 - Verify key lease dates and locate the lease document**

a. On the tenant's profile, navigate to the **Summary** tab.

b. Review the **"Renewal history"** section to confirm:

`- Status`: The lease is active.

`- Start - End`: The current lease term dates. This is critical to determine if the lease was signed after the PM partnered with Latchel.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-172.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

c. Look for the lease document itself in one of two places:

- **Primary Location:** The **"Recent files"** section on the Summary tab.
- **Alternative Location:** The main **"Files"** tab, which contains a complete list of all documents associated with the tenant.

<Check>
You can also check the lease start date to make sure it was within the time period the PM acquired Latchel and confirm if this should or should not have a GL code.
</Check>

### **Step 3 - Inspect all documents to find the Latchel addendum**

a. Once you locate the main lease document, open it.

b. Use **Ctrl+F** to search for keywords like "Latchel," "maintenance," "resident benefit," or any other relevant terms.

<Check>
**IMPORTANT:** An addendum can be validated in two ways:

1. The formal addendum document is present.
2. The lease explicitly states the charge amount and its purpose is for Latchel's services. This also counts as a valid addendum.
</Check>

The addendum is often a separate file. If you do not find it within the main lease agreement, you **must** review every other document in the **"Files"** tab to ensure you do not miss it.
