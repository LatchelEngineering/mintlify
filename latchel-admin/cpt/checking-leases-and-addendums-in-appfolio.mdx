<Note>
The purpose of this document is to provide the information needed to be able to check leases and addendums in Appfolio to be able to report this in the Tracker. The goal is to provide a reliable method to find and verify a tenant's lease agreement and any associated Resident Benefit Program addendums. This ensures our Assisted Ledgering tracker is accurate, empowering CSMs to confidently report on compliance and help customers maximize their profitability.
</Note>

## **Checking Leases**

### **Step-by-step guide**

### **Step 1 - Find and access the primary lease document for a specific tenant**

a. Log in to the PM's Appfolio account.

b. Use the main search bar to find the tenant by name, ensuring you select the correct person by verifying the property address and the **(Current)** status.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-71.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

c. On the tenant's **Summary** page, scroll down to the **"Lease Information"** section.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-72.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

e. Click on the link in the **"Lease documents"** section to open the primary lease file.

<Check>
_Alternative Location:_ If no document is found there, also check the **"Attachments"** section on the same page, as some PMs store leases there instead.
</Check>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/CPT-73.png" alt="" />
</Frame>

### **Step 2 - Confirm if the tenant is contractually obligated via an addendum**

a. With the lease document open, use **Ctrl+F** to search for keywords like "Latchel," "resident benefit," "addendum," or the specific charge amount.

<Check>
**IMPORTANT:** We consider a property ledgerable if _any_ resident benefit addendum is present, even if it does not explicitly mention "Latchel."
</Check>

b. The addendum is often a separate file. If you cannot find it within the main lease document, you must expand your search. Review all files in these locations on the tenant's profile:

- The **"Attachments"** section.
- The **"Email"** tab.
- The **"Letters"** tab.

The final success criterion is finding the service and charge amount documented. As long as the charge amount is stated in _any_ of these documents (lease, addendum, letter, etc.), it is considered a valid and active addendum for our tracking purposes.
