  The purpose of this document is to describe the types of reports found in DoorLoop, to be able to identify the particular features they offer, and recognize how they could be useful for our purposes.
</Note>

# Types of reports and how they work/information they display

<Check>
  In DoorLoop we have default reports we can use, but they can also be customized.
</Check>

<Accordion title="ePay Transactions Report">
  If you need to view all ePay payment transactions made by tenants or any ePay payments that you made to vendors, owners, or tenants, you'll see them all here in the ePay Transactions report. The ePay Transactions report will also show other ePay items such as Expense payments, bill payments, and refunds if you chose to use ePay as the payment method.

  <Frame>
    ![00000000000.png](/images/CPT-354.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Lease Late Fees Report">
  The Lease Late Fees report is where you would be able to view all leases and see whether or not they have Late Fees active or not. While there is other useful information on the report such as the lease start and end date, the renewal status, and how much their current rent is, the purpose of this report is just to view if Lease Late Fees are active or not. However, and interesting feature of this report is that you can actually turn late fees on and off from here as well as do that in bulk, which can save you time.

  <Frame>
    ![000000000000.png](/images/CPT-355.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Management Fees Report">
  The Management Fees Report is where you'll now go to see a list of all properties and which ones of those have management fees attached to them. This way, you no longer need to go into each property individually to see if they have management fees on them or not. The Management Fees Report is also a great place to manage the management fees in bulk.

  <Frame>
    ![0000000000000.png](/images/CPT-356.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Owner Statement Report">
  This is a report that provides a summary of financial transactions and information related to a property owner's real estate investment. This statement is typically prepared and provided by the property management company or the property manager to the property owner on a regular basis, usually monthly or quarterly. The owner's statement serves as a clear and concise record of the property's financial performance during a specific period. It includes details on income received and expenses incurred, allowing the property owner to understand the financial health of their investment property.

  <Frame>
    ![00000000000000.png](/images/CPT-357.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Undeposited Funds Report">
  Sometimes the Property Manager will need to confirm if a payment was has been deposited to their account or not, a quick way to check the undeposited funds is to look at this report.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Deleted Transactions List Report">
  While not a part of the default list of reports, you can create a Deleted Transactions List Report with a few clicks. This report is useful because you can see when certain transactions were deleted and also who deleted them. This allows for some accountability on your account to view who made changes.

  <Frame>
    ![000000000000000.png](/images/CPT-358.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Rent Roll Report">
  A rent roll report provides a comprehensive overview of a property, detailing key information about each leased unit or space within a property. The primary purpose of a rent roll report is to help property owners and managers track rental income, assess the financial performance of a property, and make informed decisions about their real estate investments.

  <Frame>
    ![0000000000000000.png](/images/CPT-359.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Recurring Payments (Auto-Pay) Report">
  If you need to quickly see which of the tenants have auto-pay set up, you can view this on this report. Note that this report will show all recurring payments, including those created by the property manager and those created by the tenant.

  <Frame>
    ![00000000000000000.png](/images/CPT-360.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cash Flow Statement Report">
  The cash flow statement reports the cash generated and spent during a specific period of time In respect to DoorLoop, the Cash Flow Statement report will show money coming in and going out, so all income that is received from all different sources, and expenses and distributions going out. After deducting all held liabilities, this will show the total amount of cash that is available.

  <Frame>
    ![000000000000000000.png](/images/CPT-361.png)
  </Frame>
</Accordion>

## Features within reports

As mentioned before many of the reports are customizable and can be filtered to show a specific set of data depending on your needs. The Group By filter is used to sort your report and arrange them by a specific set of data. Using the Transactions List Report, you can group all the data by **Date**, **Type**, **Name**, **Account**, **Property**, **Payment Method**, and **Deposit**. This however will be different based on the report you are using.
