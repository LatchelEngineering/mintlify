## What is the Product Release?

We are thrilled to announce Latchel's brand new Work Order Detail experience. We have fully re-designed the work order detail page and our updates include:

- A new look and feel, bringing a modern touch to your work order management
- Prioritized visibility for open tasks that need your attention
- A reorganized structure for quicker access to the information you need

## What was the Previous State?

The previous work order design had all information on one page and it was very difficult to find specific/important information. It was also very difficult to quickly understand what needed to be done on a work order to move it forward.

  ![Previous work order design](/images/Eng-114.png)

## Who Is Impacted?

All Latchel Users. PMs and Ops/Admins have different default views, but we will be rolling a feature out in the future to give Admin and Ops visibility into what it looks like on the PM side. Until then, Ops/Admins can access help guides or the PM-specific tutorial for reference.

## When Is It Relevant?

This new design applies to any work order page in the Latchel App.

## How Is It Used?

With the exception of the new functionalities below, the content is almost all the same between the old and new design. It's instead just broken out into different places. Please reference the videos below for a walk through for the PM and Ops/Admin views.

### High-Level List of Changes and New Functionalities

- The Take Action drop down has been renamed to an Action drop down.
- Information is broken out into different tabs.
- Critical Notes are separated and will have a blue dot if there are any critical notes to consider for the WO.
- Activity log will stay in the place that a user leaves it when going to a different tab.
- Description is now called the Summary.
- Work Order Summary is now called Special Note. The Special Note tab will have a blue dot if there is something populated.
- Create a Note and Create a Task have been consolidated into the Action drop down. Users are now able to create a note or task while clicking between any tabs within the WO detail page.
- The most recent open tasks will be shown as floating notifications in the top right corner of the page.
  - We will only show a maximum of two open tasks in the floating notifications. If there are more than two, users can press View All to look at the full open task log.
  - Users have the option to hide these.
  - They will always show up on default when you open a WO or refresh the page.
- Clicking View Details will bring the user to the exact place in the activity log where it was created. This makes it easy for users to see the context around when the task was created.

## What Are Some Risks or Ways Things Could Go Wrong?

PMs are being launched on the new WO detail page on 5/16 and Ops is being launched on 5/30. There's a potential risk around the Ops deployment being 14 days after the PM deployment. If PMs have any questions specifically about the new design, the majority of the Ops team will not be able to support since they will not be trained or have that view. Some actions that Ops is considering to mitigate this risk are:

- Prioritize a fast and condensed training video focusing on key aspects of the new view.
- Set up dedicated channels for the new feature where the trained coaches can handle escalations related to this new view.
- Objective: untrained support team members can quickly consult trained coaches to clarify doubts or seek guidance in dealing with new feature-related queries.
- Assemble a comprehensive list of customer work order resources crafted by Malen's team. We can create this in the help center so associates only need to link to a single help article page, where the customer will find a directory of all work order guides.
- Refresh everyone's memory on escalation protocols: What to do if the answer eludes us? --\> Product Support ticket.
- If possible, ensure trained associates are working during peak SMS and Webchat hours until everyone is trained up.
